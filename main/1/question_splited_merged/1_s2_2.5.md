Problem 2.5. The beta-decay of a neutron is isotropic in the rest frame of the neutron, with the velocity of the emitted electron $\mathbf{v}_{\mathbf{e}} = 0.77$ . If the neutron is moving with velocity $\beta$ through the laboratory, what values of the electron's laboratory momentum vector $\underline{\mathbf{P}}$ are possible?

## Solution

Solution 2.5. Let the neutron travel in the $\mathbf{x}$ direction. In its rest frame the electron has momentum 4-vector
$$
\mathbf {P} ^ {\prime} = \left(\mathrm {E} ^ {\prime}, \mathrm {P} ^ {\prime} \cos \theta , \mathrm {P} ^ {\prime} \sin \theta , 0\right), \quad \mathrm {E} ^ {\prime} = m _ {\mathrm {e}} \left(1 - v _ {\mathrm {e}} ^ {2}\right) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}}
$$
(where we have, without loss of generality, oriented our coordinate system so $\underline{\mathbf{P}}'$ is in the x-y plane), and $\mathbf{P}'$ is isotropic. Using the Lorentz transformation to get the lab momentum $\underline{\mathbf{P}}$ , we have:
$$
\mathrm {P} _ {\mathbf {x}} = \gamma \left(\mathrm {P} ^ {\prime} \cos \theta + \beta \mathrm {E} ^ {\prime}\right)
$$
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {y}} = \mathbf {P} ^ {\prime} \sin \theta
$$
or
$$
\left(\frac {\mathrm {P _ {x}} - \gamma \beta \mathrm {E ^ {\prime}}}{\gamma \mathrm {P ^ {\prime}}}\right) ^ {2} + \left(\frac {\mathrm {P _ {y}}}{\mathrm {P ^ {\prime}}}\right) ^ {2} = 1.
$$
The last equation says that in the lab, the momentum vector in momentum space lies on an ellipse of semi major axis $\gamma \mathbf{P}^{\prime}$ , semiminor axis $\mathbf{P}^{\prime}$ , and origin $(\gamma \beta \mathbf{E}^{\prime}, 0)$ . The plot of such an ellipse falls into three cases:
