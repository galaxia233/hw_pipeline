Problem 17.1. Show that the constant M which occurs in the Kerr metric is the mass of the system, and that the constant a is the angular momentum per mass.

## Solution

Solution 17.1. One finds the mass and angular momentum of an asymptotically Minkowskian (flat) metric by finding a coordinate system in which it takes the form
$$
\mathrm {g} _ {0 0} = - \left(1 - \frac {2 \tilde {\mathrm {M}}}{\mathrm {r}} + \mathcal {O} (\mathrm {r} ^ {- 2})\right) \tag {1}
$$
$$
\mathbf {g} _ {0 j} = - \left(4 \varepsilon_ {j k l} \tilde {\mathbf {S}} \frac {\mathbf {k} \mathbf {x} ^ {1}}{\mathbf {r} ^ {3}} + \mathcal {O} (\mathbf {r} ^ {- 3})\right). \tag {2}
$$
The constants $\tilde{\mathbf{M}}$ and $\tilde{\mathbf{S}}^k$ which occur in the expansion are then the mass and intrinsic angular momentum (see Weinberg, Section 9.4 or MTW, Section 19.3).
Expanding the Kerr metric in Boyer-Lindquist coordinates in powers of $\mathbf{r}^{-1}$ gives the leading terms
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = - \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}} + \dots\right) \mathrm {d t} ^ {2} - \left(\frac {4 \mathrm {a M}}{\mathrm {r}} \sin^ {2} \theta + \dots\right) \mathrm {d t} \mathrm {d} \phi \\ + (1 + \dots) [ d r ^ {2} + r ^ {2} (d \theta^ {2} + \sin^ {2} \theta d \phi^ {2}) ] \\ \end{array}
$$
Transforming to cartesian coordinates by $\mathbf{x} \equiv \mathbf{r} \sin \theta \cos \phi$ , $\mathbf{y} \equiv \mathbf{r} \sin \theta \sin \phi$ , $\mathbf{z} \equiv \mathbf{r} \cos \theta$ gives
$$
\begin{array}{l} d s ^ {2} = - \left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r} + \dots\right) d t ^ {2} - \left(\frac {4 a M}{r ^ {3}} + \dots\right) (x d y - y d x) d t \tag {3} \\ + (1 + \dots) (\mathrm {d} x ^ {2} + \mathrm {d} y ^ {2} + \mathrm {d} z ^ {2}) \\ \end{array}
$$
and comparison with Equations (1) and (2) gives at once
$$
\tilde {\mathbf {M}} = \mathbf {M}
$$
$$
\tilde {\mathbf {S}} = \mathbf {a M} \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {z}}} ^ {-}.
$$
