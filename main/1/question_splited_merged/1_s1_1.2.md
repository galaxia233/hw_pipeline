Problem 1.2. Find the matrix for the Lorentz transformation consisting of a boost $\mathbf{v}_{\mathbf{x}}$ in the $\mathbf{x}$ -direction followed by a boost $\mathbf{v}_{\mathbf{y}}$ in the $\mathbf{y}$ -direction. Show that the boosts performed in the reverse order would give a different transformation.

## Solution

Solution 1.2. The product of the transformations is
$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} & 0 & \underline {{0}} \\ \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \underline {{1}} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} & 0 & \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {y}} & \underline {{0}} \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {y}} & 0 & \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \underline {{1}} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {y}} & \underline {{0}} \\ \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {y}} & 0 \\ \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {y}} & 0 & \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \underline {{1}} \end{array} \right],
$$
but
$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} & 0 & \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {y}} & \overline {{0}} \\ 0 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {y}} & 0 & \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \underline {{1}} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} & 0 & \overline {{0}} \\ \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \underline {{1}} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {y}} & \overline {{0}} \\ \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} & 0 & 0 \\ \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {y}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {x}} \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {y}} & \gamma_ {\mathbf {y}} & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & \underline {{1}} \end{array} \right]
$$
which is different.
