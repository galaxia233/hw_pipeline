10.5. The metric for an axially symmetric rotating star admits two Killing vectors, $\pmb{\xi}_{(t)}$ and $\pmb{\xi}_{(\phi)}$ . Assume there are no other independent Killing vectors. Prove that $\pmb{\xi}_{(t)}$ and $\pmb{\xi}_{(\phi)}$ commute.

## Solution

Solution 10.5. The commutator must be a Killing field according to Problem 10.3. By hypothesis any Killing vector must be a sum (with constant coefficients) of $\xi_{(t)}$ and $\xi_{(\phi)}$ :
$$
\left[ \boldsymbol {\xi} _ {(\phi)}, \boldsymbol {\xi} _ {(t)} \right] = a \boldsymbol {\xi} _ {(\phi)} + b \boldsymbol {\xi} _ {(t)}.
$$
At infinity $\xi_{(\phi)}\rightarrow \partial /\partial \phi$ and $\pmb{\xi}_{(t)}\rightarrow \partial /\partial t$ so the commutator $\to 0$ . This means that a and b must be zero, i.e. that the commutator vanishes everywhere. (B. Carter, [Commun. Math. Phys. 17, 233 (1970)] has proven that $[\pmb {\xi}_{(\phi)},\pmb {\xi}_{(t)}] = 0$ under very general conditions even when there are other Killing vectors.)
