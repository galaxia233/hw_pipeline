Problem 5.14. In an electron gas of temperature $\mathrm{T} < \mathrm{m_e c^2 / k}$ a photon of energy $\mathbf{E} < \mathbf{m_e c^2}$ undergoes collisions and is Compton scattered. Show that, to lowest order in $\mathbf{E}$ and $\mathbf{T}$ the average energy lost by a photon in a collision is
$$
<   \Delta E > = (E / m _ {e} c ^ {2}) (E - 4 k T).
$$

## Solution

Solution 5.14. Let the average transfer of energy be $\langle \Delta E \rangle$ . We are given that $\mathrm{E} / \mathrm{mc}^2 \ll 1$ and $\mathrm{T} / \mathrm{mc}^2 \ll 1$ (units with $k = 1$ ), so we want only the first nonvanishing terms in the double series expansion
$$
\begin{array}{l} <   \Delta E > = m c ^ {2} [ a _ {1} + a _ {2} (E / m) + a _ {3} (T / m) \\ \left. + \alpha_ {4} \left(\mathrm {E} ^ {2} / \mathrm {m} ^ {2}\right) + \alpha_ {5} \left(\mathrm {ET} / \mathrm {m} ^ {2}\right) + \alpha_ {6} \left(\mathrm {T} ^ {2} / \mathrm {m} ^ {2}\right) + \dots \right]. \tag {1} \\ \end{array}
$$
If $\mathrm{T} = \mathrm{E} = 0$ , nothing is happening, so $a_1 = 0$ . If $\mathrm{T} = 0$ , $\mathrm{E} \neq 0$ , we have standard Compton scattering with a cross section $\mathrm{d}\sigma / \mathrm{d}\Omega \propto (1 + \cos^2\theta)$ and an energy transfer $\Delta \mathrm{E} = (\mathrm{E}^2 / \mathrm{m})(1 - \cos \theta)$ . Since the cross section is forward-backward symmetric, the $\cos \theta$ term cancels out when we average over angles, and
$$
<   \Delta E > = (E ^ {2} / m), \quad T = 0.
$$
This implies that $a_2 = 0$ and $a_4 = 1$ . If $E = 0$ , $T \neq 0$ , the photon has zero energy and is vacuous, so $a_3 = a_6 = 0$ .
Finally, we need $\alpha_{5}$ : Take a thought experiment where there is a dilute black-body flux of photons with the same temperature as the gas so that
$$
d (\text {n u m b e r o f p h o t o n s}) / d E = \text {c o n s t a n t} \times E ^ {2} e ^ { - E / T}.
$$
Plugging in Equation (1) with $a_1 = a_2 = a_3 = a_6 = 0$ , $a_4 = 1$ , and requiring thermal equilibrium, namely $\int_{0}^{\infty} < \Delta E > E^2 e^{-E / T} dE = 0$ , gives $0 = (3T / m)(4T + a_5T)$ or $a_5 = -4$ . Expression (1) now takes the final form
$$
<   \mathbf {E} > = (\mathrm {E} ^ {2} - 4 \mathrm {TE}) / \mathrm {mc} ^ {2} + \dots .
$$
