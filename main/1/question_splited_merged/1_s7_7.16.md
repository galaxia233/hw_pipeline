Problem 7.16. On the surface of a two-sphere, $\mathrm{ds}^2 = \mathrm{d}\theta^2 + \sin^2\theta \mathrm{d}\phi^2$ , the vector $\mathbf{A}$ is equal to $\mathbf{e}_{\theta}$ at $\theta = \theta_0$ , $\phi = 0$ . What is $\mathbf{A}$ after it is parallel transported around the circle $\theta = \theta_0$ ? What is the magnitude of $\mathbf{A}$ ?

## Solution

Solution 7.16. A is parallel transported along the $\phi$ -coordinate line (i.e., $\theta = \text{constant}$ ), so
$$
0 = \mathrm {A} _ {; \phi} ^ {\alpha} = \mathrm {A} _ {, \phi} ^ {\alpha} + \Gamma_ {\beta \phi} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {A} ^ {\beta}. \tag {1}
$$
The only nonzero Christoffel symbols are $\Gamma_{\phi \phi}^{\theta} = -\sin \theta \cos \theta$ and $\Gamma_{\theta \phi}^{\phi} = \cot \theta$ , so Equation (1) gives
$$
\mathbf {A} ^ {\theta}, \phi - \sin \theta \cos \theta \mathbf {A} ^ {\phi} = 0 \tag {2}
$$
$$
\mathbf {A} _ {, \phi} ^ {\phi} + \cot \theta \mathbf {A} ^ {\theta} = 0. \tag {3}
$$
Throughout this problem, $\theta$ is kept constant at $\theta_0$ . Equations (2) and (3) are easily solved, e.g., by differentiating Equation (2) with respect to $\phi$ :
$$
\mathbf {A} _ {, \phi \phi} ^ {\theta} = \sin \theta \cos \theta \mathbf {A} _ {, \phi} ^ {\phi} = - \cos^ {2} \theta \mathbf {A} ^ {\theta}.
$$
The solution then is
$$
\mathbf {A} ^ {\theta} = a \cos (\phi \cos \theta) + \beta \sin (\phi \cos \theta)
$$
where $\alpha, \beta$ are constants. Equation (2) now gives
$$
\mathbf {A} ^ {\phi} = - a \sin (\phi \cos \theta) / \sin \theta + \beta \cos (\phi \cos \theta) / \sin \theta .
$$
At $\phi = 0$ , $\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{e}_{\theta}$ , i.e. $\mathbf{A}^{\theta} = \mathbf{1}$ , $\mathbf{A}^{\phi} = 0$ . Thus $a = 1$ , $\beta = 0$ , and
$$
\mathbf {A} ^ {\theta} = \cos (\phi \cos \theta)
$$
$$
\mathbf {A} ^ {\phi} = - \sin (\phi \cos \theta) / \sin \theta .
$$
At $\phi = 2\pi$ , after transport around the circle, the vector is
$$
\mathbf {A} = \cos (2 \pi \cos \theta) \mathbf {e} _ {\theta} - \sin (2 \pi \cos \theta) / \sin \theta \mathbf {e} _ {\phi} \neq \mathbf {e} _ {\theta},
$$
but the magnitude is unchanged
$$
(\mathbf {A} \cdot \mathbf {A}) _ {2 \pi} = \cos^ {2} (2 \pi \cos \theta) \mathbf {e} _ {\theta} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\theta} + \sin^ {2} (2 \pi \cos \theta) / \sin^ {2} \theta \mathbf {e} _ {\phi} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\phi} = 1 = (\mathbf {A} \cdot \mathbf {A}) _ {0}.
$$
