Problem 19.26. In terms of the conserved entropy per baryon $\sigma$ of a hot big bang model, find the temperature at which hydrogen characteristically recombined, i.e. had an equilibrium ionization fraction of 0.5. Evaluate your answer for $\sigma = 10^{8}$ , $10^{9}$ .

## Solution

Solution 19.26. The Saha equation for the equilibrium fractional ionization of hydrogen is (units with $k = c = 1$ )
$$
\frac {\mathrm {x} ^ {2}}{1 - \mathrm {x}} = \frac {(2 \pi \mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {e}} \mathrm {T}) ^ {\frac {3}{2}} \mathrm {e} ^ {- \mathrm {B} / \mathrm {T}}}{\mathrm {n h} ^ {3}} \tag {1}
$$
where B is the ionization energy $= \frac{1}{2} a^2 m_e (a = 1/137)$ , and n is the number density of protons plus atoms. The value of n is related to $\sigma$ , the entropy per baryon by
$$
\sigma = \frac {4}{3} \frac {\mathsf {a T} ^ {3}}{\mathsf {n}}
$$
so if we take $x = 1/2$ and use $a = 8\pi^5 / 15h^3$ , Equation (1) becomes
$$
\frac {2 ^ {\frac {1}{2}} 4 \pi^ {7 / 2}}{4 5} \left. \frac {1}{\sigma} \left(\frac {\mathrm {T}}{\mathrm {m _ {e}}}\right) ^ {\frac {3}{2}} \right. = \exp \left[ - \frac {\mathrm {m _ {e}} / \mathrm {T}}{2 (1 3 7) ^ {2}} \right].
$$
This is easily solved by iteration. For example, iterating twice:
$$
\frac {\mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {e}}}{\mathrm {T}} = 2 (1 3 7) ^ {2} \left(\log \frac {\sigma}{6 . 9 0 8} + \frac {3}{2} \log \frac {\mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {e}}}{\mathrm {T}}\right)
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {T} \approx \frac {\mathrm {m _ {e}}}{2 (1 3 7) ^ {2}} \left[ \log \frac {\sigma}{6 . 9 0 8} + \frac {3}{2} \left(1 0. 5 3 + \log \log \frac {\sigma}{6 . 9 0 8}\right) \right] ^ {- 1} \\ = \left\{ \begin{array}{l} 4 3 3 0 ^ {0} \mathrm {K} \text {f o r} \sigma = 1 0 ^ {8} \\ 4 0 5 0 ^ {0} \mathrm {K} \text {f o r} \sigma = 1 0 ^ {9} \end{array} . \right. \\ \end{array}
$$
