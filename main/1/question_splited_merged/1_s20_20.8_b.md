Problem 20.8. Newtonian gravitation theory can be modified and made covariant if the force equation on a point particle is written as
$$
\mathrm {d p} ^ {\mu} = - \eta^ {\mu \nu} \Phi_ {, \nu} \mathrm {p} _ {\beta} \mathrm {d x} ^ {\beta} + \mathrm {p} ^ {\alpha} \Phi_ {, \alpha} \mathrm {d x} ^ {\mu}
$$
where $\Phi$ is a scalar potential which is related to stress energy by
$$
\Phi_ {; \mu} ^ {; \mu} = 4 \pi   T _ {\mu} ^ {\mu}    .
$$
Investigate whether this theory agrees with experiment and observation:
(a) Is this theory in agreement with the experiments of Eotvos and Dicke showing the equivalence of inertial and passive gravitational mass?   
(b) Is this theory in agreement with the Pound-Rebka experiment on the gravitational redshift of a photon on the earth's surface?

## Solution

 (b) Yes. Near the earth the earth is flat. Take $\mathbf{z}$ to be in the vertical direction, then we have $\Phi = \Phi(\mathbf{z})$ and the “source equation” for $\Phi$ is $\partial^2 \Phi / \partial z^2 = 0$ . Above the earth's surface then, the potential is
$$
\Phi = a z + b.
$$
The constant $a$ is determined by dropping a massive particle from rest and setting its initial acceleration to $g$ . With $u^0 = 1$ , $u^j = 0$ in the force equation, we have
$$
- \mathrm {m g} = \mathrm {d p} ^ {z} / \mathrm {d r} = - \mathrm {m} \Phi , z = - \mathrm {m a}
$$
and hence $\mathbf{a} = \mathbf{g}$
Now consider a photon moving vertically upward. It loses energy
$$
\mathrm {d p} ^ {0} = - \mathrm {p} ^ {\alpha} \Phi_ {, \alpha} \mathrm {d x} ^ {0} = - \mathrm {p} ^ {z} \Phi_ {, z} \mathrm {d t}
$$
in time $\mathbf{dt} = \mathbf{dz}$ . Since $\mathbf{p}^z = \mathbf{p}^0$ for a photon, the energy loss is then
$$
\mathrm {d p} ^ {0} = - \mathrm {p} ^ {0} \mathrm {g d z}.
$$
For an earth-based experiment $\mathbf{g}\Delta z << 1$ and
$$
\Delta \mathbf {p} ^ {0} / \mathbf {p} ^ {0} = \Delta \nu / \nu = - \mathbf {g} \Delta z
$$
in agreement with the Pound-Rebka experiment.
