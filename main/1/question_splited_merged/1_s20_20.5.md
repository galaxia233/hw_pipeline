Problem 20.5. In addition to the general relativistic deflection of electromagnetic waves by the sun, there is a frequency-dependent deflection caused by the solar corona, which must be taken into account in the interpretation of measurements. Estimate the impact parameter at which the general relativistic and coronal deflections of an electromagnetic wave of frequency $\nu$ make approximately equal contributions. Take an approximate coronal electron density of
$$
\log_ {1 0} \left(\frac {\mathrm {n} _ {\mathrm {e}}}{1 \mathrm {c m} ^ {- 3}}\right) = 8. 4 - 6. 5 \log_ {1 0} \left(\frac {\mathrm {r}}{\mathrm {R} _ {\odot}}\right)
$$
for $\mathbf{r} \leq 4\mathbf{R}_{\odot}$ . Evaluate your answer, in solar radii, for $\nu = 1000\mathrm{MHz}$ .

## Solution

Solution 20.5. The index of refraction of a plasma is $n = (1 - \nu_{\mathfrak{p}}^2 /\nu^2)^{\frac{1}{2}}$ where $\nu_{\mathfrak{p}}$ is the plasma frequency $\nu_{\mathfrak{p}}^{2}\equiv \mathtt{n}_{e}e^{2} / \pi \mathtt{m}_{e}$ . The well-known equation describing the path of a ray through an inhomogeneous medium is [see, e.g. B. Rossi, Optics (Addison-Wesley, 1957), p. 54]
$$
\mathrm {d} (\mathrm {n m}) / \mathrm {d} \ell = \underline {{\nabla}} \mathrm {n} \tag {1}
$$
where $\underline{\mathfrak{m}}$ is a unit vector tangent to the ray and $\ell$ is the distance along the ray. Since the deflection angle we are computing is small, we can correctly find the first-order change in $\underline{\mathfrak{m}}$ by integrating Equation (1) along the unperturbed trajectory $\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{b}, -\infty < \mathbf{x} < +\infty$ . From the formula given for $\mathfrak{n}_{\mathbf{e}}$ we have
$$
n \approx 1 - \frac {1}{2} \frac {\nu p ^ {2}}{\nu^ {2}} \approx 1 -. 0 1 0 1 \left(\frac {r}{R _ {\odot}}\right) ^ {- 6. 5} \left(\frac {\nu}{1 0 ^ {9} H z}\right) ^ {- 2}
$$
or
$$
\underset {\sim} {\nabla} \mathrm {n} \approx \frac {. 0 6 5 7}{R _ {\odot}} \left(\frac {\mathrm {r}}{R _ {\odot}}\right) ^ {- 7. 5} \left(\frac {\nu}{1 0 ^ {9} \mathrm {H z}}\right) ^ {- 2} \hat {\mathrm {r}}. \tag {2}
$$
Thus, using $n = 1$ at $\pm \infty$ and $r = (x^2 + y^2)^{\frac{1}{2}}$ ,
$$
\left. \underline {{\mathfrak {m}}} \right| _ {+ \infty} - \underline {{\mathfrak {m}}} \left| _ {- \infty} \approx \frac {0 6 5 7}{R _ {\odot}} \left(\frac {\nu}{1 0 ^ {9} H z}\right) ^ {- 2} \int_ {- \infty} ^ {\infty} \left(\frac {r}{R _ {\odot}}\right) ^ {- 7. 5} \left[ \hat {x} \left(\frac {x}{r}\right) + \hat {y} \left(\frac {y}{r}\right) \right] d x. \right. \tag {3}
$$
The term in $\hat{\mathbf{x}}$ vanishes by symmetry in $\pm \mathbf{x}$ , while the $\hat{\mathbf{y}}$ term can be written as $2\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}$ . Since $\underline{\mathfrak{m}}$ is a unit vector, for small angles $(\underline{\mathfrak{m}}|_{\infty} - \underline{\mathfrak{m}}|_{-0}) \cdot \hat{\mathbf{y}}$ is the scattering angle we seek. Defining
$$
\eta \equiv \mathrm {b} / \mathrm {R} _ {\odot}, \quad \mathrm {z} \equiv \mathrm {r} / \mathrm {b}
$$
and using
$$
\mathbf {y} = \mathbf {b}, \quad \mathbf {x} ^ {2} = \mathbf {b} ^ {2} \left(\mathbf {z} ^ {2} - 1\right), \quad \mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} = \mathbf {b} \mathbf {z} \left(\mathbf {z} ^ {2} - 1\right) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d} \mathbf {z},
$$
we get
$$
\theta_ {\text {c o r o n a l}} \approx . 1 3 1 \left(\frac {\nu}{1 0 ^ {9} \mathrm {H z}}\right) ^ {- 2} \eta^ {- 6. 5} \int_ {1} ^ {\infty} z ^ {- 7. 5} \left(z ^ {2} - 1\right) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} d z. \tag {4}
$$
On putting $t = 1 / z^2$ , we recognize the integral in Equation (4) as a beta function, $\frac{1}{2} B(3.75, .5) = .158$ . Thus our final expression for the coronal deflection is
$$
\theta_ {\text {c o r o n a l}} \approx . 0 2 1 \left(\frac {\nu}{1 0 ^ {9} \mathrm {H z}}\right) ^ {- 2} \eta^ {- 6. 5}. \tag {5}
$$
The general relativistic deflection, $\theta_{\mathbf{GR}}$ , is
$$
\theta_ {\mathbf {G R}} \approx \frac {8 . 5 \times 1 0 ^ {- 6}}{\eta} \tag {6}
$$
(see e.g. Problem 15.6). The two deflections are equal when
$$
\frac {8 . 5 \times 1 0 ^ {- 6}}{\eta} \approx . 0 2 1 \left(\frac {\nu}{1 0 ^ {9} \mathrm {H z}}\right) ^ {- 2} \eta^ {- 6. 5}
$$
or
$$
\eta \equiv \frac {b}{R _ {\odot}} \approx 4. 1 \left(\frac {\nu}{1 0 ^ {9} \mathrm {H z}}\right) ^ {- . 3 6}.
$$
For smaller impact parameters $b$ , the coronal deflection dominates.
