# Problem 2.2.
(a) When a photon scatters off a charged particle which is moving with a speed very nearly that of light, the photon is said to have undergone an inverse Compton scattering. Consider an inverse Compton scattering in which a charged particle of rest mass $m$ and total mass-energy (as seen in the lab frame) $\mathbf{E} \gg \mathfrak{m}$ , collides head-on with a photon of frequency $\nu (\mathrm{h}\nu \ll \mathrm{m})$ . What is the maximum energy the particle can transfer to the photon?

## Solution

Solution 2.2. Use $\gamma$ to denote the photon, and prime to denote values after scattering. We proceed by eliminating $\mathbf{P}'$ from the equation of 4-momentum conservation:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \left(\mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime} + \mathbf {P} ^ {\prime} - \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma}\right) ^ {2} = \mathbf {P} ^ {2} = - \mathbf {m} ^ {2} \\ \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} \cdot \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime} = \mathbf {P} \cdot (\mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} - \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime}). \\ \end{array}
$$
Maximum energy transfer obviously occurs for a $180^{\circ}$ scattering angle. With this condition and $\left|\underline{\mathbf{P}}_{\gamma}\right| = \mathbf{E}_{\gamma}$ , the above equation becomes
$$
\begin{array}{l} - \mathbf {E} _ {\gamma} \mathbf {E} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime} + \underline {{\mathbf {P}}} _ {\gamma} \cdot \underline {{\mathbf {P}}} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime} = - \mathbf {E} (\mathbf {E} _ {\gamma} - \mathbf {E} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime}) + \underline {{\mathbf {P}}} \cdot (\underline {{\mathbf {P}}} _ {\gamma} - \underline {{\mathbf {P}}} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime}) \\ - 2 \mathbf {E} _ {\gamma} \mathbf {E} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime} = - \mathbf {E} (\mathbf {E} _ {\gamma} - \mathbf {E} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime}) + \mathbf {P} (- \mathbf {E} _ {\gamma} - \mathbf {E} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime}) \\ \end{array}
$$
so that
$$
E _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime} = \frac {E _ {\gamma} (E + P)}{2 E _ {\gamma} + E - P} \approx \frac {E}{1 + m ^ {2} / 4 E E _ {\gamma}}.
$$
The second expression for $\mathbf{E}_{\gamma}^{\prime}$ is a result of using the approximation
$$
\mathbf {P} = \left(\mathbf {E} ^ {2} - \mathbf {m} ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \approx \mathbf {E} - \frac {1}{2} \mathbf {\nabla m} ^ {2} / \mathbf {E}
$$
appropriate to our assumed condition $\mathbf{E} \gg \mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{e}}$ .
For $3^{0}\mathrm{K}$ , the photon energy is of order $\mathrm{kT} \approx 3 \times 10^{-4}\mathrm{eV}$ . Using this, $\mathfrak{m}_{\mathrm{proton}} = .938 \times 10^{9}\mathrm{eV}$ , and $\mathbf{E} = 10^{20}\mathrm{eV}$ in our equation we find $\mathbf{E}_{\gamma}^{\prime} \approx 10^{19}\mathrm{eV}$ .
