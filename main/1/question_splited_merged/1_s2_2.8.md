Problem 2.8. Calculate the threshold energy of a nucleon $\mathbf{N}$ for it to undergo the reaction
$$
\gamma + \mathbf {N} \rightarrow \mathbf {N} + \pi
$$
where $\gamma$ represents a photon of temperature $3^0\mathrm{K}$ . Assume the collision is head-on; take the photon energy to be $\sim \mathrm{kT}$ ; $\mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{N}} = 940\mathrm{MeV}$ ; $\mathfrak{m}_{\pi} = 140\mathrm{MeV}$ . (This effect probably produces a cut-off in the cosmic-ray spectrum at this threshold energy.)

## Solution

Solution 2.8. Conservation of 4-momentum gives us
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} + \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {N}} = \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {N} ^ {\prime}} + \mathbf {P} _ {\pi} \quad \left(\mathbf {N} ^ {\prime} = \text {n u c l e o n c o m i n g o u t o f r e a c t i o n}\right) \\ (\mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} + \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {N}}) ^ {2} = (\mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {N} ^ {\prime}} + \mathbf {P} _ {\pi}) ^ {2}. \\ \end{array}
$$
In the lab frame (where $\mathbf{E}_{\gamma} \sim 3^{0}\mathbf{K} \sim 2.5 \times 10^{-10}\mathrm{MeV}$ ),
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} = (\mathbf {E} _ {\gamma}, \underline {{\mathbf {P}}} _ {\gamma}), \quad \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {N}} = (\mathbf {E} _ {\mathbf {N}}, \underline {{\mathbf {P}}} _ {\mathbf {N}}) .
$$
In the C.M. frame at threshold,
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {N} ^ {\prime}} + \mathbf {P} _ {\pi} = \left(\mathrm {m} _ {\mathbf {N}} + \mathrm {m} _ {\pi}, 0\right)
$$
so that
$$
\begin{array}{l} 2 \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} \cdot \mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {N}} - \mathbf {m} _ {\mathrm {N}} ^ {2} = - (\mathbf {m} _ {\mathrm {N}} + \mathbf {m} _ {\pi}) ^ {2} \\ - 2 \mathrm {E} _ {\gamma} \mathrm {E} _ {\mathrm {N}} + 2 \underset {\sim \gamma} {\mathrm {P}} \cdot \underset {\sim} {\mathrm {P}} _ {\mathrm {N}} = - 2 \mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {N}} \mathrm {m} _ {\pi} - \mathrm {m} _ {\pi} ^ {2}. \\ \end{array}
$$
Now $\mathbf{P}_{\gamma} = \mathbf{E}_{\gamma}$ since $\mathfrak{m}_{\gamma} = 0$ and, for a head-on collision $\mathbf{\underline{P}}_{\gamma} \cdot \mathbf{\underline{P}}_{\mathbf{N}} = -\mathbf{P}_{\gamma} \mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{N}}$ , so that
$$
\begin{array}{l} E _ {N} + \left(E _ {N} ^ {2} - m _ {N} ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} = \frac {2 m _ {N} m _ {\pi} + m _ {\pi} ^ {2}}{2 E _ {\gamma}} \\ = \frac {2 \cdot 9 4 0 \cdot 1 4 0 + 1 4 0 ^ {2}}{2 \cdot (2 . 5 \times 1 0 ^ {- 1 0})} \mathrm {M e V} = 6 \times 1 0 ^ {1 4} \mathrm {M e V}. \\ \end{array}
$$
Since $\mathbf{E}_{\mathbf{N}} >> \mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{N}}$ , we can replace $(\mathbf{E}_{\mathbf{N}}^{2} - \mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{N}}^{2})^{\frac{1}{2}}$ by $\mathbf{E}_{\mathbf{N}}$ . Thus $\mathbf{E}_{\mathbf{N}} \approx 3 \times 10^{14} \mathrm{MeV}$ .
