Problem 9.9. Show that the second derivatives of a scalar field commute (i.e. show that $\mathbf{S}_{;a\beta} = \mathbf{S}_{;b\alpha}$ ). For third derivatives $\mathbf{S}_{;a\beta\gamma}$ , compute $\mathbf{S}_{;(a\beta)\gamma}$ and $\mathbf{S}_{;a[\beta\gamma]}$ .

## Solution

Solution 9.9. The second derivates of a scalar are
$$
\mathbf {S} _ {; a \beta} = (\mathbf {S} _ {, a}) _ {; \beta} = \mathbf {S} _ {, a \beta} - \mathbf {S} _ {, \sigma} \Gamma_ {a \beta} ^ {\sigma}.
$$
This expression is obviously symmetric in $\alpha$ and $\beta$ .
To investigate third derivatives use local flat coordinates as in Problem 9.8. We have then
$$
\mathsf {S} _ {; a \beta \gamma} = (\mathsf {S} _ {, a \beta} - \mathsf {S} _ {\sigma} \Gamma_ {a \beta} ^ {\sigma}) _ {, \gamma} = \mathsf {S} _ {, a \beta \gamma} - \mathsf {S} _ {\sigma} \mathsf {I} _ {a \beta , \gamma} ^ {\sigma} = \mathsf {S} _ {; (a \beta) \gamma}.
$$
From this expression and from the result of Problem 9.8 we also have
$$
\mathsf {S} _ {; \alpha [ \beta \gamma ]} = \frac {1}{2} \mathsf {S} _ {; \sigma} \mathsf {R} _ {\alpha \beta \gamma} ^ {\sigma}.
$$
