Problem 18.12. An elastic rod can be used to detect gravitational waves not only at its lowest normal mode frequency $\omega_0$ , but also at harmonics
$\omega_{n} \equiv n \omega_{0}$ . What is the sensitivity of the $n^{\text{th}}$ mode relative to the zeroth, i.e. how does the ratio of maximum squared amplitude of the displacement to energy flux of wave vary with $n$ ? (Assume the rod has the same mechanical damping time for all modes.)

## Solution

Solution 18.12. The normal modes of the rod (taken to be oriented along the $x$ -axis) are found from the equation
$$
\frac {\partial^ {2} \xi}{\partial t ^ {2}} + \frac {1}{r} \frac {\partial \xi}{\partial t} - a ^ {2} \frac {\partial^ {2} \xi}{\partial x ^ {2}} = 0. \tag {1}
$$
Here $\xi(\mathbf{x}, t)$ is the displacement of an element of the rod, $\tau$ is the damping time and $a$ is the speed of sound. The modes are of the form
$$
\xi = e ^ {- i \omega t} e ^ {- t / 2 \tau} u (x) \tag {2}
$$
where
$$
a ^ {2} \frac {d ^ {2} u}{d x ^ {2}} + \left(\omega^ {2} + \frac {1}{4 r ^ {2}}\right) u = 0. \tag {3}
$$
