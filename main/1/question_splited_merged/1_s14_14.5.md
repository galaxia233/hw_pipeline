Problem 14.5. Show that a highly relativistic fluid $(\mathfrak{p} = \frac{1}{3}\rho)$ in hydrostatic equilibrium in a gravitational field can never have a free surface (i.e. a surface where $\rho \rightarrow 0$ ).

## Solution

Solution 14.5. Using $\mathfrak{p} = \frac{1}{3}\rho$ in the equation of hydrostatic equilibrium (Problem 14.4) $\mathfrak{p}_{,\lambda} = (\rho +\mathfrak{p})\left[\log (-\mathfrak{g}_{00})^{\frac{1}{2}}\right]_{,\lambda}$ we get
$$
- \log (- g _ {0 0}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} = \frac {1}{4} \int \frac {d \rho}{\rho}.
$$
This integrates to $\rho = \text{constant} \times (-\mathbf{g}_{00})^{-2}$ . As long as $\mathbf{g}_{00}$ is finite, therefore, $\rho$ cannot vanish and thus there is no free surface.
