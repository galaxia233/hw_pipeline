Problem 7.9. If a geodesic is timelike at a given point $\mathbf{P}$ , show that it is timelike everywhere along its length, and similarly for spacelike or null, geodesics.

## Solution

Solution 7.9. A geodesic with tangent vector $\mathbf{u}$ is spacelike, timelike, or null according to whether $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u}$ is $>0$ , $=0$ , $<0$ . But $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u}$ is conserved along the geodesic, since
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} (\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {u}) = 2 \mathbf {u} \cdot \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u} = 0,
$$
since the geodesic equation is $\nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u} = 0$
