Problem 16.18. Show that in a rigidly rotating, self-gravitating, perfect fluid star, the surfaces of constant $\mathfrak{p}$ and $\rho$ coincide.

## Solution

Solution 16.18. First, note that $\mathbf{dp} = (\rho + \mathfrak{p})\mathbf{d}\log \mathfrak{u}^{\mathfrak{t}}$ (hydrostatic equilibrium; see Problem 16.17). Next take the exterior derivative of this equation, and use $\mathbf{ddp} = 0$ to get
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = \mathrm {d} (\rho + \mathrm {p}) \wedge \mathrm {d} \log \mathrm {u} ^ {\mathrm {t}} \\ = \mathbf {d} (\rho + \mathfrak {p}) \wedge (\rho + \mathfrak {p}) ^ {- 1} \mathbf {d} \rho \\ \end{array}
$$
which implies that
$$
\mathrm {d} \rho \wedge \mathrm {d p} = 0,
$$
and therefore that the surface $\rho =$ constant coincides with the surface $\mathbf{p} =$ constant.
