Problem 3.26. Show that
$$
\mathbf {V} _ {\sigma} \mathbf {V} ^ {\sigma} = - \frac {1}{3 !} \left(* \mathbf {V}\right) _ {a \beta \gamma} \left(* \mathbf {V}\right) ^ {a \beta \gamma} .
$$

## Solution

Solution 3.26. From the definition $\mathbf{\nabla}*\mathbf{V}^{\alpha \beta \gamma} = \mathbf{V}_{\lambda}\varepsilon^{\lambda \alpha \beta \gamma}$ , and
$$
* \mathbf {V} _ {\alpha \beta \gamma} * \mathbf {V} ^ {\alpha \beta \gamma} = \mathbf {V} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {V} _ {\lambda} \varepsilon_ {\mu \alpha \beta \gamma} \varepsilon^ {\lambda a \beta \gamma}.
$$
Since $\mu$ must be a different index than $\alpha$ , $\beta$ , or $\gamma$ , and $\lambda$ must also be, it follows that
$$
\varepsilon_ {\mu \alpha \beta \gamma} \varepsilon^ {\lambda a \beta \gamma} = \mathbf {C} \delta_ {\mu} ^ {\lambda}
$$
for some constant $C$ . If we sum over $\mu$ and $\lambda$ , use $\delta_{\mu}^{\mu} = 4$ , and use the result of Problem 3.22 we see that $C = -6$ so
$$
* \mathrm {V} _ {\alpha \beta \gamma} * \mathrm {V} ^ {\alpha \beta \gamma} = - 6 \delta_ {\mu} ^ {\lambda} \mathrm {V} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {V} _ {\lambda} = - 6 \mathrm {V} ^ {\sigma} \mathrm {V} _ {\sigma}.
$$
