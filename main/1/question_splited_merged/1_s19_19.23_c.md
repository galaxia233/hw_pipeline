19.23 A closed $(\mathbf{k} = 1)$ Friedmann universe has Hubble constant $H_0$ and deceleration parameter $q_0$ . Assume that the universe has always been matter dominated.
(c) What is the total proper volume now occupied by the matter which we see, looking out into the sky?

## Solution

(c) The farthest coordinate radius that we see is, according to Equation (8),
$$
r _ {0} = \sin (\eta_ {0}) = (2 q _ {0} - 1) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} / q _ {0}
$$
where Equation (7) has been used. The volume out to this coordinate radius at the present epoch is (analogously to part (a))
$$
\begin{array}{l} V = 4 \pi \int_ {0} ^ {\mathrm {r} _ {0}} \frac {R _ {0} ^ {3} \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d r}}{\left(1 - \mathrm {r} ^ {2}\right) _ {-} ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} = 2 \pi R _ {0} ^ {3} \left[ \sin^ {- 1} \mathrm {r} _ {0} - \mathrm {r} _ {0} \left(1 - \mathrm {r} _ {0} ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \right] \\ = \frac {2 \pi}{\mathrm {H} _ {0} ^ {3} \left(2 \mathrm {q} _ {0} - 1\right) ^ {\frac {3}{2}}} \left[ \cos^ {- 1} \left(\frac {1 - \mathrm {q} _ {0}}{\mathrm {q} _ {0}}\right) - \frac {\left(1 - \mathrm {q} _ {0}\right) \left(2 \mathrm {q} _ {0} - 1\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{\mathrm {q} _ {0} ^ {2}} \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$
