Problem 18.9. Show that gravitational radiation generated by an axisymmetric system carries no net angular momentum. (Do not assume that the sources have weak internal gravitational fields.)

## Solution

Solution 18.9. Let the $z$ axis be the symmetry axis and let $\phi$ be the cyclic axial coordinate. At an instant of time, $t =$ constant, in the asymptotically flat region far outside the sources through which the waves are propagating, the angular momentum inside a closed 2-surface $r = r_1$ is (by symmetry only an axial component)
$$
J _ {1} = - (1 6 \pi) ^ {- 1} \int_ {\mathrm {r} = \mathrm {r} _ {1}} \xi^ {\mu ; \nu} \mathrm {d} ^ {2} \Sigma_ {\mu \nu} \tag {1}
$$
where $\pmb{\xi} = \partial/\partial \phi$ . (See Problem 16.23. The proof goes through even if there is no time Killing vector and even in the presence of waves since waves modify only the space-space parts of the metric - see MTW, Equation 19.5.) Similarly, the angular momentum inside a closed 2-surface $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_2(\mathbf{r}_2 > \mathbf{r}_1)$ is
$$
J _ {2} = - (1 6 \pi) ^ {- 1} \int_ {\mathrm {r} = \mathrm {r} _ {2}} \xi^ {\mu ; \nu} \mathrm {d} ^ {2} \Sigma_ {\mu \nu}. \tag {2}
$$
Thus the angular momentum of the waves between $\mathbf{r}_1$ and $\mathbf{r}_2$ is
$$
\begin{array}{l} J _ {2} - J _ {1} = - (1 6 \pi) ^ {- 1} \int_ {\mathbf {r} = \mathbf {r} _ {2}} \xi^ {\mu ; \nu}   d ^ {2} \Sigma_ {\mu \nu} + (1 6 \pi) ^ {- 1} \int_ {\mathbf {r} = \mathbf {r} _ {1}} \xi^ {\mu ; \nu}   d ^ {2} \Sigma_ {\mu \nu} \\ = \int_ {\mathbf {r} _ {1} \leq \mathbf {r} \leq \mathbf {r} _ {2}} \mathrm {T} _ {\nu} ^ {\mu} \xi^ {\nu} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} \Sigma_ {\mu} \\ \end{array}
$$
where we have used Stokes' theorem as in Solution 16.23. Since $\mathbf{T}_{\nu}^{\mu}$ vanishes for $\mathbf{r}_1 \leq \mathbf{r} \leq \mathbf{r}_2$ , $\mathbf{J}_2 - \mathbf{J}_1$ is zero and so the angular momentum flux of the waves vanishes.
