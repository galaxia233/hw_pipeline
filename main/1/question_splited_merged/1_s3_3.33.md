Problem 3.33. What is the invariant volume element of contravariant momentum $\mathsf{d}^4\mathbf{P}$ for 4-dimensional momentum space? What is the invariant 3-volume "on the mass shell", i.e. when the constraint $(-\mathbf{P}\cdot \mathbf{P})^{\frac{1}{2}} = \mathfrak{m}$ is imposed?

## Solution

Solution 3.33. Since the contravariant momentum vector $\mathbf{P}^{\alpha}$ transforms just like the contravariant displacement vector $\mathbf{x}^{\alpha}$ , the 4-dimensional momentum element must be of the same form as the invariant 4-volume element (Problem 3.32), so
$$
\mathrm {d} ^ {4} \mathrm {P} = (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {x}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {y}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {z}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {t}}.
$$
For the 3-volume element we multiply by a $\delta$ -function of the constraint and integrate over $\mathbf{P}^t$ :
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} \mathrm {P} = \int \delta \left[ \left(- \mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \mathrm {P} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {P} ^ {\beta}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} - \mathrm {m} \right] (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {x}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {y}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {z}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {t}} \\ = (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {x}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {y}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathrm {z}} \times \left[ - \frac {1}{2} \left(- \mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \mathrm {P} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {P} ^ {\beta}\right) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \cdot 2 \mathrm {g} _ {\mathrm {t} \alpha} \mathrm {P} ^ {\alpha} \right] ^ {- 1}. \\ \end{array}
$$
(Here we have used the identity
$$
\int \delta (f (x)) d x = \frac {1}{| f ^ {\prime} (x _ {1}) |}
$$
where $\mathbf{x}_1$ is a zero of f.) Rewriting the term in brackets, we get
$$
\mathrm {d} ^ {3} \mathrm {P} = (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathbf {x}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathbf {y}} \mathrm {d P} ^ {\mathbf {z}} \left(\frac {\mathrm {m}}{- \mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {t}}}\right).
$$
Notice that in a local orthonormal frame comoving with the particles in momentum space, this reduces to
$$
\mathrm {d} ^ {3} \mathbf {P} = \mathrm {d} \mathbf {P} ^ {\mathbf {x}} \mathrm {d} \mathbf {P} ^ {\mathbf {y}} \mathrm {d} \mathbf {P} ^ {\mathbf {z}}
$$
as it should.
Sometimes $\mathbf{d}^3\mathbf{P}$ is renormalized by dividing by $m$ . This gives an invariant volume element which is valid for massless particles as well.
