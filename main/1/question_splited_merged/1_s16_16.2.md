Problem 16.2. Suppose that an observer, at rest at some point inside a spherical relativistic star, measures the radial pressure-buoyant force, $\mathbf{F}_{\mathrm{buoy}}$ , on a small element of volume V, using the usual laboratory techniques. What value will he find for $\mathbf{F}_{\mathrm{buoy}}$ , in terms of $\rho$ , p, m, V, and dp/dr? If he equates this buoyant force to an equal and opposite gravitational force, $\mathbf{F}_{\mathrm{grav}}$ , what will $\mathbf{F}_{\mathrm{grav}}$ be in terms of $\rho$ , p, m, V, and r? How do these results differ from the corresponding Newtonian results?

## Solution

Solution 16.2. The observer makes measurements in his local orthonormal
frame $\mathbf{e}_{\hat{\mathfrak{t}}} = \mathbf{e}^{-\Phi}\mathbf{e}_{\mathfrak{t}},\quad \mathbf{e}_{\hat{\mathfrak{r}}} = \mathbf{e}^{-\lambda}\mathbf{e}_{\mathfrak{r}},\quad \mathbf{e}_{\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}} = \mathbf{r}^{-1}\mathbf{e}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}},\quad \mathbf{e}_{\hat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}} = (\mathbf{r}\sin \theta)^{-1}\mathbf{e}_{\boldsymbol{\phi}};$
$$
\tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {t}} = e ^ {\Phi} \widetilde {d t}, \quad \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {r}} = e ^ {\lambda} \widetilde {d r}, \quad \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\theta}} = r \widetilde {d \theta}, \quad \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} = r \sin \theta d \widetilde {\phi}.
$$
Here the metric is as given in the introduction, except $(1 - 2\mathrm{m} / \mathrm{r})^{-1}\equiv \mathrm{e}^{2\lambda}$ . The volume of a fluid element which he measures is
$$
\begin{array}{l} V = \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {r}} \wedge \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\theta}} \wedge \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} \\ = e ^ {\lambda} r ^ {2} \sin \theta \widetilde {d r} \wedge \widetilde {d \theta} \wedge \widetilde {d \phi} \\ = e ^ {\lambda} r ^ {2} \sin \theta d r d \theta d \phi . \\ \end{array}
$$
The pressure at opposite points on the vertical walls of the fluid element are the same, but because of the radial pressure gradient, the top and bottom pressures are not:
$$
\left| p _ {t o p} - p _ {b o t t o m} \right| = \left| p _ {, r} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {r}} \right| = \left| p _ {, r} e ^ {- \lambda} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {r}} \right|.
$$
Thus,
$$
\begin{array}{l} | \mathbf {F} _ {\text {b u o y ,}} | = | (\mathrm {p} _ {\text {t o p}} - \mathrm {p} _ {\text {b o t t o m}}) \times \text {a r e a} | \\ = | \mathbf {p} _ {r, r} e ^ {- \lambda_ {\omega} \hat {r} _ {\omega} \hat {\theta} _ {\omega} \hat {\phi}} | \\ = | p _ {r} e ^ {- \lambda V} |. \\ \end{array}
$$
Since the direction of the buoyant force is radial,
$$
\mathbf {F} _ {\text {b u o y ,}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {- \lambda} \mathrm {p} _ {, r} \mathrm {V e} _ {\hat {\mathrm {r}}} = \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {m}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {p} _ {, r} \mathrm {V e} _ {\hat {\mathrm {r}}} .
$$
Using the OV equation for $\mathbf{p}_{\cdot ,\mathbf{r}}$ we find
$$
\mathbf {F} _ {\text {g r a v .}} = - \mathbf {F} _ {\text {b u o y .}} = - \frac {(\rho + p) (m + 4 \pi r ^ {3} p) V e _ {\mathrm {f}}}{r ^ {2} \left(1 - \frac {2 m}{r}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}
$$
The Newtonian result is
$$
\mathrm {p} _ {\mathrm {r}} \mathrm {V e} _ {\mathrm {r}} = - \frac {\rho \mathrm {m V}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} \mathrm {e} _ {\mathrm {r}} ^ {\prime}.
$$
