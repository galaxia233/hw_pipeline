Problem 9.6. Set up a coordinate system on a torus (the 2-dimensional surface of a doughnut in Euclidean 3-space). Calculate all components of $\mathbf{g}_{\mu \nu}$ , $\Gamma_{\alpha \beta}^{\mu}$ and $\mathbb{R}_{\alpha \beta \gamma \delta}$ .

## Solution

Solution 9.6. From the diagram, two orthogonal infinitesimal displacements have length $\mathrm{ad}\phi$ and $(\mathbf{b} + \mathbf{a}\sin \phi)\mathrm{d}\theta$ so $\mathrm{ds}^2 = \mathbf{a}^2\mathrm{d}\phi^2 +$
$(\mathbf{b} + \mathbf{a}\sin \phi)^2\mathrm{d}\theta^2$ or $\mathbf{g}_{\theta \theta} = (\mathbf{b} + \mathbf{a}\sin \phi)^2$ $\mathbf{g}_{\phi \phi} = \mathbf{a}^2$ $\mathbf{g}_{\theta \phi} = \mathbf{g}_{\phi \theta} = 0$ . The formula for the Christoffel symbols now gives the nonvanishing $\Gamma$ 's
directly
$$
\Gamma_ {\theta \theta} ^ {\phi} = - a ^ {- 1} (b + a \sin \phi) \cos \phi
$$
$$
\Gamma_ {\theta \phi} ^ {\theta} = \Gamma_ {\phi \theta} ^ {\theta} = a \cos \phi (b + a \sin \phi) ^ {- 1}.
$$

In 2 dimensions the Riemann tensor has only one independent component. Take it to be $\mathbf{R}_{\phi \theta \phi \theta}$ , then the standard formula gives
$$
R _ {\phi \theta \phi \theta} = a \sin \phi (b + a \sin \phi).
$$
