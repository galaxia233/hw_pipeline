# Problem 15.2.   
(b) Prove that all orbits are stably planar.

## Solution

(b) Using the constant of motion $\mathbf{L}^2$ of Problem 15.1, we have
$$
\left(\frac {\mathrm {d} \theta}{\mathrm {d} \lambda}\right) ^ {2} = \left(\mathrm {g} ^ {\theta \theta} \mathrm {p} _ {\theta}\right) ^ {2} = \frac {1}{\mathrm {r} ^ {4}} \left(\mathrm {L} ^ {2} - \frac {\mathrm {p} _ {\phi} ^ {2}}{\sin^ {2} \theta}\right). \tag {1}
$$
Suppose that the unperturbed orbit is $\theta = \pi /2$ , $\mathbf{L} = \mathfrak{p}_{\phi} = \mathbf{K}$ . Let the particle be perturbed out of the plane of the orbit i.e. $\theta = \pi /2 + \delta \theta$ , $\mathbf{L} = \mathbf{K} + \delta \mathbf{L}$ , $\mathfrak{p}_{\phi} = \mathbf{K} + \delta \mathfrak{p}_{\phi}$ . To first order in $\delta \mathbf{L}$ and $\delta \mathfrak{p}_{\phi}$ and second order in $\delta \theta$ , Equation (1) gives
$$
\left[ \frac {\mathrm {d} (\delta \theta)}{\mathrm {d} \lambda} \right] ^ {2} = \frac {1}{\mathrm {r} ^ {4}} \left[ 2 \mathrm {K} \delta \mathrm {L} - 2 \mathrm {K} \delta \mathrm {p} _ {\phi} - \mathrm {K} ^ {2} (\delta \theta) ^ {2} \right]
$$
and hence
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} (\delta \theta)}{\mathrm {d} \lambda^ {2}} = - \frac {\mathrm {K} ^ {2}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {4}} \delta \theta .
$$
This shows that $\delta \theta$ oscillates about $\theta = \pi / 2$ and does not grow in time. Hence the orbits are stably planar.
