Problem 19.28. A Friedmann cosmology has temperature $\mathbf{T}_1$ at expansion scale $\mathbf{R}_1$ , and is dominated by relativistic electrons, positrons, muons, photons, and neutrinos in thermal equilibrium. Later, at expansion scale $\mathbf{R}_2$ , the muon pairs have annihilated, but the other particles are still relativistic and in equilibrium. Find the temperature $\mathbf{T}_2$ in terms of $\mathbf{T}_1$ , $\mathbf{R}_1$ , and $\mathbf{R}_2$ .

## Solution

Solution 19.28. For relativistic particles, $\mathbf{E} \approx \mathbf{p}$ , the equilibrium density at a temperature $\mathbf{T}$ is (see the solution to Problem 19.27):
$$
\rho (T) = \int E n (p) d p = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} a T ^ {4} & \text {f o r p h o t o n s} \\ \frac {7}{1 6} a T ^ {4} & \text {f o r e a c h n e u t r i n o s p e c i e s} \\ \frac {7}{8} a T ^ {4} & \text {f o r e a c h e l e c t r o n o r m u o n s p e c i e s .} \end{array} \right.
$$
Here a is Stefan's constant $= (8\pi^{5}\mathbf{k}^{4}) / (15\mathbf{h}^{3})$ . For this problem, then, we have
$$
\rho (\mathrm {T}) = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \left(1 + 4 \cdot \frac {7}{1 6} + 4 \cdot \frac {7}{8}\right) \mathrm {a T} ^ {4} & \mathrm {a t R _ {1}} \\ \left(1 + 4 \cdot \frac {7}{1 6} + 2 \cdot \frac {7}{8}\right) \mathrm {a T} ^ {4} & \mathrm {a t R _ {2}} \end{array} \right\} \equiv \mathrm {K a T} ^ {4},
$$
$$
\mathrm {K} _ {1} = 2 5 / 4, \quad \mathrm {K} _ {2} = 1 8 / 4.
$$
We now use the fact that the expansion of the universe is isentropic. The entropy per volume of such a relativistic gas is calculated from the first law of thermodynamics to be
$$
\frac {\mathrm {S}}{\mathrm {V}} = \frac {4}{3} \mathrm {K a T} ^ {3}.
$$
Since $V$ , the volume, varies as the expansion factor cubed, we have
$$
\text {c o n s t a n t} = \mathrm {S} \propto \frac {4}{3} \mathrm {K a} (\mathrm {R T}) ^ {3},
$$
giving for isentropic expansion
$$
\mathrm {K} _ {1} \mathrm {R} _ {1} ^ {3} \mathrm {T} _ {1} ^ {3} = \mathrm {K} _ {2} \mathrm {R} _ {2} ^ {3} \mathrm {T} _ {2} ^ {3}
$$
and
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {2} = \mathrm {T} _ {1} \left(\frac {\mathrm {R} _ {1}}{\mathrm {R} _ {2}}\right) \left(\frac {2 5}{1 8}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{3}}.
$$
Physically, this result says that in annihilating, the muons dump their energy (actually entropy) into the remaining particles, and increase their
temperature by a factor $(25 / 18)^{\frac{1}{3}}$ over the expected first-power decrease of T with R.
