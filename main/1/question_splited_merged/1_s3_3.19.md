Problem 3.19. Prove that the Kronecker delta, $\delta_{\nu}^{\mu}$ , is a tensor.

## Solution

Solution 3.19. One way is to show that $\delta_{\nu}^{\mu}$ can be combined with the components of two vectors to form a scalar. But $\mathbf{A}^{\nu}\mathbf{B}_{\mu}\delta_{\nu}^{\mu} = \mathbf{A}\cdot \mathbf{B}$ , hence $\pmb{\delta}$ is a tensor.
Alternately one can check the transformation properties
$$
\frac {\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\mu^ {\prime}}}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}} \frac {\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\beta}}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\nu^ {\prime}}} \delta^ {\alpha} \beta = \frac {\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\mu^ {\prime}}}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}} \frac {\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\nu^ {\prime}}} = \delta_ {\nu^ {\prime}} ^ {\mu^ {\prime}},
$$
where the last equality follows from the fact that $\partial_{\mathbf{x}}^{\mu'} / \partial_{\mathbf{x}}^{\alpha}$ and $\partial_{\mathbf{x}}^{\alpha} / \partial_{\mathbf{x}}^{\mu'}$ are matrix inverses of each other. Hence $\delta_{\nu}^{\mu}$ transforms as a tensor.
