Problem 9.19. Compute the Riemann tensor, Ricci tensor, and scalar curvature of the conformally-flat metric $\mathbf{g}_{\mu \nu} = \mathrm{e}^{2\phi}\eta_{\mu \nu}$ where $\phi = \phi (\mathbf{x}^{\mu})$ is an arbitrary function.

## Solution

Section 9.19. In a conformally flat spacetime the Weyl tensor vanishes, so the Riemann tensor can easily be computed from the Ricci tensor
$$
\mathrm {R} _ {\mu \nu} = - \left(\log | \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right) _ {, \mu \nu} + \Gamma_ {\mu \nu , \alpha} ^ {\alpha} + \left(\log | \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right) _ {, \alpha} \Gamma_ {\mu \nu} ^ {\alpha} - \Gamma_ {\mu \alpha} ^ {\beta} \Gamma_ {\nu \beta} ^ {\alpha}.
$$
The expressions we need are
$$
\begin{array}{l} \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta} ^ {a} = - \phi^ {, a} \eta_ {\gamma \beta} + \phi_ {, \beta} \delta_ {\gamma} ^ {a} + \phi_ {, \gamma} \delta_ {\beta} ^ {a} \\ \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta , a} ^ {a} = - \nabla^ {2} \phi \eta_ {\gamma \beta} + 2 \phi , \beta \gamma \\ \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta} ^ {a} \Gamma_ {\mu a} ^ {\gamma} = 6 \phi_ {, \mu} \phi_ {, \beta} - 2 (\nabla \phi) ^ {2} \eta_ {\mu \beta} \\ - \mathbf {g} = \mathbf {e} ^ {8 \phi}, \quad \log | \mathbf {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} = 4 \phi \\ \left(\log \left| \mathbf {g} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right) _ {, \alpha} \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta} ^ {\alpha} = - 4 (\nabla \phi) ^ {2} \eta_ {\gamma \beta} + 8 \phi , \beta \phi , \gamma \\ \left(\log | \mathbf {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right), \gamma \beta = 4 \phi , \gamma \beta . \\ \end{array}
$$
In the above expressions raising and lowering have been done with $\eta_{\mu \nu}$ , e.g.
$$
\phi^ {, a} \equiv \eta^ {\mu a} \phi_ {, \mu}; \quad \nabla^ {2} \phi \equiv \eta^ {\mu a} \phi_ {, \mu a}; \quad (\nabla \phi) ^ {2} \equiv \eta^ {\mu a} \phi_ {, \mu} \phi_ {, a}.
$$
The Ricci tensor and scalar are then
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {R} _ {\mu \nu} = - 2 \phi_ {, \mu \nu} + 2 \phi_ {, \mu} \phi_ {, \nu} - \eta_ {\mu \nu} [ \nabla^ {2} \phi + 2 (\nabla \phi) ^ {2} ] \\ \mathbf {R} \equiv \mathbf {R} _ {\mu} ^ {\mu} = \mathbf {e} ^ {- 2 \phi} \eta^ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {R} _ {\mu \nu} = - 6 \mathbf {e} ^ {- 2 \phi} [ \nabla^ {2} \phi + (\nabla \phi) ^ {2} ] \\ \end{array}
$$
and the Riemann tensor is
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathsf {R} _ {\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} = \mathsf {C} _ {\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} + \frac {1}{2} \left(\mathsf {g} _ {\alpha \gamma} \mathsf {R} _ {\beta \delta} - \mathsf {g} _ {\alpha \delta} \mathsf {R} _ {\beta \gamma} - \mathsf {g} _ {\beta \gamma} \mathsf {R} _ {\alpha \delta} + \mathsf {g} _ {\beta \delta} \mathsf {R} _ {\alpha \gamma}\right) \\ - \frac {1}{6} \left(\mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \gamma} \mathbf {g} _ {\beta \delta} - \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \delta} \mathbf {g} _ {\beta \gamma}\right) \mathbf {R} \\ = \frac {1}{2} \mathrm {e} ^ {2 \phi} (\eta_ {\alpha \gamma} \mathrm {R} _ {\beta \delta} - \eta_ {\alpha \delta} \mathrm {R} _ {\beta \gamma} - \eta_ {\beta \gamma} \mathrm {R} _ {\alpha \delta} + \eta_ {\beta \delta} \mathrm {R} _ {\alpha \gamma}) \\ - \frac {1}{6} \mathrm {e} ^ {4 \phi} (\eta_ {\alpha \gamma} \eta_ {\beta \delta} - \eta_ {\alpha \delta} \eta_ {\beta \gamma}) \mathsf {R} \\ = \mathrm {e} ^ {2 \phi} [ \eta_ {\alpha \gamma} (\phi , \beta \phi , \delta - \phi , \beta \delta) - \eta_ {\alpha \delta} (\phi , \beta \phi , \gamma - \phi , \beta \gamma) \\ - \eta_ {\beta \gamma} (\phi , a \phi , \delta - \phi , a \delta) + \eta_ {\beta \delta} (\phi , a \phi , \gamma - \phi , a \gamma) \\ \left. + (\nabla \phi) ^ {2} (- \eta_ {a \gamma} \eta_ {\beta \delta} + \eta_ {a \delta} \eta_ {\beta \gamma}) ] \right.. \\ \end{array}
$$
