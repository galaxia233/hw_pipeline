Problem 1.12. Define an imaginary coordinate $\mathbf{w} = \mathbf{i} \mathbf{t}$ . Show that a rotation of angle $\theta$ in the $\mathbf{x_i}, \mathbf{w}$ plane $(\mathrm{i} = 1,2,3)$ , where $\theta$ is a pure imaginary number, corresponds to a pure Lorentz boost in $t$ , $\mathbf{x}$ , $\mathbf{y}$ , $\mathbf{z}$ coordinates. How is the boost velocity $\mathbf{v}$ related to the angle $\theta$ ?

## Solution

Solution 1.12. The Lorentz transformation is
$$
t ^ {\prime} = \gamma (t - v x)
$$
$$
\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime} = \gamma (\mathbf {x} - v t) ;
$$
if $\mathbf{w} \equiv \mathbf{i}\mathbf{t}, \mathbf{v}' \equiv \mathbf{i}\mathbf{t}'$ , this becomes
$$
w ^ {\prime} = \gamma (w - i v x)
$$
$$
\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime} = \gamma (\mathbf {x} + \mathbf {i v w}).
$$
To make this look like a rotation
$$
w ^ {\prime} = \cos \theta w - \sin \theta x
$$
$$
\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime} = \cos \theta \mathbf {x} + \sin \theta \mathbf {w},
$$
we obviously want
$$
\sin \theta = i v \gamma
$$
$$
\cos \theta = \gamma .
$$
$$
\sin^ {2} \theta + \cos^ {2} \theta = \gamma^ {2} (1 - v ^ {2}) = 1.
$$
So $\theta = \arcsin (\mathrm{i}v\gamma) = \arctan (\mathrm{i}v) = +\mathrm{i}\tanh^{-1}\mathrm{v}$ .
