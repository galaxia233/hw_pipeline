Problem 11.4. Show that a gyroscope with no applied torques Fermi-Walker transports its spin vector.

## Solution

Solution 11.4. Consider an observer in the local inertial frame comoving with the center of mass of the gyroscope. Because there are no torques,
he sees no precession of the spin axis, so $\underline{\mathrm{dS}}/\mathrm{dt} = 0$ . But in this frame the 4-velocity of the gyroscope is $\mathbf{u} = (1,0)$ , so we can write the condition of zero torque as
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {S} = \mathbf {g} \mathbf {u}
$$
where $\mathbf{g}$ is some constant of proportionality. We find $\mathbf{g}$ from the fact that $\mathbf{S} \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$ :
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} (\mathbf {S} \cdot \mathbf {u}) = (\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {S}) \cdot \mathbf {u} + \mathbf {S} \cdot (\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u}) \\ = \mathbf {g} \mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {u} + \mathbf {S} \cdot \mathbf {a} \\ = - \mathbf {g} + \mathbf {S} \cdot \mathbf {a}. \\ \end{array}
$$
Thus
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {S} = (\mathbf {S} \cdot \mathbf {a}) \mathbf {u}
$$
which is the equation for Fermi-Walker transport
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {S} = (\mathbf {S} \cdot \mathbf {a}) \mathbf {u} - (\mathbf {S} \cdot \mathbf {u}) \mathbf {a}
$$
with $\mathbf{S} \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$ .
