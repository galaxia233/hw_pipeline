10.10. If $\pmb{\xi}(\mathbf{x}^{\mu})$ is a Killing vector field and $\mathbf{u}$ is the tangent vector to a geodesic, show that $\pmb{\xi} \cdot \mathbf{u}$ is constant along the geodesic.

## Solution

Solution 10.10. The change in $\mathbf{u} \cdot \pmb{\xi}$ along the geodesic is given by
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} (\mathbf {u} \cdot \boldsymbol {\xi}) = (\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u}) \cdot \boldsymbol {\xi} + \mathbf {u} \cdot (\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \boldsymbol {\xi}).
$$
Now $\nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u} = 0$ since $\mathbf{u}$ is tangent to a geodesic, and $\mathbf{u} \cdot \nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\pmb{\xi} = 0$ since $\pmb{\xi}$ is a Killing vector. In components,
$$
\mathbf {u} \cdot \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \boldsymbol {\xi} = \mathbf {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {u} ^ {\beta} \xi_ {\alpha ; \beta} = \mathbf {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {u} ^ {\beta} \xi_ {(\alpha ; \beta)} = 0.
$$
Thus $\mathbf{u} \cdot \pmb{\xi} = \text{constant}$ along a geodesic.
