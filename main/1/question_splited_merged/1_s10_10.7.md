10.7. If $\pmb{\xi}$ is a Killing vector, prove that
$$
\xi_ {\mu ; \alpha \beta} = \mathsf {R} _ {\gamma \beta \alpha \mu} \xi^ {\gamma}.
$$

## Solution

Solution 10.7. For any vector $\xi$
$$
\xi_ {\sigma ; \rho \mu} - \xi_ {\sigma ; \mu \rho} = \mathrm {R} _ {\sigma \rho \mu} ^ {\lambda} \xi_ {\lambda} \tag {1}
$$
by the definition of $\mathbf{R}_{\alpha \beta \gamma} \delta$ . Add to Equation (1) its two permutations and use the cyclic identity for the Riemann tensor
$$
\mathbf {R} _ {\sigma \rho \mu} ^ {\lambda} + \mathbf {R} _ {\mu \sigma \rho} ^ {\lambda} + \mathbf {R} _ {\rho \mu \sigma} ^ {\lambda} = 0
$$
to obtain the identity
$$
0 = \xi_ {\sigma ; \rho \mu} - \xi_ {\sigma ; \mu \rho} + \xi_ {\mu ; \sigma \rho} - \xi_ {\mu ; \rho \sigma} + \xi_ {\rho ; \mu \sigma} - \xi_ {\rho ; \sigma \mu}. (2).
$$
For a Killing vector, Equation (2) becomes
$$
0 = \xi_ {\sigma ; \rho \mu} - \xi_ {\sigma ; \mu \rho} - \xi_ {\mu ; \rho \sigma}, (3),
$$
and substituting Equation (3) into Equation (1), one obtains
$$
\xi_ {\mu ; \rho \sigma} = \mathrm {R} _ {\sigma \rho \mu} ^ {\lambda} \xi_ {\lambda}. \tag {4}.
$$
