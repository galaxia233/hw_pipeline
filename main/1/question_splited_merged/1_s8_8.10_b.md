Problem 8.10. [Note: This problem assumes a familiarity with the Cartan calculus, beyond the scope of most relativity texts. Succeeding problems do not depend on it.] In the language of differential forms the generalized
Stokes' theorem is
$$
\int_ {\Omega} \mathrm {d} \boldsymbol {\theta} = \int_ {\partial \Omega} \boldsymbol {\theta}.
$$
To what does this theorem reduce, in the following cases
(b) $\Omega$ is 4-dimensional, $\pmb{\theta} = \mathbf{f}^{\mu}\mathbf{d}^{3}\pmb{\Sigma}_{\mu}$ .

## Solution

(b) $\pmb{\theta} = \mathbf{f}^{\mu}\mathbf{d}^{3}\pmb{\Sigma}_{\mu} = \mathbf{f}^{\mu}\frac{1}{3!}\varepsilon_{\mu \alpha \beta \gamma}\widetilde{\mathbf{dx}}^{\alpha}\wedge \widetilde{\mathbf{dx}}^{\beta}\wedge \widetilde{\mathbf{dx}}^{\gamma}$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {d} \boldsymbol {\theta} = \frac {1}{3 !} \left(\mathrm {f} ^ {\mu}, \lambda \varepsilon_ {\mu \alpha \beta \gamma} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\lambda} + \mathrm {f} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {d} \varepsilon_ {\mu \alpha \beta \gamma}\right) \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\alpha} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\beta} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\gamma} \\ = \frac {1}{3 !} \left(\mathrm {f} ^ {\mu}, _ {\lambda} \varepsilon_ {\mu \alpha \beta \gamma} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\lambda} + \mathrm {f} ^ {\mu} \frac {1}{2} \frac {\mathrm {g} , _ {\lambda}}{\mathrm {g}} \varepsilon_ {\mu \alpha \beta \gamma} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\lambda}\right) \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\alpha} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\beta} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\gamma} \\ = \frac {1}{3 !} | \mathrm {g} | ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} (| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} f ^ {\mu}), \lambda^ {\varepsilon} _ {\mu \alpha \beta \gamma} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\lambda} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\alpha} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\beta} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\gamma} \\ = \frac {- 1}{3 ! \left| \mathrm {g} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left(\left| \mathrm {g} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {f} ^ {\mu}\right) _ {, \lambda} \varepsilon_ {\mu a \beta \gamma} \varepsilon^ {\lambda a \beta \gamma} | \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {0} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {1} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {2} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {3} \\ = \frac {1}{3 ! \left| \mathbf {g} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left(\left| \mathbf {g} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {f} ^ {\mu}\right) _ {, \lambda} \delta_ {\mu} ^ {\lambda} 3! \left| \mathbf {g} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \widetilde {\mathbf {d x}} ^ {0} \wedge \widetilde {\mathbf {d x}} ^ {1} \wedge \widetilde {\mathbf {d x}} ^ {2} \wedge \widetilde {\mathbf {d x}} ^ {3} \\ = \mathrm {f} _ {; \mu} ^ {\mu} \left| \mathrm {g} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {0} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {1} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {2} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {3} . \\ \end{array}
$$
The theorem then reduces to
$$
\int_ {\Omega} \nabla \cdot \mathbf {f} \left| \mathbf {g} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d} ^ {4} \mathbf {x} = \int_ {\partial \Omega} \mathbf {f} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} \Sigma_ {\mu}.
$$
