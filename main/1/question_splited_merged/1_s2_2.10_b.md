Problem 2.10. Consider the reaction $\mathbf{A} \rightarrow \mathbf{B} + \mathbf{C}$ (with particle masses $\mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{A}}, \mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{B}}, \mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{C}}$ ).
(b) An atom of mass M at rest decays to a state of rest energy M-δ by emitting a photon of energy hν. Show that hν < δ. In the Mössbauer effect, why is hν = δ?

## Solution

(b) Here $\mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{A}} = \mathbf{M}$ , $\mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{B}} = 0$ , $\mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{C}} = \mathbf{M} - \delta$ , so
$$
\mathbf {E} _ {\mathbf {B}} = \mathrm {h} \nu = \frac {\mathrm {M} ^ {2} - (\mathrm {M} - \delta) ^ {2}}{2 \mathrm {M}} = \delta - \frac {\delta^ {2}}{2 \mathrm {M}} <   \delta .
$$
Physically $\mathfrak{h}\nu \neq \delta$ because some energy goes into the recoil of M which is necessary to conserve momentum. In the Mössbauer effect, the recoil momentum is shared among $\sim 10^{23}$ atoms, so the recoil energy is negligible.
