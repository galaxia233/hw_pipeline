Problem 16.15. What is the smallest possible proper circumference of a self-supporting spherical shell of mass $M$ , if its matter satisfies the dominant energy condition (as do all known forms of matter)?

## Solution

Solution 16.15. The dominant energy condition requires
$$
\left| \mathbf {T} ^ {\widehat {0} \widehat {0}} \right| > \left| \mathbf {T} ^ {\widehat {\mathrm {k}} \widehat {\mathrm {k}}} \right|. \tag {1}
$$
Using the results of Problem 16.14, and integrating Equation (1) through the thin shell, we have the inequality
$$
\left(1 + \frac {\mathrm {M}}{2 \mathrm {R}}\right) ^ {- 3} > \frac {1}{2} \frac {\mathrm {M} / 2 \mathrm {R}}{1 - \mathrm {M} / 2 \mathrm {R}} \left(1 + \frac {\mathrm {M}}{2 \mathrm {R}}\right) ^ {- 3} \tag {2}
$$
where $\mathbf{R}$ is the isotropic radial coordinate. Letting $\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{M} / 2\mathbf{R}$ we have $2 - 2\mathbf{x} > \mathbf{x}$ or $\mathbf{x} < 2/3$ , giving $\mathbf{R} > \frac{3}{4}\mathbf{M}$ , or for the curvature radial coordinate $\mathcal{R}$
$$
\mathcal {R} = \mathrm {R} \left(1 + \frac {\mathrm {M}}{2 \mathrm {R}}\right) ^ {2} > \frac {2 5}{1 2} \mathrm {M}, \tag {3}
$$
about 1.04 Schwarzschild radii.
