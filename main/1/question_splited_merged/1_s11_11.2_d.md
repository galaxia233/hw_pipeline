Problem 11.2. Show
(d) invariant under translations. Here $\mathfrak{u}^{\alpha}$ is the "center of mass 4-velocity" $\mathfrak{u}^{\alpha} \equiv \mathbb{P}^{\alpha} / (-\mathbb{P}^{\beta} \mathbb{P}_{\beta})^{\frac{1}{2}}$ and $\mathbb{P}^{\alpha}$ is the total momentum $\mathbb{P}^{\alpha} \equiv \int \mathrm{d}^{3} \mathbf{x} \mathbf{T}^{\alpha 0}$ .

## Solution

(d) From (b),
$$
\mathsf {J} ^ {\alpha \beta} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma} + \mathbf {a} ^ {\sigma}) = \mathbf {a} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {P} ^ {\beta} - \mathbf {a} ^ {\beta} \mathbf {P} ^ {\alpha} + \mathsf {J} ^ {\alpha \beta} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma}).
$$
and thus
$$
\mathsf {S} _ {\alpha} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma} + \mathsf {a} ^ {\sigma}) = - \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon_ {\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} (\mathsf {a} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {P} ^ {\beta} - \mathsf {a} ^ {\beta} \mathbf {P} ^ {\alpha}) \mathbf {P} ^ {\delta} / | \mathbf {P} | + \mathsf {S} _ {\alpha} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma}) .
$$
The first term on the right vanishes ( $\varepsilon$ is totally antisymmetric and $\mathbf{P}^{\mu}$ occurs quadratically) so $\mathbf{S}_a(\mathbf{x}^\sigma +\mathbf{a}^\sigma) = \mathbf{S}_a(\mathbf{x}^\sigma)$ .
