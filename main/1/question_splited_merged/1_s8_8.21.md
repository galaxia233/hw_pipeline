Problem 8.21. If $\Omega$ is a p-form, show that $\mathcal{L}_{\mathbf{X}}(\mathrm{d}\Omega) = \mathrm{d}(\mathcal{L}_{\mathbf{X}}\Omega)$ .

## Solution

Solution 8.21. Consider a 1-form $\tilde{\mathbf{U}} = \mathbf{U}_{\alpha}\widetilde{\mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}}^{\alpha}$ . First calculate $\mathrm{d}\mathcal{L}_{\mathbf{x}}^{\varrho}\tilde{\mathbf{U}}$
$$
\mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {x}} \tilde {\mathbf {U}} = (\mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {U} _ {\beta , a} + \mathbf {U} _ {a} \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}, \beta) \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\beta}
$$
$$
\mathbf {d} \left(\mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {x}} \tilde {\mathbf {U}}\right) = \left(\mathrm {x} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {U} _ {\beta , \alpha} + \mathrm {U} _ {\alpha} \mathrm {x} ^ {\alpha}, \beta\right), \gamma \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\gamma} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\beta} \tag {1}
$$
Now calculate $\mathfrak{L}_{\mathbf{x}}(\tilde{\mathbf{d}\mathbf{U}})$
$$
\mathrm {d} \widetilde {\mathbf {U}} = \mathrm {U} _ {\alpha , \gamma} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\gamma} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\alpha}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {x}} (\mathrm {d} \tilde {\mathbf {U}}) = \mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {x}} \left(\mathrm {U} _ {a, \gamma}\right) \widetilde {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x}} ^ {\gamma} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x}} ^ {a} + \mathrm {U} _ {a, \gamma} \mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {x}} \left(\widetilde {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x}} ^ {\gamma}\right) \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x}} ^ {a} \\ + \mathrm {U} _ {\alpha , \gamma} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\gamma} \wedge \mathscr {L} _ {\mathrm {x}} (\widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\alpha}) \\ = \mathrm {x} ^ {\tau} \mathrm {U} _ {a, \gamma \tau} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\gamma} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {a} + \mathrm {U} _ {a, \gamma} \mathrm {x} ^ {\gamma}, _ {\tau} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\tau} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {a} + \mathrm {U} _ {a, \gamma} \mathrm {x} ^ {a}, _ {\tau} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\gamma} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\tau} \\ = \left[ \left(\mathbf {x} ^ {\tau} \mathrm {U} _ {\beta , \tau} + \mathrm {U} _ {\alpha} \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}, \beta\right), \gamma - \mathrm {U} _ {\alpha} \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}, \beta \gamma \right] \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\gamma} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\beta}. \tag {2} \\ \end{array}
$$
Equation (2) is obtained merely by relabeling indices. By symmetry of partial derivatives and antisymmetry of wedge product, the last term in brackets on the right hand side of Equation (2) vanishes. Comparing Equations (1) and (2), we have for 1-forms:
$$
\mathbf {d} \left(\mathscr {L} _ {\mathbf {X}} \tilde {\mathbf {U}}\right) = \mathscr {L} _ {\mathbf {X}} (\mathbf {d} \tilde {\mathbf {U}}).
$$
The generalization of this method from 1-form to p-form is straightforward.
