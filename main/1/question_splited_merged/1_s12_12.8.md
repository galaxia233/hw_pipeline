Problem 12.8. Define a stress tensor for the Newtonian gravitational potential $\mathbf{U}$ as follows:
$$
\mathbf {T} _ {\mathrm {j k}} \equiv \frac {1}{4 \pi} \left(\mathbf {U} _ {, \mathrm {j}} \mathbf {U} _ {, \mathrm {k}} - \frac {1}{2} \delta_ {\mathrm {j k}} \mathbf {U} _ {, \mathrm {n}} \mathbf {U} ^ {, \mathrm {n}}\right) .
$$
Show that the Newtonian equations of motion for stressed matter with proper density $\rho_0$ and velocity $\underline{\mathbf{v}}$ can be written in the form
$$
\rho_ {0} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathrm {j}}}{\mathrm {d} t} = - \frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {k}}} \left(\mathbf {T} _ {\mathbf {j k}} + \mathbf {t} _ {\mathbf {j k}}\right)
$$
$$
\left(\rho_ {0} \mathrm {v} _ {\mathrm {j}}\right), \mathrm {t} + \left(\mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {j k}} + \mathrm {t} _ {\mathrm {j k}} + \rho_ {0} \mathrm {v} _ {\mathrm {j}} \mathrm {v} _ {\mathrm {k}}\right), \mathrm {k} = 0,
$$
where $t_{jk}$ is the ordinary 3-dimensional stress tensor.

## Solution

Solution 12.8. Since Poisson's equation is $\mathbf{U}_{,\mathbf{k}\mathbf{k}} = 4\pi \rho_{0}$ we have
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {j k}, \mathrm {k}} = \frac {1}{4 \pi} \left(\mathrm {U} _ {\mathrm {, j k}} \mathrm {U} _ {\mathrm {, k}} + \mathrm {U} _ {\mathrm {, j}} \mathrm {U} _ {\mathrm {, k k}} - \mathrm {U} _ {\mathrm {, n j}} \mathrm {U} _ {\mathrm {, n}}\right) = \rho_ {0} \mathrm {U} _ {\mathrm {, j}}.
$$
Thus the Newtonian equation of motion can be written as
$$
\rho_ {0} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathrm {j}}}{\mathrm {d} t} = - \frac {\partial t _ {\mathrm {j k}}}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {k}}} - \rho_ {0} \frac {\partial \mathbf {U}}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {j}}} = \frac {- \partial}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {k}}} \left(\mathbf {T} _ {\mathrm {j k}} + t _ {\mathrm {j k}}\right) _ {\mathrm {, k}}. \tag {1}
$$
Now use the Newtonian equation of mass conservation
$$
\partial \rho_ {0} / \partial t + \left(\rho_ {0} v _ {\cdot k} ^ {k}\right) _ {, k} = 0. \tag {2}
$$
From Equation (2) and the expression for the convective derivative $\mathrm{d} / \mathrm{d}t = \partial /\partial t + \mathbf{v_k}\partial /\partial \mathbf{x^k}$ , one can write
$$
\rho_ {0} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathrm {j}}}{\mathrm {d} t} = \rho_ {0} \left(\frac {\partial \mathbf {v} _ {\mathrm {j}}}{\mathrm {d} t} + \mathbf {v} ^ {\mathbf {k}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathrm {j}, \mathbf {k}} ^ {\mathrm {j}}\right) = (\rho_ {0} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathrm {j}}) _ {\mathrm {, t}} + (\rho_ {0} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathrm {j}} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {k}}) _ {\mathrm {, k}}
$$
and thus Equation (1) can be written as
$$
\left(\rho_ {0} \mathrm {v} _ {\mathrm {j}}\right) _ {, \mathrm {t}} + \left(\mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {j k}} + \mathrm {t} _ {\mathrm {j k}} + \rho_ {0} \mathrm {v} _ {\mathrm {j}} \mathrm {v} _ {\mathrm {k}}\right) _ {, \mathrm {k}} = 0. \tag {3}
$$
