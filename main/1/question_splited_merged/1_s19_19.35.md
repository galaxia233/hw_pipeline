Problem 19.35. Prove that for a physically possible perfect fluid no solution of the Einstein equations is homogeneous, everywhere isotropic, and static. (Before Hubble's discovery, Einstein considered this a failing of the theory and introduced the "cosmological constant" term as a remedy.)

## Solution

Solution 19.35. The Friedmann equations (Problem 19.15) are
$$
\ddot {\mathbf {R}} = - \frac {4}{3} \pi \mathrm {G} (\rho + 3 \mathrm {p}) \mathbf {R}
$$
$$
\ddot {\mathbf {R}} \ddot {\mathbf {R}} + 2 \mathbf {R} + 2 \mathbf {k} = 4 \pi \mathbf {G} (\rho - \mathbf {p}) \mathbf {R} ^ {2}.
$$
For a static solution, all time derivatives must vanish, so we get two conditions, $\rho + 3p = 0$ and $\rho = 3k / 8\pi GR^2$ , which have no solution for a fluid with positive energy density and pressure (i.e. any known fluid).
