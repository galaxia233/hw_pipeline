15.14 (b) In the Schwarzschild geometry show that all the physical components of the Riemann tensor are invariant for a boost in the $\mathbf{r}$ -direction, but that all physical components are not invariant for a boost in the $\theta$ or $\phi$ direction.

## Solution

(b) In the Schwarzschild geometry the nonvanishing physical components of the Riemann tensor are
$$
\mathrm {R} _ {\mathrm {t r t r}} = \mathrm {R} _ {\hat {\theta}} \hat {\phi} \hat {\theta} \hat {\phi} = - 2 \mathrm {M / r ^ {3}}
$$
$$
\mathrm {R} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} \hat {\theta} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} \hat {\theta} = \mathrm {R} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} \hat {\phi} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} \hat {\phi} = - \mathrm {R} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \hat {\theta} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \hat {\theta} = - \mathrm {R} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \hat {\phi} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \hat {\phi} = \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r} ^ {3}
$$
and the components related to these by symmetry. To show that all physical components are invariant for a radial boost we can explicitly calculate 20 independent, boosted components in a straightforward manner. Another way is to define, in the Schwarzschild geometry, the vectors
$$
\mathbf {l} \equiv \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} + \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \qquad \mathbf {n} \equiv \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} - \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \qquad \mathbf {m} \equiv \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} + \mathbf {i} \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\phi}}}
$$
and to observe that the Riemann tensor can be written as a sum of products of the vectors:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {R} = \frac {1}{2} \frac {\mathbf {M}}{\mathbf {r} ^ {3}} \left\{- (\mathbf {n} \wedge \mathbf {l}) (\mathbf {n} \wedge \mathbf {l}) + (\mathbf {m} \wedge \mathbf {m} ^ {*}) (\mathbf {m} \wedge \mathbf {m} ^ {*}) \right. \\ + \operatorname {R e a l} \left[ (\mathbf {n} \wedge \mathbf {m}) (\mathbf {l} \wedge \mathbf {m} ^ {*}) + (\mathbf {l} \wedge \mathbf {m} ^ {*}) (\mathbf {n} \wedge \mathbf {m}) \right] \}. \\ \end{array}
$$
For a boost in the $r$ -direction with velocity parameter $\psi$ , clearly the components of $m$ are invariant. From $e_{\hat{t}} = \cosh \psi e_{\hat{t}'} - \sinh \psi e_{\hat{r}'}$ and $e_{\hat{r}} = \cosh \psi e_{\hat{r}'} - \sinh \psi e_{\hat{t}'}$ it follows that
$$
\mathbf {l} = \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} ^ {\wedge} + \mathbf {e} _ {\mathbf {r}} ^ {\wedge} = \mathbf {e} ^ {- \psi} \left(\mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} ^ {\wedge^ {\prime}} + \mathbf {e} _ {\mathbf {r}} ^ {\wedge^ {\prime}}\right)
$$
$$
\mathbf {n} = \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} - \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} = \mathbf {e} ^ {\psi} \left(\mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}} ^ {\prime}} + \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}} ^ {\prime}}\right)
$$
so that
$$
1 _ {\hat {\mu} ^ {\prime}} n _ {\hat {\nu} ^ {\prime}} = \dot {1} _ {\hat {\mu}} n _ {\hat {\nu}}.
$$
From the form of $\mathbf{R}$ then, it is clear that all components are invariant.
To see that this same conclusion does not apply to boosts in other directions, notice that for a boost in the $\phi$ direction, with velocity parameter $\psi$
$$
\begin{array}{l} R _ {\hat {r} ^ {\prime} \hat {t} ^ {\prime} \hat {r} ^ {\prime}} \hat {\phi} ^ {\prime} = \Lambda^ {a} _ {\hat {t} ^ {\prime}} \Lambda^ {\beta} _ {\hat {\phi} ^ {\prime}} R _ {\hat {r} a \hat {r} \beta} \\ = \sinh \psi \cosh \psi \left(R _ {\hat {r} \hat {t} \hat {r} \hat {t}} ^ {\prime} + R _ {\hat {r} \hat {\phi} \hat {\sigma} \hat {\phi}} ^ {\prime}\right) \\ = - \left(\mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r} ^ {3}\right) \sinh \psi \cosh \psi \\ \neq \mathrm {R} _ {\hat {\mathrm {f}} \hat {\mathrm {r}} \hat {\mathrm {f}}} ^ {-} \hat {\phi}. \\ \end{array}
$$
