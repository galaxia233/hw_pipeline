Problem 21.7. A Lagrangian for the Brans-Dicke theory is
$$
L = \left(\Phi R - \omega \Phi_ {, a} \Phi^ {, a} \Phi^ {- 1} + 1 6 \pi L _ {\text {m a t t e r}}\right)
$$
where $\Phi =$ scalar field, $\mathbf{R} =$ curvature scalar, $\omega =$ coupling constant.
Derive the field equations from $\delta \int \mathbf{L}(-\mathbf{g})^{\frac{1}{2}}\mathrm{d}^{4}\mathbf{x} = 0$ by varying $\mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta}$ and $\Phi$ .

## Solution

Solution 21.7. Variation of the Lagrangian with respect to $\Phi$ yields
$$
(- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \left(\mathbf {R} + \frac {\omega}{\Phi^ {2}} \Phi_ {, a} \Phi^ {, a}\right) = - 2 \omega \frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {a}} \left(\frac {\Phi^ {, a} (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{\Phi}\right)
$$
and thus
$$
2 \omega \Phi^ {- 1} \square \Phi - \omega \Phi^ {- 2} \Phi_ {, a} \Phi^ {, a} + R = 0. \tag {1}
$$
(We have used here
$$
\square \Phi \equiv \Phi_ {, a} ^ {; a} = \frac {1}{(- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left. (\Phi^ {, a} (- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}) _ {, a} \cdot\right)
$$
Variation of L with respect to $\mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta}$ involves three terms:
$$
\frac {\delta \left(- \omega \Phi , ^ {a} \Phi , ^ {b} \beta_ {g _ {a} \beta} \Phi^ {- 1} (- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right)}{\delta g _ {a \beta}} = - \frac {\omega}{\Phi} \left[ \Phi , ^ {a} \Phi , ^ {b} (\neg a) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} - \frac {1}{2} \Phi , ^ {a} \Phi , _ {\mu} g ^ {a \beta} (- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \right] \tag {2a}
$$
and
$$
\mathrm {(i i)} \qquad \delta (\Phi \mathsf {R} (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}) = (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \Phi \mathbf {G} ^ {\alpha \beta} \delta \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} + \Phi (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {g} ^ {\alpha \beta} \delta \mathsf {R} _ {\alpha \beta}, \qquad \mathrm {(2 b)}
$$
$$
(- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {g} ^ {a \beta} \delta \mathsf {R} _ {a \beta} = \frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma}} \left[ (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} (\mathbf {g} ^ {a \beta} \delta \Gamma_ {a \beta} ^ {\gamma} - \mathbf {g} ^ {a \gamma} \delta \Gamma_ {a \tau} ^ {\tau}) \right] \qquad (2 c)
$$
(see Problem 21.4). Also, we have from the expression for the $\Gamma$ 's as derivatives of the g's
$$
\begin{array}{l} \delta \Gamma_ {\mu \nu} ^ {\lambda} = - \mathrm {g} ^ {\lambda \rho} \delta \mathrm {g} _ {\rho \sigma} \Gamma_ {\mu \nu} ^ {\sigma} + \frac {1}{2} \mathrm {g} ^ {\lambda \rho} \left[ \left(\delta \mathrm {g} _ {\rho \mu}\right), _ {\nu} + \left(\delta \mathrm {g} _ {\rho \nu}\right), _ {\mu} - \left(\delta \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu}\right), _ {\rho} \right] \tag {2d} \\ = \frac {1}{2} \mathbf {g} ^ {\lambda \rho} [ (\delta \mathbf {g} _ {\rho \mu}) _ {; \nu} + (\delta \mathbf {g} _ {\rho \nu}) _ {; \mu} - (\delta \mathbf {g} _ {\mu \nu}) _ {; \rho} ]. \\ \end{array}.
$$
Using Equations (2c) and (2d), we can then write
$$
\Phi (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {g} ^ {\alpha \beta} \delta \mathbf {R} _ {\alpha \beta} \rightarrow \delta \mathbf {g} ^ {\alpha \beta} (- \mathbf {\Phi} _ {, \alpha ; \beta} + \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \square \Phi) (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \qquad \qquad (2 \mathrm {e})
$$
where $\rightarrow$ denotes the operation of dropping all exact divergences (which vanish when integrated over). Combining Equations (2b) and (2e) we have
$$
\delta (\Phi \mathbf {R} (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}) = (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \delta \mathbf {g} ^ {\alpha \beta} [ - \Phi_ {, \alpha ; \beta} + \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \square \Phi + \Phi \mathbf {G} _ {\alpha \beta} ]. (2 \mathrm {f})
$$
The remaining term needed is
$$
1 6 \pi \frac {\delta \left(\mathrm {L} _ {\text {m a t t e r}} (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right)}{\delta \mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta}} = - 8 \pi (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {T} ^ {\alpha \beta}. \tag {2g}
$$
From Equations (2a) and (2f), and (2g), we get the field equations obtained by varying $\mathbf{g}^{\alpha \beta}$ :
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {G} _ {\alpha \beta} + \Phi^ {- 1} \left(\mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \square \Phi - \Phi_ {, \alpha ; \beta}\right) - \omega \Phi^ {- 2} \left(\Phi_ {, \alpha} \Phi_ {, \beta} - \frac {1}{2} \mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \Phi_ {, \mu} \Phi^ {\prime , \mu}\right) \tag {3} \\ - 8 \pi \Phi^ {- 1} T _ {\alpha} \beta = 0. \\ \end{array}.
$$
