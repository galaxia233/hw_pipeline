Problem 15.6. Show that the trajectory of light rays in the Schwarzschild
metric obeys
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {u}}{\mathrm {d} \phi^ {2}} + \mathbf {u} = 3 \mathbf {u} ^ {2}
$$
where $\mathbf{u} \equiv \mathbf{M} / \mathbf{r}$ , and $\mathbf{r}$ is the Schwarzschild radial coordinate. Denote the minimum value of $\mathbf{r}$ along the trajectory by $\mathbf{b}$ , the "impact parameter." In the case $\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{b} < 1$ what is the deflection of a photon as it passes a spherical gravitating body? Give a formula for the deflection angle to lowest nonvanishing order in $(\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{b}) .$

## Solution

Solution 15.6. Choose $\theta = \pi /2$ and $\mathbf{p}^{\theta} = 0$ . If $\lambda$ is an affine parameter such that $\mathbf{p}^{\mathbf{r}} = \mathrm{d}\mathbf{r} / \mathrm{d}\lambda$ and $\mathbf{p}^{\phi} = \mathrm{d}\phi /\mathrm{d}\lambda = \mathbf{r}^{-2}\mathbf{p}_{\phi}$ , then
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}{\mathrm {d} \phi} = \frac {\mathbf {p} ^ {\mathbf {r}}}{\mathbf {p} ^ {\phi}}.
$$
From $\mathbf{p} \cdot \mathbf{p} = 0$ we get a relation amongst $\mathbf{p}^{\mathbf{r}}$ , $\mathbf{p}^{\phi}$ , and $\mathbf{p}^{0}$ which can be solved for $\mathrm{dr} / \mathrm{d}\phi$ :
$$
\left(\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r} / \mathrm {d} \phi\right) ^ {2} = \mathrm {r} ^ {4} (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) [ \gamma (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) ^ {- 1} - 1 / \mathrm {r} ^ {2} ] \tag {1}
$$
where $\gamma \equiv p_0^2 /p_\phi^2 = \text{constant}$ . If we now introduce $u\equiv M / r$ , Equation (1) becomes
$$
\left(u ^ {\prime}\right) ^ {2} = (1 - 2 u) \left[ \gamma M ^ {2} (1 - 2 u) ^ {- 1} - u ^ {2} \right] = \gamma M ^ {2} - u ^ {2} + 2 u ^ {3} \tag {2}
$$
where $\mathbf{\nabla}^{\prime}$ denotes $\mathrm{d} / \mathrm{d}\phi$ . If we differentiate this equation we get the very simple second-order equation
$$
u ^ {\prime \prime} + u = 3 u ^ {2}. \tag {3}
$$
In the limit $\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{b} \ll 1$ the zeroth order solution can be taken to be the "straight line" solution $r \sin \phi = b$ or
$$
\mathbf {u} _ {0} = (\mathbf {M} / \mathbf {b}) \sin \phi .
$$
Now write $\mathbf{u} = \mathbf{u}_0 + \mathbf{u}_1 + \dots$ with $\mathbf{u}_1 \ll 1$ so that Equation (3) is approximately
$$
\mathbf {u} _ {1} ^ {\prime \prime} + \mathbf {u} _ {1} \approx 3 \mathbf {u} _ {0} ^ {2} = 3 \left(\frac {\mathbf {M}}{\mathbf {b}}\right) ^ {2} \sin^ {2} \phi = \frac {3}{2} \left(\frac {\mathbf {M}}{\mathbf {b}}\right) ^ {2} \left(1 - \cos 2 \phi\right).
$$
This can be solved by inspection, and we get
$$
u \approx \left(\frac {M}{b}\right) \sin \phi + \frac {1}{2} \left(\frac {M}{b}\right) ^ {2} (3 + \cos 2 \phi).
$$
Now we can find the total deflection angle by calculating the two angles at which $r = \infty (u = 0)$ . These angles must satisfy:
$$
2 \sin \phi \approx - (\mathrm {M} / \mathrm {b}) (3 + \cos 2 \phi)
$$
$$
\phi \approx - 2 (\mathrm {M} / \mathrm {b}) \quad \text {a n d} \quad \phi \approx \pi + 2 (\mathrm {M} / \mathrm {b}).
$$
Thus the total deflection angle is 4M/b.
