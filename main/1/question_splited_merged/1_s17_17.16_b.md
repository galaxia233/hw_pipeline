Problem 17.16 (b) Show that the equation can be reduced to ordinary differential equations by separation of variables.

## Solution

(b) In Equation (1) we take the $t$ and $\phi$ dependence to be
$$
\Phi (t, r, \theta , \phi) = e ^ {- i \omega t} e ^ {i m \phi} R (r) S (\theta)
$$
and we divide through by $\Phi$ . This gives us
$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {1}{R} \frac {d}{d r} \left(\Delta \frac {d R}{d r}\right) + \omega^ {2} \frac {\left(r ^ {2} + a ^ {2}\right) ^ {2}}{\Delta} - \frac {4 M a r \omega m}{\Delta} + \frac {a ^ {2} m ^ {2}}{\Delta} \tag {2} \\ = \frac {- 1}{S \sin \theta} \frac {d}{d \theta} \left(\sin \theta \frac {d S}{d \theta}\right) + a ^ {2} \omega^ {2} \sin^ {2} \theta + \frac {m ^ {2}}{\sin^ {2} \theta}. \\ \end{array}
$$
Since the left-hand side of Equation (2) is a function of $\mathbf{r}$ alone and the right-hand side must be a function of $\theta$ alone; each side must be a constant $A$ , thus
$$
\frac {1}{\sin \theta} \frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} \theta} \left(\sin \theta \frac {\mathrm {d} S}{\mathrm {d} \theta}\right) - \left(a ^ {2} \omega^ {2} \sin^ {2} \theta + \frac {\mathrm {m} ^ {2}}{\sin^ {2} \theta} - A\right) S = 0 \tag {3}
$$
and
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}} \left(\Delta \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {R}}{\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}\right) + \left[ \frac {\omega^ {2} \left(\mathrm {r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {a} ^ {2}\right) ^ {2} - 4 \operatorname {M a r} \omega \mathrm {m} + \mathrm {a} ^ {2} \mathrm {m} ^ {2}}{\Delta} - \mathrm {A} \right] \mathbf {R} = 0. \tag {4}
$$
Since S must be regular at $\theta = 0$ and $\pi$ , Equation (3) is an eigenvalue equation for A. The function S is in fact a "spheroidal wave function"; in the simplest case when $a\omega = 0$ , we have $S = P_{\ell_m}(\cos \theta)$ and $A = \ell (\ell +1)$
