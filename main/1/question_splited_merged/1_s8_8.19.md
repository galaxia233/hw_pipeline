Problem 8.19. You are given a vector field $\mathbf{v}^{\mathrm{i}} = (-\mathbf{y},\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z}^{\alpha}),\alpha = \text{constant}$ , in a 3-space with metric $\mathrm{ds}^2 = \mathrm{dx}^2 +\mathrm{dy}^2 +\mathrm{dz}^2$ . A certain vector $\underline{\mathbf{u}}$ is Lie transported along $\underline{\mathbf{v}}$ from a point $\mathbf{A}$ to a point $\mathbf{B}$ and is then parallel transported back to $\mathbf{A}$ by the reverse route. For what value of $\alpha$ is there a $\underline{\mathbf{u}}$ which is always left unchanged by this process?

## Solution

Solution 8.19. If $\underline{\mathbf{u}}$ can be returned to A unchanged, then in principle it can be extended into a vector field which is both parallel transported and Lie transported from A to B. Let $\underline{\mathbf{u}} = (\mathbf{u}^{\mathbf{x}},\mathbf{u}^{\mathbf{y}},\mathbf{u}^{\mathbf{z}})$ , and consider the identity
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {v} - \nabla_ {\mathbf {v}} \mathbf {u} = [ \underline {{\mathbf {u}}}, \underline {{\mathbf {v}}} ] = \mathscr {L} _ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {v} = - \mathscr {L} _ {\mathbf {v}} \mathbf {u}. \tag {1}
$$
Setting the appropriate terms to zero in Equation (1), i.e. $\nabla_{\mathbf{v}}\mathbf{u} = \mathcal{L}_{\mathbf{v}}\mathbf{u} = 0$ and noting that the Christoffel symbols vanish, we have
$$
\begin{array}{l} \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {v} = 0 = \mathbf {u} ^ {\mathrm {i}} \mathbf {v} _ {, i} ^ {\mathrm {k}} = 0 \tag {2} \\ - u ^ {y} = u ^ {x} = a u ^ {z} z ^ {a - 1} = 0. \\ \end{array}
$$
So $\underline{\mathbf{u}}$ can be nonzero (and have $\mathfrak{u}^z\neq 0$ ) only if
$$
\alpha = 0. \tag {3}
$$
