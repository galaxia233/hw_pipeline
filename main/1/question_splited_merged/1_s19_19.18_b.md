Problem 19.18. Solve the first-order Friedmann equation
$$
\left(\frac {\dot {\mathrm {R}}}{\bar {\mathrm {R}}}\right) ^ {2} = \frac {8 \pi \mathrm {G}}{3} \rho - \frac {\mathrm {k}}{\mathrm {R} ^ {2}}
$$
for $\mathbf{R}(t)$ when the density $\rho$ is dominated by radiation. Express any parameters of the present epoch in terms of $\mathbf{H}_0$ and $\mathbf{q}_0$ .

## Solution

(b) When radiation dominates, the mass-energy in a given comoving volume is not constant. There is an extra decrease in density from the redshift of photons, so
$$
\rho = \rho_ {0} \left(\frac {\mathbf {R} _ {0}}{\mathbf {R}}\right) ^ {4}.
$$
The analog of Equation (2) is
$$
\left(\dot {\frac {\mathrm {R}}{\mathrm {R}}}\right) ^ {2} = \left(\frac {\mathrm {d R / d \eta}}{\mathrm {R} ^ {2}}\right) ^ {2} = \frac {8 \pi \mathrm {G}}{3} \rho_ {0} \left(\frac {\mathrm {R} _ {0}}{\mathrm {R}}\right) ^ {4} - \frac {\mathrm {k}}{\mathrm {R} ^ {2}}
$$
or
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathrm {R}}{\left(\frac {8}{3} \pi \mathrm {G} \rho_ {0} \mathrm {R} _ {0} ^ {4} - \mathrm {k R} ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} = \mathrm {d} \eta
$$
which has solutions:
$$
R = \left(\frac {8 \pi}{3} G \rho_ {0} R _ {0} ^ {4}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \times \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \sin \eta & k = 1 \\ \eta & k = 0 \\ \sinh \eta & k = - 1. \end{array} \right. \tag {6}
$$
Instead of Equation (4), we now get (Problem 19.15)
$$
\mathbf {q} _ {0} = \frac {8 \pi \mathrm {G}}{3} \frac {\rho_ {0}}{\mathrm {H} _ {0} ^ {2}},
$$
and instead of Equation (5), we have
$$
R _ {0} ^ {2} = \frac {k}{\left(q _ {0} - 1\right) H _ {0} ^ {2}} \quad (k = \pm 1).
$$
Equation (6) therefore becomes
$$
\frac {8 \pi}{3} G \rho_ {0} R _ {0} ^ {4} = \left\{ \begin{array}{c c} \frac {q _ {0}}{(q _ {0} - 1) ^ {2} H _ {0} ^ {2}} & k = \pm 1 \\ H _ {0} ^ {2} R _ {0} ^ {4} & k = 0 \end{array} \right.
$$
and integrating for t gives
$$
t = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \frac {1}{H _ {0}} \left[ \frac {q _ {0} ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{q _ {0} - 1} \right] & (1 - \cos \eta) \\ \frac {1}{2} H _ {0} R _ {0} ^ {2} \eta^ {2} & \\ \frac {1}{H _ {0}} \left[ \frac {q _ {0} ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{1 - q _ {0}} \right] & (\cosh \eta - 1) \end{array} \quad k = 1 \right.
$$
