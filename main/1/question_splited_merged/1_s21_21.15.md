Problem 21.15. Let $\mathbf{T_{ab}} = \mathbf{T_{AA'B'B'}}$ . Show that if $\mathbf{T_{ab}}$ is antisymmetric then its dual in a spinor representation is $*\mathbf{T_{ab}} = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{i}(\mathbf{T_{ABB'A'}} - \mathbf{T_{BAA'B'}})$ .

## Solution

Solution 21.15. Since $\varepsilon_{\mathbf{ab}}^{\mathbf{cd}} = \mathrm{i}(\delta_{\mathbf{A}}^{\mathbf{C}}\delta_{\mathbf{B}}^{\mathbf{D}}\delta_{\mathbf{B}}^{\mathbf{C}'}\delta_{\mathbf{A}}^{\mathbf{D}'}$ (see e.g. F. A. E. Pirani, Lectures on General Relativity, p. 315) and ${}^{*}\mathrm{T}_{\mathrm{ab}} = \frac{\mathrm{i}}{2}\varepsilon_{\mathrm{ab}}^{\mathrm{cd}}\mathrm{T}_{\mathrm{cd}}$ the result follows immediately.
