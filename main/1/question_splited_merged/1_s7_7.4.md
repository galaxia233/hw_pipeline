Problem 7.4. For the 2-dimensional metric $\mathrm{ds}^2 = (\mathrm{dx}^2 -\mathrm{dt}^2) / \mathrm{t}^2$ , find all connection coefficients $\Gamma_{\alpha \beta \gamma}$ , and find all timelike geodesic curves.

## Solution

Solution 7.4. The nonvanishing derivatives of the metric tensor are $\mathbf{g}_{\mathbf{x}\mathbf{x},t} = -\mathbf{g}_{\mathbf{tt},t} = -2 / t^3$ . Thus the nonvanishing connection coefficients are
$$
\Gamma_ {t t t} = 1 / t ^ {3}, \quad \Gamma_ {\mathbf {x x t}} = \Gamma_ {\mathbf {x t x}} = - \Gamma_ {\mathbf {t x x}} = - 1 / t ^ {3}.
$$
To find the geodesics it is easiest to proceed directly from their definition as curves of extreme length. Let a geodesic be $\mathbf{x}(t)$ . Then (dot represents $d/dt$ )
$$
0 = \delta \int (\mathrm {d} s ^ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} = \delta \int (1 - \dot {x} ^ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \frac {\mathrm {d} t}{t}.
$$
The Euler-Lagrange equation for this extremization is
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} t} \left[ \frac {\dot {\mathbf {x}}}{t \left(1 - \dot {\mathbf {x}} ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \right] = 0.
$$
This is easily solved by letting $\tanh \theta \equiv \dot{\mathbf{x}}$ :
$$
\frac {\sinh \theta}{t} = \text {c o n s t a n t},
$$
which integrates to
$$
\left(\mathbf {x} - \mathbf {x} _ {0}\right) ^ {2} = t ^ {2} + \mathsf {a} ^ {2}.
$$
Thus the geodesics are hyperbolas asymptotic to the light cones:
