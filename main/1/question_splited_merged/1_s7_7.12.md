Problem 7.12. Show that in flat spacetime, the conservation law for the 4-momentum of a freely moving particle can be written $\nabla_{\mathbf{p}} \mathbf{p} = 0$ . Show that particles with nonzero rest mass move along timelike geodesics.

## Solution

Solution 7.12. The components of $\nabla_{\mathbf{p}}\mathbf{p}$ are
$$
(\nabla_ {\mathbf {p}} \mathbf {p}) ^ {\beta} = \operatorname {m u} ^ {\alpha} \left(\mathbf {p} ^ {\beta} _ {, a} + \Gamma^ {\beta} _ {\sigma a} \mathbf {p} ^ {\sigma}\right) = \operatorname {m} \left(\frac {\mathrm {d p} ^ {\beta}}{\mathrm {d} \tau} + \mathrm {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {p} ^ {\sigma} \Gamma^ {\beta} _ {\sigma a}\right). \tag {1}
$$
In flat space time, one can always find a global coordinate system (Minkowski coordinates) in which all of the Christoffel symbols vanish. In that coordinate system, conservation of 4-momentum, $\frac{\mathrm{d}\mathbf{p}}{\mathrm{d}\tau} = 0$ , can be written as
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {p}} \mathbf {p} = 0 \tag {2}
$$
by Equation (1), but Equation (2) is a tensor equation so it must be the correct expression for momentum conservation in any frame. The momentum vector is proportional to the 4-velocity of particles with mass; for these particles $\mathbf{p} \cdot \mathbf{p} = -\mathfrak{m}^2$ , so the geodesics are timelike.
