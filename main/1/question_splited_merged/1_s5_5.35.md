Problem 5.35. For a perfect Maxwell-Boltzmann gas, find $\gamma(T)$ , the ratio of specific heats, as a function of temperature.

## Solution

Solution 5.35. From Problem 5.32,
$$
\gamma = \left(\mathrm {c} _ {\mathrm {v}} + \mathrm {k}\right) / \mathrm {c} _ {\mathrm {v}} = 1 + \frac {\mathrm {k}}{\mathrm {d U} / \mathrm {d T}}
$$
where, from Problem 5.34
$$
\mathrm {U} (\mathrm {T}) = \mathrm {m} \left\{\mathrm {K} _ {1} (\beta) / \mathrm {K} _ {2} (\beta) + 3 / \beta \right\}, \quad \beta = \mathrm {m} / \mathrm {k T}.
$$
This gives the formal answer. Note that for $\mathrm{kT} \ll \mathrm{m}$ , $\mathbf{U} = \mathbf{m} + 3\mathrm{kT} / 2$ , $\gamma = 5 / 3$ , and that for $\mathrm{kT} \gg \mathrm{m}$ , $\mathbf{U} = 3\mathrm{kT}$ and $\gamma = 4 / 3$ .
