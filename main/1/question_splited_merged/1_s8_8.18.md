Problem 8.18. It is desired to transport a vector $\mathbf{v}$ along a curve with tangent vector $\mathbf{u}$ . Which of the following are necessary for parallel transport? - for Fermi-Walker transport? - for Lie transport?: metric; affine connection; $\mathbf{u}(\mathbf{x})$ defined off the curve.

## Solution

Solution 8.18. The parallel transport law $\mathfrak{u}^{\alpha_{\mathbf{v}}\beta}_{;a} = 0$ requires only the affine connection $\Gamma^{\alpha}_{\beta \gamma}$ . The Fermi-Walker transport law
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {v} = \mathbf {u} (\mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {v}) - \mathbf {a} (\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {v}) \quad \mathbf {a} \equiv \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u}
$$
requires, in addition, a metric for the dot products. The Lie transport equation $\nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{v} - \nabla_{\mathbf{v}}\mathbf{u} = 0$ requires $\mathbf{u}$ to be a vector field defined off the curve, not just a tangent vector along the curve, because the gradient of $\mathbf{u}$ in the v-direction must be defined, and $\mathbf{v}$ is not in general along the curve. Although the formula above includes $\nabla$ 's, a connection is actually not needed, because the $\Gamma$ 's cancel out due to the antisymmetry of the equation. Obviously no metric is required for Lie differentiation.
