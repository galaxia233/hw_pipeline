10.4. In Euclidean 3-space show that the 3 Killing vectors describing rotations around the $x, y$ , and $z$ axes are linearly dependent at any given point but that no constant coefficient combination of them is zero. Show that the generators of the rotation group $O(3)$ are thus 2-surface forming, even though the group is 3-dimensional. Explain.

## Solution

Solution 10.4. The three Killing vectors describing rotations are just the angular momentum operators, $\mathbf{J}_{\mathbf{z}} = \mathbf{x}\partial_{\mathbf{y}} - \mathbf{y}\partial_{\mathbf{x}}$ , etc. (See Problem 10.1.) At a point $(\mathbf{x}_0,\mathbf{y}_0,\mathbf{z}_0)$
$$
\begin{array}{l} J _ {z} = (- y _ {0}, x _ {0}, 0) \\ \mathbf {J _ {y}} = (z _ {0}, 0, - \mathbf {x} _ {0}) \\ \mathrm {J} _ {\mathbf {x}} = \left(0, - z _ {0}, y _ {0}\right), \\ \end{array}
$$
so $\mathbf{J_y} = -(z_0 / y_0)\mathbf{J_z} - (x_0 / y_0)\mathbf{J_x}$ and the three vectors only span a 2-dimensional surface at the point.
Suppose, however, that for some constants $a$ and $b$
$$
a J _ {x} + b J _ {y} + J _ {z} = 0
$$
were true at every point, then we would have
$$
a \left[ J _ {x}, J _ {y} \right] + b \left[ J _ {y}, J _ {y} \right] + \left[ J _ {z}, J _ {y} \right] = 0.
$$
This requires $\mathbf{J}_{\mathbf{z}} \propto \mathbf{J}_{\mathbf{x}}$ , a result which is obviously false. Thus, over the whole sphere, $\mathbf{J}_{\mathbf{x}}, \mathbf{J}_{\mathbf{y}}, \mathbf{J}_{\mathbf{z}}$ span a 3-dimensional space. The explanation is that 2-spheres are 2-dimensional, but orientations of 2-spheres are 3-dimensional (e.g. the 3 Euler angles).
