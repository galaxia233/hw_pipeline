Problem 17.16 (f) Show that, for a wave of the form $\Phi = \exp(-\mathrm{i}\omega t + \mathrm{i}\mathrm{m}\phi)\mathrm{f}(\mathbf{r},\theta)$ , energy flows out of the hole if $0 < \omega / \mathfrak{m} < \mathfrak{a} / (2\mathbf{M}\mathbf{r}_{+})$ . Compare to Problem 17.15.

## Solution

(f) The stress-energy tensor for a scalar field is
$$
4 \pi \mathrm {T} _ {\alpha \beta} = \Phi_ {, (\alpha} \Phi_ {, \beta)} ^ {*} - \frac {1}{2} \mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} | \Phi_ {, \gamma} \Phi_ {, \gamma} |.
$$
(The complex conjugation is necessary because the representation of $\Phi$ is complex.) The energy flux vector is $\mathbf{J}_{\beta} = -\mathbf{T}_{\alpha}\beta \xi^{\alpha}$ , where $\pmb{\xi}$ is the time Killing vector $\partial/\partial t$ (see Problem 10.11). The energy flux into the horizon is found by integrating the radial component of $\mathbf{J}$ over the
2-surface $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_{+}$
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {E}}{\mathrm {d} t} = \int \mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {t}} ^ {\mathrm {r}} | \mathbf {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d} \theta \mathrm {d} \phi .
$$
Since
$$
\begin{array}{l} 4 \pi \mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {t}} ^ {\mathrm {r}} = \operatorname {R e} \left(\Phi_ {, \mathrm {t}} \Phi^ {*}, \mathrm {r}\right) = \operatorname {R e} \left(\Phi_ {, \mathrm {t}} \Phi^ {*} \frac {\mathrm {r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {a} ^ {2}}{\sum}\right) \\ = \omega (\omega - \mathrm {m} \omega_ {+}) S ^ {2} (\theta) \frac {2 \mathrm {M r} _ {+}}{\sum}, \\ \end{array}
$$
we have
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathrm {E}}{\mathrm {d} t} = \omega (\omega - \mathrm {m} \omega_ {+}) \frac {2 \mathrm {M r} _ {+}}{4 \pi} \int \mathrm {S} ^ {2} (\theta) \sin \theta \mathrm {d} \theta \mathrm {d} \phi
$$
and $\mathrm{dE} / \mathrm{dt}$ is negative, i.e. energy flows out of the horizon if $\omega -\mathfrak{m}\omega_{+} < 0$ i.e. if $0 <   \omega /\mathfrak{m} <   \omega_{+}$ in agreement with Problem 17.15.
