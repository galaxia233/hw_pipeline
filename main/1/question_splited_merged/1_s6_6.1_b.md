Problem 6.1.
(b) For an unaccelerated particle, show that the component of the 4-momentum $\mathbf{P_u}$ is constant, but that $\mathbf{P_v}$ is not.

## Solution

(b) Solving Equations (1) for du and dv gives
$$
\mathrm {d v} = \mathrm {d x} \cosh u - \mathrm {d t} \sinh u
$$
$$
\mathrm {d} u = v ^ {- 1} (\mathrm {d} t \cosh u - d x \sinh u)
$$
and thus for a particle of unit mass,
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {u}} = \mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {u u}} \mathbf {P} ^ {\mathrm {u}} = - \mathbf {v} ^ {2} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {u}}{\mathrm {d} \tau} = - \mathbf {v} \cosh \mathbf {u} \frac {\mathrm {d} t}{\mathrm {d} \tau} + \mathbf {v} \sinh \mathbf {u} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x}}{\mathrm {d} \tau} = - \mathbf {x} \frac {\mathrm {d} t}{\mathrm {d} \tau} + \mathbf {t} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x}}{\mathrm {d} \tau}.
$$
For an unaccelerated particle $\mathbf{x} = \text{const.} + \frac{\mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}}{\mathrm{d}t} t$ , and $\mathrm{dt} / \mathrm{d}\tau$ , $\mathrm{dx} / \mathrm{d}\tau$ are also both constant, so
$$
\mathrm {P _ {u}} = \text {c o n s t .} - \mathrm {t} \frac {\mathrm {d x}}{\mathrm {d t}} \frac {\mathrm {d t}}{\mathrm {d} r} + \mathrm {t} \frac {\mathrm {d x}}{\mathrm {d} r} = \text {c o n s t a n t .}
$$
Now for $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{v}}$ , use
$$
- \mathrm {\bf m} ^ {2} = \bf P \cdot \bf P = g ^ {v v} (P _ {v}) ^ {2} + g ^ {u u} (P _ {u}) ^ {2} = (P _ {v}) ^ {2} - (P _ {u}) ^ {2} / v ^ {2}.
$$
So, $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{v}}^{2} = \mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{u}}^{2} / \mathbf{v}^{2} - \mathbf{m}^{2}$ , which is not constant since in general $\mathbf{v}$ varies along the particle's trajectory. It turns out that whenever the metric coefficient does not depend on some coordinate (in this case $\mathbf{u}$ ), then
the covariant momentum of that coordinate (here $\mathbf{P_u}$ ) is conserved. (See Problem 7.13 or MTW, p. 651.)
