Problem 16.1. Find basis vectors (and a dual basis of 1-forms) for orthonormal tetrads in a spherical geometry. Take the legs of the tetrad to be in the $\mathbf{t}$ , $\mathbf{r}$ , $\theta$ , and $\phi$ directions of the isotropic coordinate system whose metric is
$$
d s ^ {2} = - e ^ {2 \Phi} d t ^ {2} + e ^ {2 \mu} (d r ^ {2} + r ^ {2} d \Omega^ {2}).
$$

## Solution

Solution 16.1. Since an observer's time axis points along his 4-velocity, if he is stationary in the $(t,r,\theta,\phi)$ coordinate system then his time axis must be along $\partial/\partial t$ (and normalized properly). Accordingly, take $\mathbf{e}_{\hat{\mathbf{o}}} = \mathbf{f}\partial/\partial t$ , where $\mathbf{f}$ is to be determined by normalization:
$$
- \mathbf {1} = \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {0}}} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {0}}} = \mathbf {f} ^ {2} \frac {\partial}{\partial t} \cdot \frac {\partial}{\partial t} = \mathbf {f} ^ {2} \mathbf {g} _ {0 0} = - \mathbf {f} ^ {2} \mathbf {e} ^ {2 \hat {\boldsymbol {\Phi}}}.
$$
Thus $\mathbf{f} = \mathbf{e}^{-\Phi}$ and
$$
\mathbf {e} _ {\hat {0}} = \mathbf {e} ^ {- \Phi} \frac {\partial}{\partial t}. \tag {1}
$$
The three directions, $\partial/\partial\theta$ , $\partial/\partial\phi$ , $\partial/\partial\mathbf{r}$ are all orthogonal to $\partial/\partial t$ and choosing basis vectors along them, with unit normalization (same technique as above) yields
$$
\mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {- \mu} \frac {\partial}{\partial \mathrm {r}}, \quad \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\theta}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {- \mu} \mathrm {r} ^ {- 1} \frac {\partial}{\partial \theta}, \quad \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\phi}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {- \mu} (\mathrm {r} \sin \theta) ^ {- 1} \frac {\partial}{\partial \phi}. \tag {2}
$$
The basis vectors in Equations (1) and (2) constitute an orthonormal proper reference frame for a stationary observer. Dual 1-forms, $\tilde{\omega}^{\hat{a}}$ , are found by the requirement
$$
<   \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {a}}, e _ {\hat {\beta}} > = \delta^ {a} \beta \tag {3}
$$
and are easily seen to be
$$
\tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {0}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {\phi} \widetilde {\mathrm {d t}}, \quad \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {r}}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {\mu} \widetilde {\mathrm {d r}}, \quad \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\theta}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {r} \widetilde {\mathrm {d} \theta}, \quad \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {r} \sin \theta \widetilde {\mathrm {d} \phi}. \tag {4}
$$
