Problem 7.18. Show that the scalar product of two vectors is not altered as they are both Fermi-Walker transported along a curve $\mathcal{C}$ .

## Solution

Solution 7.18. Let the two vectors be $\mathbf{x}$ and $\mathbf{y}$ . Then the Fermi-Walker transport law reads
$$
\begin{array}{l} \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {x} = (\mathbf {u} \otimes \mathbf {a} - \mathbf {a} \otimes \mathbf {u}) \cdot \mathbf {x} \\ \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {y} = (\mathbf {u} \otimes \mathbf {a} - \mathbf {a} \otimes \mathbf {u}) \cdot \mathbf {y}, \\ \end{array}
$$
where $\mathbf{u}$ is the tangent vector to the curve $\mathcal{C}$ and $\mathbf{a} = \nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u}$ . Using the product rule we evaluate the change in the dot product along the curve:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} (\mathbf {x} \cdot \mathbf {y}) = (\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {x}) \cdot \mathbf {y} + \mathbf {x} \cdot (\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {y}) \\ = (\mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {x}) (\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {y}) - (\mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {y}) (\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {x}) + (\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {x}) (\mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {y}) - (\mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {x}) (\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {y}) = 0. \\ \end{array}
$$
The scalar product is unaltered.
