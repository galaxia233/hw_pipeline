Problem 1.3. If two frames move with 3-velocities $\underline{\mathbf{v}}_1$ and $\underline{\mathbf{v}}_2$ , show that their relative velocity is given by
$$
v ^ {2} = \frac {\left(v _ {1} - v _ {2}\right) ^ {2} - \left(v _ {1} \times v _ {2}\right) ^ {2}}{\left(1 - v _ {1} \cdot v _ {2}\right) ^ {2}}.
$$

## Solution

Solution 1.3. Let $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$ be the 4-velocities of the two frames. In frame 1, $\mathbf{u}_1 = (1,0)$ , $\mathbf{u}_2 = (\gamma, \gamma \mathbf{v})$ where $\gamma = (1 - v^2)^{-\frac{1}{2}}$ . Since $\gamma = -\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2$ ,
we just have to evaluate this scalar in a general frame where $\mathbf{u}_1 = (\gamma_1, \gamma_1 \underline{\mathbf{v}}_1)$ , $\mathbf{u}_2 = (\gamma_2, \gamma_2 \underline{\mathbf{v}}_2)$ :
$$
\gamma = - \mathbf {u} _ {1} \cdot \mathbf {u} _ {2} = \gamma_ {1} \gamma_ {2} - \gamma_ {1} \gamma_ {2} \underset {\sim} {\mathrm {v}} _ {1} \cdot \underset {\sim} {\mathrm {v}} _ {2} = (1 - \mathrm {v} ^ {2}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}}
$$
$$
1 - v ^ {2} = \left(y _ {1} y _ {2}\right) ^ {- 2} / \left(1 - \underline {{v}} _ {1} \cdot \underline {{v}} _ {2}\right) ^ {2}
$$
$$
v ^ {2} = \frac {\left(1 - \underline {{v}} _ {1} \cdot \underline {{v}} _ {2}\right) ^ {2} - \left(1 - v _ {1} ^ {2}\right) \left(1 - v _ {2} ^ {2}\right)}{\left(1 - \underline {{v}} _ {1} \cdot \underline {{v}} _ {2}\right) ^ {2}} = \frac {\left(\underline {{v}} _ {1} - \underline {{v}} _ {2}\right) ^ {2} - \left(\underline {{v}} _ {1} \times \underline {{v}} _ {2}\right) ^ {2}}{\left(1 - \underline {{v}} _ {1} \cdot \underline {{v}} _ {2}\right) ^ {2}}.
$$
(The solution could also be verified by a painfully tedious application of Lorentz transformations.)
