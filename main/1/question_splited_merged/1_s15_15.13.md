Problem 15.13. Give the coordinate transformation from Schwarzschild coordinates, in which $\mathrm{ds}^2 = -\mathrm{e}^{2\phi}\mathrm{dt}^2 + \mathrm{e}^{2\Lambda}\mathrm{dr}^2 + \mathrm{r}^2\mathrm{d}\Omega^2$ to "isotropic coordinates", in which $\mathrm{ds}^2 = -\mathrm{e}^{2\phi}\mathrm{dt}^2 + \mathrm{e}^{2\mu}(\mathrm{d}\overline{\mathbf{r}}^2 + \overline{\mathbf{r}}^2\mathrm{d}\Omega^2)$ . Specialize to the vacuum Schwarzschild solution and construct coordinate diagrams showing the relation between $(\mathfrak{t}, \mathfrak{r})$ and $(\mathfrak{t}, \overline{\mathbf{r}})$ coordinates. Is the area $A$ of the surface $\overline{\mathbf{r}} =$ constant, $\mathfrak{t} =$ constant given by $A = 4\pi \overline{\mathbf{r}}^2$ ? Construct an embedding diagram (see MTW p. 613) for the spacelike hypersurface $t = 0$ , for $0 < \overline{\mathbf{r}} < \infty$.

## Solution

Solution 15.13. We clearly need a transformation such that
$$
r ^ {2} = e ^ {2 \mu - r ^ {2}} \tag {1a}
$$
$$
e ^ {2 \Lambda} d r ^ {2} = e ^ {2 \mu} d \overline {{r}} ^ {2}. \tag {1b}
$$
When these are combined, we get a differential equation for the transformation $\overline{\mathbf{r}} = \overline{\mathbf{r}}(\mathbf{r})$ in terms of the known function $\Lambda$ :
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \overline {{r}}}{\mathrm {r}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {\Lambda} \frac {\mathrm {d} r}{\mathrm {r}}.
$$
This can immediately be integrated, and our solution is
$$
\bar {\mathrm {r}} = \text {c o n s t a n t} \times \exp \left(\int_ {r} \frac {\mathrm {e} ^ {\Lambda}}{\mathrm {r}} \mathrm {d r}\right) \tag {2a}
$$
$$
\mathrm {e} ^ {2 \mu} = \mathrm {r} ^ {2} / \overline {{\mathrm {r}}} ^ {2}. \tag {2b}
$$
For the Schwarzschild metric $e^{\Lambda} = (1 - 2M / r)^{-\frac{1}{2}}$ and we have from Equation (2a)
$$
\overline {{r}} = \text {c o n s t a n t} \times \exp \left(\int \frac {\mathrm {d r}}{\left(r ^ {2} - 2 \mathrm {M r}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}\right)
$$
which gives
$$
\mathrm {r} = \bar {\mathrm {r}} (1 + \mathrm {M} / 2 \bar {\mathrm {r}}) ^ {2} \tag {3}
$$
if we choose the integration constant so that $\mathbf{r} \to \overline{\mathbf{r}}$ as $\mathbf{r} \to \infty$ . Equation (3) can also be expressed as
$$
\overline {{\mathbf {r}}} = \frac {1}{2} [ \mathbf {r} - \mathbf {M} \pm (\mathbf {r} (\mathbf {r} - 2 \mathbf {M})) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} ]. \tag {4}
$$
From Equation (2b) we have
$$
\mathrm {e} ^ {2 \mu} = (1 + \mathrm {M} / 2 \overline {{\mathrm {r}}}) ^ {4}. \tag {5}
$$
From this it follows that the area of a sphere with $r$ and $t$ constant is
$$
A = (1 + M / 2 \bar {r}) ^ {4} \bar {r} ^ {2} \int d \theta \sin \theta d \phi = 4 \pi \bar {r} ^ {2} (1 + M / 2 \bar {r}) ^ {4}. \tag {6}
$$
In constructing the embedding diagram note that (i) according to Equation (4) the mapping from $\mathbf{r}$ to $\overline{\mathbf{r}}$ is double valued and (ii) the coordinate $\overline{\mathbf{r}}$ only describes the region of the Schwarzschild geometry with $\mathbf{r} \geq 2\mathbf{M}$ (In Equation (4), $\overline{\mathbf{r}}$ is complex for $\mathbf{r} < 2\mathbf{M}$ .)
The embedding diagram for a surface of constant $t$ and $r$ requires a function $z(r)$ such that
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = \mathrm {d z} ^ {2} + \mathrm {d r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2} = \left[ 1 + (\mathrm {d z} / \mathrm {d r}) ^ {2} \right] \mathrm {d r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2} \\ = (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) ^ {- 1} \mathrm {d r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2}. \\ \end{array}
$$
The solution is
$$
z = \left[ 8 M (r - 2 M) \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}. \tag {7}
$$
This parabola is plotted in the accompanying figure. The embedding surface is the paraboloid generated by rotating this curve about the $r$ axis.
