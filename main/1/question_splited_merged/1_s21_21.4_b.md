Problem 21.4. Consider the action
$$
\mathbf {S} = (1 6 \pi) ^ {- 1} \int (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathsf {R d} ^ {4} \mathbf {x} + \int \mathsf {L} _ {\mathrm {m a t t e r}} (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathsf {d} ^ {4} \mathbf {x} ,
$$
where $\mathbf{R}$ is the Ricci scalar and $\mathbf{L}_{\mathrm{matter}}$ contains g's but no $\Gamma$ 's (so that $\Gamma$ 's are present only in $\mathbf{R}$ ).
(b) Now assume $\Gamma$ 's are Christoffel symbols used to define covariant derivatives in the usual way. Show that $\delta S = 0$ (where now $\delta \Gamma^{\alpha}{}_{\beta \nu}$ is not independent of $\delta g^{\alpha \beta}$ ) leads to the Einstein field equations.

## Solution

(b) Although $\Gamma_{\mu \nu}^{a}$ (now taken to be the Christoffel symbols formed from $\mathfrak{g}_{\alpha \beta}$ ) is not a tensor, $\delta \Gamma_{\mu \nu}^{a}$ is a tensor (Problem 8.26). Thus we can simplify the calculation of $\delta \mathbb{R}_{\mu \nu}$ by working in a local inertial frame, where the $\Gamma$ 's vanish. In this frame, Equation (2) gives
$$
\delta \mathbf {R} _ {\mu \nu} = \delta \Gamma_ {\mu \nu , a} ^ {a} - \delta \Gamma_ {\mu a, \nu} ^ {a} = \delta \Gamma_ {\mu \nu ; a} ^ {a} - \delta \Gamma_ {\mu a; \nu} ^ {a}. \tag {20}
$$
Since this is a tensor equation, it is valid in any coordinate system. The $\Gamma$ 's are Christoffel symbols, so $g_{\mu \nu; a} = 0$ and
$$
\begin{array}{l} (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \nu} \delta \mathsf {R} _ {\mu \nu} = (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \left[ (\mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \nu} \delta \Gamma_ {\mu \nu} ^ {a}); a - (\delta \Gamma_ {\mu a} ^ {a} \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \nu}); _ {\nu} \right] \\ = \left((- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} g ^ {\mu \nu} \delta \Gamma_ {\mu \nu} ^ {a}\right) _ {, a} - \left(\delta \Gamma_ {\mu a} ^ {a} g ^ {\mu \nu} (- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right), \\ \end{array}
$$
(where we have used Problem 7.7(g) in the second line). Since this is a perfect divergence, it does not contribute to $\delta S$ . The remaining terms in Equations (1) and (18) lead to Equation (19) as in part (a).
