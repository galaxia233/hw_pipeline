Problem 2.13 (b) Compute the proper time for the occupants of a rocket ship to travel the 30,000 light years from the Earth to the center of the galaxy. Assume they maintain an acceleration of $1\mathrm{g}$ for half the trip and decelerate at $1\mathrm{g}$ for the remaining half.

## Solution

(b) To travel halfway requires $\mathbf{x} = 15,000$ L.Y., or
$$
\tau \approx \cosh^ {- 1} (1 5, 0 0 0 + 1) \mathrm {y r} \approx 1 0. 3 \mathrm {y r}. \tag {10}
$$
The deceleration half of the trip is identical, so the total time is 20.6 years.
