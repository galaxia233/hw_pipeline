Problem 3.29. Show that if the antisymmetric tensor $\mathfrak{p}^{\alpha \beta}$ is a bivector (i.e. $\mathfrak{p}^{\alpha \beta} = \mathbf{A}^{[\alpha \mathbf{B}^{\beta}]}.$ ) then
$$
\mathsf {p} ^ {\alpha \beta} \mathsf {p} ^ {\gamma \delta} + \mathsf {p} ^ {\alpha \gamma} \mathsf {p} ^ {\delta \beta} + \mathsf {p} ^ {\alpha \delta} \mathsf {p} ^ {\beta \gamma} = 0
$$
(the Plücker relations).

## Solution

Solution 3.29. If $\mathfrak{p}^{\alpha \beta} = \mathbf{A}^{[a_{\mathbf{B}}\beta ]}$ , then
$$
\det {\left[ \begin{array}{l l l} \mathsf {p} ^ {\alpha \beta} & \mathsf {p} ^ {\alpha \gamma} & \mathsf {p} ^ {\alpha \delta} \\ \mathsf {A} ^ {\beta} & \mathsf {A} ^ {\alpha} & \mathsf {A} ^ {\delta} \\ \mathsf {B} ^ {\beta} & \mathsf {B} ^ {\gamma} & \mathsf {B} ^ {\delta} \end{array} \right]} = 0
$$
since the first row is a linear combination of the other two rows (first row $= -\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{A}^{\alpha}\times$ third row $+\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{B}^{\alpha}\times$ second row). Multiplying out the determinant gives the desired result.
