Problem 5.33. For a perfect Maxwell-Boltzmann gas, show that if $\gamma$ is approximately constant in some regime of interest, then $p = Kn^{\gamma}$ and $\rho = mn + Kn^{\gamma} / (\gamma - 1)$ under adiabatic conditions. (K = constant, m = mass of particles.)

## Solution

Solution 5.33. From Problem 5.32,
$$
\gamma = \Gamma_ {1} = \left. \frac {\partial \log p}{\partial \log n} \right| _ {s}
$$
so if $\gamma =$ constant,
$$
\mathbf {p} = \mathbf {K n} ^ {\gamma}.
$$
For adiabatic changes the first law of thermodynamics is
$$
\mathrm {d} \rho = \frac {\rho + \mathrm {p}}{\mathrm {n}} \mathrm {d n} = \left(\frac {\rho}{\mathrm {n}} + \mathrm {K n} ^ {\gamma - 1}\right) \mathrm {d n},
$$
or
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} n} \left(\frac {\rho}{n}\right) = K n ^ {\gamma - 2}
$$
so that the solution is
$$
\frac {\rho}{n} = \frac {K n ^ {\gamma - 1}}{\gamma - 1} + c o n s t a n t.
$$
But when $\mathfrak{n} \to 0$ , then $\rho / \mathfrak{n} \to \mathfrak{m}$ , so
$$
\rho = \operatorname {m n} + \frac {\operatorname {K n} ^ {\gamma}}{\gamma - 1}.
$$
