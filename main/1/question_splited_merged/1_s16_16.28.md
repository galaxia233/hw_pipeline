Problem 16.28. For spherically symmetric pressure free collapse show that the fall of a shell is governed by the mass interior to that shell in the same manner as the radial fall of a particle in the Schwarzschild geometry is governed by the Schwarzschild mass:
$$
\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathrm {r} / \mathrm {d} \tau^ {2} = - \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r} ^ {2}.
$$

## Solution

Solution 16.28. In Problem 16.25 we saw that we can choose $\Phi=0$ for pressure-free collapse. From $G_{R}^{R}=0$ (see Problem 9.20) with $\Phi=0$ we have then
$$
\begin{aligned}
0 & =2 \mathrm{r} \ddot{\mathrm{r}}+1-\mathrm{r}^{\prime 2} \mathrm{e}^{-2 \Lambda}+\dot{\mathrm{r}}^{2} \\
& =2 \mathrm{r} \ddot{\mathrm{r}}+1-\Gamma^{2}+\mathrm{U}^{2}
\end{aligned}
$$
and therefore (see Problem 16.26)
$$
\ddot{\mathbf{r}}=\frac{\mathrm{d}^{2} \mathbf{r}}{\mathrm{d} r^{2}}=-\frac{\mathbf{M}}{\mathbf{r}^{2}}.
$$
