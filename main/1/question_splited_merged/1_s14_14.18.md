Problem 14.18. Show that except in the case $\underline{\mathbf{E}}\cdot \underline{\mathbf{B}} = 0$ , the sourceless Maxwell equations $\mathbf{F}^{\beta \nu}_{;\nu} = 0$ follow from the requirements $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{T} = 0$ , where $\mathbf{T}$ is the electromagnetic stress-energy tensor, and from the fact that $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ is derived from a vector potential.

## Solution

Solution 14.18. For the electromagnetic stress-energy
$$
4 \pi \mathrm {T} ^ {\mu \nu} = - \left(\mathrm {F} _ {\alpha} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {F} ^ {\alpha \nu} + \frac {1}{4} \mathrm {g} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {F} _ {\alpha} \beta^ {\mathrm {F} ^ {\alpha} \beta}\right) \tag {1}
$$
$$
4 \pi \mathrm {T} ^ {\mu \nu} _ {; \nu} = - \left(\mathrm {F} _ {\alpha ; \nu} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {F} ^ {\alpha \nu} + \mathrm {F} _ {\alpha} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {F} ^ {\alpha \nu} _ {; \nu} + \frac {1}{2} \mathrm {F} _ {\alpha} \beta \mathrm {F} ^ {\alpha \beta ; \mu}\right) = 0. \tag {2}
$$
Now, the first and last terms above equal
$$
\begin{array}{l} - \mathbf {F} ^ {\alpha \beta} \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \tau} \left(\frac {1}{2} \mathbf {F} _ {\alpha \beta ; \tau} + \mathbf {F} _ {\tau \alpha ; \beta}\right) = - \frac {1}{2} \mathbf {F} ^ {\alpha \beta} \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \tau} \left(\mathbf {F} _ {\alpha \beta ; \tau} + \mathbf {F} _ {\tau \alpha ; \beta} - \mathbf {F} _ {\tau \beta ; \alpha}\right) \\ = - \frac {1}{2} F ^ {\alpha \beta} g ^ {\mu \tau} \left(F _ {\alpha \beta ; r} + F _ {r \alpha ; \beta} + F _ {\beta r; \alpha}\right) \\ \propto \mathrm {F} ^ {\alpha \beta} \mathrm {g} ^ {\mu r} \mathrm {F} _ {[ \alpha \beta ; r ]} = 0. \\ \end{array}
$$
If $\mathbf{F}$ is derivable from a potential $\mathbf{A}$ in the usual way, then $\mathbf{F}[\alpha \beta; \tau] = 0$ and we are left with
$$
0 = 4 \pi \mathrm {T} _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = - \mathrm {F} _ {a} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {F} _ {; \nu} ^ {a \nu}.
$$
This implies that $\mathbf{F}_{; \nu}^{a \nu} = 0$ except in the case that the determinant of the coefficients $\mathbf{F}_{\alpha}^{\mu}$ vanishes. But $\det (\mathbf{F}_{\alpha}^{\mu}) = -(\underline{\mathbf{E}} \cdot \underline{\mathbf{B}})^2$ .
