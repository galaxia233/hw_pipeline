Problem 16.19. Show that the surface of a rigidly rotating star, with angular velocity of rotation $\Omega$ as seen at $\infty$ , is given by
$$
\mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {t t}} + 2 \mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {t} \phi} \Omega + \mathbf {g} _ {\phi \phi} \Omega^ {2} = \text {c o n s t a n t}.
$$

## Solution

Solution 16.19. The surface is a surface of constant $\mathfrak{p}$ (that is $\mathfrak{p} = 0$ ). By Problem 16.18, it is a surface of constant $\rho$ , and also of constant $\mathfrak{u}^{\mathfrak{t}}$ , since
$$
\mathrm {d p} \propto \mathrm {d u} ^ {\mathrm {t}}.
$$
But, in the notation of Solution 16.17,
$$
\left(u ^ {t}\right) ^ {- 1} = \left| \boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \boldsymbol {\xi} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} = \left| g _ {t t} + 2 g _ {t} \phi \Omega + g _ {\phi} \phi \Omega^ {2} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}}
$$
so on the surface
$$
\mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {t t}} + 2 \mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {t} \phi} \boldsymbol {\Omega} + \mathbf {g} _ {\phi \phi} \boldsymbol {\Omega} ^ {2} = \mathrm {c o n s t a n t}.
$$
