15.18. A perfectly adiabatic gas with equation of state $p = \mathrm{Kn}^{\gamma}$ , $4/3 \leq \gamma \leq 5/3$ , $\gamma$ constant, accretes spherically onto a Schwarzschild black hole of mass $M$ . The speed of sound in the gas at radial infinity is $a_{\infty}$ . At what radius does the inward flow become supersonic? (Give answer only to leading term in $a_{\infty}/c$ .)

## Solution

Solution 15.18. The relativistic Bernoulli equation (Problem 14.7)
$$
u _ {0} = \left(\frac {n}{\rho + p}\right) \left(\frac {n _ {\infty}}{p _ {\infty} + \rho_ {\infty}}\right) ^ {- 1} \tag {1}
$$
can be expressed in terms of the fluid's proper radial velocity $\mathbf{v} = (1 - 2\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{r})\mathrm{d}\mathbf{r} / \mathrm{d}t$ , by using the relationship between $\mathbf{u}_0$ and $\mathrm{dr} / \mathrm{dt}$
$$
\mathrm {g} _ {0 0} \left(\mathrm {u} ^ {0}\right) ^ {2} + \mathrm {g} _ {\mathrm {r r}} \left(\mathrm {u} ^ {\mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {2} = - 1,
$$
to yield
$$
v ^ {2} = 1 - \frac {\left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r}\right) \left(\frac {\rho + p}{n}\right) ^ {2}}{\left(\frac {\rho_ {\infty} + p _ {\infty}}{n _ {\infty}}\right) ^ {2}}. \tag {2}
$$
One can now rest mass conservation $(\mathbf{u}^{\alpha}\mathbf{n}|\mathbf{g}|^{\frac{1}{2}})_{,\alpha} = 0$ to obtain (after again expressing $\mathbf{u}^{\mathbf{r}}$ in terms of $\mathbf{v}$ )
$$
\frac {\mathrm {v} \left(1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {n r} ^ {2}}{\left(1 - \mathrm {v} ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} = \text {c o n s t a n t} \equiv \frac {\dot {\mathrm {M}}}{4 \pi}. \tag {3}
$$
Incidentally, in this solution $\mathfrak{n}$ is a rest mass density, not a number density: $\mathfrak{n}_{\text{here}} \equiv \mathfrak{m}_{\mathfrak{p}} \mathfrak{n}_{\text{usual}}$ .
Now, we would like to express $n$ in terms of the sound speed $a$ (using $p = Kn^{\gamma}$ ; compare Solution 5.25):
$$
a ^ {2} = \frac {\mathrm {d p}}{\mathrm {d} \rho} = \frac {\gamma \mathrm {K n} ^ {\gamma - 1}}{1 + \gamma \mathrm {K n} ^ {\gamma - 1} / (\gamma - 1)}.
$$
This implies
$$
\gamma \mathrm {K n} ^ {\gamma - 1} = \frac {\mathrm {a} ^ {2}}{1 - \mathrm {a} ^ {2} / (\gamma - 1)} = \frac {\mathrm {a} ^ {2} (\rho + \mathrm {p})}{\mathrm {n}} \tag {4}
$$
or
$$
n = \left[ \frac {a ^ {2}}{\gamma K \left(1 - a ^ {2} / (\gamma - 1)\right)} \right] ^ {\frac {1}{\gamma} - 1}. \tag {5}
$$
For the purposes of finding a self-consistent solution for some constant M and some v(r), Equations (2), (3) and (5) can be thought of as defining M as a function of r and v. We can then find the radius at which the flow becomes supersonic by setting v = a and setting dM = 0. First we must express r in terms of a. Using Equations (3) and (4), (with v=a), we get
$$
r = \frac {2 M}{1 - \left(1 - a ^ {2} / (y - 1)\right) ^ {2} \left(1 - a ^ {2}\right) \tau_ {\infty}} \tag {6}
$$
where
$$
\tau_ {\infty} \equiv \left(\frac {\rho + \mathrm {p}}{\mathrm {n}}\right) _ {\infty}.
$$
Substituting Equations (6), (5) into Equation (3), we get
$$
\frac {\dot {\mathrm {M}}}{4 \pi} = \frac {4 \mathrm {M} ^ {2} \mathrm {a} \left(1 - \frac {\mathrm {a} ^ {2}}{\gamma - 1}\right) \left[ \frac {\mathrm {a} ^ {2}}{\gamma \mathrm {K} \left(1 - \mathrm {a} ^ {2} / (\gamma - 1)\right)} \right] ^ {\frac {1}{\gamma - 1}}}{\left[ 1 - \left(1 - \mathrm {a} ^ {2}\right) \left(1 - \mathrm {a} ^ {2} / (\gamma - 1)\right) ^ {2} r _ {\infty} \right] ^ {2}}. \tag {7}
$$
Now, we expand Equation (4)
$$
\tau_ {\infty} \approx 1 + \frac {a _ {\infty} ^ {2}}{\gamma - 1}
$$
and work to lowest order in a. From $d\log M / da = 0$ , we have
$$
a ^ {2} = \frac {2 a _ {\infty} ^ {2}}{5 - 3 \gamma}, \quad \gamma \neq 5 / 3 \tag {8a}
$$
$$
a ^ {2} = 2 \cdot 3 ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} a _ {\infty}, \quad \gamma = 5 / 3. \tag {8b}
$$
Using Equations (8) and Equation (6), we conclude finally that the radius at which the flow becomes supersonic is
$$
r _ {s} = \left(\frac {5 - 3 \gamma}{8}\right) \frac {2 M}{a _ {\infty} ^ {2}} \quad \gamma \neq 5 / 3 \tag {9a}
$$
$$
\mathrm {r} _ {\mathrm {s}} = \frac {3 ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {M}}{4 \mathrm {a} _ {\infty}} \quad \gamma = 5 / 3. \tag {9b}.
$$
