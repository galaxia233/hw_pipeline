Problem 8.11. Show that there exists no tensor with components constructed from the 10 metric coefficients $\mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta}$ and their 40 first derivatives $\mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta, \mu}$ - except $\mathbf{g}$ itself and products of it with itself, e.g. $\mathbf{g} \otimes \mathbf{g}$ .

## Solution

Solution 8.11. Suppose there were such a tensor. What would be its components at some arbitrary point in a freely falling local orthonormal frame? In this frame all the $\mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta, \mu}$ vanish (because all the $\Gamma_{\alpha \beta \mu}$ vanish), and $\mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta} = \eta_{\alpha \beta}$ . Thus the tensor must be some algebraic combination of $\eta_{\alpha \beta}$ , "zero," and possibly $\delta^{\alpha}{}_{\beta}$ and $\varepsilon^{a\beta\gamma\delta}$ . You can very quickly convince yourself that the only nontrivial one is $\eta \otimes \eta \otimes \dots \otimes \eta$ (the $\delta$ only renames indices, e.g. $\delta^{\alpha}{}_{\beta}\eta_{\alpha\gamma} = \eta_{\beta\gamma}$ and the $\varepsilon^{a\beta\gamma\delta}$ gives zero by its antisymmetries, or else just gets some of its indices lowered). Since we chose an arbitrary point, the conclusion holds at all points.
