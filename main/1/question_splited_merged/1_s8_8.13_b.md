Problem 8.13. The Lie derivative of a scalar function is defined to be the directional derivative: $\mathcal{L}_{\mathbf{x}}\mathbf{f} = \nabla_{\mathbf{x}}\mathbf{f}$ . For a vector field $\mathbf{y}$ , we define
Lie differentiation as $\mathcal{L}_{\mathbf{x}}\mathbf{y} \equiv [\mathbf{x},\mathbf{y}] = \nabla_{\mathbf{x}}\mathbf{y} - \nabla_{\mathbf{y}}\mathbf{x}$ . The Lie derivative obeys all the usual rules for a derivative operator and always gives a tensor of the same rank as the tensor differentiated.
(b) What is the Lie derivative of a tensor whose components are $\mathbf{T}^{\alpha}\beta$ ?

## Solution

(b) For simplicity, work in a coordinate basis:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {X}} \mathbf {T} = \mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {X}} (\mathbf {T} ^ {a} \mathbf {\beta} \mathbf {e} _ {a} \otimes \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\boldsymbol {\beta}}) \\ = \left(\mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {x}} \mathrm {T} ^ {\alpha} \beta\right) \mathbf {e} _ {\alpha} \otimes \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\beta} + \mathrm {T} ^ {\alpha} \beta \left(\mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {e} _ {\alpha}\right) \otimes \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\beta} + \mathrm {T} ^ {\alpha} \beta \mathbf {e} _ {\alpha} \otimes \left(\mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {x}} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\beta}\right) \\ = \mathrm {T} _ {\beta , \gamma} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {x} ^ {\gamma} \mathrm {e} _ {\alpha} \otimes \tilde {\omega} ^ {\beta} - \mathrm {T} _ {\beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {x} ^ {\gamma}, _ {\alpha} \mathrm {e} _ {\gamma} \otimes \tilde {\omega} ^ {\beta} + \mathrm {T} _ {\beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {x} ^ {\beta}, _ {\gamma} \mathrm {e} _ {\alpha} \otimes \tilde {\omega} ^ {\gamma} \\ = \left(\mathrm {T} _ {\beta , \gamma} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma} - \mathrm {T} _ {\beta , \mu} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {x} _ {, \mu} ^ {\alpha} + \mathrm {T} _ {\mu} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {x} _ {, \beta} ^ {\mu}\right) \mathbf {e} _ {\alpha} \otimes \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\beta}. \\ \end{array}
$$
This is often written
$$
\mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {x}} \mathbf {T} ^ {\alpha} _ {\beta} = \mathbf {T} _ {\beta , \gamma} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma} - \mathbf {T} _ {\beta , \mu} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {x} _ {, \mu} ^ {\alpha} + \mathbf {T} _ {\mu} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {x} _ {, \beta} ^ {\mu}
$$
and the commas can be replaced by semicolons as in part (a).
