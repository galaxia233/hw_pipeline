Problem 9.13. Two nearby geodesics have affine parameters such that nearby points on the two geodesics have very close values of affine parameter $\lambda$ . Let $u^{\alpha} \equiv dx^{\alpha} / d\lambda$ be the tangent to one of the geodesics, and let $\mathbf{n}$ be the differential vector connecting points of equal affine parameter on the two geodesics. Prove the equation of geodesic deviation
$$
\frac {\mathrm {D} ^ {2} \mathrm {n} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d} \lambda^ {2}} + \mathrm {R} ^ {\alpha} _ {\beta \gamma \delta} \mathrm {u} ^ {\beta} \mathrm {n} ^ {\gamma} \mathrm {u} ^ {\delta} = 0.
$$

## Solution

Solution 9.13. Let $\mathbf{x}^{\mu} = \mathbf{x}^{\mu}(\lambda, \mathfrak{n})$ be a family of geodesics with affine parameter $\lambda$ . The parameter $\mathfrak{n}$ labels the individual geodesics. The tangent to the geodesics is $\mathfrak{u}^a \equiv \partial \mathbf{x}^a / \partial \lambda$ and the differential vector which connects points of equal affine parameter on adjacent curves is
$$
\mathbf {n} ^ {\alpha} = \partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha} / \partial \mathbf {n}.
$$
The solution to this problem is most transparent in abstract notation:
Write
$$
\mathbf {u} = \partial / \partial \lambda , \quad \mathbf {n} = \partial / \partial \mathbf {n}.
$$
Note that $[\mathbf{u},\mathbf{n}] = 0$ since the partial derivatives commute. (The vanishing of this commutator in fact is the definition of $\mathbf{n}$ as the connecting vector, according to Problem 8.14.) Now use the Riemann operator (see Problem 9.12):
$$
\mathbf {R} (\mathbf {u}, \mathbf {n}) \mathbf {u} \equiv \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \nabla_ {\mathbf {n}} \mathbf {u} - \nabla_ {\mathbf {n}} \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u} - \nabla_ {[ \mathbf {u}, \mathbf {n} ]} \mathbf {u} = \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \nabla_ {\mathbf {n}} \mathbf {u} = \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {n} ,
$$
where the last equality follows from the vanishing of $[\mathbf{u},\mathbf{n}]$ . In components our result, according to Problem 9.12(b), reads
$$
\frac {\mathrm {D} ^ {2} \mathbf {\hat {n}} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d} \lambda^ {2}} = (\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {\hat {n}}) ^ {\alpha} = - \mathrm {R} _ {\beta \gamma \delta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {u} ^ {\beta} \mathbf {\hat {n}} ^ {\gamma} \mathbf {u} ^ {\delta} .
$$
