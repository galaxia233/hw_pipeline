Problem 3.7. $\mathbf{F}^{\alpha \beta}$ is antisymmetric on its two indices. Show that
$$
\mathrm {F} _ {\mu} ^ {\alpha}, \nu \mathrm {F} _ {\alpha} ^ {\nu} = - \mathrm {F} _ {\mu \alpha , \beta} \mathrm {F} ^ {\alpha \beta}.
$$

## Solution

Solution 3.7. Since $\eta^{\alpha \beta}$ is constant we have
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {F} _ {\mu , \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {F} _ {\alpha} ^ {\beta} = (\mathbf {F} _ {\mu \gamma} \eta^ {\gamma \alpha}) _ {, \beta} (\mathbf {F} ^ {\beta \sigma} \eta_ {\sigma \alpha}) \\ = \mathbf {F} _ {\mu \gamma , \beta} \mathbf {F} ^ {\beta \sigma} (\eta^ {\gamma \alpha} \eta_ {\sigma \alpha}) = \mathbf {F} _ {\mu \gamma , \beta} \mathbf {F} ^ {\beta \sigma} \delta_ {\sigma} ^ {\alpha} = \mathbf {F} _ {\mu \gamma , \beta} \mathbf {F} ^ {\beta \gamma} \\ = - \mathrm {F} _ {\mu a, \beta} \mathrm {F} ^ {\alpha \beta}, \\ \end{array}
$$
where the last equality follows from relabelling dummy indices, and by antisymmetry.
