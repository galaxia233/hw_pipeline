Problem 3.25. Let $\mathbf{F}$ be an antisymmetric second-rank tensor with components $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ . From $\mathbf{F}$ construct another second-rank, anti-symmetric tensor, $*\mathbf{F}$ , called the dual of $\mathbf{F}$ , as follows
$$
* \mathbf {F} = \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon^ {\mu \nu \alpha \beta} \mathbf {F} _ {\alpha \beta} \mathbf {e} _ {\mu} \otimes \mathbf {e} _ {\nu}.
$$
Show that $\mathbf{\Phi}(*\mathbf{F}) = -\mathbf{F}$

## Solution

Solution 3.25.
$$
* \mathrm {F} ^ {\mu \nu} = \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon^ {\mu \nu a \beta} \mathrm {F} _ {a} \beta \tag {1}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} * \mathrm {F} _ {\mu \nu} = \frac {1}{2} \eta_ {\mu \sigma} \eta_ {\nu \tau} \varepsilon^ {\sigma \tau \alpha \beta} \mathrm {F} _ {\alpha \beta} \equiv \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon_ {\mu \nu} ^ {\alpha \beta} \mathrm {F} _ {\alpha \beta} \\ = \frac {1}{2} \left(\varepsilon_ {\mu \nu \lambda \gamma} \eta^ {\alpha \lambda} \eta^ {\beta \gamma}\right) F _ {\alpha \beta} = \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon_ {\mu \nu \lambda \dot {\gamma}} \left(\eta^ {\alpha \lambda} \eta^ {\beta \gamma} F _ {\alpha \beta}\right) \\ = \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon_ {\mu \nu \alpha \beta} \mathbf {F} ^ {\alpha \beta} \tag {2} \\ \end{array}
$$
$$
* (* \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu}) = \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon^ {\mu \nu a \beta} (* \mathbf {F} _ {\alpha \beta}) = \frac {1}{4} \varepsilon^ {\mu \nu a \beta} \varepsilon_ {\alpha \beta \lambda \sigma} \mathbf {F} ^ {\lambda \sigma}. \tag {3}
$$
Now, $\varepsilon^{\mu \nu \alpha \beta} \varepsilon_{\alpha \beta \lambda \sigma} \equiv -2 \delta_{\lambda \sigma}^{\mu \nu}$ (this defines the symbol $\delta_{\lambda \sigma}^{\mu \nu}$ ). One should convince oneself that $\delta_{\lambda \sigma}^{\mu \nu}$ has the following properties:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \delta_ {\lambda} ^ {\mu \nu} = + 1 \quad \text {i f} \lambda = \mu , \sigma = \nu \\ = - 1 \text {i f} \lambda = \nu , \sigma = \mu \\ = 0 \text {o t h e r w i s e}. \\ \end{array}
$$
Thus
$$
\delta_ {\lambda \sigma} ^ {\mu \nu} = \delta_ {\lambda} ^ {\mu} \delta_ {\sigma} ^ {\nu} - \delta_ {\sigma} ^ {\mu} \delta_ {\lambda} ^ {\nu}
$$
and therefore
$$
* (\mathbf {\nabla} \ast \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu}) = - \frac {1}{2} \delta_ {\lambda \sigma} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {F} ^ {\lambda \sigma} = - \frac {1}{2} \left(\mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} - \mathbf {F} ^ {\nu \mu}\right) = - \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu}.
$$
