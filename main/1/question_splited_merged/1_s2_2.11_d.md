Problem 2.11. Consider the reaction $1 + 2 \rightarrow 3 + 4$ . The lab frame is defined to be the one in which $\underline{\mathbf{P}}_2 = 0$ . The C.M. frame is defined to be the one in which $\underline{\mathbf{P}}_1^{\mathbf{C.M.}} + \underline{\mathbf{P}}_2^{\mathbf{C.M.}} = 0$ . Show that:
(d) $\gamma_{\mathbf{C.M.}} = (\mathrm{E_1 + m_2}) / \mathrm{E}_{\mathrm{total}}^{\mathbf{C.M.}}$ (v.C.M. $\equiv$ velocity of C.M. in lab frame, and $\gamma_{\mathbf{C.M.}}\equiv (1 - \mathrm{v}^{2}_{\mathbf{C.M.}})^{-\frac{1}{2}}.$ )

## Solution

(d) If $\mathbf{u}$ is the 4-velocity of any observer, then it is easy to verify in that observer's frame (hence in all frames) that
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {(3)} \equiv \mathbf {P} + (\mathbf {P} \cdot \mathbf {u}) \mathbf {u}
$$
is the 3-momentum measured by that observer. Therefore $\mathfrak{u}_{\mathbf{C},\mathbf{M}}$ is defined by
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {\text {t o t a l}} + \left(\mathbf {P} _ {\text {t o t a l}} \cdot \mathbf {u} _ {\mathbf {C}. \mathbf {M}.}\right) \mathbf {u} _ {\mathbf {C}. \mathbf {M}.} = 0.
$$
Now if two observers have 4-velocities $\mathbf{u}_1$ and $\mathbf{u}_2$ , then $\gamma = -\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2$ is the Lorentz transformation factor between the two frames. (Proof: In rest-frame of 1, $\mathbf{u}_1 \cdot \mathbf{u}_2 = (1,0) \cdot (\gamma, \gamma_{\underline{\mathbf{v}}}) = -\gamma$ ). So $\gamma_{\mathbf{C.M.}} = -\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u}_{\mathbf{C.M.}}$ gives the transformation to the C.M. frame. Thus
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {t o t}} \cdot \mathbf {u} + (\mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {t o t a l}} \cdot \mathbf {u} _ {\mathbf {C . M .}}) \mathbf {u} _ {\mathbf {C . M .}} \cdot \mathbf {u} = 0 \\ - \mathrm {E} _ {\text {t o t a l}} + \mathrm {E} _ {\text {t o t a l}} ^ {\mathrm {C . M .}} \gamma_ {\mathrm {C . M .}} = 0 \\ \gamma_ {\mathbf {C}. \mathbf {M}.} = \mathrm {E} _ {\text {t o t a l}} / \mathrm {E} _ {\text {t o t}} ^ {\mathbf {C}. \mathbf {M}.}. \\ \end{array}
$$
In our case, $\mathbf{E}_{\mathrm{total}} = \mathbf{E}_1 + \mathfrak{m}_2$ , hence
$$
\gamma_ {\mathbf {C}. \mathbf {M}.} = (\mathrm {E} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}) / \mathrm {E} _ {\text {t o t a l}} ^ {\mathbf {C}. \mathbf {M}.}.
$$
