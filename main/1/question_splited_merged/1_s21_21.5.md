Problem 21.5. The Lagrangian density for a scalar field is
$\mathbf{L} = -(8\pi)^{-1}(\Phi_{;a}\Phi^{;a} + \mathfrak{m}^{2}\Phi^{2})$ . Find the equations of motion and the stress-energy tensor. Verify explicitly that the stress-energy tensor has vanishing divergence.

## Solution

Solution 21.5. Taking the variation of the action
$$
S = - (8 \pi) ^ {- 1} \int (\Phi_ {; a} \Phi^ {; a} + m ^ {2} \Phi^ {2}) (- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} d ^ {4} x
$$
givessus
$$
0 = \frac {\delta \mathbf {L}}{\delta \Phi} = \left[ 2 \mathfrak {m} ^ {2} \Phi - 2 (- \mathfrak {g}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} [ (- \mathfrak {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \Phi^ {, a} ] _ {, a} \right] (- \mathfrak {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}
$$
or
$$
\square \Phi - \mathrm {m} ^ {2} \Phi = 0. \tag {1}
$$
From Problem 21.3 the stress-energy is
$$
\begin{array}{l} T _ {\mu \nu} \equiv - 2 (- g) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \frac {\delta [ (- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} L ]}{\delta g ^ {\mu \nu}} \\ = \frac {1}{4 \pi} (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \left[ \Phi_ {, \mu} \Phi_ {, \nu} (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} - \frac {1}{2} (\Phi_ {, a} \Phi^ {, a} + \mathrm {m} ^ {2} \Phi^ {2}) (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu} \right] \\ = \frac {1}{4 \pi} \left[ \Phi_ {, \mu} \Phi_ {, \nu} - \frac {1}{2} g _ {\mu \nu} \left(\Phi_ {, a} \Phi^ {, a} + m ^ {2} \Phi^ {2}\right) \right] \tag {2} \\ \end{array}
$$
and hence its divergence is
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = \frac {1}{4 \pi} \left(\Phi_ {; \nu} ^ {, \mu} \Phi_ {; \nu} ^ {, \nu} + \Phi_ {; \nu} ^ {, \mu} \square \Phi - \Phi_ {, a} ^ {; \mu} \Phi_ {, a} ^ {, a} - \mathrm {m} ^ {2} \Phi \Phi_ {, a} ^ {, \mu}\right).
$$
For a scalar field $\Phi$ , we also have the relation
$$
\Phi_ {; \nu} ^ {\prime \mu} = \Phi_ {, \nu} ^ {i \mu}, \tag {4}
$$
thus Equation (3) becomes
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = \frac {\Phi^ {\prime} \mu}{4 \pi} (\square \Phi - \mathrm {m} ^ {2} \Phi) \tag {5}
$$
which, by Equation (1), vanishes.
