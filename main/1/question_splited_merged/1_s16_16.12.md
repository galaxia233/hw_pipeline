Problem 16.12. Solve the relativistic equations of stellar structure for a static, spherically symmetric star of uniform density. Show that the mass and radius of the star satisfy $\mathbf{R} / 2\mathbf{M} > 9 / 8$ . What is the smallest $\mathbf{R} / 2\mathbf{M}$ can be if the dominant energy condition (see Problem 13.7) holds?

## Solution

# CHAPTER 16: SOLUTIONS
$$
\frac {\mathrm {p}}{\rho_ {0}} = \frac {\left(1 - 2 \mathrm {M r} ^ {2} / \mathrm {R} ^ {3}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} - \left(1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {R}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{3 \left(1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {R}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} - \left(1 - 2 \mathrm {M r} ^ {2} / \mathrm {R} ^ {3}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \tag {7}
$$
The constant of integration has been determined by demanding $p = 0$ at $r = R$ . The quantity $\Phi$ is most easily found from the equation:
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \Phi}{\mathrm {d} p} = \frac {\mathrm {d} \Phi / \mathrm {d} r}{\mathrm {d} p / \mathrm {d} r} = - \frac {1}{\rho_ {0} + p}.
$$
Integrate to find
$$
\begin{array}{l} e ^ {\Phi} = \text {c o n s t a n t} / (\rho_ {0} + p) \\ = \frac {3}{2} (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {R}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} - \frac {1}{2} (1 - 2 \mathrm {M r} ^ {2} / \mathrm {R} ^ {3}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \tag {8} \\ \end{array}
$$
where Equation (7) has been used to fix the constant of integration so that
$$
e ^ {\Phi (r = R)} = (1 - 2 M / R) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}.
$$
(That is, so that the metric will match smoothly to the Schwarzschild metric outside the star.)
We thus have a 1-parameter family of stellar models, conveniently parametrized by the central pressure
$$
\frac {P _ {C}}{\rho_ {0}} = \frac {1 - (1 - 2 M / R) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{3 (1 - 2 M / R) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} - 1}. \tag {9}
$$
The central pressure becomes infinite when
$$
3 \left(1 - \frac {2 M}{R}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} - 1 = 0
$$
i.e. the limiting value of $\mathbf{R} / 2\mathbf{M}$ is $9 / 8$
The dominant energy condition demands $p_c < \rho_0$ , so that the right side of Equation (9) is $< 1$ . This implies $R / 2M > 4 / 3$ .
