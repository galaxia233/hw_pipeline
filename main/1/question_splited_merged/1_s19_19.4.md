Problem 19.4. Homogeneous, isotropic spatial hypersurfaces must (by spherical symmetry) have a line element of the form
$$
\mathrm {d} \sigma^ {2} = a ^ {2} \left[ f ^ {2} (r) d r ^ {2} + r ^ {2} d \Omega^ {2} \right], \quad a = c o n s t a n t.
$$
Show that $f^2(r)$ must have the form $(1 - \mathrm{kr}^2)^{-1}$ , where $k = 0, \pm 1$ .

## Solution

Solution 19.4. Homogeneity implies that the scalar curvature $\mathbf{R}$ of the hypersurface must be constant. $\mathbf{R}$ can be found by specializing the formulas of Problem 9.20, or from the formula
$$
R _ {i j} = \Gamma^ {k} _ {i j, k} - (\log | g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}) _ {, i j} + \Gamma^ {k} _ {i j} (\log | g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}) _ {, k} - \Gamma^ {k} _ {i m} \Gamma^ {m} _ {j k}
$$
where $|g|^{\frac{1}{2}} = a^3 \mathrm{fr}^2 \sin \theta$ and the $\Gamma$ 's are found from the Lagrangian for geodesics or as in Problem 7.6. This gives
$$
\begin{array}{l} R _ {\theta \theta} = \Gamma^ {r} _ {\theta \theta , r} - (\log (\sin \theta)), _ {\theta \theta} + \Gamma^ {r} _ {\theta \theta} (\log f r ^ {2}), _ {r} - \Gamma^ {\phi} _ {\theta \phi} \Gamma^ {\phi} _ {\theta \phi} - 2 \Gamma^ {\theta} _ {\theta r} \Gamma^ {r} _ {\theta \theta} \\ = - \left(\operatorname {r f} ^ {- 2}\right) _ {, \mathrm {r}} + \operatorname {c o s e c} ^ {2} \theta - \mathrm {r} ^ {- 1} \mathrm {f} ^ {- 3} \left(\mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {f}\right) _ {, \mathrm {r}} - \cot^ {2} \theta + 2 \mathrm {f} ^ {- 2} \\ = \left[ r ^ {2} \left(1 - f ^ {- 2}\right) \right] _ {, r} (2 r) ^ {- 1}. \\ \end{array}
$$
Since
$$
\mathbf {R} _ {\widehat {\theta} \widehat {\theta}} = \mathbf {R} _ {\theta \theta} (\mathbf {a} ^ {2} \mathbf {r} ^ {2}) ^ {- 1},
$$
and
$$
R _ {\hat {r} \hat {r}} = R _ {\hat {\phi} \hat {\phi}} = R _ {\hat {\theta} \hat {\theta}}
$$
by isotropy, we get
$$
\mathrm {R} = \mathrm {R} _ {\hat {\mathrm {r}} \hat {\mathrm {r}}} + \mathrm {R} \hat {\theta} \hat {\theta} + \mathrm {R} \hat {\phi} \hat {\phi} = 3 \left[ \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \left(1 - \mathrm {f} ^ {- 2}\right) \right], _ {\mathrm {r}} \left(2 \mathrm {a} ^ {2} \mathrm {r} ^ {3}\right) ^ {- 1} \equiv \mathrm {A}
$$
that is
$$
\mathbf {r} ^ {2} \left(1 - \mathrm {f} ^ {- 2}\right) = \mathbf {B r} ^ {4} + \mathbf {C}.
$$
Since $\mathbf{f} \to 1$ as $\mathbf{r} \to 0$ , we have $\mathbf{C} = 0$ . Thus
$$
f ^ {2} = \left(1 - B r ^ {2}\right) ^ {- 1}.
$$
If $\mathbf{B} \neq 0$ , we can scale $\mathbf{r}$ by letting $\mathbf{r}' = |\mathbf{B}|^{\frac{1}{2}}\mathbf{r}$ . The metric then takes the prescribed form with radial coordinate $\mathbf{r}'$ .
