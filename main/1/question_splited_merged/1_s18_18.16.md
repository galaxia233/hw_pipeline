Problem 18.16. Estimate the number of gravitons emitted in an asymmetric explosion of energy E.

## Solution

Solution 18.16. The characteristic quadrupole gravitational-wave power of a physical process is $(\mathrm{G} / \mathrm{c}^{5})|\ddot{\mathrm{Q}}|^{2}\sim (\mathrm{G} / \mathrm{c}^{5})\mathrm{M}^{2}\mathrm{v}^{4} / \mathrm{T}^{2}$ where M, v, T are relevant masses, velocities and times for the process. This can be conveniently written as
$$
\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {G W}} \sim \frac {\mathrm {G}}{\mathrm {c} ^ {5}} \left(\frac {\mathrm {M v} ^ {2}}{\mathrm {T}}\right) ^ {2} \sim \frac {\mathrm {P} ^ {2}}{3 \times 1 0 ^ {5 9} \mathrm {e r g s / s e c .}}.
$$
Here $\mathrm{P} \sim \mathrm{Mv}^2 / \mathrm{T}$ represents the characteristic "power" of internal energy flows.
For an explosion of energy E, characteristic time $\pmb{\tau}$ the "internal power flow" is $\mathbf{E} / \tau$ . If we assume that this internal power flow couples efficiently to gravitational waves, then in $c = G = 1$ units
$$
\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {G W}} \sim (\mathrm {E} / \tau) ^ {2}
$$
and
$$
\mathrm {E} _ {\mathrm {G W}} \sim \mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {G W}} \tau \sim \mathrm {E} ^ {2} / \tau .
$$
The typical graviton emerging from this explosion will have energy $\hbar \omega \sim \hbar / \tau$ so the number of gravitons emerging will be of order
$$
N \sim \frac {E ^ {2} / \tau}{\hbar / \tau} = \frac {E ^ {2}}{\hbar} \sim \left(\frac {E}{1 0 ^ {1 6} \mathrm {e r g s}}\right) ^ {2}.
$$
