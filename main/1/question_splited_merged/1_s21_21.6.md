Problem 21.6. The electromagnetic Lagrangian density is
$\mathbf{L} = -(16\pi)^{-1}\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}\mathbf{F}_{\mu \nu}$ where $\mathbf{F}_{\mu \nu} = \mathbf{A}_{\nu ;\mu} - \mathbf{A}_{\mu ;\nu}$ . Show that the Maxwell equations $\mathbf{F}^{\alpha \beta}$ ; $\beta = 0$ follow from setting to zero the variation of $\int \mathbf{L}(-\mathbf{g})^{\frac{1}{2}}\mathrm{d}^{4}\mathbf{x}$ with respect to $\mathbf{A}_{\mu}$ . Find the stress-energy from the prescription
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {\mu \nu} = - 2 \frac {\delta \mathrm {L}}{\delta \mathrm {g} ^ {\mu \nu}} + \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {L}.
$$
Show that an equivalent Lagrangian density is
$$
\mathbf {L} = - \frac {1}{1 6 \pi} \mathbf {F} _ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} - \frac {1}{4 \pi} \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {A} _ {\mu ; \nu}
$$
where $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ is antisymmetric and $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ and $\mathbf{A}_{\mu}$ must be varied independently.

## Solution

Solution 21.6. Varying the action integral gives us
$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\delta \left(\mathrm {L} [ - \mathrm {g} ] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right)}{\delta \mathrm {A} _ {\mu}} = - \frac {1}{4 \pi} \frac {\delta \left(\mathrm {A} _ {[ \mu ; \nu ]} \mathrm {A} ^ {[ \mu ; \nu ]} [ - \mathrm {g} ] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right)}{\delta \mathrm {A} _ {\mu}} = - \frac {1}{4 \pi} \frac {\delta \left(\mathrm {A} _ {[ \mu , \nu ]} \mathrm {A} ^ {[ \mu , \nu ]} [ - \mathrm {g} ] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right)}{\delta \mathrm {A} _ {\mu}} \\ = - \frac {1}{4 \pi} \cdot 2 \cdot 2 \cdot \frac {\partial}{\partial_ {\mathbf {x}} ^ {\mu}} \left(\mathrm {A} ^ {[ \mu , \nu ]} [ - \mathbf {g} ] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right) = - \frac {1}{2 \pi} \frac {\partial}{\partial_ {\mathbf {x}} ^ {\mu}} \left(\mathrm {F} ^ {\nu \mu} [ - \mathbf {g} ] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right) \\ = - \frac {\left[ - \mathrm {g} \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{2 \pi} \mathrm {F} _ {; \mu} ^ {\nu \mu}, \tag {1} \\ \end{array}
$$
where we have used Problem 7.7 (i).
The prescription for the stress-energy gives
$$
\begin{array}{l} T _ {\alpha \beta} \equiv - 2 \frac {\delta L}{\delta g ^ {\alpha \beta}} + L g _ {\alpha \beta} = \frac {1}{8 \pi} \frac {\delta \left(F _ {\mu \nu} F _ {\sigma \tau} g ^ {\mu \sigma} g ^ {\nu \tau}\right)}{\delta g ^ {\alpha \beta}} - \frac {1}{1 6 \pi} F _ {\mu \nu} F ^ {\mu \nu} g _ {\alpha \beta} \tag {2} \\ = \frac {1}{4 \pi} F _ {\alpha} ^ {\nu} F _ {\beta \nu} - \frac {1}{1 6 \pi} F _ {\mu \nu} F ^ {\mu \nu} g _ {\alpha} \beta . \\ \end{array}.
$$
Consider now the Lagrangian density
$$
\mathrm {L} = \left(- \frac {1}{1 6 \pi} \mathrm {F} _ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {F} ^ {\mu \nu} - \frac {1}{4 \pi} \mathrm {F} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {A} _ {\mu ; \nu}\right) (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}. \tag {3}
$$
Variation of L with respect to $\mathbf{A}_{\mu}$ yields
$$
\mathbf {F} _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = 0. \tag {4}
$$
Variation of L with respect to $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ yields
$$
\mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} = 2 \mathbf {A} ^ {[ \nu ; \mu ]}. \tag {5}.
$$
