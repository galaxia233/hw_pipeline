Problem 17.5. An observer in a circular orbit of circumference $2\pi r$ around a charged, spherical, black hole (a Reissner-Nordstrom black hole) of mass M and charge Q measures local electric and magnetic fields. What are their strengths and orientations?

## Solution

Solution 17.5. The Reissner-Nordström metric is
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = - \mathrm {A d t} ^ {2} + \mathrm {A} ^ {- 1} \mathrm {d r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} (\mathrm {d} \theta^ {2} + \sin^ {2} \theta \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2})
$$
where $\mathbf{A} \equiv 1 - 2\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{r} + \mathbf{Q}^2 / \mathbf{r}^2$ . We first find the Keplerian frequency $\Omega \equiv \frac{\mathrm{d}\phi}{\mathrm{d}t}$ of circular orbits, in the same manner as in Solution 17.4. Here
$$
\Gamma_ {\mathrm {r t t}} = - \frac {1}{2} \mathrm {g} _ { \mathrm {t t}, \mathrm {r}} = \frac {\mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} - \frac {\mathrm {Q} ^ {2}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {3}}
$$
$$
2 \Gamma_ {\mathrm {r} t \phi} = - \mathbf {g} _ { \mathrm {t} \phi , \mathrm {r}} = 0
$$
$$
\Gamma_ {r \phi \phi} = - \frac {1}{2} g _ {\phi \phi , r} = - r
$$
so the quadratic equation for $\Omega$ becomes
$$
\Omega^ {2} \mathrm {r} - \left(\frac {\mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} - \frac {\mathrm {Q} ^ {2}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {3}}\right) = 0
$$
and the orbital frequency is
$$
\Omega = \pm \left(\frac {M}{r ^ {3}} - \frac {Q ^ {2}}{r ^ {4}}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}.
$$
The proper velocity of the orbiting observer relative to a coordinate stationary observer is
$$
\hat {\mathrm {v}} = \frac {\mathrm {d} \hat {\phi}}{\mathrm {d} \hat {\mathrm {t}}} = \frac {\mathrm {r} \mathrm {d} \phi}{\mathrm {A} ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d t}} = \left(\frac {\mathrm {M r} - \mathrm {Q} ^ {2}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2} - 2 \mathrm {M r} + \mathrm {Q} ^ {2}}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}.
$$
(Carets indicate orthonormal components in the stationary frame.) In the stationary frame the local electromagnetic field has only the component
$$
E _ {\mathrm {r}} = \frac {Q}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}}.
$$
Lorentz transforming this with the standard relations for E-M fields gives, in the orbiting frame, all components zero except
$$
E _ {\mathrm {r}} = (1 - \hat {\mathrm {v}} ^ {2}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \frac {Q}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} = \frac {Q}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} \left(\frac {\mathrm {r} ^ {2} - 2 \mathrm {M r} + Q ^ {2}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2} - 3 \mathrm {M r} + 2 Q ^ {2}}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}
$$
$$
\mathbf {B} _ {\hat {\theta}} = (1 - \hat {\mathbf {v}} ^ {2}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \hat {\mathbf {v}} \frac {\mathrm {Q}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} = \frac {\mathrm {Q}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} \left(\frac {\mathrm {M r} - \mathrm {Q} ^ {2}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2} - 3 \mathrm {M r} + 2 \mathrm {Q} ^ {2}}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}.
$$
