Problem 12.1. A small satellite has a circular frequency $\omega$ in an orbit of radius $r$ about a central object of mass $m$ . From the known value of $\omega$ show that it is possible to determine neither $r$ nor $m$ individually, but only the effective "Kepler density" $3m / 4\pi r^3$ of the object as averaged over a sphere of the same radius as the orbit. Give the formula for $\omega^2$ in terms of this Kepler density.

## Solution

Solution 12.1. Equating gravitational acceleration $\mathbf{Gm} / \mathbf{r}^2$ to centripetal acceleration $\omega^2\mathbf{r}$ gives Kepler's law: $\omega = (\mathrm{Gm} / \mathrm{r}^{3})^{\frac{1}{2}}$ . The average density inside the sphere of radius $\mathbf{r}$ is $\overline{\rho} = 3\mathrm{m} / 4\pi \mathrm{r}^3$ , so $\omega = (4\pi \mathrm{G}\overline{\rho} /3)^{\frac{1}{2}}$ . This result means, for example, that a grain of sand would orbit a steel ball bearing at the ball bearings surface with about the same $90\mathrm{min}$ . period as a satellite orbiting just above the earth's surface.
