Problem 8.7. The components of the Maxwell tensor $\mathbf{F}_{\alpha \beta}$ can be regarded as the components of a 2-form $\mathbf{F}$ . Show that Maxwell's equations in vacuum can be written $\mathrm{d}\mathbf{F} = \mathbf{0}$ , $\mathrm{d}*\mathbf{F} = \mathbf{0}$ .

## Solution

Solution 8.7. The 2-form is $\mathbf{F} = \mathbf{F}_{\mu \nu}\widetilde{\mathbf{dx}}^{\mu}\wedge \widetilde{\mathbf{dx}}^{\nu}$ so that
$$
\mathbf {0} = \mathrm {d} \mathbf {F} = \mathbf {F} _ {\mu \nu , \lambda} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\lambda} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\mu} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\nu}
$$
gives us the Maxwell's equations $\mathbf{F}_{[\mu \nu ;\lambda]} = 0$ . Similarly $\mathbf{d}*\mathbf{F} = \mathbf{0}$ gives $*\mathbf{F}_{[\lambda \sigma ;\nu]} = 0$ , but this is equivalent to (see Problem 3.25)
$$
0 = \varepsilon^ {\mu \nu \lambda \sigma} * F _ {\lambda \sigma ; \nu} = 2 * * F ^ {\mu \nu} _ {; \nu} = - 2 F ^ {\mu \nu} _ {; \nu},
$$
and so we have the remaining Maxwell's equations $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}_{;\nu} = 0$
