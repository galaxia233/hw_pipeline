Problem 7.3. Consider the familiar metric space
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = \mathrm {d r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \theta^ {2}.
$$
(b) Use the results in (a) to get a first-order differential equation for $\mathbf{r}(\theta)$ . [That is: Eliminate $s$ as a parameter and replace by $\theta$ ].

## Solution

(b) From one of the first integrals above:
$$
\left(\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}{\mathrm {d} \theta} \frac {\mathrm {d} \theta}{\mathrm {d} \mathbf {s}}\right) ^ {2} + \mathbf {r} ^ {2} \left(\frac {\mathrm {d} \theta}{\mathrm {d} \mathbf {s}}\right) ^ {2} = 1.
$$
Now substitute $\mathrm{d}\theta /\mathrm{d}s = R_0 / \mathrm{r}^2$ ( $R_0 = \text{some constant}$ ):
$$
\left(\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}{\mathrm {d} \theta}\right) ^ {2} + \mathbf {r} ^ {2} = \mathbf {r} ^ {4} / \mathbf {R} _ {0} ^ {4}.
$$
