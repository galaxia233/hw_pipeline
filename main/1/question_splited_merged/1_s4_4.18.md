Problem 4.18. Write Ohm's law $\underline{\mathbf{J}} = \sigma \underline{\mathbf{E}}$ invariantly in terms of $\mathbf{J}^{\mu}$ , $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ , $\sigma$ and $\mathfrak{u}^{\mu}$ (the 4-velocity of the conducting element).

## Solution

Solution 4.18. If $\mathfrak{u}^{\mu}$ is the 4-velocity of the conducting element, then the $\underline{\mathbf{E}}$ field seen by charge carriers in the conducting element is $\mathbf{E}^{\mu} = \mathbf{F}_{\nu}^{\mu} \mathfrak{u}^{\nu}$ . A vector which reduces to $\underline{\mathbf{J}}$ in the rest frame of the charge carriers is $\mathbf{J}^{\mu} + \mathfrak{u}^{\mu} \mathbf{J}_{\nu} \mathfrak{u}^{\nu}$ . Ohm's equation can then be written as
$\mathbf{J}^{\mu} + \mathfrak{u}^{\mu}\mathbf{J}_{\nu}\mathfrak{u}^{\nu} = \sigma \mathbf{F}_{\nu}^{\mu}\mathfrak{u}^{\nu}$ . Since this is a tensor equation and is correct in the rest frame of the conducting element, it must be correct in all frames.
