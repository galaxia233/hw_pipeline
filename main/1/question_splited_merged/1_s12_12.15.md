Problem 12.15. New particles of mass $\mathfrak{m}_0$ are created which carry a new kind of charge, evidenced by a classical inverse-square force law. A container holds a perfect monatomic gas of these particles in thermal equilibrium. The total charge $Q$ in the container is measured by the average force on test particles outside, and it is observed to vary with temperature as $Q \propto Q_0(1 + 6kT / m_0)$ . What is the spin of the new force field?

## Solution

Solution 12.15. The geometrical object which tells about the charge is a scalar, vector, 2-tensor, etc. for spins 0, 1, 2, etc. For example, the charge density of a particle of rest-charge $\mathbf{q}_0$ moving along a worldline $\mathbf{x}^{\mu} = \mathbf{z}^{\mu}(\tau)$ with 4-velocity $\mathbf{u}$ is
$$
\rho \left(\mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma}\right) = q _ {0} \int \delta^ {4} \left[ \mathbf {x} ^ {\mu} - z ^ {\mu} (\tau) \right] d \tau \quad (s c a l a r)
$$
$$
J ^ {\mu} \left(\mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma}\right) = q _ {0} \int u ^ {\mu} \delta^ {4} \left[ \mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma} - z ^ {\sigma} (r) \right] d r \tag {1}
$$
$$
\mathrm {T} ^ {\mu \nu} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma}) = q _ {0} \int \mathrm {u} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {u} ^ {\nu} \delta^ {4} [ \mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma} - \mathbf {z} ^ {\sigma} (\tau) ] \mathrm {d} \tau \quad \text {(s p i n 2)}
$$
$$
\mathrm {T} ^ {\mu \nu \dots \rho} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma}) = q _ {0} \int u ^ {\mu} u ^ {\nu} \dots u ^ {\rho} \delta^ {4} [ \mathbf {x} ^ {\sigma} - z ^ {\sigma} (\tau) ] d \tau . \quad (\text {s p i n} \mathbf {s})
$$
The charge of a particle in a laboratory volume, measured by the "Coulomb" part of its interaction in the lab is
$$
\begin{array}{l} q = \int T ^ {0 0 \dots 0} d ^ {3} x (labframe) \\ = (- 1) ^ {s} \int T ^ {\alpha \beta \dots \gamma} u _ {\alpha} ^ {L A B} u _ {\beta} ^ {L A B} \dots u _ {\gamma} ^ {L A B} d ^ {3} x. (2) \\ \end{array}
$$
Substituting Equation (1) into Equation (2) gives the measured charge of a single particle
$$
\begin{array}{l} q = q _ {0} \int d ^ {3} x \int d \tau (- u \cdot u ^ {\mathrm {L A B}}) ^ {s} \delta^ {4} [ x ^ {\sigma} - z ^ {\sigma} (r) ] \\ = q _ {0} \int d ^ {3} x d t \left(\frac {d \tau}{d t}\right) (- u \cdot u ^ {\mathrm {L A B}}) ^ {s} \delta^ {4} [ x ^ {\sigma} - z ^ {\sigma} (\tau) ]. \\ \end{array}
$$
Integrating out the delta function, and using $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u}^{\mathrm{LAB}} = -\gamma$ and $\mathrm{d}\tau / \mathrm{d}t = 1 / \gamma$ , we get $q = q_0 \gamma^{s-1} \approx q_0 \left(1 + \frac{s-1}{2} v^2\right)$ for $v << c$ . Now for a perfect gas we have $<v^2> = 3kT / m_0$ . So when we sum the charges of all the particles in the gas we get
$$
\mathrm {Q} = \sum_ {\mathbf {n}} \mathbf {q} = \mathrm {n q} _ {0} \left[ 1 + \frac {\mathbf {s} - 1}{2} \left(\frac {3 \mathrm {k T}}{\mathbf {m} _ {0}}\right) \right],
$$
and from the temperature dependence given in the problem we now read off $\mathbf{s} = 5$ .
