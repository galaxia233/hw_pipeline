Problem 9.2. How many independent components does the Riemann tensor have in $\mathbf{n}$ dimensions?

## Solution

Solution 9.2. The antisymmetry of $(\alpha \beta)$ and $(\gamma \delta)$ in $\mathbf{R}_{\alpha \beta \gamma \delta}$ means that there are $\mathbf{M} = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{n}(\mathbf{n} - 1)$ ways of choosing nontrivial pairs $(\alpha \beta)$ and similarly $\mathbf{M}$ ways of nontrivially choosing $(\gamma \delta)$ . Since the tensor is symmetric with respect to interchange of the pairs $(\alpha \beta)$ and $(\gamma \delta)$ there are $\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{M}(\mathbf{M} + 1)$ independent ways of choosing $\alpha \beta \gamma \delta$ when the pair symmetries are considered.
The Riemann tensor also possesses the cyclic symmetry
$$
\mathrm {A} _ {\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} = \mathrm {R} _ {\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} + \mathrm {R} _ {\alpha \delta \beta \gamma} + \mathrm {R} _ {\alpha \gamma \delta \beta} = 0.
$$
The pair symmetries guarantee that $\mathbf{A}_{\alpha \beta \gamma \delta}$ is totally antisymmetric so that the constraint $\mathbf{A}_{\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} = 0$ is trivial unless $a, \beta, \gamma, \delta$ are all distinct. The number of added constraints is then the number of combinations of 4 objects which can be taken from $\mathfrak{n}$ objects $= \mathfrak{n}! / (\mathfrak{n} - 4)! 4!$ (This formula gives zero, and is therefore valid, if $\mathfrak{n} < 4$ in which case the permutation symmetry gives no additional constraints.) The number of independent components is then
$$
\frac {1}{2} \mathrm {M} (\mathrm {M} + 1) - \mathrm {n}! / (\mathrm {n} - 4)! 4! = \mathrm {n} ^ {2} (\mathrm {n} ^ {2} - 1) / 1 2.
$$
