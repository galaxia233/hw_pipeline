Problem 14.3. Derive the Euler equation $(\rho + p)\nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u} = -[\nabla p + (\nabla_{\mathbf{u}}p)\mathbf{u}]$ from $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}_{;\nu} = 0$ , and show that this equation has the correct Newtonian limit.

## Solution

Solution 14.3. For a perfect fluid the stress-energy tensor is $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu} = (\rho + p) \mathbf{u}^{\mu} \mathbf{u}^{\nu} + p \mathbf{g}^{\mu \nu}$ , thus the equations of motions read
$$
0 = \mathrm {T} _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = (\rho + \mathrm {p}) _ {; \nu} \mathrm {u} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {u} ^ {\nu} + (\rho + \mathrm {p}) \left(\mathrm {u} _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {u} ^ {\nu} + \mathrm {u} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {u} _ {; \nu} ^ {\nu}\right) + \mathrm {p} _ {, \nu} \mathrm {g} ^ {\mu \nu}.
$$
To get the Euler equation, project this equation perpendicular to $\mathbf{u}$ using the projection tensor
$$
\mathrm {P} _ {\alpha \beta} = \mathrm {u} _ {\alpha} \mathrm {u} _ {\beta} + \mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta}.
$$
The result is
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = \mathrm {P} _ {\alpha \mu} \mathrm {T} _ {\nu} ^ {\mu \nu} \\ = 0 + P _ {a \mu} (\rho + p) u ^ {\mu} _ {; \nu} u ^ {\nu} + 0 + p _ {, \nu} g ^ {\mu \nu} P _ {a \mu} \\ = (\rho + p) u _ {\alpha ; \nu} u ^ {\nu} + p, a + p, \nu u ^ {\nu} u _ {\alpha} \\ \end{array}
$$
which is the Euler equation.
To take the Newtonian limit (see Solution 14.8), write the Euler equation as
$$
\rho_ {0} (1 + \pi + p / \rho_ {0}) (u _ {j, \nu} u ^ {\nu} - \Gamma_ {\nu j} ^ {a} u _ {a} u ^ {\nu}) = - p _ {, j} - u _ {j} d p / d \tau .
$$
Set $\mathbf{u}^0 = 1$ , $\mathbf{u}_0 = -1$ , $\mathbf{u}_j = \mathbf{v}_j$ and get
$$
\rho_ {0} \left(\mathrm {d v} _ {\mathrm {j}} / \mathrm {d} \tau + \Gamma_ {0 \mathrm {j}} ^ {0}\right) \approx - \mathrm {p},
$$
But
$$
\Gamma_ {0 j} ^ {0} \approx - \Gamma_ {0 0 j} = - \frac {1}{2} g _ {0 0, j} \approx \phi_ {, j}.
$$
Thus
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {v} _ {\mathrm {j}}}{\mathrm {d} \tau} = - \phi_ {, \mathrm {j}} - \frac {1}{\rho_ {0}} \mathbf {p} _ {, \mathrm {j}}.
$$
