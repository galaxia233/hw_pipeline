Problem 1.10. Show that $\mathbf{A} = 3^{\frac{1}{2}}\mathbf{e}_{t} + 2^{\frac{1}{2}}\mathbf{e}_{x}$ is a unit timelike vector in special relativity. Show that the angle between $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{e}_{t}$ is not real.

## Solution

Solution 1.10. The length of $\mathbf{A}$ is $|\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{A}|^{\frac{1}{2}}$ and
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {A} \cdot \mathbf {A} = \left(3 ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {e} _ {t} + 2 ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {e} _ {\mathbf {x}}\right) \cdot \left(3 ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {e} _ {t} + 2 ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {e} _ { \mathbf {x}}\right) \\ = 3 \mathbf {e} _ {\mathrm {t}} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\mathrm {t}} + 2 \mathbf {e} _ {\mathrm {x}} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\mathrm {x}} + 2 \sqrt {6} \mathbf {e} _ {\mathrm {t}} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\mathrm {x}} \\ = - 3 + 2 + 0 = - 1. \\ \end{array}
$$
If $\theta$ is the angle between A and $\mathbf{e}_{\mathrm{t}}$ then
$$
\cos \theta = \frac {\mathbf {A} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {t}}{| \mathbf {A} \cdot \mathbf {A} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} | \mathbf {e} _ {t} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {t} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} = - 3 ^ {\frac {1}{2}}
$$
which is satisfied by no real $\theta$ .
