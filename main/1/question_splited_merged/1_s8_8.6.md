Problem 8.6. Consider a 2-form in $n$ -dimensional space:
$$
\alpha = f \left(x ^ {1}, x ^ {2}, \dots , x ^ {n}\right) \widetilde {\mathrm {d} x ^ {1}} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d} x ^ {2}}.
$$
Suppose for some region of space including $\mathbf{x}^1 = 0$ , that
$$
\mathrm {d} \alpha = 0.
$$
Construct the 1-form
$$
\widetilde {\boldsymbol {\beta}} = \left[ \mathrm {x} ^ {1} \int_ {0} ^ {1} \mathrm {f} (\xi \mathrm {x} ^ {1}, \mathrm {x} ^ {2}, \dots , \mathrm {x} ^ {\mathrm {n}}) \mathrm {d} \xi \right] \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {2}
$$
and show that $\alpha = \mathrm{d}\tilde{\beta}$

## Solution

Solution 8.6. Define
$$
\mathbf {g} \equiv \left[ x ^ {1} \int_ {0} ^ {1} f \left(\xi x ^ {1}, x ^ {2}, \dots x ^ {n}\right) d \xi \right],
$$
so that $\tilde{\pmb{\beta}} = \mathbf{g}\widetilde{\mathbf{dx}}^2$ and
$$
\mathrm {d} \widetilde {\boldsymbol {\beta}} = \mathrm {g} _ {, i} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {i} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {2} = \mathrm {g} _ {, 1} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {1} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {2} + \mathrm {g} _ {, 3} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {3} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {2} + \dots .
$$
Now
$$
\mathbf {g}, _ {1} = \left[ \int_ {0} ^ {1} f \left(\xi \mathbf {x} ^ {1}, \mathbf {x} ^ {2}, \dots \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {n}}\right) d \xi \right] + \left[ \mathbf {x} ^ {1} \int_ {0} ^ {1} \xi f ^ {\prime} \left(\xi \mathbf {x} ^ {1}, \mathbf {x} ^ {2}, \dots \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {n}}\right) d \xi \right]
$$
where $f'$ indicates a derivative with respect to the first argument. The second term above can be written as
$$
\begin{array}{l} \int_ {0} ^ {1} \mathrm {x} ^ {1} \xi \mathrm {f} ^ {\prime} (\xi \mathrm {x} ^ {1}, \mathrm {x} ^ {2} \dots) \mathrm {d} \xi = \int_ {0} ^ {1} \xi \frac {\partial}{\partial \xi} \mathrm {f} (\xi \mathrm {x} ^ {1}, \mathrm {x} ^ {2}, \dots) \mathrm {d} \xi \\ = \left. \xi \mathrm {f} (\xi \mathrm {x} ^ {1}, \mathrm {x} ^ {2}, \dots) \right| _ {0} ^ {1} - \int_ {0} ^ {1} \mathrm {f} (\xi \mathrm {x} ^ {1}, \mathrm {x} ^ {2}, \dots) \mathrm {d} \xi , \\ \end{array}
$$
where we have integrated by parts; we have then
$$
g _ {, 1} = \xi f (\xi x ^ {1}, x ^ {2}, \dots x ^ {n}) | _ {0} ^ {1} = f (x ^ {1}, x ^ {2}, \dots x ^ {n}).
$$
Next, $\mathbf{d}\alpha = 0$ tells us that
$$
\mathbf {d} \alpha = f _ {, 3} \mathbf {d x} ^ {3} \wedge \mathbf {d x} ^ {1} \wedge \mathbf {d x} ^ {2} + f _ {, 4} \mathbf {d x} ^ {4} \wedge \mathbf {d x} ^ {1} \wedge \mathbf {d x} ^ {2} + \dots = 0 ,
$$
which can only be true if $f_{,3} = f_{,4} = f_{,5} = \dots f_{,n} = 0$ , and hence
$$
\mathbf {g} _ {, 3} = \left[ \mathbf {x} ^ {1} \int_ {0} ^ {1} \mathrm {f} _ {, 3} \left(\xi \mathbf {x} ^ {1}, \mathbf {x} ^ {2}, \dots , \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {n}}\right) \mathrm {d} \xi \right] = 0.
$$
Similarly $\mathbf{g}_{,4} = \mathbf{g}_{,5} = \dots \mathbf{g}_{,n} = 0$ , so that finally
$$
\mathrm {d} \widetilde {\boldsymbol {\beta}} = \mathrm {g}, 1 \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {1} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {2} = \mathrm {f} \left(\mathrm {x} ^ {1}, \mathrm {x} ^ {2}, \dots \mathrm {x} ^ {\mathrm {n}}\right) \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {1} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {2} = \alpha .
$$
