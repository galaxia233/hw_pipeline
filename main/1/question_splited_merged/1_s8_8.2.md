Problem 8.2. The usual basis for polar coordinates, $\mathbf{e}_{\hat{\mathbf{r}}} = \mathbf{e}_{\mathbf{r}}$ , $\mathbf{e}_{\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}} = \mathbf{r}^{-1}\mathbf{e}_{\boldsymbol{\theta}}$ is not a coordinate basis. Consider the one-form basis $\tilde{\pmb{\omega}}^{\hat{\mathbf{i}}}$ dual to this basis
$$
<   \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {i}}}, \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {j}}} > = \delta_ {\mathbf {j}} ^ {\mathbf {i}}.
$$
Find the function $f$ such that $\tilde{\omega}^{\hat{\mathbf{r}}} = \widetilde{\mathbf{df}}$ and prove that there does not exist a function $g$ such that $\tilde{\omega}^{\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}} = \widetilde{\mathbf{dg}}$ . Do this without imposing any metric on the polar coordinates.

## Solution

Solution 8.2. It is easy to see that $\mathbf{f}$ is just the coordinate $\mathbf{r}$ , that is $\widetilde{\mathrm{df}} = \widetilde{\mathrm{dr}}$ , since
$$
\begin{array}{l} <   \widetilde {\mathrm {d r}}, \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} > = <   \widetilde {\mathrm {d r}}, \mathbf {e} _ {\mathbf {r}} > = 1 \\ <   \widetilde {\mathrm {d r}}, \mathbf {e} _ {\widehat {\theta}} > = \mathrm {r} ^ {- 1} <   \widetilde {\mathrm {d r}}, \mathbf {e} _ {\theta} > = 0. \\ \end{array}
$$
Now suppose there exists a g such that $\widetilde{\mathrm{dg}} = \tilde{\omega}^{\hat{\theta}}$ then
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = \langle \widetilde {\mathrm {d g}}, \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \rangle = \langle \widetilde {\mathrm {d g}}, \mathbf {e} _ {\mathbf {r}} \rangle = \partial \mathbf {g} / \partial \mathbf {r} \\ 1 = <   \widetilde {\mathrm {d g}}, \mathrm {e} _ {\hat {\theta}} > = \mathrm {r} ^ {- 1} <   \widetilde {\mathrm {d g}}, \mathrm {e} _ {\theta} > = \mathrm {r} ^ {- 1} \partial \mathrm {g} / \partial \theta . \\ \end{array}
$$
Clearly the conditions $\partial \mathbf{g} / \partial \mathbf{r} = 0$ and $\partial \mathbf{g} / \partial \theta = \mathbf{r}$ are incompatible.
