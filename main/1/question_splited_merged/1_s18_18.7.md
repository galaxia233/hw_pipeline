Problem 18.7. Two point masses $\mathfrak{m}_1$ and $\mathfrak{m}_2$ are in a Newtonian elliptical orbit with semimajor axis $a$ and eccentricity $e$ . Compute $da/dt$ and $de/dt$ due to gravitational radiation reaction. Show that the elliptical orbit tends to be circularized.

## Solution

Solution 18.7. In terms of the total orbital energy $\mathbf{E}$ and angular momentum $\mathbf{L}$ , the semimajor axis and eccentricity are
$$
a = - m _ {1} m _ {1} / 2 E \tag {1}
$$
$$
e ^ {2} = 1 + \frac {2 E L ^ {2} \left(m _ {1} + m _ {2}\right)}{m _ {1} ^ {3} m _ {2} ^ {3}} \tag {2}
$$
so that
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} a}{\mathrm {d} t} = \frac {m _ {1} m _ {2}}{2 E ^ {2}} \frac {\mathrm {d} E}{\mathrm {d} t} \tag {3}
$$
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {e}}{\mathrm {d} t} = \frac {\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}}{\mathrm {m} _ {1} ^ {3} \mathrm {m} _ {2} ^ {3} \mathrm {e}} \left(\mathrm {L} ^ {2} \frac {\mathrm {d E}}{\mathrm {d t}} + 2 \mathrm {E L} \frac {\mathrm {d L}}{\mathrm {d t}}\right). \tag {4}
$$
If the separation of the two particles is $\mathbf{r}$ , where
$$
r = \frac {a \left(1 - e ^ {2}\right)}{1 + e \cos \theta} \tag {5}
$$
(see figure), then
$$
\mathbf {r} _ {1} = \frac {\mathbf {m} _ {2}}{\mathbf {m} _ {1} + \mathbf {m} _ {2}} \mathbf {r}
$$
$$
\mathbf {r} _ {2} = \frac {\mathbf {m} _ {1}}{\mathbf {m} _ {1} + \mathbf {m} _ {2}} \mathbf {r}
$$
so the components of the quadrupole tensor are
$$
I _ {x x} = m _ {1} x _ {1} ^ {2} + m _ {2} x _ {2} ^ {2} = \frac {m _ {1} m _ {2}}{m _ {1} + m _ {2}} r ^ {2} \cos^ {2} \theta
$$
![](images/c10df870c1f14883a972caca37b0c38a825b71f25231dc036ce9a146443dfb8f.jpg)
$$
\mathbf {I} _ {\mathbf {y y}} = \frac {\mathbf {m _ {1}} \mathbf {m _ {2}}}{\mathbf {m _ {1}} + \mathbf {m _ {2}}} \mathbf {r} ^ {2} \sin^ {2} \theta
$$
$$
I _ {x y} = \frac {m _ {1} m _ {2}}{m _ {1} + m _ {2}} r ^ {2} \sin \theta \cos \theta \tag {6}
$$
$$
\mathbf {I} \equiv \mathbf {I _ {x x}} + \mathbf {I _ {y y}} = \frac {\mathbf {m _ {1}} \mathbf {m _ {2}}}{\mathbf {m _ {1}} + \mathbf {m _ {2}}} \mathbf {\Omega r ^ {2}} .
$$
According to the Newtonian equations of motion
$$
\dot {\theta} = \frac {\left[ \left(\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}\right) \mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right) \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} \tag {7}
$$
so Equation (5) gives
$$
\dot {\mathbf {r}} = \mathrm {e} \sin \theta \left[ \frac {\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}}{\mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right)} \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}. \tag {8}
$$
Now we compute the successive time derivatives of $\mathbf{I}_{ij}$ , using Equations (5), (7) and (8) to simplify the expressions. The results are
$$
\dot {\mathrm {I } _ {\mathbf {x x}}} = \frac {- 2 \mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2} \mathrm {r} \cos \theta \sin \theta}{[ (\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}) \mathrm {a} (1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}) ] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \tag {9}
$$
$$
\ddot {\mathrm {I } _ {\mathbf {x x}}} = \frac {- 2 \mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2}}{\mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right)} (\cos 2 \theta + \mathrm {e} \cos^ {3} \theta) \tag {10}
$$
$$
\dot {\mathrm {I } _ {\mathbf {x x}}} = \frac {2 \mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2}}{\mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right)} (2 \sin 2 \theta + 3 \mathrm {e} \cos^ {2} \theta \sin \theta) \dot {\theta} \tag {11}
$$
$$
\dot {\mathbf {I } _ {\mathbf {y y}}} = \frac {2 \mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2}}{\left[ \left(\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}\right) \mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right) \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \mathrm {r} (\sin \theta \cos \theta + \mathrm {e} \sin \theta) \tag {12}
$$
$$
\ddot {\mathrm {I } _ {\mathrm {y y}}} = \frac {2 \mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2}}{\mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right)} \left(\cos 2 \theta + \mathrm {e} \cos \theta + \mathrm {e} \cos^ {3} \theta + \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right) \tag {13}
$$
$$
\dot {\mathbf {I } _ {\mathbf {y y}}} = \frac {- 2 \mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2}}{\mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right)} (2 \sin 2 \theta + \mathrm {e} \sin \theta + 3 \mathrm {e} \cos^ {2} \theta \sin \theta) \dot {\theta} \tag {14}
$$
$$
\dot {\mathbf {I } _ {\mathbf {x y}}} = \frac {\mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2} \mathrm {r} \left(\cos^ {2} \theta - \sin^ {2} \theta + \mathrm {e} \cos \theta\right)}{\left[ \left(\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}\right) \mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right) \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \tag {15}
$$
$$
\ddot {\mathrm {I } _ {\mathrm {x y}}} = \frac {- 2 \mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2}}{\mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right)} (\sin 2 \theta + \mathrm {e} \sin \theta + \mathrm {e} \sin \theta \cos^ {2} \theta) \tag {16}
$$
$$
\dot {\mathrm {I } _ {\mathrm {x y}}} = \frac {- 2 \mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2}}{\mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right)} (2 \cos 2 \theta - \mathrm {e} \cos \theta + 3 \mathrm {e} \cos^ {3} \theta) \dot {\theta} \tag {17}
$$
$$
\cdots \mathrm {I} = \cdots \mathrm {I} _ {\mathbf {x x}} + \cdots \mathrm {I} _ {\mathbf {y y}} = \frac {- 2 \mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2} \mathrm {e} \sin \theta \dot {\theta}}{\mathrm {a} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right)}. \tag {18}
$$
The rate of loss of energy is then:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {E}}{\mathrm {d} t} = - \frac {1}{5} \overset {\dots} {\mathbf {I } _ {\mathbf {j k}}} ^ {\dots} \overset {\dots} {\mathbf {I } _ {\mathbf {j k}}} = - \frac {1}{5} \left(\overset {\dots} {\mathbf {I } _ {\mathbf {j k}}} ^ {\dots} \overset {\dots} {\mathbf {I } _ {\mathbf {j k}}} - \frac {1}{3} \overset {\dots} {\mathbf {I }} ^ {2}\right) \\ = - \frac {1}{5} \left(\stackrel {\dots} {\textbf {I } _ {\textbf {x x}}} ^ {2} + 2 \stackrel {\dots} {\textbf {I } _ {\textbf {x y}}} ^ {2} + \stackrel {\dots} {\textbf {I } _ {\textbf {y y}}} ^ {2} - \frac {1}{3} \stackrel {\dots} {\textbf {I }} ^ {2}\right) \\ = \frac {- 8 m _ {1} ^ {2} m _ {2} ^ {2}}{1 5 a ^ {2} (1 - e ^ {2}) ^ {2}} \left[ 1 2 (1 + e \cos \theta) ^ {2} + e ^ {2} \sin^ {2} \theta \right] \dot {\theta} ^ {2} \\ \end{array}
$$
where we have used Equations (9)-(18) and straightforward algebraic simplifications. Now we must average over an orbit. The period of an orbit is (Kepler's third law)
$$
\mathrm {T} = \int_ {0} ^ {2 \pi} \frac {1}{\dot {\theta}} \mathrm {d} \theta = \frac {2 \pi \mathrm {a} ^ {\frac {3}{2}}}{(\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}
$$
so that
$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\langle \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {E}}{\mathrm {d} t} \right\rangle = \frac {1}{\mathrm {T}} \int_ {0} ^ {\mathrm {T}} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {E}}{\mathrm {d} t} \mathrm {d} t = \frac {1}{\mathrm {T}} \int_ {0} ^ {2 \pi} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {E}}{\mathrm {d} t} \frac {1}{\dot {\theta}} \mathrm {d} \theta \\ = \frac {- 3 2}{5} \frac {\mathrm {m} _ {1} ^ {2} \mathrm {m} _ {2} ^ {2} \left(\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}\right)}{\mathrm {a} ^ {5} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right) ^ {7 / 2}} \left(1 + \frac {7 3}{2 4} \mathrm {e} ^ {2} + \frac {3 7}{9 6} \mathrm {e} ^ {4}\right). \tag {19} \\ \end{array}
$$
Similarly, the rate of loss of angular momentum is
$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {d L}{d t} = \frac {- 2}{5} \varepsilon_ {z i j} \ddot {\mathbf {I } _ {i k}} \dddot {\mathbf {I } _ {k j}} = \frac {- 2}{5} \varepsilon_ {z i j} \dddot {\mathbf {I } _ {i k}} \dddot {\mathbf {I } _ {k j}} \\ = \frac {- 2}{5} \left[ \overset {\prime} {I } _ {\mathbf {x y}} \left(\overset {\prime} {I } _ {\mathbf {y y}} - \overset {\prime} {I } _ {\mathbf {x x}}\right) + \overset {\prime} {I } _ {\mathbf {x y}} \left(\overset {\prime} {I } _ {\mathbf {x x}} - \overset {\prime} {I } _ {\mathbf {y y}}\right) \right] \\ = \frac {- 8}{5} \frac {m _ {1} ^ {2} m _ {2} ^ {2}}{a ^ {2} \left(1 - e ^ {2}\right) ^ {2}} [ 4 + 1 0 e \cos \theta + e ^ {2} (9 \cos^ {2} \theta - 1) \\ + \mathrm {e} ^ {3} (3 \cos^ {3} \theta - \cos \theta) ] \dot {\theta} \\ \end{array}
$$
which when time averaged gives us
$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\langle \frac {\mathrm {d} L}{\mathrm {d} t} \right\rangle = \frac {1}{T} \int_ {0} ^ {2 \pi} \frac {\mathrm {d} L}{\mathrm {d} t} \frac {1}{\dot {\theta}} \mathrm {d} \theta \\ = \frac {- 3 2}{5} \frac {\mathrm {m} _ {1} ^ {2} \mathrm {m} _ {2} ^ {2} \left(\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{\mathrm {a} ^ {7 / 2} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right) ^ {2}} \left(1 + \frac {7}{8} \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right). \tag {20} \\ \end{array}
$$
From Equations (3) and (4) we have, finally:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\langle \frac {\mathrm {d} a}{\mathrm {d} t} \right\rangle = \frac {2 a ^ {2}}{\mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2}} \left\langle \frac {\mathrm {d} E}{\mathrm {d} t} \right\rangle \\ = \frac {- 6 4}{5} \frac {\mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2} \left(\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}\right)}{\mathrm {a} ^ {3} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right) ^ {7 / 2}} \left(1 + \frac {7 3}{2 4} \mathrm {e} ^ {2} + \frac {3 7}{9 6} \mathrm {e} ^ {4}\right) \tag {21} \\ \end{array}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \left\langle \frac {d e}{d t} \right\rangle = \frac {m _ {1} + m _ {2}}{m _ {1} m _ {2} e} \left[ \frac {a (1 - e ^ {2})}{m _ {1} + m _ {2}} \left\langle \frac {d E}{d t} \right\rangle - \frac {\left(1 - e ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{a ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \left(m _ {1} + m _ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left\langle \frac {d L}{d t} \right\rangle \right] \\ = \frac {- 3 0 4}{1 5} \frac {\mathrm {m} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2} \left(\mathrm {m} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}\right) \mathrm {e}}{\mathrm {a} ^ {4} \left(1 - \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right) ^ {5 / 2}} \left(1 + \frac {1 2 1}{3 0 4} \mathrm {e} ^ {2}\right). \tag {22} \\ \end{array}
$$
From Equation (22) we see that $\mathrm{de} / \mathrm{dt}$ is negative, so the eccentricity decreases because of radiation reaction.
If we put $e = 0$ in Equation (21), we recover the results of Problem 18.6. For the integration of Equations (21) and (22) when $e \neq 0$ , see P. C. Peters, Phys. Rev. 136, 1224 (1964).
