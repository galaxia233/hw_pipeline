Problem 17.15. Use the theorem that the area of a black hole is nondecreasing (cf. Problem 17.14) to prove that a Kerr black hole amplifies (rather than absorbs) certain modes of an incident radiation field.

## Solution

Solution 17.15. The area A and “reduced area” $\tilde{\mathbf{A}}$ of a Kerr black hole are
$$
A \equiv 8 \pi \tilde {A} = 8 \pi \left[ M ^ {2} + \left(M ^ {4} - J ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \right] \tag {1}
$$
or equivalently,
$$
\tilde {\mathbf {A}} ^ {2} - 2 \tilde {\mathbf {A}} \mathbf {M} ^ {2} + \mathbf {J} ^ {2} = 0. \tag {2}
$$
Taking the first variation, we have
$$
\left(\tilde {\mathbf {A}} - \mathbf {M} ^ {2}\right) \delta \tilde {\mathbf {A}} = 2 \tilde {\mathbf {A}} \mathbf {M} \delta \mathbf {M} - \mathbf {J} \delta \mathbf {J}. \tag {3}
$$
The left-hand side is nonnegative by the second law. Let a particular wave mode have the $t$ and $\phi$ dependence $\exp(-\mathrm{i}\omega t + \mathrm{i}m\phi)$ . All scalar, electromagnetic and gravitational waves satisfy
$$
\delta \mathbf {M} = \frac {\omega}{\mathrm {m}} \delta \mathbf {J} \tag {4}
$$
so we have
$$
\left(2 \mathrm {A M} - \mathrm {J} \frac {\mathrm {m}}{\omega}\right) \delta \mathrm {M} \geq 0. \tag {5}
$$
For an amplified wave conservation of energy requires that the mass of the hole decrease, so we want $\delta \mathbf{M} < 0$ ; thus the term in parentheses in Equation (5) must be negative. This condition can be rewritten as
$$
\frac {2 \mathrm {M r} _ {+}}{\mathrm {a}} - \frac {\mathrm {m}}{\bar {\omega}} <   0 \tag {6}
$$
where $\mathbf{r}_{+}$ is the coordinate radius of the event horizon, or (if we take into account that $\mathfrak{m}$ and $\omega$ can have either sign) the condition is
$$
0 \leqslant \omega \leqslant \frac {\mathrm {m a}}{2 \mathrm {M r} _ {+}} \tag {7}
$$
where $\lesseqqgtr$ means "lies between." Note that $a / 2\mathrm{Mr}_{+}$ is just $\Omega$ the "angular velocity of the black hole" (see Problem 17.10).
