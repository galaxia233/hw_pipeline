Problem 11.5.
(b) In this case show that the angular momentum ("intrinsic angular momentum") can be found from the spin vector as
$$
\mathbf {J} _ {\mathbf {(C . M .)}} ^ {\alpha \beta} \equiv \mathsf {S} ^ {\alpha \beta} = - \varepsilon^ {\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} \mathsf {S} _ {\gamma} \mathsf {u} _ {\delta} \mathrm {.}
$$

## Solution

(b) From the definition of $S_{\delta}$ :
$$
- \varepsilon^ {\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} \mathbf {S} _ {\gamma} \mathbf {u} _ {\delta} = \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon^ {\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} \varepsilon_ {\gamma \mu \nu \sigma} \mathbf {J} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {u} ^ {\sigma} \mathbf {u} _ {\delta}.
$$
Now perform the summation over $\gamma$ [see Problems 3.27, 3.28]:
$$
- \frac {1}{2} \delta_ {\mu \nu \sigma} ^ {\alpha \beta \delta} \mathbf {J} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {u} ^ {\sigma} \mathbf {u} _ {\delta} = - \mathbf {J} ^ {\alpha \beta} \mathbf {u} ^ {\delta} \mathbf {u} _ {\delta} = \mathbf {J} ^ {\alpha \beta} \equiv \mathsf {S} ^ {\alpha \beta}.
$$
[Only the $\mathbf{J}^{\alpha \beta}$ terms are kept in the sum; by part (a) we cannot have a $\delta$ index on $\mathbf{J}$ due to the presence of $\mathfrak{u}_{\delta}$ .]
