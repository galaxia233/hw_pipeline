Problem 2.4. A particle of rest mass $\mathfrak{m}_1$ and velocity $\underline{\mathbf{v}}_1$ collides with a stationary particle of rest mass $\mathfrak{m}_2$ and is absorbed by it. Find the rest mass $\mathfrak{m}$ and velocity $\underline{\mathbf{v}}$ of the resultant compound system.

## Solution

Solution 2.4. If $\mathbf{p}$ is the 4-momentum of the resultant system, and $\gamma \equiv (1 - v_1^2)^{-\frac{1}{2}}$ , we have
$$
\mathbf {p} = \left(\mathrm {m} _ {1} \gamma , \mathrm {m} _ {1} \gamma \underline {{\mathrm {v}}} _ {1}\right) + \left(\mathrm {m} _ {2}, \underline {{0}}\right)
$$
and
$$
\mathbf {m} = \left(- \mathbf {p} \cdot \mathbf {p}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} = \left(\mathbf {m _ {1}} ^ {2} + \mathbf {m _ {2}} ^ {2} + 2 \gamma \mathbf {m _ {1}} \mathbf {m _ {2}}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}
$$
$$
\underline {{\mathbf {v}}} = \frac {\underline {{\mathbf {p}}}}{\underline {{\mathbf {E}}}} = \frac {\mathbf {m} _ {1} \gamma \underline {{\mathbf {v}}} _ {1}}{\mathbf {m} _ {1} \gamma + \mathbf {m} _ {2}} = \frac {\underline {{\mathbf {v}}} _ {1}}{1 + \mathbf {m} _ {2} / \mathbf {m} _ {1} \gamma}.
$$
