# Problem 8.12.
(b) Show that in an orthonormal frame, $\Gamma_{\alpha \beta \gamma}$ is antisymmetric on the first two indices.

## Solution

(b) In an orthonormal frame
$$
\begin{array}{l} \Gamma_ {\mu \alpha \beta} + \Gamma_ {\alpha \mu \beta} = \mathbf {e} _ {\mu} \cdot \nabla_ {\beta} \mathbf {e} _ {\alpha} + \mathbf {e} _ {\alpha} \cdot \nabla_ {\beta} \mathbf {e} _ {\mu} \\ = \nabla_ {\beta} \left(\mathbf {e} _ {\mu} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\alpha}\right) = \nabla_ {\beta} \left(\eta_ {\mu a}\right) = 0. \\ \end{array}
$$
