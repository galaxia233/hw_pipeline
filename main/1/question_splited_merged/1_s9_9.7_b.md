Problem 9.7. In a space of fewer than 4 dimensions simple expressions can be given for the Riemann tensor.
(b) Express the Riemann tensor for a 2-dimensional space in terms of the metric and the Ricci scalar.

## Solution

(b) In two dimensions the relations, due to symmetries, among the components of the Riemann tensor may be summarized in any coordinate system as
$$
R _ {\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} = (g _ {\alpha \gamma} g _ {\beta \delta} - g _ {\alpha \delta} g _ {\beta \gamma}) f
$$
but
$$
\mathbf {R} = \mathbf {R} _ {\alpha \beta} ^ {\alpha \beta} = (\mathbf {g} _ {\alpha} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {g} _ {\beta} ^ {\beta} - \mathbf {g} _ {\beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha} ^ {\beta}) \mathbf {f} = (4 - 2) \mathbf {f} = 2 \mathbf {f}.
$$
Thus, in any coordinate system
$$
\mathsf {R} _ {a \beta \gamma \delta} = \frac {1}{2} \left(\mathsf {g} _ {a \gamma} \mathsf {g} _ {\beta \delta} - \mathsf {g} _ {a \delta} \mathsf {g} _ {\beta \gamma}\right) \mathsf {R} .
$$
