Problem 7.24. Prove that any congruence of null curves that is hypersurface-orthogonal consists of null geodesics.

## Solution

Let $\mathbf{k}$ be the tangent vector to the congruence of null curves. Since $\mathbf{k}$ is hypersurface orthogonal,
$$
\mathbf {k} _ {\alpha} = \mathbf {h f} _ {, \alpha}
$$
for some scalar functions $f$ and $h$ (see Problem 7.23). Because $k$ is null,
$$
\mathbf {f} _ {, a} \mathbf {f} ^ {, a} = 0.
$$
From these two equations we have
$$
\begin{array}{l} k _ {\alpha ; \beta} k ^ {\beta} = (h, \beta^ {f}, a + h f, \alpha ; \beta) h f ^ {\prime} \beta \\ f _ {, \alpha ; \beta} f ^ {, \beta} = f _ {, \beta ; \alpha} f ^ {, \beta} = \frac {1}{2} (f _ {, \beta} f ^ {, \beta}); _ {\alpha} = 0 \\ \end{array}
$$
and thus
$$
\mathbf {k} _ {\alpha ; \beta} \mathbf {k} ^ {\beta} = (\mathrm {h}, \beta^ {\mathrm {f}}, \beta) \mathbf {k} _ {\alpha}
$$
so the geodesic equation $\nabla_{\mathbf{k}}\mathbf{k} \propto \mathbf{k}$ is satisfied. If $\mathbf{k}^{\alpha} = \mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}^{\alpha} / \mathrm{d}\lambda$ , we can introduce an affine parameter $\lambda' = \lambda'(\lambda)$ to write the equation in the standard form $\nabla_{\overline{\mathbf{k}}} \overline{\mathbf{k}} = 0$ where $\overline{\mathbf{k}}^{\alpha} = \mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}^{\alpha} / \mathrm{d}\lambda'$ .
