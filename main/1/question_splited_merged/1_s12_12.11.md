Problem 12.11. Show that the Newtonian equation of motion of a test particle in a Newtonian gravitational potential $\Phi$ can be written as a geodesic equation in 4-dimensional spacetime. Compute the Christoffel symbols and the Riemann tensor, and show that they are not derivable from a metric.

## Solution

Solution 12.11. The preferred coordinates of Newtonian theory are "universal time" $t$ and Galilean space coordinates $x^j$ . The equation of motion
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {x} ^ { \mathrm {j}}}{\mathrm {d t} ^ {2}} = - \frac {\partial \Phi}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ { \mathrm {j}}}
$$
can be written in 4-dimensional form:
$$
\frac {d ^ {2} t}{d \lambda^ {2}} = 0
$$
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {x} ^ { \mathrm {j}}}{\mathrm {d} \lambda^ {2}} + \frac {\partial \Phi}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ { \mathrm {j}}} \bigg (\frac {\mathrm {d t}}{\mathrm {d} \lambda} \bigg) ^ {2} = 0.
$$
From this we read off the Christoffel symbols:
$$
\Gamma_ {0 0} ^ {j} = \frac {\partial \Phi}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {j}}; \quad \text {a l l o t h e r} \Gamma_ {\beta \gamma} ^ { \alpha} \text {v a n i s h}.
$$
The standard formula for the components of the Riemann tensor gives
$$
R _ {0 k 0} ^ {j} = - R _ {0 0 k} ^ {j} = \frac {\partial^ {2} \Phi}{\partial x ^ {j} \partial x ^ {k}}; \quad \text {a l l o t h e r} \quad R _ { \beta \gamma \delta} ^ { \alpha} \text {v a n i s h}.
$$
Suppose these were derivable from a metric, then we would have
$$
R _ {j 0 k 0} = g _ {j \alpha} R _ {0 k 0} ^ { \alpha} = g _ {j m} \frac {\partial^ {2} \Phi}{\partial x ^ {m} \partial x ^ {k}},
$$
but
$$
\mathrm {R} _ {0 \mathrm {j k 0}} = \mathrm {g} _ {0 \alpha} \mathrm {R} _ { \mathrm {j k 0}} ^ { \alpha} = 0 \neq - \mathrm {R} _ { \mathrm {j 0 k 0}}.
$$
This violates the antisymmetry property $\mathbf{R}_{a\beta \gamma \delta}$ has if it is derived from a metric.
