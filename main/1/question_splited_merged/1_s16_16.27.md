Problem 16.27. In a collapsing star made of perfect fluid show that once a mass shell at comoving radius $\mathbf{R}$ has collapsed far enough so that $2\mathfrak{m}(\mathbf{R},\mathfrak{t}) / \mathfrak{r}(\mathbf{R},\mathfrak{t}) > 1$ , the mass shell will collapse to $\mathbf{r} = 0$ in a finite proper time.

## Solution

Solution 16.27. From Problem 16.26 we have
$$
\mathrm {d r} / \mathrm{d} \tau=U \tag {1}
$$
$$
\mathrm {dm} / \mathrm{d} \tau=-4 \pi \mathrm{r}^{2} \mathrm{pU} \tag {2}
$$
$$
U=\frac{d r}{d r}=\pm\left[\Gamma^{2}+\left(\frac{2 m}{r}-1\right)\right]^{\frac{1}{2}}. \tag {3}
$$
The first two equations show that $\mathrm{dr} / \mathrm{d}\tau$ and $\mathrm{dm} / \mathrm{d}\tau$ have opposite signs, so for the shell to pass from $2\mathrm{m} / \mathrm{r}<1$ to $2\mathrm{m} / \mathrm{r}>1$ it must be that
$$
\dot{\mathbf{r}} \leq 0, \quad \dot{\mathbf{m}} \geq 0
$$
upon passage through $\mathbf{r}=2\mathbf{m}$ . From Equation (3) then
$$
\frac{\mathrm{dr}}{\mathrm{d} \tau}=-\left[\Gamma^{2}+\left(\frac{2 \mathrm{m}}{\mathrm{r}}-1\right)\right]^{\frac{1}{2}}.
$$
The radius therefore will continue to fall as long as $\Gamma^2+\left(\frac{2 \mathfrak{m}}{\mathfrak{r}}-1\right)>0$ , but as long as the radius is decreasing, the mass must be increasing and the factor $\frac{2 \mathfrak{m}}{\mathfrak{r}}-1$ continues to increase, so the collapse cannot stop.
Suppose the shell has reached the point $2 \mathfrak{m} / \mathfrak{r}-1=\varepsilon>0$ . Since this factor continues to increase, then
$$
\mathrm{dr} / \mathrm{d} \tau \leq \varepsilon^{\frac{1}{2}}
$$
and the shell will reach $r=0$ in a proper time $\leq 2m / \varepsilon^{\frac{1}{2}}$ . [This solution is due to J. M. Bardeen.]
