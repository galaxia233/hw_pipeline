Problem 7.11. An affine parameter $\lambda$ is one for which the equation of geodesic motion has the form
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d} \lambda^ {2}} + \Gamma_ {\beta \gamma} ^ {\alpha} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\beta}}{\mathrm {d} \lambda} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma}}{\mathrm {d} \lambda} = 0.
$$
Show that all affine parameters are related by linear transformations with constant coefficients.

## Solution

Solution 7.11. Define a new curve parametrization by the functional relationship $\mathbf{s} = \mathbf{f}(\lambda)$ . Then derivatives are related by
$$
\frac {d}{d \lambda} = f ^ {\prime} \frac {d}{d s} \quad \frac {d ^ {2}}{d \lambda^ {2}} = f ^ {\prime \prime} \frac {d}{d s} + f ^ {\prime 2} \frac {d ^ {2}}{d s ^ {2}}, \tag {1}
$$
where $\mathbf{f}' \equiv \frac{\mathrm{df}}{\mathrm{d}\lambda}$ . With the new parametrization then the geodesic equation becomes
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d s} ^ {2}} + \frac {\mathrm {f} ^ {\prime \prime}}{\mathrm {f} ^ {\prime 2}} \frac {\mathrm {d x} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d s}} + \Gamma^ {\alpha} _ {\beta \gamma} \frac {\mathrm {d x} ^ {\beta}}{\mathrm {d s}} \frac {\mathrm {d x} ^ {\gamma}}{\mathrm {d s}} = 0. \tag {2}
$$
Equation (2) is in the "standard form" (s an affine parametrization) if the second term vanishes. For s to be an affine parameter, f" must vanish i.e. s and $\lambda$ must be linearly related.
