Problem 17.13. Show that the surface area of the horizon of a Kerr-Newman black hole (area of surface $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_{+}$ , $t = \text{constant}$ , in Boyer-Lindquist coordinates) is $4\pi \left[\left[\mathbf{M} + (\mathbf{M}^{2} - \mathbf{Q}^{2} - \mathbf{a}^{2})^{\frac{1}{2}}\right]^{2} + \mathbf{a}^{2}\right]$ .

## Solution

Solution 17.13. At the outer horizon $(\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_{+})$ the metric with $\mathrm{dt} = \mathrm{dr} = 0$ becomes
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = \left(\mathrm {r} _ { + } ^ {2} + \mathrm {a} ^ {2} \cos^ {2} \theta\right) \mathrm {d} \theta^ {2} + \frac {\left(\mathrm {r} _ { + } ^ {2} + \mathrm {a} ^ {2}\right) ^ {2} \sin^ {2} \theta \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2}}{\left(\mathrm {r} _ { + } ^ {2} + \mathrm {a} ^ {2} \cos^ {2} \theta\right)} .
$$
The area of the horizon is then
$$
A = \iint g ^ { \frac {1}{2}} d \theta d \phi = \iint \left(r _ { + } ^ {2} + a ^ {2}\right) \sin \theta d \theta d \phi = 4 \pi \left(r _ { + } ^ {2} + a ^ {2}\right).
$$
Since $\mathbf{r}_{+}$ is the larger solution of $\Delta = 0$ we have $\mathbf{r}_{+} = \mathbf{M} + (\mathbf{M}^{2} + \mathbf{Q}^{2} - \mathbf{a}^{2})^{\frac{1}{2}}$ from which the result follows.
