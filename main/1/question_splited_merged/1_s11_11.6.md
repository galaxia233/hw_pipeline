Problem 11.6. Two bodies A and B have momenta $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{A}}$ and $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{B}}$ and spin $\mathbf{S}_{\mathbf{A}}$ and $\mathbf{S}_{\mathbf{B}}$ ; their centers of mass are on a collision course. After
colliding they stick together to form a composite body C (spin $\mathbf{S}_{\mathbf{C}}$ ). Calculate $\mathbf{S}_{\mathbf{C}}$ in terms of $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{A}}, \mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{B}}, \mathbf{S}_{\mathbf{A}}$ and $\mathbf{S}_{\mathbf{B}}$ .

## Solution

Solution 11.6. First we calculate the total angular momentum. We work in the center-of-momentum frame and take as our origin 0 the event at which the center of mass of A and that of B would collide. About this origin both systems A and B have only intrinsic angular momentum (see Problem 11.5), so
$$
\mathbf {J} _ {\mathbf {A} + \mathbf {B}} ^ {\alpha \beta} (\mathbf {0}) = \mathbf {S} _ {\mathbf {A}} ^ {\alpha \beta} + \mathbf {S} _ {\mathbf {B}} ^ {\alpha \beta} = - \varepsilon^ {\alpha \beta \mu \nu} (\mathbf {S} _ {\mathbf {A} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {A} ^ {\nu}} + \mathbf {S} _ {\mathbf {B} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {B} ^ {\nu}})
$$
and by conservation of angular momentum this is equal to $\int_{\mathbf{C}}^{\alpha \beta} (\mathbf{0}) \, \mathrm{d}\mathbf{C}$ . We can now use this result in the definition of the spin vector to find
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {\Delta} _ {\mathbf {C} ^ {\sigma}} = - \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon_ {\sigma a \beta \lambda} \mathbf {\Delta} _ {\mathbf {C}} ^ {a \beta} \mathbf {\Delta} _ {\mathbf {C}} ^ {\mathbf {u} ^ {\lambda}} \\ = \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon_ {\sigma \alpha \beta \lambda} \varepsilon^ {\alpha \beta \mu \nu} _ {\mathbf {A} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {A} ^ {\nu}} (\mathbf {S} _ {\mu} \mathbf {u} _ {\nu} + \mathbf {S} _ {\mu} \mathbf {\Gamma} _ {\mathbf {B}} \mathbf {u} _ {\nu}) \mathbf {\Gamma} _ {\mathbf {C}} ^ {\lambda}. \\ \end{array}
$$
According to Problem 3.28 (see also Problem 3.27)
$$
\varepsilon_ {\sigma \alpha \beta \lambda} \varepsilon^ {\alpha \beta \mu \nu} = - 2 \delta_ {\sigma \lambda} ^ {\mu \nu}
$$
so that
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathop{\rm S}\limits_{\mathbf{C}}\sigma = -\delta^{\mu \nu}_{\sigma \lambda}(\mathop{\rm S}\limits_{\mathbf{A}}{}^{\mu}\mathop{\rm u}\limits_{\mathbf{A}} + \mathop{\rm S}\limits_{\mathbf{B}}{}^{\mu}\mathop{\rm u}\limits_{\mathbf{B}})^{\mathsf{u}^{\lambda}}_{\mathbf{C}} \\ = - \mathrm {S} _ {\sigma} (\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {u}) - \mathrm {S} _ {\sigma} (\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {u}) + \mathrm {u} _ {\sigma} (\mathbf {S} \cdot \mathbf {u}) + \mathrm {u} _ {\sigma} (\mathbf {S} \cdot \mathbf {u})   . \\ \end{array}
$$
We can now substitute $\frac{(\mathbf{P} + \mathbf{P})}{|\mathbf{P} + \mathbf{P}|}$ for $\mathbf{u}$ to complete the solution.
