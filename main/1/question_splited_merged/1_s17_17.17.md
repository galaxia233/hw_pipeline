Problem 17.17. Charged particles are dropped radially into a Reissner-Nordström black hole with $Q^2 < M^2$ . Show that it is never possible to drop in enough charge to make $Q^2 > M^2$ (a solution which would be a naked singularity, not a black hole).

## Solution

Solution 17.17. Suppose charge $\delta Q$ and energy $\delta E$ are dropped into the hole. We then have
$$
\delta \left(\mathrm {Q} ^ {2} - \mathrm {M} ^ {2}\right) = 2 \mathrm {Q} \delta \mathrm {Q} - 2 \mathrm {M} \delta \mathrm {E}. \tag {1}
$$
Not all values of $\delta Q$ and $\delta E$ are allowed, however. The radial "effective potential" equation (see e.g. MTW, Equation 33.32) for a charged particle falling in the Reissner-Nordström geometry is
$$
\mathrm {r} ^ {2} \frac {\mathrm {d r}}{\mathrm {d} \tau} = - \left\{\left(\mathrm {r} ^ {2} \delta \mathrm {E} - Q \delta \mathrm {Q r}\right) ^ {2} - \Delta \left(\mu_ {0} ^ {2} \mathrm {r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {L} _ {z} ^ {2} + \mathcal {Z}\right) \right\} ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \tag {2}
$$
where $\mathbf{E}, \mathbf{L}_z$ and $\mathcal{Q}$ are conserved quantities, and $\mu_0$ is the particle rest mass. A particle which crosses the event horizon into the black hole has $\mathrm{d}\mathbf{r} / \mathrm{d}\tau \leq 0$ at the event horizon $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_{+}$ . Since $\Delta = 0$ at $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_{+}$ , (2) becomes
$$
r ^ {2} \frac {d r}{d r} = - \left(r _ {+} ^ {2} \delta E - Q \delta Q r _ {+}\right) \leq 0 \tag {3}
$$
and hence $\delta \mathrm{E} > \mathrm{Q}\delta \mathrm{Q} / \mathrm{r}_{+}$ . Combining this with Equation (1) we see that
$$
\delta \left(Q ^ {2} - M ^ {2}\right) \leq 2 \delta E \left(\mathrm {r} _ {+} - M\right) = 2 \delta E \left(M ^ {2} - Q ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}. \tag {4}
$$
As $\mathbf{Q}^2 \to \mathbf{M}^2$ the right side goes to zero as a square root, so no sequence of adding $\delta \mathbf{E}$ 's can make the integrated left side positive.
