Problem 8.17. Another definition of the Lie derivative of a geometric object $\Phi^{\mathbf{A}}[\mathbf{x}^{\mu}(\mathbf{P})]$ (A represents all the tensor indices, $\mathbf{x}^{\mu}(\mathbf{P})$ are the coordinates of a point $\mathbf{P}$ ) is as follows: Make an infinitesimal point transformation $\mathbf{P}_0 \to \mathbf{P_N}$ by $\mathbf{x}^{\mu}(\mathbf{P}_0) = \mathbf{x}^{\mu}(\mathbf{P_N}) + \xi^{\mu}(\mathbf{P_N})$ . (Since $\xi^{\mu}$ is infinitesimal, it can in fact be evaluated at either $\mathbf{P}_0$ or $\mathbf{P_N}$ .) Also make an infinitesimal coordinate transformation that makes the numerical values of the coordinates of $\mathbf{P_N}$ the same as those of $\mathbf{P}_0$ in the original coordinates:
$$
\overline {{\mathbf {x}}} ^ {\mu} (\mathbf {P _ {N}}) = \mathbf {x} ^ {\mu} (\mathbf {P _ {0}}).
$$
Then define
$$
\mathcal {L} _ {\boldsymbol {\xi}} \Phi^ {\mathbf {A}} (\mathrm {P} _ {0}) = \underset {\boldsymbol {\xi} \to 0} {\text {L I M}} \left[ \Phi^ {\mathbf {A}} (\mathrm {P} _ {0}) - \overline {{\Phi}} ^ {\mathbf {A}} (\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {N}}) \right]  .
$$
Show that this definition is equivalent to that of Problem 8.13 by examining the cases (i) $\Phi^{\mathbf{A}} = \mathbf{a}$ scalar field (ii) $\Phi^{\mathbf{A}} = \mathbf{A}_{\mu}$ (iii) $\Phi^{\mathbf{A}} = \mathbf{T}_{\mu}^{\nu}$ .

## Solution

Solution 8.17.
(i) First the scalar case:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \Phi (\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {N}}) = \Phi (\mathrm {P} _ {0}) + [ \mathrm {x} ^ {\mu} (\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {N}}) - \mathrm {x} ^ {\mu} (\mathrm {P} _ {0}) ] \Phi_ {, \mu} (\mathrm {P} _ {0}) + \dots \\ \mathbf {\Phi} = \Phi (\mathbf {P} _ {0}) - \xi^ {\mu} \Phi_ {, \mu}, \\ \end{array}
$$
and $\overline{\Phi} (\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{N}}) = \Phi (\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{N}})$ since $\Phi$ is a scalar, hence
$$
\mathcal {L} _ {\xi} \Phi = \Phi_ {, \mu} \xi^ {\mu}.
$$
(ii) Since $\overline{\mathbf{x}}^{\mu}(\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{N}}) = \mathbf{x}^{\mu}(\mathbf{P}_{0}) = \mathbf{x}^{\mu}(\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{N}}) + \boldsymbol{\xi}^{\mu}$ , we have for vector fields
$$
\overline {{{\mathsf {A}}}} _ {\mu} (\mathbf {P _ {N}}) = (\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {a} / \partial \overline {{{\mathbf {x}}}} ^ {\mu}) \mathsf {A} _ {\mu} (\mathbf {P _ {N}}) = (\delta_ {\mu} ^ {a} - \xi_ {, \mu} ^ {a}) \mathsf {A} _ {\mu} (\mathbf {P _ {N}}).
$$
Now we combine this with
$$
\mathbf {A} _ {\mu} (\mathbf {P _ {N}}) = \mathbf {A} _ {\mu} (\mathbf {P _ {0}}) - \mathbf {A} _ {\mu , \beta} \xi^ {\beta}
$$
to get
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathcal {L} _ {\boldsymbol {\xi}} \mathrm {A} _ {\mu} = \mathrm {A} _ {\mu} \left(\mathrm {P} _ {0}\right) - \overline {{\mathrm {A}}} _ {\mu} \left(\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {N}}\right) \\ = \mathrm {A} _ {\mu} (\mathrm {P} _ {0}) - \left(\delta_ {\mu} ^ {\alpha} - \xi_ {, \mu} ^ {\alpha}\right) \left(\mathrm {A} _ {\alpha} (\mathrm {P} _ {0}) - \mathrm {A} _ {\alpha , \beta} \xi^ {\beta}\right) \\ = \mathrm {A} _ {\mu , \beta} \xi^ {\beta} + \mathrm {A} _ {\alpha} \xi^ {\alpha}, \mu . \\ \end{array}
$$
(iii) The tensor case is handled similarly:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \bar {\mathrm {T} } _ {\nu} ^ {\mu} \left(\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {N}}\right) = \left(\partial \bar {\mathrm {x} } ^ {\mu} / \partial \mathrm {x} ^ {a}\right) \left(\partial_ {\mathrm {x} } ^ {\beta} / \partial \bar {\mathrm {x} } ^ {\nu}\right) \mathrm {T} _ {\beta} ^ {a} \left(\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {N}}\right) \\ = \left(\delta_ {\alpha} ^ {\mu} + \xi_ {, \alpha} ^ {\mu}\right) \left(\delta_ {\nu} ^ {\beta} - \xi_ {, \nu} ^ {\beta}\right) \left[ T _ {\beta} ^ {\alpha} (P _ {0}) - T _ {\beta , \gamma} ^ {\alpha} \xi^ {\gamma} \right] \\ = \mathrm {T} _ {\nu} ^ {\mu} (\mathrm {P} _ {0}) - \mathrm {T} _ {\nu , \gamma} ^ {\mu} \xi^ {\gamma} - \mathrm {T} _ {\beta} ^ {\mu} \xi^ {\beta}, _ {\nu} + \mathrm {T} _ {\nu} ^ {a} \xi^ {\mu}, _ {a} \\ \mathcal {L} _ {\boldsymbol {\xi}} \mathrm {T} _ {\nu} ^ {\mu} = \mathrm {T} _ {\nu , \gamma} ^ {\mu} \xi^ {\gamma} + \mathrm {T} _ {\beta} ^ {\mu} \xi^ {\beta}, _ {\nu} - \mathrm {T} _ {\nu} ^ {a} \xi^ {\mu}, _ {a}. \\ \end{array}
$$
