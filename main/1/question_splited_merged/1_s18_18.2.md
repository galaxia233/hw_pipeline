Problem 18.2. A gravitationally bound dynamical system (e.g. a binary star) has, in order of magnitude, mass M and size R. Estimate the time for radiation reaction forces to affect the system substantially, and compare this time-scale with the dynamical time-scale for the system.

## Solution

Solution 18.2. The gravitational binding energy of the system is of order $\mathbf{M}^2 / \mathbf{R}$ and the kinetic energy is of order $\mathbf{MR}^2 / \mathbf{T}_{\mathrm{dyn}}^2$ , where $\mathbf{T}_{\mathrm{dyn}}$ characterizes motions of the system. For a system in equilibrium the virial theorem gives
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {d y n}} ^ {2} \sim \mathrm {R} ^ {3} / \mathrm {M}.
$$
The rate at which energy is radiated gravitationally is governed by the square of the third time derivative of the quadrupole moment or roughly
$$
\mathrm {P} ^ {\mathrm {G W}} \sim \mathrm {M} ^ {2} \mathrm {R} ^ {4} / \mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {d y n}} ^ {6}.
$$
The time for radiation reaction to affect the system is the time it takes to radiate a significant fraction of its energy as gravitational waves:
$$
\mathrm {T} = \frac {\mathrm {K . E .}}{\mathrm {P} ^ {\mathrm {G W}}} \sim \frac {\mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {d y n}} ^ {4}}{\mathrm {M R} ^ {2}} \sim \frac {\mathrm {R T} _ {\mathrm {d y n}} ^ {2}}{\mathrm {M} ^ {2}}.
$$
The ratio of this gravitational wave characteristic time to the dynamical time is then of order
$$
\frac {\mathrm {T}}{\mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {d y n}}} \sim \frac {\mathrm {R T} _ {\mathrm {d y n}}}{\mathrm {M} ^ {2}} \sim \left(\frac {\mathrm {R}}{\mathrm {M}}\right) ^ {\frac {5}{2}}.
$$
Evidently gravitational radiation reaction forces play an appreciable role only in relativistically compact systems.
