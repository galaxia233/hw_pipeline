Problem 19.25. In a "hot" Friedmann cosmology, two unrelated important epochs are when matter first begins to dominate radiation ( $\rho_{\text{matter}} \approx \rho_{\text{radiation}}$ ), and when protons and electrons recombine to form hydrogen. Given that these epochs happen to be nearly the same in our universe, deduce a numerical value for the conserved entropy per baryon $\sigma \equiv 4aT^3 / 3n$ (where $T =$ temperature, $a =$ radiation constant, $n =$ number density of baryons).

## Solution

Solution 19.25. We work with units which make $c = k = 1$ . The density of matter ( $m_p =$ average baryon mass) is $\rho_{\text{matt}} = m_p n$ . The density of radiation is $\rho_{\text{rad}} = K a T^4$ , where $a$ is the radiation constant $= (8\pi^5) / (15 h^3)$ and $K$ is 1 (considering photons only) or $1 + \frac{7}{4} \left( \frac{4}{11} \right)^{4/3} = 1.454$ (considering photons and neutrinos - see Weinberg, p. 537). Equating these give
$$
1 = \frac {\rho_ {\mathrm {r a d}}}{\rho_ {\mathrm {m a t t}}} = \frac {\mathrm {K a T} ^ {4}}{\mathrm {n m} _ {\mathrm {p}}} \tag {1}
$$
The Saha equation for the fractional ionization $\mathbf{x}$ of hydrogen is
$$
\frac {\mathrm {x} ^ {2}}{1 - \mathrm {x}} = \frac {(2 \pi \mathrm {m _ {e} k T}) ^ {\frac {3}{2}} \exp \left(- \frac {1}{2} \alpha^ {2} \mathrm {m _ {e} / k T}\right)}{\mathrm {n h} ^ {3}}
$$
$(\mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{e}} = \text{electron mass}, a = 1 / 137)$ . If recombination is defined by the left side of this equation being about unity, Equation (1) gives us
$$
\exp \left(\frac {1}{2} a ^ {2} m _ {\mathrm {e}} / k T\right) = \frac {1 5 (2 \pi m _ {\mathrm {e}} T) ^ {\frac {3}{2}} m _ {\mathrm {p}}}{8 \pi^ {5} K T ^ {4}}.
$$
Obviously the exponential sets the scale of the solution, $\mathbf{T} \sim \alpha^2 \mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{e}}$ ; guessing a coefficient, computing the right side, and iterating once gives
more accurately: $\mathbf{T} \approx \frac{1}{80} \alpha^2 \mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{e}}$ (cf. Problem 19.26). So, finally, when matter dominance and recombination occur at the same temperature $\mathbf{T}$ , we get
$$
\begin{array}{l} \sigma = \frac {4}{3} \frac {\mathrm {a T} ^ {3}}{\mathrm {n}} = \frac {4}{3} \frac {\mathrm {m _ {p}}}{\mathrm {T K}} \left(\frac {\mathrm {K a T} ^ {4}}{\mathrm {m _ {p}} \mathrm {n}}\right) \\ = \frac {4}{3} \frac {\mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {p}}}{\mathrm {K} \left(\frac {1}{8 0} \alpha^ {2} \mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {e}}\right)} = \frac {3 2 0}{3 \mathrm {K}} (1 3 7) ^ {2} (1 8 3 6) \\ \approx 4 \times 1 0 ^ {9}. \\ \end{array}
$$
