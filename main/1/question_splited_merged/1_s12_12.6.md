Problem 12.6. Show that an electron does not fall down when released in the center of an evacuated perfectly conducting closed container in a uniform gravitational field. ("Perfect conductor") $\equiv$ perfectly rigid positive lattice with perfectly mobile conduction electrons.)

## Solution

Solution 12.6. Let $\phi^{\mathbf{g}}$ and $\phi^{\mathbf{e}}$ be respectively the gravitational and electrostatic potentials. The potential energy of an electron is then
$$
\Phi = \mathrm {m} \phi^ {\mathbf {g}} + \mathrm {e} \phi^ {\mathbf {e}},
$$
$(\mathbf{m}, \mathbf{e} = \text{electron mass, charge})$ and the force on an electron is $\underline{\mathbf{F}} = -\nabla \Phi$ . In a static situation there can be no force component tangential to the conducting surface, and hence $\Phi$ must be a constant on the inside surface. Now there is neither mass $(\nabla^2 \phi^{\mathrm{g}} = 0)$ nor charge $(\nabla^2 \phi^{\mathrm{e}} = 0)$ inside the container so $\nabla^2 \Phi = 0$ . The solution to Laplace's equation $\nabla^2 \Phi = 0$ inside the conductor equipotential is then $\Phi = \text{constant}$ . If an electron is then introduced into this field, it will feel no force. [Note: Since the electron is introduced at the center of the container, image-charge forces can be ignored by symmetry.]
