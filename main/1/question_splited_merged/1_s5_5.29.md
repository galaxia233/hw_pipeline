Problem 5.29. In a uniformly accelerating system, show that the condition for thermal equilibrium is not $\mathbf{T} = \text{constant} = \mathbf{T}_0$ but rather is
$$
\mathrm {T} = \mathrm {T} _ {0} \exp (- \underline {{\mathbf {a}}} \cdot \underline {{\mathbf {x}}})
$$
where $\underline{\mathbf{x}}$ is coordinate position in the accelerating frame.

## Solution

Solution 5.29. An equilibrium state obtains when there is no entropy generation, so according to the results of Problem 5.28
$$
- \mathbf {q} \cdot \mathbf {a} = \frac { \nabla \mathrm {T} } { \mathrm {T} } \cdot \mathbf {q}
$$
must hold for all heat flows $\mathbf{q}$ . Thus we must have $\mathbf{a} = -\nabla \ell \mathbf {ln} \mathbf {T}$ . The solution to this equation is $\ell \mathbf {ln} \mathbf {T} = -\underline{\mathbf {a}} \cdot \underline{\mathbf {x}} + \text{constant}$ , or
$$
\mathrm {T} = \mathrm {T} _ { 0 } \exp (- \underline {{\underline {{a}}}} \cdot \underline {{\underline {{x}}}}).
$$
