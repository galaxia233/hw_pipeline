Problem 12.19. Estimate the Bohr radius of a "gravitational atom", e.g. two neutrons bound by their gravitational attraction in their lowest energy state.

## Solution

Solution 12.19. Force balance gives us
$$
\mathrm {G m} _ {\mathbf {N}} ^ {2} / \mathrm {r} ^ {2} = \mathrm {m} _ {\mathbf {N}} \omega^ {2} \left(\frac {1}{2} \mathrm {r}\right).
$$
According to Bohr quantization the separation radius must satisfy
$$
2 m \omega \left(\frac {1}{2} r\right) ^ {2} = n \pi .
$$
From these two equations, the separation is
$$
r = 2 n ^ {2} \hbar^ {2} / G m _ {N} ^ {3}.
$$
The lowest energy level is the $n = 1$ level. In this level the separation is
$$
r = 2 \hbar^ {2} / G m _ {N} ^ {3} = 6 \times 1 0 ^ {2 4} c m \approx 6 \times 1 0 ^ {6} l i g h t y e a r s!
$$
