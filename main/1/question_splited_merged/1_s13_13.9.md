Problem 13.9. A static metric is generated by a perfect fluid. Show that the fluid 4-velocity is parallel to the time Killing vector.

## Solution

Solution 13.9. We use Killing's equation $\xi_{(\alpha ;\beta)} = 0$ and the "hypersurface-orthogonal" statement (see Problem 10.8) of staticity $\xi_{[\alpha ;\beta}\xi_{\gamma ]} = 0$ , to derive
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = (\xi_ {[ \alpha ; \beta} \xi_ {\gamma ]}) ^ {j \gamma} \\ = \frac {1}{3} \left(\xi_ {a; \beta} \xi_ {\gamma} + \xi_ {\gamma ; a} \xi_ {\beta} + \xi_ {\beta ; \gamma} \xi_ {a}\right) ^ {i \gamma} \\ = \frac {1}{3} \left(R _ {\lambda \gamma \beta a} \xi^ {\lambda} \xi^ {\gamma} + R _ {\lambda \alpha \gamma} ^ {\gamma} \xi^ {\lambda} \xi_ {\beta} + R _ {\lambda \gamma \beta} ^ {\gamma} \xi^ {\lambda} \xi_ {a}\right) \\ \end{array}
$$
where we have used the result $\xi_{\mu ;a\beta} = \mathbf{R}_{\gamma \beta a\mu}\xi^{\gamma}$ from Problem 10.7. The first term above vanishes by symmetry and the remaining terms give Ricci components, so we have
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = \xi^ {\lambda} R _ {\lambda [ a} \xi_ {\beta ]} = 8 \pi \xi^ {\lambda} \left(T _ {\lambda [ a} - \frac {1}{2} g _ {\lambda [ a} T\right) \xi_ {\beta ]} \\ = 8 \pi \xi^ {\lambda} \left(\left(\rho + p\right) u _ {\lambda} u _ {[ \alpha} + \frac {1}{2} (\rho - p) g _ {\lambda} [ a)\right) \xi_ {\beta ]}. \\ \end{array}
$$
The last term vanishes trivially so we can conclude $\mathbf{u}_{[\alpha}\xi_{\beta ]} = 0$ and hence $\mathbf{u}\propto \pmb{\xi}$
A less rigorous proof is possible if the equivalent “stationary and time reversible” condition for staticity (see Problem 10.8) is used. In this case we see immediately that if $\mathbf{u}$ is not parallel to $\pmb{\xi}$ then $T^{0i} \neq 0$ in the “time reversible frame.” But this implies $G^{0i} \neq 0$ which is incompatible with time reversibility of the metric.
