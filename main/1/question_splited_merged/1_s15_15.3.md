Problem 15.3. A particle falls radially into a Schwarzschild metric. As measured by proper time at infinity, what is its inward coordinate velocity (dr/dt) at a (curvature-) radius $r$ ? What is the locally-measured velocity relative to a stationary observer at the same radius?

## Solution

Solution 15.3. We have two constants for radial fall: $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u} = -1$ and $\mathbf{u} \cdot \partial/\partial t = \mathbf{u}_0 = -(1 - 2\mathbf{M}/\mathbf{r})\mathbf{u}^0$ . From these we get
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {u} = - 1 = - (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) \left(\mathrm {u} ^ {0}\right) ^ {2} + (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) ^ {- 1} \left(\mathrm {u} ^ {\mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {2} \\ = \left[ - (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) + (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) ^ {- 1} (\mathrm {d r} / \mathrm {d} t) ^ {2} \right] \left(\mathrm {u} ^ {0}\right) ^ {2} \\ = \left[ - (1 - 2 M / r) + (1 - 2 M / r) ^ {- 1} (d r / d t) ^ {2} \right] \left(u _ {0}\right) ^ {2} (1 - 2 M / r) ^ {2}. \\ \end{array}
$$
Solving for $(\mathrm{dr} / \mathrm{dt})^2$ we find
$$
\left(\mathrm {d r} / \mathrm {d t}\right) ^ {2} = (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) ^ {2} \left[ 1 - (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) \left(\mathrm {u} _ {0}\right) ^ {- 2} \right].
$$
A stationary observer measures time intervals $\hat{\mathbf{d}}\mathbf{t} = (1 - 2\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{r})^{\frac{1}{2}}\mathbf{d}\mathbf{t}$ and radial distances $\hat{\mathbf{d}}\mathbf{r} = (1 - 2\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{r})^{-\frac{1}{2}}\mathbf{d}\mathbf{r}$ so he measures velocity
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \hat {\mathbf {r}}}{\mathrm {d} \hat {\mathbf {t}}} = (1 - 2 \mathbf {M} / \mathbf {r}) ^ {- 1} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}{\mathrm {d} t}.
$$
Notice that independent of the value of $\mathbf{u}_0$ this locally measured velocity approaches the velocity of light as r approaches 2M.
