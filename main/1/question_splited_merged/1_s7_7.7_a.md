Problem 7.7. Prove the following identities:
(a) $\mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta ,\gamma} = \Gamma_{\alpha}\beta \gamma +\Gamma_{\beta \alpha \gamma}$

## Solution

Solution 7.7.
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta , \gamma} = \nabla_ {\gamma} \left(\mathbf {e} _ {\alpha} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\beta}\right) = \left(\nabla_ {\gamma} \mathbf {e} _ {\alpha}\right) \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\beta} + \mathbf {e} _ {\alpha} \cdot \left(\nabla_ {\gamma} \mathbf {e} _ {\beta}\right) \\ = \Gamma_ {\alpha \gamma} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {e} _ {\mu} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\beta} + \Gamma_ {\beta \gamma} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {e} _ {\mu} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {\alpha} \\ = \Gamma_ {\beta a \gamma} + \Gamma_ {a \beta \gamma}. \\ \end {array}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \mu} \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \beta} = \delta_ {\alpha} ^ {\beta} \tag {b} \\ \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \mu , \gamma} \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \beta} + \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \mu} \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \beta}, _ {\gamma} = 0 \\ \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \mu} \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \beta} _ {, \gamma} = - \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \mu , \gamma} \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \beta}. \\ \end {array}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {(c)} \quad \mathrm {g} ^ {\alpha \beta} _ {, \gamma} = - \mathrm {g} _ {\lambda \mu , \gamma} \mathrm {g} ^ {\mu \beta} \mathrm {g} ^ {\lambda \alpha} = - (\Gamma_ {\lambda \mu \gamma} + \Gamma_ {\mu \lambda \gamma}) \mathrm {g} ^ {\mu \beta} \mathrm {g} ^ {\lambda \alpha} \\ = - \Gamma_ {\mu \gamma} ^ {\alpha} \mathsf {g} ^ {\mu \beta} - \Gamma_ {\lambda \gamma} ^ {\beta} \mathsf {g} ^ {\lambda a} . \\ \end {array}
$$
(Here we have used the result in (a).)
(d) For any matrix $\| \mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta}\|$
$$
\left(\log \det  \left\| \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \right\|\right) _ {, a} = \operatorname {T r} \left\| \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \right\| ^ {- 1} \left\| \mathbf {g} _ {\mu \nu , a} \right\|,
$$
therefore
$$
(\log \mathrm {g}), a = \mathrm {g} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu}, a
$$
and
$$
\frac {\mathrm {g} _ {, a}}{\mathrm {g}} = \mathrm {g} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu , a}.
$$
And so, by (b)
$$
\mathrm {g} _ {, a} = \mathrm {g g} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu , a} = - \mathrm {g g} _ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {g} ^ {\mu \nu}, a
$$
(e) In a coordinate frame, $\Gamma_{\alpha \beta}^{\mu} = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{g}^{\mu \nu}(\mathbf{g}_{\nu \alpha ,\beta} + \mathbf{g}_{\nu \beta ,\alpha} - \mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta ,\nu})$ and since the last two terms cancel $\Gamma_{\alpha \beta}^{\alpha} = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{g}^{\alpha \nu}\mathbf{g}_{\nu \alpha ,\beta}$ . Thus, by (d)
$$
\Gamma_ {a \beta} ^ {a} = \frac {1}{2} g, \beta / g = \frac {1}{2} (\log | g |), \beta = (\log | g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}), \beta .
$$
(f) $\mathbf{g}^{\mu \nu}\Gamma_{\mu \nu}^{\alpha} = -\mathbf{g}^{\alpha \beta},\beta -\Gamma^{\beta}_{\lambda \beta}\mathbf{g}^{\lambda \alpha}$ (putting $\beta = \gamma$ in (c) and summing)
$$
\begin{array}{l} = - \mathrm {g} ^ {\alpha \beta}, \beta - (\log | \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}), \lambda \mathrm {g} ^ {\lambda \alpha} \quad \text {b y (e)} \\ = - \mathrm {g} _ {, \nu} ^ {a \nu} - \left(\log \left| \mathrm {g} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right) _ {, \nu} \mathrm {g} ^ {a \nu} \quad \text {c h a n g i n g d u m m y i n d i c e s} \\ = - \mathrm {g} ^ {a \nu} _ {, \nu} - | \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} _ {, \nu} \mathrm {g} ^ {a \nu} | \mathrm {g} | ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \\ = - \frac {1}{| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left(\mathrm {g} ^ {\alpha \nu} | \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right) _ {, \nu}. \\ \end {array}
$$
(g) $\mathbf{A}_{;a}^{\alpha} = \mathbf{A}_{,a}^{\alpha} + \Gamma_{\beta a}^{\alpha}\mathbf{A}^{\beta} = \mathbf{A}_{,a}^{\alpha} + \frac{1}{|\mathbf{g}|^{\frac{1}{2}}}(|\mathbf{g}|^{\frac{1}{2}}),\beta \mathbf{A}^{\beta}$ by (e)
$$
= \frac {1}{| g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left(| g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} A ^ {a}\right), a
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {(h)} \quad \mathrm {A} _ {\alpha} ^ {\beta}; \beta = \mathrm {A} _ {\alpha} ^ {\beta}, \beta + \Gamma_ {\mu \beta} ^ {\beta} \mathrm {A} _ {\alpha} ^ {\mu} - \Gamma_ {\alpha \beta} ^ {\lambda} \mathrm {A} _ {\lambda} ^ {\beta} \\ = \mathrm {A} _ {\alpha} ^ {\beta}, \beta + \frac {1}{| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} (| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}), \mu \mathrm {A} _ {\alpha} ^ {\mu} - \Gamma_ {\alpha \mu} ^ {\lambda} \mathrm {A} _ {\lambda} ^ {\mu} \\ = \frac {1}{| \mathbf {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left(| \mathbf {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {A} _ {\alpha} ^ {\beta}\right), \beta - \Gamma_ {\alpha \mu} ^ {\lambda} \mathbf {A} _ {\lambda} ^ {\mu} \\ \end {array}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha \beta}; \beta = \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha \beta}, \beta + \Gamma_ {\mu \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {A} ^ {\mu \beta} + \Gamma_ {\mu \beta} ^ {\beta} \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha \mu} \tag {i} \\ = \mathrm {A} ^ {\alpha \beta}, \beta + \Gamma_ {\mu \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {A} ^ {\mu \beta} + \frac {1}{| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} (| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}), \mu \mathrm {A} ^ {\alpha \mu} \\ = \frac {1}{| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left(\mathrm {A} ^ {\alpha \beta} | \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right), \beta + \Gamma_ {\mu \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {A} ^ {\mu \beta}. \\ \end {array}
$$
But $\Gamma_{\mu \beta}^{\alpha} = \Gamma_{(\mu \beta)}^{\alpha}$ in a coordinate frame, so if $\mathbf{A}^{\mu \beta} = \mathbf{A}^{[\mu \beta]}$ , the last term vanishes, and
$$
\mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha \beta}; \beta = \frac {1}{| \mathbf {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} (| \mathbf {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha \beta}), \beta .
$$
$$
\square S = \left(S, _ {\alpha} g ^ {\alpha \beta}\right); \beta = \frac {1}{\left| g \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left(\left| g \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} S, _ {\alpha} g ^ {\alpha \beta}\right), \beta . \tag {j}
$$
(Here (g) has been used.)
