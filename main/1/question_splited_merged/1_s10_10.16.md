10.16. Show that a Killing vector is an admissible solution for the vector potential of Maxwell's equations for a test field in a vacuum spacetime. What electromagnetic field corresponds to the Killing vector $\partial/\partial\phi$ in Minkowski space?

## Solution

Solution 10.16. For a test electromagnetic field, all we have to check is that Maxwell's equations are satisfied in the given background metric. If $\mathbf{A}^{\mu}$ , the vector potential, is a Killing vector, then
$$
\mathrm {A} _ {; \mu} ^ {\mu} = 0
$$
i.e. the Lorentz condition is satisfied, and the wave equation
$$
\mathrm {A} _ {\nu} ^ { \mu ; \nu} - \mathrm {R} _ {\nu} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {A} ^ {\nu} = 0
$$
is satisfied by Problem 10.6 and the fact that $\mathbf{R}_{\nu}^{\mu} = 0$ in vacuum.
If in Minkowski space in spherical polar coordinates $\mathbf{A} \propto \partial/\partial \phi$ , i.e. only $\mathbf{A}^{\phi} = \mathbf{constant}$ is nonzero, then $\mathbf{A}^{\hat{\phi}} \propto \mathbf{r} \sin \theta$ , or in conventional 3-dimensional notation,
$$
\underline {{\mathbf {A}}} = \text {a r} \sin \theta \underline {{\mathbf {e}}} _ {\hat {\phi}} \quad (\mathbf {a} = \text {c o n s t a n t}) .
$$
Hence $\underline{\mathbf{E}} = 0$ and $\underline{\mathbf{B}} = \nabla \times \underline{\mathbf{A}} = 2\mathbf{a}(\cos \theta \underline{\mathbf{e}_{\hat{\mathbf{r}}}} - \sin \theta \mathbf{e}_{\hat{\theta}}) = 2\mathbf{a}\underline{\mathbf{e}_{\hat{\mathbf{z}}}}$ . Thus there is a uniform magnetic field parallel to the $z$ axis. (This problem is due to Robert M. Wald.)
