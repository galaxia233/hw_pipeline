Problem 1.16. A particle moves with 3-velocity $\underline{\mathbf{u}}$ and 3-acceleration $\underline{\mathbf{a}}$ as seen by an inertial observer $\mathcal{O}$ . Another inertial observer $\mathcal{O}'$ has 3-velocity $\underline{\mathbf{v}}$ relative to $\mathcal{O}$ . Show that the components of acceleration of the particle parallel and perpendicular to $\underline{\mathbf{v}}$ as measured by $\mathcal{O}'$ are
$$
\begin{array}{l} \underline {{\mathsf {a}}} ^ {\prime} \| = \frac {\left(1 - \mathrm {v} ^ {2}\right) ^ {3 / 2}}{\left(1 - \underline {{\mathrm {v}}} \cdot \underline {{\mathrm {u}}}\right) ^ {3}} \underline {{\mathsf {a}}} \| \\ \underline {{{\mathsf {a}}}} _ {\perp} ^ {\prime} = \frac {(1 - \mathsf {v} ^ {2})}{(1 - \underline {{{\mathsf {v}}}} \cdot \underline {{{\mathsf {u}}}}) ^ {3}} \left[ \underline {{{\mathsf {a}}}} _ {\perp} - \underline {{{\mathsf {v}}}} \times (\underline {{{\mathsf {a}}}} \times \underline {{{\mathsf {u}}}}) \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$

## Solution

Solution 1.16. The transformation of 3-velocity, from Problem 1.8, is
$$
u _ {\perp} ^ {\prime} = \frac {\gamma^ {- 1}}{(1 - v u _ {\parallel})} u _ {\perp}, \quad u _ {\parallel} = \frac {u _ {\parallel} - v}{1 - v u _ {\parallel}}
$$
and the time transformation is
$$
t ^ {\prime} = \gamma (t - \underline {{v}} \cdot \underline {{x}}).
$$
Now take differentials:
$$
\mathrm {d} u _ {\perp} ^ {\prime} = \frac {\gamma^ {- 1} \mathrm {d} u _ {\perp}}{(1 - \mathrm {v} u _ {\parallel})} + \frac {\mathrm {v} \gamma^ {- 1} u _ {\perp}}{(1 - \mathrm {v} u _ {\parallel}) ^ {2}} \mathrm {d} u _ {\parallel} = \frac {\gamma^ {- 1} [ (1 - \mathrm {v} u _ {\parallel}) \mathrm {d} u _ {\perp} + \mathrm {v} u _ {\perp} \mathrm {d} u _ {\parallel} ]}{(1 - \mathrm {v} u _ {\parallel}) ^ {2}}
$$
$$
\mathrm {d} u _ {\parallel} ^ {\prime} = \frac {\mathrm {d} u _ {\parallel}}{1 - v u _ {\parallel}} - \frac {v (v - u _ {\parallel}) d u _ {\parallel}}{(1 - v u _ {\parallel}) ^ {2}} = \frac {\gamma^ {- 2} d u _ {\parallel}}{(1 - v u _ {\parallel}) ^ {2}}
$$
$$
\mathrm {d t} ^ {\prime} = \gamma (\mathrm {d t} - \underline {{v}} \cdot \underline {{d x}}) = \gamma \mathrm {d t} (1 - \underline {{v}} \cdot \underline {{u}}) = \gamma \mathrm {d t} (1 - v u _ {\parallel})
$$
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \underline {{u}} ^ {\prime}}{\mathrm {d} t ^ {\prime}} \equiv \underline {{a}} ^ {\prime} \| = \frac {\gamma^ {- 3}}{(1 - \mathrm {v} u _ {\|}) ^ {3}} \underline {{a}} \| = \frac {\gamma^ {- 3}}{(1 - \underline {{v}} \cdot \underline {{u}}) ^ {3}} \underline {{a}} \|. \quad (\text {A n s w e r})
$$
and
$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\mathrm {d} \underline {{u}} _ {\perp} ^ {\prime}}{\mathrm {d} t ^ {\prime}} \equiv \underline {{a}} _ {\perp} ^ {\prime} = \frac {\gamma^ {- 2}}{(1 - \mathrm {v u} _ {\parallel}) ^ {3}} \left[ (1 - \mathrm {v u} _ {\parallel}) \underline {{a}} _ {\perp} + \mathrm {v} \underline {{u}} _ {\perp} a _ {\parallel} \right] \\ = \frac {\gamma^ {- 2}}{(1 - v u _ {\|}) ^ {3}} [ \underline {{a}} _ {\perp} - v (u _ {\|} \underline {{a}} _ {\perp} - \underline {{u}} _ {\perp} a _ {\|}) ] \\ = \frac {\gamma^ {- 2}}{(1 - \underline {{\mathrm {v}}} \cdot \underline {{\mathrm {u}}}) ^ {3}} [ \underline {{\mathrm {a}}} _ {\perp} - \underline {{\mathrm {v}}} \times (\underline {{\mathrm {a}}} \times \underline {{\mathrm {u}}}) ]. \tag {Answer} \\ \end{array}
$$
In the last line we have used the fact that
$$
\begin{array}{l} \underline {{\underline {{a}}}} \times \underline {{\underline {{u}}}} = (\underline {{\underline {{a}}}} _ {\perp} + \underline {{\underline {{a}}}} _ {\parallel}) \times (\underline {{\underline {{u}}}} _ {\perp} + \underline {{\underline {{u}}}} _ {\parallel}) \\ = \underline {{\mathbf {a}}} _ {\perp} \times \underline {{\mathbf {u}}} \| + \underline {{\mathbf {a}}} \| \times \underline {{\mathbf {u}}} _ {\perp} \\ \end{array}
$$
and the rule for expanding vector triple products.]
