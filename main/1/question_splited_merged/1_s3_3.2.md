Problem 3.2. Find 4 linearly independent null vectors in Minkowski space. Can you find 4 which are orthogonal?

## Solution

Solution 3.2. Four linearly independent null vectors are e.g.
$$
\mathbf {e} _ {z} + \mathbf {e} _ {t}, \mathbf {e} _ {z} - \mathbf {e} _ {t}, \mathbf {e} _ {x} + \mathbf {e} _ {t}, \mathbf {e} _ {y} + \mathbf {e} _ {t}.
$$
Suppose there existed 4 null vectors A, B, C, D which were mutually orthogonal. Linear independence implies that any vector can be written
$$
\mathbf {V} = \mathbf {a A} + \mathbf {b B} + \mathbf {c C} + \mathbf {d D}
$$
but the length of this vector is clearly zero if $\mathbf{A}$ , $\mathbf{B}$ , $\mathbf{C}$ , $\mathbf{D}$ are null and mutually orthogonal. Hence 4 such vectors cannot exist.
