10.13. Given a divergenceless stress energy tensor in flat spacetime, i.e.
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = 0 \quad \mathrm {R} _ {\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} = 0
$$
show that one may construct ten global conservation laws and hence ten conserved quantities.

## Solution

Solution 10.13. From Problem 10.11, we know that for each Killing vector $\pmb{\xi}$ , $\mathbf{J}^{\mu} \equiv \mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu} \pmb{\xi}_{\nu}$ is a conserved vector, i.e.
$$
\mathrm {J} _ {; \mu} ^ {\mu} = | \mathrm {g} | ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \left(| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {J} ^ {\alpha}\right) _ {, \alpha} = 0. \tag {1}
$$
From Problem 10.9, we know that in flat spacetime there are ten linearly independent Killing vectors $\xi_{\mathbf{(i)}}^{\mu}$ , $i = 1 - 10$ . Thus there are ten conserved vectors
$$
\mathbf {J} _ {(\mathbf {i})} ^ {\mu} \equiv \mathbf {T} ^ {\mu \nu} \xi_ {(\mathbf {i}) \nu}. \tag {2}
$$
From Equations (1) and (2), one may construct ten globally conserved
quantities
$$
Q _ {(i)} \equiv \int | g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} J _ {(i)} ^ {0} d ^ {3} x, \quad i = 1 - 1 0 \tag {3}
$$
since
$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\mathrm {d} Q _ {(i)}}{\mathrm {d} t} = \int \left(| g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} J _ {(i)} ^ {0}\right), _ {0} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} x = - \int \left(| g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} J _ {(i)} ^ {k}\right), _ {k} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} x \\ = - \int | g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} J _ {(i)} ^ {k} d ^ {2} \Sigma_ {k}. \\ \end{array}
$$
This last expression represents the flux of the $\mathbf{J}_{(\mathbf{i})}$ current through the (2-dimensional) surface bounding the integration volume. If $\mathbf{T}$ falls off fast enough at large distances, and the integration volume is infinite, this flux term vanishes and $\frac{\mathrm{d}\mathbf{Q}_{(\mathbf{i})}}{\mathrm{d}t} = 0$ . [Alternative proof: apply Solution 10.12.]
The four Q's derived from translation Killing vectors correspond to conserved energy and momentum; the three derived from rotation Killing vectors correspond to conserved angular momenta. To see the meaning of the three derived from boost Killing vectors, write (e.g. in flat cartesian coordinates with $\xi_{\nu} = [x, -t, 0, 0]$ )
$$
\begin{array}{l} Q = \int T ^ {0 \nu} \xi_ {\nu} d x d y d z \\ = \int x T ^ {0 0} d x d y d z - t \int T ^ {0 x} d x d y d z \\ = \mathrm {x} _ {\left(\mathrm {C}. \mathrm {M}. \right.)} \mathrm {M} _ {\left(\mathrm {C}. \mathrm {M}. \right.)} - t \mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {x} _ {\left(\mathrm {C}. \mathrm {M}. \right.)}} = \mathrm {M} _ {\left(\mathrm {C}. \mathrm {M}. \right.)} \left(\mathrm {x} _ {\left(\mathrm {C}. \mathrm {M}. \right.)} - \mathrm {v} _ {\left(\mathrm {C}. \mathrm {M}. \right.)} t\right) \\ \end{array}
$$
which is a component of the conserved “origin of uniform motion” for the center of mass (C.M.) of the system. $(\mathbf{x}_{(\mathbf{C},\mathbf{M},\cdot)}$ is defined to be $\int \mathbf{x} \, \mathrm{T}^{00} \, \mathrm{d}\mathbf{x} \, \mathrm{d}\mathbf{y} \, \mathrm{d}\mathbf{z} / \int \mathbf{T}^{00} \, \mathrm{d}\mathbf{x} \, \mathrm{d}\mathbf{y} \, \mathrm{d}\mathbf{z}.$
