Problem 14.15. Show that Maxwell's equations $\mathbf{F}_{; \nu}^{\mu \nu} = 4\pi \mathbf{J}^{\mu}$ , imply $\mathbf{J}_{; \mu}^{\mu} = 0$ .

## Solution

Solution 14.15. According to Problem 7.7 (i) and Maxwell's equations
$$
4 \pi J ^ {\mu} = F _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = \frac {1}{| g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} (| g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} F ^ {\mu \nu}) _ {, \nu}
$$
$$
4 \pi \mathrm {J} _ {; \mu} ^ {\mu} = 4 \pi \frac {1}{| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left(\mathrm {J} ^ {\mu} | \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right) _ {, \mu} = \frac {1}{| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left(| \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {F} ^ {\mu \nu}\right) _ {, \nu \mu}.
$$
Since $\mathbf{F}$ is antisymmetric this must vanish.
