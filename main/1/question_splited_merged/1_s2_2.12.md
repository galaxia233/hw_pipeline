Problem 2.12. Consider the elastic collision of a particle of mass $m_1$ with a stationary particle of mass $m_2 < m_1$ . Let $\theta_{\max}$ be the maximum scattering angle of $m_1$ . In nonrelativistic calculations, $\sin \theta_{\max} = m_2 / m_1$ . Prove that this result also holds relativistically.

## Solution

Solution 2.12. In the C.M. frame since the collision is elastic, $\mathbf{E}_1^{\mathbf{C},\mathbf{M}} = \mathbf{E}_1^{\mathbf{C},\mathbf{M}}$ , i.e.,
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {1} \cdot \mathbf {u} _ {\mathrm {C . M .}} = \mathbf {P} _ {1} \cdot \mathbf {u} _ {\mathrm {C . M .}}.
$$
(The prime denotes values after the collision.) Evaluating this equation in the lab frame yields
$$
- \mathrm {E} _ {1} + \underline {{\mathrm {P}}} _ {1} \cdot \underline {{\mathrm {v}}} _ {\mathrm {C}. \mathrm {M}}. = - \mathrm {E} _ {1} ^ {\prime} + \underline {{\mathrm {P}}} _ {1} ^ {\prime} \cdot \underline {{\mathrm {v}}} _ {\mathrm {C}. \mathrm {M}}.
$$
But $\underline{\mathbf{v}}_{\mathbf{C.M.}} = \underline{\mathbf{P}}_1 / (\mathbf{E}_1 + \mathbf{m}_2)$ , so we have
$$
- \mathrm {E} _ {1} + \frac {\mathrm {P} _ {1} ^ {2}}{\mathrm {E} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}} = - \mathrm {E} _ {1} ^ {\prime} + \frac {\mathrm {P} _ {1} ^ {\prime} \mathrm {P} _ {1} \cos \theta}{\mathrm {E} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}},
$$
or
$$
\cos \theta = \frac {\mathrm {E} _ {1} \left(\mathrm {E} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}\right) - \mathrm {E} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2} - \mathrm {m} _ {1} ^ {2}}{\mathrm {P} _ {1} \left(\mathrm {E} _ {1} ^ {2} - \mathrm {m} _ {1} ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \tag {1}
$$
(This equation could also have been derived without using the center of mass frame.) We can find the minimum value of $\cos \theta$ (maximum value of $\sin \theta$ ) by setting $d\cos \theta / dE_1' = 0$ , solving for $E_1'$ , and then substituting into Equation (1). A quicker way is to consider the intersection of the graph of $\cos \theta (E_1')$ with the straight line $\cos \theta = K$ . The points of intersection are given by
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {K} ^ {2} \mathrm {P} _ {1} ^ {2} \left(\mathrm {E} _ {1} ^ {2}, - \mathrm {m} _ {1} ^ {2}\right) = \mathrm {E} _ {1} ^ {2}, \left(\mathrm {E} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}\right) ^ {2} \tag {2} \\ - 2 \left(\mathrm {E} _ {1} + \mathrm {m} _ {2}\right) \left(\mathrm {E} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2} + \mathrm {m} _ {1} ^ {2}\right) \mathrm {E} _ {1} ^ {\prime} + \left(\mathrm {E} _ {1} \mathrm {m} _ {2} + \mathrm {m} _ {1} ^ {2}\right) ^ {2}. \\ \end{array}
$$
At the minimum value of $\cos \theta$ , the straight line is tangent to the curve and the discriminant of Equation (2), a quadratic in $\mathbf{E}_1'$ , is zero. This gives
$$
0 = - \left(\mathbf {E} _ {1} + \mathbf {m} _ {2}\right) ^ {2} \mathbf {K} ^ {2} \mathbf {P} _ {1} ^ {2} \mathbf {m} _ {1} ^ {2} + \mathbf {K} ^ {2} \mathbf {P} _ {1} ^ {2} (\mathbf {E} _ {1} \mathbf {m} _ {2} + \mathbf {m} _ {1} ^ {2}) ^ {2} + \mathbf {K} ^ {4} \mathbf {P} _ {1} ^ {4} \mathbf {m} _ {1} ^ {2},
$$
or
$$
K ^ {2} = \frac {\left(E _ {1} + m _ {2}\right) ^ {2} m _ {1} ^ {2} - \left(E _ {1} m _ {2} + m _ {1} ^ {2}\right) ^ {2}}{m _ {1} ^ {2} P _ {1} ^ {2}} = \frac {m _ {1} ^ {2} - m _ {2} ^ {2}}{m _ {1} ^ {2}}
$$
and, finally
$$
\begin{array}{l} \cos \theta_ {\min } = \frac {\left(m _ {1} ^ {2} - m _ {2} ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{m _ {1}}. \\ \sin \theta_ {\max } = m _ {2} / m _ {1}. \\ \end{array}
$$
