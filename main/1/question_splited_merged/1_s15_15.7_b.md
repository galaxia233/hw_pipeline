# Problem 15.7.
(b) Suppose that the central star is somewhat oblate or prolate, so that the form of the classical Newtonian potential is $\Phi (\mathbf{r}) = -\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{r} - \mathbf{A}\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{r}^3$ where A is related to the magnitude of the oblateness or prolateness. Calculate the advance (if oblate) of the periastron, to lowest order in $\mathbf{A} / \mathbf{r}^2$ , per orbit. (This is a purely Newtonian calculation.)

## Solution

(b) From the Newtonian orbital equations, derive
$$
\left(\frac {\mathrm {d} u}{\mathrm {d} \phi}\right) ^ {2} + u ^ {2} + \frac {2 \Phi (u)}{L ^ {2}} = \frac {2 E _ {\infty}}{L ^ {2}}.
$$
With $\Phi = -\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{r} - \mathbf{A}\mathbf{M} / \mathbf{r}^3$ , this becomes
$$
\left(\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {u}}{\mathrm {d} \phi}\right) ^ {2} + \mathbf {u} ^ {2} - \frac {2 \mathbf{m u}}{\mathbf {L} ^ {2}} - \frac {2 \mathbf{a m u} ^ {3}}{\mathbf {L} ^ {2}} = \frac {2 \mathbf{E} _ {\infty}}{\mathbf {L} ^ {2}}.
$$
Put this in the form
$$
\left(\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {u}}{\mathrm {d} \phi}\right) ^ {2} + (1 - \mathbf {c}) (\mathbf {u} - \mathbf {u} _ {0}) ^ {2} - \frac {2 \mathbf{a m}}{\mathbf {L} ^ {2}} (\mathbf {u} - \mathbf {u} _ {0}) ^ {3} = \text {c o n s t a n t}.
$$
By comparing terms of order $\mathbf{u}^2$ , conclude that $c = 6a\mathbf{u}_0\mathbf{m} / \mathbf{L}^2$ . For nearly circular orbits $\mathbf{L}^2 \approx \mathbf{mr}$ so that $c = 6a / r_0^2$ . From our experience with part (a) we know that the solution is periodic in the variable $(1 + c)^{\frac{1}{2}}\phi$ so that the angular spread from periastron to periastron is
$$
\Delta \phi = \frac {2 \pi}{(1 - c) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} = \frac {2 \pi}{\left(\frac {1 - 6 a}{r _ {0} ^ {2}}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}
$$
and therefore
$$
\left. \frac {\delta \phi}{2 \pi} \right| _ {\text {p e r o r b i t}} = \frac {3 a}{r _ {0} ^ {2}}.
$$
(The periastron advances if $a > 0$ (oblate) and regresses if $a < 0$ (prolate).)
