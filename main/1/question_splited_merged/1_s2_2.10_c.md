Problem 2.10. Consider the reaction $\mathbf{A} \rightarrow \mathbf{B} + \mathbf{C}$ (with particle masses $\mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{A}}, \mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{B}}, \mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{C}}$ ).
(c) If A decays while moving in the lab frame, find the relation between the angle at which B comes off, and the energies of A and B.

## Solution

(c) In this case $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{A}} = (\mathbf{E}_{\mathbf{A}},\mathbf{\underline{P}}_{\mathbf{A}}),\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{B}} = (\mathbf{E}_{\mathbf{B}},\mathbf{\underline{P}}_{\mathbf{B}}),$ so (1) becomes
$$
- \mathsf {m} _ {\mathbf {A}} ^ {2} - \mathsf {m} _ {\mathbf {B}} ^ {2} + 2 \mathsf {E} _ {\mathbf {A}} \mathsf {E} _ {\mathbf {B}} - 2 (\mathsf {E} _ {\mathbf {A}} ^ {2} - \mathsf {m} _ {\mathbf {A}} ^ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} (\mathsf {E} _ {\mathbf {B}} ^ {2} - \mathsf {m} _ {\mathbf {B}} ^ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \cos \theta = - \mathsf {m} _ {\mathbf {C}} ^ {2}.
$$
