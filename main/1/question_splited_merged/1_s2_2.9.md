Problem 2.9. Consider the reaction $\pi^{+} + n \rightarrow K^{+} + \Lambda^{0}$ . The rest masses of the particles are $m_{\pi} = 140 \, \text{MeV}$ , $m_{n} = 940 \, \text{MeV}$ , $m_{K} = 494 \, \text{MeV}$ , $m_{\Lambda} = 1115 \, \text{MeV}$ . What is the threshold kinetic energy of the $\pi$ to create a $K$ at an angle of $90^{\circ}$ in the lab in which the $n$ is at rest?

## Solution

Solution 2.9. We proceed by conserving 4-momentum and removing information about the $\Lambda$ particle:
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {\pi} + \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {n}} = \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {K}} + \mathbf {P} _ {\Lambda}
$$
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {\Lambda} ^ {2} = - \mathbf {m} _ {\Lambda} ^ {2} = (\mathbf {P} _ {\pi} + \mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {n}} - \mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {K}}) ^ {2} = - \mathbf {m} _ {\pi} ^ {2} - \mathbf {m} _ {\mathrm {n}} ^ {2} - \mathbf {m} _ {\mathrm {K}} ^ {2} + 2 \mathbf {P} _ {\pi} \cdot \mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {n}} - 2 \mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {n}} \cdot \mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {K}} - 2 \mathbf {P} _ {\pi} \cdot \mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {K}}.
$$
In the lab
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {P} _ {\pi} = \left(\mathrm {E} _ {\pi}, \underline {{\mathbf {P}}} _ {\pi}\right) \\ \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {n}} = \left(\mathrm {m} _ {\mathbf {n}}, 0\right), \quad \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {K}} = \left(\mathrm {E} _ {\mathbf {K}}, \underline {{\mathrm {P}}} _ {\mathbf {K}}\right) \\ \end{array}
$$
so,
$$
- \mathsf {m} _ {\pi} ^ {2} - \mathsf {m} _ {\mathsf {n}} ^ {2} - \mathsf {m} _ {\mathbf {K}} ^ {2} - 2 \mathsf {m} _ {\mathsf {n}} \mathbf {E} _ {\pi} + 2 \mathsf {m} _ {\mathsf {n}} \mathbf {E} _ {\mathbf {K}} + 2 \mathbf {E} _ {\pi} \mathbf {E} _ {\mathbf {K}} - 2 \mathbf {\widetilde {P}} _ {\pi} \boldsymbol {\cdot} \mathbf {\widetilde {P}} _ {\mathbf {K}} = - \mathsf {m} _ {\boldsymbol {\Lambda}} ^ {2} .
$$
We now set $\underline{\mathbf{P}}_{\pi} \cdot \underline{\mathbf{P}}_{\mathbf{K}} = 0$ ( $90^{\circ}$ scattering) and find
$$
\mathrm {E} _ {\pi} = \frac {\mathrm {m} _ {\Lambda} ^ {2} - \mathrm {m} _ {\pi} ^ {2} - \mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {n}} ^ {2} - \mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {K}} ^ {2} + 2 \mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {n}} \mathrm {E} _ {\mathrm {K}}}{2 (\mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {n}} - \mathrm {E} _ {\mathrm {K}})} .
$$
Thus, to make $\mathbf{E}_{\pi}$ a minimum we must make $\mathbf{E}_{\mathbf{K}}$ as small as possible (as you might have guessed!), so we try $\mathbf{E}_{\mathbf{K}} = \mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{K}}$ . Thus
$$
\begin{array}{l} E _ {\pi (\text {t h r e s h o l d})} = \frac {m _ {\Lambda} ^ {2} - m _ {\pi} ^ {2} - m _ {n} ^ {2} - m _ {K} ^ {2} + 2 m _ {n} m _ {K}}{2 \left(m _ {n} - m _ {K}\right)} \\ = \frac {m _ {\Lambda} ^ {2} - m _ {\pi} ^ {2}}{2 \left(m _ {n} - m _ {K}\right)} - \frac {m _ {n} - m _ {K}}{2} = \frac {1 1 1 5 ^ {2} - 1 4 0 ^ {2}}{2 (9 4 0 - 4 9 4)} - \frac {9 4 0 - 4 9 4}{2} \\ = 1 1 4 9 \mathrm {M e V}. \\ \end{array}
$$
hence threshold kinetic energy $= 1149 - 140 = 1009\mathrm{MeV}$
