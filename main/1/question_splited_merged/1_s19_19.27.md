Problem 19.27. In a radiation dominated Friedmann cosmology at times near the big bang singularity, calculate the temperature $T$ as a function of (proper cosmological) time $t$ . Assume that only photons, electrons and positrons contribute to the energy density $\rho$ . How is the answer changed if neutrinos and antineutrinos are also allowed?

## Solution

(This equation holds for any value of $\mathbf{k}$ in the limit $\eta \to 0$ .) Thus we have
$$
t ^ {2} = \frac {3 R ^ {4}}{3 2 \pi \rho_ {0} R _ {0} ^ {4}} = \frac {3}{3 2 \pi \rho} \tag {2}
$$
where we have used $\rho \propto \mathbb{R}^{-4}$ for the radiation-dominated era. The photon energy-density is
$$
\rho_ {\gamma} = \frac {8 \pi}{\mathrm {h} ^ {3}} \int_ {0} ^ {\infty} \frac {\mathrm {q} ^ {3} \mathrm {d q}}{\exp (\mathrm {q} / \mathrm {k T}) - 1} = \mathrm {a T} ^ {4}
$$
where $a$ is the Stefan-Boltzmann constant $8\pi^5 k^4 /15h^3$ . For highly relativistic electrons and positrons (which obey Fermi-Dirac Statistics)
$$
\rho_ {\mathrm {e +}} = \rho_ {\mathrm {e -}} = \frac {8 \pi}{\mathrm {h} ^ {3}} \int_ {0} ^ {\infty} \frac {\mathrm {q} ^ {3} \mathrm {d q}}{\exp (\mathrm {q / k T}) + 1} = \frac {7}{8} \mathrm {a T} ^ {4}.
$$
Thus the total mass-energy density is
$$
\rho = \rho_ {\gamma} + \rho_ {e +} + \rho_ {e -} = \frac {1 1}{4} a T ^ {4}
$$
and so
$$
T = \left(\frac {4}{1 1} \cdot \frac {3}{3 2 \pi a}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{4}} t ^ {- \frac {1}{2}}.
$$
If neutrinos are included and we assume they have zero chemical potential (cf. Weinberg, Section 15.6), then since they have only one spin state,
$$
\begin{array}{l} \rho_ {\nu_ {\mathbf {e}}} = \rho_ {\overline {{\nu}} _ {\mathbf {e}}} = \rho_ {\nu_ {\mu}} = \rho_ {\overline {{\nu}} _ {\mu}} = \frac {7}{1 6} a T ^ {4} \\ \rho = \frac {1 1}{4} a T ^ {4} + \frac {7}{4} a T ^ {4} = \frac {9}{2} a T ^ {4} \\ T = \left(\frac {2}{9} \cdot \frac {3}{3 2 \pi a}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{4}} t ^ {- \frac {1}{2}}. \\ \end{array}
$$
