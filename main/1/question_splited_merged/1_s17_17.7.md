Problem 17.7. For circular orbits in the equatorial plane of a Kerr black hole, prove that the marginally stable orbit has minimum energy E and minimum angular momentum L.

## Solution

Solution 17.7. Since we have three relations for the components of the 4-momentum $(\mathbf{p} \cdot \mathbf{p} = -\mathbf{m}^2, \mathbf{p}_t = \mathbf{E}, \mathbf{p}_\phi = \mathbf{L})$ the orbital equations can be reduced to the form
$$
\left(\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r} / \mathrm {d} \lambda\right) ^ {2} = \mathrm {V} (\mathrm {E}, \mathrm {L}, \mathrm {r})
$$
where $\lambda$ is an affine parameter and $V$ is some effective potential. The condition for a circular orbit is that $\mathrm{dr} / \mathrm{d}\lambda$ remain zero, i.e.
$$
V (E, L, r) = 0 \tag {1}
$$
$$
\mathrm {V} ^ {\prime} (\mathrm {E}, \mathrm {L}, \mathrm {r}) = 0 \tag {2}
$$
where $\mathbf{V}' \equiv \partial \mathbf{V} / \partial \mathbf{r}$ . By the implicit function theorem, Equations (1) and (2) can be solved for
$$
\mathbf {E} = \mathbf {E} (\mathbf {r}), \quad \mathbf {L} = \mathbf {L} (\mathbf {r})
$$
provided
$$
\left| \begin{array}{l l} \frac {\partial \mathrm {V}}{\partial \mathrm {E}} & \frac {\partial \mathrm {V}}{\partial \mathrm {L}} \\ \frac {\partial \mathrm {V} ^ {\prime}}{\partial \mathrm {E}} & \frac {\partial \mathrm {V} ^ {\prime}}{\partial \mathrm {L}} \end{array} \right| \neq 0. \tag {3}
$$
(This condition is in fact satisfied.) Then $\mathrm{dE} / \mathrm{dr}$ and $\mathrm{dL} / \mathrm{dr}$ can be found by differentiating Equations (1) and (2):
$$
0 = \frac {\mathrm {d} V}{\mathrm {d r}} = \frac {\partial V}{\partial E} \frac {\mathrm {d} E}{\mathrm {d r}} + \frac {\partial V}{\partial L} \frac {\mathrm {d} L}{\mathrm {d r}} + V ^ {\prime} \tag {4}
$$
$$
0 = \frac {\mathrm {d} V ^ {\prime}}{\mathrm {d r}} = \frac {\partial V ^ {\prime}}{\partial \mathrm {E}} \frac {\mathrm {d E}}{\mathrm {d r}} + \frac {\partial V ^ {\prime}}{\partial \mathrm {L}} \frac {\partial \mathrm {L}}{\partial \mathrm {r}} + V ^ {\prime \prime}. \tag {5}
$$
Now consider an orbit at $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_0$ perturbed to $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{r}_0 + \epsilon$ . The perturbed orbit equation is
$$
(\mathrm {d r} / \mathrm {d} \lambda) ^ {2} = \mathrm {V} (\mathbf {r} _ {0}) + \epsilon \mathrm {V} ^ {\prime} (\mathbf {r} _ {0}) + \frac {1}{2} \epsilon^ {2} \mathrm {V} ^ {\prime \prime} (\mathbf {r} _ {0}) + \dots
$$
where $\mathbf{V}(\mathbf{r}_0) = 0$ and $\mathbf{V}'(\mathbf{r}_0) = 0$ (condition for unperturbed circular orbit). The $\mathbf{V}''$ term then governs stability and $\mathbf{V}''$ must be negative if the orbit is stable. For a marginally stable orbit we have $\mathbf{V}'' = 0$ in addition to $\mathbf{V} = 0$ and $\mathbf{V}' = 0$ , so the only solution to Equations (4) and (5) is $\mathrm{dE} / \mathrm{d}\mathbf{r} = 0$ and $\mathrm{dL} / \mathrm{d}\mathbf{r} = 0$ . It is physically clear that these extrema are minima.
