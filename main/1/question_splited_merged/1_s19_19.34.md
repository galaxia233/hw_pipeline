Problem 19.34. What is the order of magnitude of the influence of the cosmological constant on the celestial mechanics of the solar system if $\Lambda \sim 10^{-57} \mathrm{~cm}^{-2}$ ?

## Solution

Solution 19.34. The effective $\rho_{\mathbf{c}}$ density corresponding to $\Lambda$ is
$$
\rho_ {c} = \frac {\Lambda}{8 \pi} = \frac {1 0 ^ {- 5 7} c m ^ {- 2}}{8 \pi} \approx 4 \times 1 0 ^ {- 3 1} g m / c m ^ {3}.
$$
Typical densities in the solar system are
$$
\rho_ {\text {b o d y}} \sim 1 \mathrm {g m} / \mathrm {c m} ^ {3}
$$
$$
\rho_ {\text {s o l a r s y s t e m}} \sim 1 0 ^ {3 3} \mathrm {g m} / (1 0 ^ {1 5} \mathrm {c m}) ^ {3} \sim 1 0 ^ {- 1 2} \mathrm {g m} / \mathrm {c m} ^ {3}.
$$
Thus, the effect of $\Lambda$ in the solar system, in order of magnitude is
$$
\frac {\rho_ {c}}{\rho_ {\text {s o l a r s y s t e m}}} \sim 1 0 ^ {- 1 9}.
$$
