Problem 13.12. A gauge transformation is an infinitesimal coordinate transformation which relabels the coordinates of a point $\mathbf{P}$ according to
$$
\mathbf {x} _ {\mathrm {n e w}} ^ {\mu} (\mathbf {P}) = \mathbf {x} _ {\mathrm {o l d}} ^ {\mu} (\mathbf {P}) + \xi^ {\mu} (\mathbf {P}).
$$
Such transformations induce changes, to first order in $\pmb{\xi}$ , of the functional forms of tensors. Find the gauge transformation laws for scalars, and components of vectors and second rank tensors. For the linearized metric perturbations $\mathbf{g}_{\mu \nu} = \eta_{\mu \nu} + \mathrm{h}_{\mu \nu}$ show in particular that
$$
\mathrm {h} _ {\mu \nu} ^ {\mathrm {n e w}} (\mathbf {x}) = \mathrm {h} _ {\mu \nu} ^ {\mathrm {o l d}} (\mathbf {x}) - 2 \xi_ {(\mu , \nu)} .
$$

## Solution

Solution 13.12. At a given point the value of a scalar is unchanged so
$$
\phi^ {\text {n e w}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\text {n e w}} (\mathrm {P})) = \phi^ {\text {o l d}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\text {o l d }} (\mathrm {P})) = \phi^ {\text {o l d}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\text {n e w}} (\mathrm {P}) - \boldsymbol {\xi} (\mathrm {P}))
$$
$$
\phi^ {\mathrm {n e w}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {n e w}}) \approx \phi^ {\mathrm {o l d}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {n e w}}) - \xi^ {a} \phi_ {, a}.
$$
For a vector field
$$
\begin{array}{l} V _ {\mu} ^ {n e w} = \frac {\partial \left(x ^ {o l d}\right) ^ {\nu}}{\partial \left(x ^ {n e w}\right) ^ {\mu}} V _ {\nu} ^ {o l d} \left(x ^ {o l d}\right) \\ = \left(\delta_ {\mu} ^ {\nu} - \xi_ {, \mu} ^ {\nu}\right) \left(V _ {\nu} ^ {\text {o l d}} \left(x ^ {\text {n e w}}\right) - V _ {\nu , \sigma} ^ {\text {o l d}} \xi^ {\sigma}\right) \\ \approx \mathrm {V} _ {\mu} ^ {\mathrm {o l d}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {n e w}}) - \mathrm {V} _ {\nu} \xi_ {, \mu} ^ {\nu} - \mathrm {V} _ {\mu , \sigma} \xi^ {\sigma}. \\ \end{array}
$$
For a tensor field, similarly
$$
\begin{array}{l} T _ {\mu \nu} ^ {n e w} \left(x ^ {n e w}\right) = \frac {\partial \left(x ^ {o l d}\right) ^ {\alpha}}{\partial \left(x ^ {n e w}\right) ^ {\mu}} \frac {\partial \left(x ^ {o l d}\right) ^ {\beta}}{\partial \left(x ^ {n e w}\right) ^ {\nu}} T _ {\alpha \beta} ^ {o l d} \left(x ^ {o l d}\right) \\ = \left(\delta_ {\mu} ^ {a} - \xi_ {, \mu} ^ {a}\right) \left(\delta_ {\nu} ^ {\beta} - \xi_ {, \nu} ^ {\beta}\right) \left(\mathrm {T} _ {\alpha \beta} ^ {\mathrm {o l d}} \left(\mathrm {x} ^ {\mathrm {n e w}}\right) - \mathrm {T} _ {\alpha \beta , \sigma} \xi^ {\sigma}\right) \\ \approx \mathrm {T} _ {\mu \nu} ^ {\mathrm {o l d}} - \mathrm {T} _ {\mu \beta} \xi_ {, \nu} ^ {\beta} - \mathrm {T} _ {\beta \nu} \xi_ {, \mu} ^ {\beta} - \mathrm {T} _ {\mu \nu , \sigma} \xi^ {\sigma}. \\ \end{array}
$$
Notice that
$$
\xi_ {\mu ; \nu} = \mathsf {g} _ {\mu \sigma} \xi_ {; \nu} ^ {\sigma} = \mathsf {g} _ {\mu \sigma} (\xi_ {, \nu} ^ {\sigma} + \xi^ {\alpha} \Gamma_ {a \nu} ^ {\sigma}) = \mathsf {g} _ {\mu \sigma} \xi_ {, \nu} ^ {\sigma} + \xi^ {\alpha} \Gamma_ {\mu a \nu}
$$
and that
$$
\xi_ {(\mu ; \nu)} = \mathbf {g} _ {\sigma (\mu} \xi_ {, \nu)} ^ {\sigma}) + \xi^ {\alpha} \left(\frac {1}{2} \mathbf {g} _ {\mu \nu , \alpha}\right) .
$$
For the metric tensor then
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu} ^ {\text {n e w}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\text {n e w}}) = \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu} ^ {\text {o l d}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\text {n e w}}) - \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu , \sigma} \xi^ {\sigma} - 2 \mathrm {g} _ {\sigma (\mu} \xi^ {\sigma}, \nu) \\ = \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu} ^ {\mathrm {o l d}} \left(\mathrm {x} ^ {\mathrm {n e w}}\right) - 2 \xi_ {(\mu ; \nu)} \\ = \eta_ {\mu \nu} + h _ {\mu \nu} ^ {o l d} \left(x ^ {n e w}\right) - 2 \xi_ {(\mu ; \nu)} \\ \end{array}
$$
and we have
$$
\mathrm {h} _ {\mu \nu} ^ {\mathrm {n e w}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {n e w}}) = \mathrm {h} _ {\mu \nu} ^ {\mathrm {o l d}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\mathrm {n e w}}) - 2 \xi_ {(\mu ; \nu)}.
$$
Since, to lowest order, the metric is Minkowskian we can take
$$
\xi_ {(\mu ; \nu)} = \xi_ {(\mu , \nu)} + \mathcal {O} (\xi h).
$$
