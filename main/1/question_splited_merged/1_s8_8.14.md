Problem 8.14. Suppose $\mathbf{A} = \mathrm{d} / \mathrm{d}\lambda$ is the tangent vector field to a congruence (set of curves) $\mathbf{x}^{\alpha} = \mathbf{x}^{\alpha}(\lambda)$ and $\mathbf{B}$ is a vector field. Show that the geometrical interpretation of the transport law $\mathfrak{L}_{\mathbf{A}}\mathbf{B} = \mathbf{0}$ is that $\mathbf{B}$ connects points of equal $\lambda$ on neighboring curves of the congruence.

## Solution

Solution 8.14.
![](images/7c8f21261efc33744042bc400d815ff14261c28fe92d8ad76dc680dbcd709f7f.jpg)
At the points $\mathbf{P}_0$ and $\mathbf{P}_1$ consider two tangent vectors $\mathbf{A}(\mathbf{P}_0)$ and $\mathbf{A}(\mathbf{P}_1)$ each extending from $\lambda = 0$ to $\lambda = \Delta \lambda = \mathcal{O}(\epsilon)$ . (Note: In the figure $\Delta \lambda$ is taken to be unity so that the $\mathbf{A}$ vectors are large and the "difference vector" $\mathbf{K}$ is exaggerated for clarity.) Suppose that at point $\mathbf{P}_1$ the vector $\mathbf{B}(\mathbf{P}_1)$ connects $\mathbf{P}_1$ and $\mathbf{P}_0$ . Let us compute the amount by which the vector field $\mathbf{B}$ fails to connect points of equal $\lambda$ along the congruence $\mathbf{A}$ , i.e. let us compute the vector $\mathbf{K}$ :
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {K} = \left[ \mathbf {B} \left(\mathbf {P} _ {1}\right) + \mathbf {A} \left(\mathbf {P} _ {0}\right) \right] - \left[ \mathbf {A} \left(\mathbf {P} _ {1}\right) + \mathbf {B} \left(\mathbf {P} _ {3}\right) \right] \\ = \left[ \mathbf {B} \left(\mathbf {P} _ {1}\right) - \mathbf {B} \left(\mathbf {P} _ {3}\right) \right] - \left[ \mathbf {A} \left(\mathbf {P} _ {1}\right) - \mathbf {A} \left(\mathbf {P} _ {0}\right) \right] \\ = \mathrm {A} ^ {\tau} \mathrm {B} ^ {\gamma}, _ {\tau} \mathbf {e} _ {\gamma} + \mathcal {O} (\mathrm {A} ^ {2} \mathrm {B}) - \mathrm {B} ^ {\tau} \mathrm {A} ^ {\gamma}, _ {\tau} \mathbf {e} _ {\gamma} + \mathcal {O} (\mathrm {B} ^ {2} \mathrm {A}) \\ = [ \mathbf {A}, \mathbf {B} ] + \mathcal {O} (\epsilon^ {3}) \text {i f} | \mathbf {A} | \sim \mathcal {O} (\epsilon) \sim | \mathbf {B} |. \\ \end{array}
$$
Thus in the limit of infinitesimal vectors A and B,
$$
\mathbf {K} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \mathbf {A}, \mathbf {B} \end{array} \right] = \mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {A}} \mathbf {B}.
$$
Thus $\mathbf{K}$ vanishes if $\mathcal{L}_{\mathbf{A}}\mathbf{B} = 0$ , i.e. if $\mathbf{B}$ is “Lie-dragged” along $\mathbf{A}$ .
