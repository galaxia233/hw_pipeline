Problem 19.39. The distance between two neighboring galaxies is $\delta \mathbf{x}^a = \mathbf{Rn}^a$ where $\mathbf{n}$ is a unit, purely spatial vector in one galaxy's rest frame. Show that
$$
\frac {\dot {R}}{R} = \sigma_ {\alpha \beta} n ^ {\alpha} n ^ {\beta} + \frac {1}{3} \theta
$$
(where $\sigma$ is the shear tensor and $\theta$ is the scalar expansion) and that averaging over all directions $\mathfrak{n}^{\alpha}$ gives
$$
< \frac {\dot {\mathbf {R}}}{\mathbf {R}} > = \frac {1}{3} \theta .
$$

## Solution

Solution 19.39. From Solution 14.9 we know that the spatial connecting vector, $\xi$ , between two nearby world lines obeys
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \boldsymbol {\xi} = \nabla_ {\boldsymbol {\xi}} \mathbf {u} + (\boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \mathbf {a}) \mathbf {u}. \tag {1}
$$
Putting $\pmb{\xi} \equiv \mathbf{R}\mathfrak{n}$ in Equation (1) gives
$$
\mathrm {R n} ^ {\alpha}; \beta \mathrm {u} ^ {\beta} + \mathrm {n} ^ {\alpha} \dot {\mathrm {R}} = \mathrm {R n} ^ {\beta} \mathrm {u} ^ {\alpha}; \beta + \mathrm {R u} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {n} ^ {\gamma} \mathrm {a} _ {\gamma} \tag {2}
$$
where $\mathbf{R} \equiv \mathrm{d}\mathbf{R} / \mathrm{d}t = \mathfrak{u}^{\alpha}\mathbf{R}_{,\alpha}$ . If we now dot $\mathfrak{n}^{\alpha}$ into Equation (2) and use the decomposition equation for $\mathfrak{u}_{\alpha};\beta$ (see Problem 5.18)
$$
\mathfrak {u} _ {\alpha ; \beta} = \omega_ {\alpha \beta} + \sigma_ {\alpha \beta} + \frac {1}{3} \theta (\mathfrak {g} _ {\alpha \beta} + \mathfrak {u} _ {\alpha} \mathfrak {u} _ {\beta}) - \mathfrak {a} _ {\alpha} \mathfrak {u} _ {\beta}
$$
we obtain
$$
\begin{array}{l} \dot {\mathbf {R}} = \mathbf {R} \mathrm {n} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {n} ^ {\beta} \left[ \omega_ {\alpha \beta} + \sigma_ {\alpha \beta} + \frac {1}{3} \theta (\mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} + \mathrm {u} _ {\alpha} \mathrm {u} _ {\beta}) - \mathrm {a} _ {\alpha} \mathrm {u} _ {\beta} \right] \\ + \mathrm {R u} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {n} ^ {\gamma} \mathrm {n} _ {\alpha} \mathrm {a} _ {\gamma}. \tag {3} \\ \end{array}
$$
Now the antisymmetry of $\omega_{\alpha \beta}$ and the fact that $\mathfrak{n}^{\alpha}$ is a spatial unit vector $(\mathfrak{n}^{\alpha}\mathfrak{u}_{\alpha} = 0)$ used in Equation (3) give us
$$
\dot {\frac {\mathbf {R}}{\mathbf {R}}} = \sigma_ {\alpha \beta} \mathfrak {n} ^ {\alpha} \mathfrak {n} ^ {\beta} + \frac {1}{3} \theta .
$$
Averaging $\sigma_{\alpha \beta} \mathfrak{n}^{\alpha} \mathfrak{n}^{\beta}$ over directions produces a term proportional to the trace of $\sigma_{\alpha \beta}$ . Since $\sigma_{\alpha \beta}$ is traceless, we are left with
$$
\left\langle \frac {\dot {\bf R}}{\dot {\bf R}} \right\rangle = \frac {1}{3} \theta .
$$
