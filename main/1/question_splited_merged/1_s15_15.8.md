Problem 15.8. A rocket ship in a circular orbit of circumference $2\pi r$ around a star of mass M fires a laser gun (rest frequency $\nu_0$ ). The gun is aimed in the orbital plane, and at an angle $\alpha$ (in the ship's frame) outward from the tangential direction of motion. What is the frequency of the laser as seen by a stationary observer at infinity?

## Solution

Solution 15.8. We solve the problem in two stages: First, compute the laser frequency as measured by a static observer at $\mathbf{r}$ ("SOR"). Second, compute the redshift between this observer and a static observer at infinity (SOI).
Let $\mathbf{u}_{\mathbb{R}}$ be the 4-velocity of the rocket and $\mathbf{p}_{\mathbb{R}}$ the photon momentum at $\mathbf{r}$ .
$$
\frac {\nu_ {\mathrm {S O R}}}{\nu_ {0}} = \frac {\mathbf {u} _ {\mathrm {S O R}} \cdot \mathbf {p} _ {\mathrm {R}}}{\mathbf {u} _ {\mathrm {R}} \cdot \mathbf {p} _ {\mathrm {R}}} \tag {1}
$$
If $\mathbf{v}$ is the proper relative velocity between the rocket (R) and SOR observer, then in the rocket frame Equation (1) gives us
$$
\frac {\nu_ {\mathrm {S O R}}}{\nu_ {0}} = \frac {\gamma \nu_ {0} (1 + \mathrm {v} \cos a)}{\nu_ {0}}
$$
or
$$
\nu_ {\mathbf {S O R}} = \gamma \nu_ {0} (1 + v \cos a) \tag {2}
$$
where $\gamma \equiv (1 - v^2)^{-\frac{1}{2}}$ . To compute $v$ , recall that circular orbits have an angular velocity
$$
\Omega \equiv \frac {\mathrm {d} \phi}{\mathrm {d} t}
$$
exactly the same as Kepler's Third Law in Newtonian gravity:
$$
\Omega = \left(\frac {\mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {3}}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}. \tag {3}
$$
(See Problem 17.4.) Thus
$$
v = \frac {d \widehat {\phi}}{d \widehat {t}} = \frac {r d \phi}{\left(1 - 2 M / r\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} d t} = \left[ \frac {M}{r (1 - 2 M / r)} \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}. \tag {4}
$$
The observer at infinity then measures a frequency
$$
\nu_ {\mathrm {S O I}} = \frac {\nu_ {\mathrm {S O R}}}{\mathrm {u} _ {\mathrm {S O R}} ^ {0}} = \nu_ {\mathrm {S O R}} (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}
$$
$$
\nu_ {\mathrm {S O I}} = \gamma \nu_ {0} (1 + \mathrm {v} \cos \alpha) (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}
$$
where $\mathbf{v}$ is given by Equation (4).
