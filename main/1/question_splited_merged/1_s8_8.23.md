Problem 8.23. Derive expressions for $\underline{\nabla} \cdot \underline{\mathbf{A}}$ and $\underline{\nabla} \times \underline{\mathbf{A}}$ in spherical polar coordinates.

## Solution

Solution 8.23. We use results (ii) and (iii) of Problem 8.22 with $\mathbf{x}^1 = \mathbf{r}$ , $\mathbf{x}^2 = \theta$ , $\mathbf{x}^3 = \phi$ and with $\mathbf{h}_1 = \mathbf{l}$ , $\mathbf{h}_2 = \mathbf{r}$ , $\mathbf{h}_3 = \mathbf{r}$ sin $\theta$ . (For simplicity we follow the usual notation here and omit the caret's indicating physical components.)
$$
\begin{array}{l} \nabla \cdot \underline {{\mathbf {A}}} = (\mathrm {r} ^ {2} \sin \theta) ^ {- 1} [ \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \sin \theta \mathrm {h} _ {\mathrm {i}} ^ {- 1} \mathrm {A} _ {\mathrm {i}} ] _ {, \mathrm {i}} \\ = \left(\mathrm {r} ^ {2} \sin \theta\right) ^ {- 1} \left[ \left(\mathrm {r} ^ {2} \sin \theta \mathrm {A} _ {\mathrm {r}}\right), \mathrm {r} + \left(\mathrm {r} \sin \theta \mathrm {A} _ {\theta}\right), \theta + \mathrm {r A} _ {\phi}, \phi \right] \\ \end{array}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} (\nabla \times \mathbf {A}) ^ {\mathbf {r}} = (\mathbf {r} \sin \theta) ^ {- 1} [ (\sin \theta \mathbf {A} _ {\phi}), \theta - \mathbf {A} _ {\theta , \phi} ] \\ (\nabla \times \mathbf {A}) ^ {\theta} = (\mathbf {r} \sin \theta) ^ {- 1} [ \mathbf {A} _ {\mathbf {r}, \phi} - (\mathbf {r} \sin \theta \mathbf {A} _ {\phi}), _ {\mathbf {r}} ] \\ (\nabla \times \mathbf {A}) ^ {\phi} = \mathbf {r} ^ {- 1} \left[ (\mathbf {r A} _ {\theta}) _ {, \mathbf {r}} - \mathbf {A} _ {\mathbf {r}, \theta} \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$
