Problem 16.11.
(b) Now let $\mathbf{M}$ be a function of the null coordinate $\mathfrak{u}$ in part (a). Show that the space-time is not vacuum, and find the corresponding $T^{\alpha \beta}$ . Give a physical interpretation. (This is the "Vaidya" metric.)

## Solution

Solution 16.11.
(b) We wish to compute the Einstein tensor $\mathbf{G}_{\alpha \beta}$ when $\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{M}(\mathbf{u})$ . The Christoffel symbols are most easily found from the Lagrangian for geodesics (see Problem 7.25):
$$
L = - \left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r}\right) \dot {u} ^ {2} - 2 \dot {u} \dot {r} + r ^ {2} \dot {\theta} ^ {2} + r ^ {2} \sin^ {2} \theta \dot {\phi} ^ {2}. \tag {3}
$$
The Euler-Lagrange equation
$$
\frac {d}{d s} \left(\frac {\partial L}{\partial \dot {r}}\right) - \frac {\partial L}{\partial r} = 0 \tag {4}
$$
givesthegeodesic equation
$$
\ddot {u} - \left(\mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r} ^ {2}\right) \dot {u} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} \dot {\theta} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} \sin^ {2} \theta \dot {\phi} ^ {2} = 0 \tag {5}
$$
from which we read off the nonzero Christoffel symbols
$$
\Gamma_ {u u} ^ {u} = - \frac {M}{r ^ {2}}, \qquad \Gamma_ {\theta \theta} ^ {u} = r, \qquad \Gamma_ {\phi \phi} ^ {u} = r \sin^ {2} \theta .
$$
Similarly, Equation (4) with $\mathfrak{r}$ replaced by $\mathfrak{u}$ , $\theta$ and $\phi$ gives the remaining Christoffel symbols
$$
\Gamma_ {\mathrm {u u}} ^ {\mathrm {r}} = \frac {\mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) - \frac {\mathrm {M} ^ {\prime}}{\mathrm {r}}, \qquad \Gamma_ {\theta \theta} ^ {\mathrm {r}} = - \mathrm {r} \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right),
$$
$$
\Gamma_ {\phi \phi} ^ {r} = - \left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r}\right) r \sin^ {2} \theta , \quad \Gamma_ {u r} ^ {r} = \frac {M}{r ^ {2}}, \quad \Gamma_ {r \theta} ^ {\theta} = \frac {1}{r},
$$
$$
\Gamma_ {\phi \phi} ^ {\theta} = - \sin \theta \cos \theta , \quad \Gamma_ {r \phi} ^ {\phi} = \frac {1}{r}, \quad \Gamma_ {\theta \phi} ^ {\phi} = \cot \theta .
$$
These all have their Schwarzschild values except $\Gamma_{uu}^{r}$ . From the formula for the Ricci tensor (MTW, Equation 8.51b)
$$
\mathrm {R} _ {\alpha \beta} = \Gamma_ {\alpha \beta , \gamma} ^ {\gamma} - (\log | \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}) _ {, \alpha \beta} + (\log | \mathrm {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}) _ {, \gamma} \Gamma_ {\alpha \beta} ^ {\gamma} - \Gamma_ {\beta \delta} ^ {\gamma} \Gamma_ {a \gamma} ^ {\delta}
$$
we see that all the $\mathbf{R}_{\alpha \beta}$ will have their Schwarzschild values (i.e. zero) except
$$
R _ {u u} = - 2 M ^ {\prime} / r ^ {2}.
$$
The Ricci scalar is
$$
R = g ^ {u u} R _ {u u} = 0
$$
so the only nonzero component of the Einstein tensor is
$$
G _ {u u} = - 2 M ^ {\prime} / r ^ {2}
$$
which implies that
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {u u}} = - \mathrm {M} ^ {\prime} / 4 \pi \mathrm {r} ^ {2},
$$
and that all other components of $\mathbf{T}$ are zero. Since the vector $\mathbf{k} = \nabla \mathbf{u}$ , that is $\mathbf{k}_{\alpha} = (1,0,0,0)$ , is null, the stress-energy tensor
$$
\mathbf {T} = - \frac {\mathbf {M} ^ {\prime}}{4 \pi \mathbf {r} ^ {2}} \mathbf {k} \otimes \mathbf {k}
$$
corresponds to a pure radiation field. Thus a physical interpretation of this solution (called the Vaidya metric) is the exterior metric of a spherical star whose mass is decreasing because of the energy carried off in radiation.
