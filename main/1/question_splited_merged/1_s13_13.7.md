Problem 13.7. The "dominant energy condition" on $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}$ requires that the weak energy condition be satisfied (all observers see a nonnegative energy density) and furthermore, that all observers see energy density greater or equal to the magnitude of the energy-flux 3-vector. Show that the statement
$$
\mathbf {u} \cdot (- \mathbf {T}) ^ {\mathbf {n}} \cdot \mathbf {u} \leq 0
$$
for all nonspacelike vectors $\mathbf{u}$ reduces to the weak energy condition for $\mathfrak{n} = 1$ and to the dominant energy condition for $\mathfrak{n} = 2$ . What about $\mathfrak{n} > 2$ ? [Here $(\mathbf{T}^2)_{\mu \nu} \equiv \mathbf{T}_\mu^\sigma \mathbf{T}_{\sigma \nu}$ and so forth.]

## Solution

Solution 13.7. For $n = 0$ the statement reads $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u} \leq 0$ and is trivial. For $n = 1$ the statement is $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{T} \cdot \mathbf{u} \geq 0$ for all timelike $\mathbf{u}$ . This is just the weak energy condition. For $n = 2$ the statement is that $(\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{T}) \cdot (\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{T}) \leq 0$ or that $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{T}$ must be nonspacelike. But for an observer with 4-velocity $\mathbf{u}$ , $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{T} = (-\rho, [\text{Energy flux}]^{\mathrm{i}})$ so, by the dominant energy condition, $|\rho| > |\text{Energy flux}|$ and $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{T}$ is indeed nonspacelike.
Now consider any $n$ and the statement
$$
(- 1) ^ {n} \mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {T} \cdot \mathbf {T} \dots \mathbf {T} \cdot \mathbf {u} \leq 0.
$$
Since $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{T}$ is nonspacelike, the statement for any $\mathfrak{n}$ is equivalent to the statement for $\mathfrak{n}-2$ . But we have shown that the dominant energy condition implies the $\mathfrak{n}=1$ and $\mathfrak{n}=2$ cases so it follows that the dominant energy condition implies the statement for all $\mathfrak{n}$ . The converse follows immediately, any stress energy satisfying the statement for $\mathfrak{n}=1$ and $\mathfrak{n}=2$ satisfies all the requirements of the dominant energy condition.
