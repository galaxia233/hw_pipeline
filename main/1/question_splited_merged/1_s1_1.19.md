Problem 1.19. A mirror is moving parallel to its plane. Show that the angle of incidence of a photon equals the angle of reflection.

## Solution

Solution 1.19. Let $\mathbf{E}$ and $\underline{\mathbf{P}}$ denote the energy and momentum of the photon. In the frame of the mirror (primed frame) $\mathbf{E}_{\mathrm{in}}' = \mathbf{E}_{\mathrm{out}}'$ , $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{x}\mathrm{in}}' = \mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{x}\mathrm{out}}'$ , $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{y}\mathrm{in}}' = -\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{y}\mathrm{out}}'$ . In the lab frame, we wish to show $\theta_{1} = \theta_{2}$ .
$$
\frac {\tan \theta_ {1}}{\tan \theta_ {2}} = \frac {\left(\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {x}} / \mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {y}}\right) _ {\text {i n}}}{\left(\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {x}} / - \mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {y}}\right) _ {\text {o u t}}} = \frac {\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {x}} \text {i n}}{\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {x}} \text {o u t}} \cdot \frac {\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {y}} ^ {\prime} \text {i n}}{- \mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {y}} ^ {\prime} \text {o u t}} = \frac {\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {x}} \text {i n}}{\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {x}} \text {o u t}},
$$
where we have used $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{y}}^{\prime} = \mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{y}}$ . Then, Lorentz transforming $\mathbf{P}_{\mathbf{x}}$
$$
\frac {\tan \theta_ {1}}{\tan \theta_ {2}} = \frac {\gamma \left(\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {x}} ^ {\prime} + \beta \mathrm {E} ^ {\prime}\right) _ {\mathrm {i n}}}{\gamma \left(\mathrm {P} _ {\mathrm {x}} ^ {\prime} + \beta \mathrm {E} ^ {\prime}\right) _ {\mathrm {o u t}}} = 1.
$$
Hence the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection in the lab frame as well as the mirror frame.
