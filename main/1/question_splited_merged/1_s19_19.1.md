Problem 19.1. Show that the equations of Newtonian gravity and hydrodynamics do not admit a cosmology which is isotropic, homogeneous, and static (i.e. an unchanging universe filled with a uniform perfect fluid).

## Solution

Solution 19.1. The relevant Newtonian equations are
$$
\nabla^ {2} \Phi = 4 \pi G \rho \quad (\text {g r a v i t a t i o n a l p o t e n t i a l}) \tag {1}
$$
$$
\partial \rho / \partial t + \nabla \cdot (\rho \underline {{v}}) = 0 \quad (\text {c o n t i n u i t y e q u a t i o n}) \tag {2}
$$
$$
\frac {\partial \underline {{v}}}{\partial t} + (\underline {{v}} \cdot \nabla) \underline {{v}} = - \frac {\nabla p}{\rho} - \nabla \Phi \quad \text {(e q u a t i o n o f m o t i o n)}. \tag {3}
$$
If the universe is static and homogeneous, then $\underline{\mathbf{v}} = 0$ , and $\rho$ and $\mathfrak{p}$ are constant in position and time. The solution of Equation (1) is then
$$
\Phi = \frac {2}{3} \pi G \rho (\underline {{r}} \cdot \underline {{r}}) + \underline {{C}} \cdot \underline {{r}} + K
$$
where $\underline{\mathbf{C}}$ and $\mathbf{K}$ are arbitrary constants of integration and $\underline{\mathbf{r}}$ is the position vector from the (arbitrary) origin. Equation (2) is identically zero. Equation (3), however, is contradictory: the left side vanishes since $\underline{\mathbf{v}} = 0$ ; on the right, $\nabla p$ vanishes by homogeneity, but $\nabla \Phi$ is not identically zero for any choice of $\underline{\mathbf{C}}$ , $\mathbf{K}$ . Thus, there is no solution to the equations.
