Problem 9.24. Suppose $\mathbf{K}$ is the curvature at a point in a 2-dimensional surface, as defined in Problem 9.23. If $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ are two vectors tangent at a point to the two surface, and $\mathbf{A}$ is parallel transported around a small circuit lying in the 2-surface, show that the change in the angle between
A and B is of magnitude
$$
\Delta \theta = | K \Delta \Sigma |
$$
where $\Delta \Sigma$ is the area enclosed by the circuit.

## Solution

Solution 9.24. Let the interior of the circuit be subdivided into infinitesimal parallelograms, and consider the change in angle for one small rectangle. If $\mathbf{u}$ and $\mathbf{v}$ represent the sides of one rectangle then from Problems 9.23 and 9.11 we have that the change in $\mathbf{A}$ after transport is
![](images/1f1bd8068fbd78fbf78d386dcffbf8ae753fc88af060de901e39f49477076b35.jpg)  
Circuit $\pmb{\Sigma}$ - approximated with rectangles
$$
\delta \mathbf {A} _ {\alpha} = - \mathbf {R} _ {\alpha \beta \mu \nu} \mathbf {A} ^ {\beta} \mathbf {u} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {v} ^ {\nu} = \mathbf {K} (\mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \mu} \mathbf {g} _ {\beta \nu} - \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \nu} \mathbf {g} _ {\beta \mu}) \mathbf {A} ^ {\beta} \mathbf {u} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {v} ^ {\nu}.
$$
Note that $\mathbf{A}^{\alpha}\delta \mathbf{A}_{\alpha} = \frac{1}{2}\delta (\mathbf{A}\cdot \mathbf{A}) = 0$ so the length of A is unchanged. The change in angle $\theta$ between A and B can be calculated from
$$
\mathbf {B} ^ {\alpha} \delta \mathbf {A} _ {\alpha} = \delta (\mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {B} _ {\alpha}) = | \mathbf {A} | | \mathbf {B} | \delta (\cos \theta) = | \mathbf {A} | | \mathbf {B} | \sin \theta \delta \theta
$$
and
$$
\mathbf {B} ^ {\alpha} \delta \mathbf {A} _ {\alpha} = \mathbf {K} [ (\mathbf {B} \cdot \mathbf {u}) (\mathbf {A} \cdot \mathbf {v}) - (\mathbf {B} \cdot \mathbf {v}) (\mathbf {A} \cdot \mathbf {u}) ] .
$$
Now choose a locally flat coordinate system on the surface with $\mathbf{u} \sim \mathbf{e}_1$ and $\mathbf{v} \sim \mathbf{e}_2$ then
$$
\begin{array}{l} \left| \left| \mathbf {A} \right| \left| \mathbf {B} \right| \sin \theta \delta \theta \right| = \mathrm {K} (\mathbf {B} ^ {1} \mathbf {A} ^ {2} - \mathbf {B} ^ {2} \mathbf {A} ^ {1}) \mathbf {u v} \\ = K | \mathbf {A} \times \mathbf {B} | u v = K \sin \theta | \mathbf {A} | | \mathbf {B} | \cdot (a r e a \enspace \text {e n c l o s e d}). \\ \end{array}
$$
Hence, for transport around a small rectangle $|\delta \theta| = K\delta \Sigma$ . If we now consider a more general circuit, the change in A due to transport around that circuit will be equal to the change in A due to transport around all the rectangles into which the circuit is divided, and the area of the circuit is the sum of the areas of the rectangles, thus $|\Delta \theta| = K\Delta \Sigma$ .
