Problem 7.10. Derive the geodesic equation from the definition of a geodesic as a curve of extremal length.

## Solution

Solution 7.10. The integral for length is
$$
\int \mathrm {d} \mathbf {s} = \int \left(- \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d} s} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\beta}}{\mathrm {d} s}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d} \mathbf {s}.
$$
To extremize length take $\delta \int \mathrm{d}s = 0$ . According to the Euler-Lagrange equations
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} \mathrm {s}} \left(\mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \mathrm {u} ^ {\alpha}\right) = \frac {1}{2} \mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \gamma , \beta} \mathrm {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {u} ^ {\gamma} \tag {1}
$$
where $\left(-\mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta}\frac{\mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}^{\alpha}}{\mathrm{d}s}\frac{\mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}^{\beta}}{\mathrm{d}s}\right) = 1$ and $\mathbf{u}^{\alpha}\equiv \frac{\mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}^{\alpha}}{\mathrm{d}s}$ have been used. Now since
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} s} \left(\mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \mathbf {u} ^ {\alpha}\right) = \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {u} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d} s} + \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma}}{\mathrm {d} s} \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta , \gamma} \mathbf {u} ^ {\alpha} = \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {u} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d} s} + \mathbf {u} ^ {\gamma} \mathbf {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta , \gamma}
$$
Equation (1) becomes
$$
\mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathrm {x} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d s} ^ {2}} + \mathrm {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {u} ^ {\gamma} \left(\mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta , \gamma} - \frac {1}{2} \mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \gamma , \beta}\right) = 0. \tag {2}
$$
Now, use
$$
\mathsf {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathsf {u} ^ {\gamma} \mathsf {g} _ {\alpha \beta , \gamma} = \mathsf {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathsf {u} ^ {\gamma} \cdot \frac {1}{2} \left(\mathsf {g} _ {\alpha \beta , \gamma} + \mathsf {g} _ {\gamma \beta , a}\right)
$$
and multiply Equation (2) by $g^{\beta \tau}$ to obtain
$$
\begin{array}{l} \frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {x} ^ {\tau}}{\mathrm {d s} ^ {2}} + \frac {1}{2} \mathrm {g} ^ {\beta \tau} \left(\mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta , \gamma} + \mathrm {g} _ {\gamma \beta , \alpha} - \mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \gamma , \beta}\right) \mathrm {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {u} ^ {\gamma} \tag {3} \\ = \frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {x} ^ {\tau}}{\mathrm {d s} ^ {2}} + \Gamma_ {\alpha \gamma} ^ {\tau} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d s}} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma}}{\mathrm {d s}} = 0. \\ \end {array}
$$
