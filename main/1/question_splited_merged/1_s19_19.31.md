Problem 19.31. A universe is isotropic, homogeneous, and contains only pressureless dust and "vacuum polarization" stress energy, so that $\mathbf{T}_{\mu \nu} = \rho_0 \mathfrak{u}_\mu \mathfrak{u}_\nu - \frac{\Lambda}{8\pi} \mathfrak{g}_{\mu \nu}$ where $\mathfrak{u}^\mu$ is the 4-velocity field of the matter. Show that there is a static solution for the metric, but that it is unstable. (This cosmology is called the "Einstein universe".)

## Solution

Solution 19.31. The complete set of equations for isotropic, homogeneous universes is (see Problems 19.13 or 19.14)
$$
\left(\frac {\dot {\mathrm {R}}}{\dot {\mathrm {R}}}\right) ^ {2} = \frac {- \mathrm {k}}{\mathrm {R} ^ {2}} + \frac {8 \pi \rho}{3} \tag {1a}
$$
$$
2 \ddot {\frac {R}{R}} = - \left(\dot {\frac {R}{R}}\right) ^ {2} - \frac {k}{R ^ {2}} - 8 \pi p. \tag {1b}
$$
Since the universe contains only dust stress-energy, $\rho_0$ , and "vacuum polarization" stress-energy $\Lambda$ , we have
$$
\rho = \rho_ {0} + \Lambda / 8 \pi \tag {2a}
$$
$$
p = - \Lambda / 8 \pi . \tag {2b}
$$
Since all time derivatives of $\mathbf{R}$ higher than the second may be expressed as linear combinations of $\dot{\mathbf{R}}$ and $\ddot{\mathbf{R}}$ by Equations (1), the necessary and sufficient condition for a static solution is
$$
\dot {\mathbf {R}} = \ddot {\mathbf {R}} = 0. \tag {3}
$$
With the conditions of Equations (3) and (2), Equations (1) become
$$
\mathrm {k} / \mathrm {R} ^ {2} = (8 \pi \rho_ {0} + \Lambda) / 3 \tag {4a}
$$
$$
\mathrm {k} / \mathrm {R} ^ {2} = \Lambda \tag {4b}
$$
which imply
$$
\rho_ {0} = \Lambda / 4 \pi \tag {5a}
$$
$$
\mathbf {R} = \Lambda^ {- \frac {1}{2}}. \tag {5b}
$$
Since $\rho_0 > 0$ , Equation (4b) now implies $k = +1$ . To investigate the stability of the "Einstein universe," first combine Equations (1) to
give
$$
\frac {2 \ddot {\mathrm {R}}}{\mathrm {R}} = - \frac {8 \pi}{3} \rho - 8 \pi \mathrm {p} = - \frac {8 \pi}{3} \rho_ {0} + \frac {2}{3} \Lambda \tag {6}
$$
and let
$$
\mathbf {R} = \boldsymbol {\Lambda} ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} + \delta \mathbf {R} \tag {7a}
$$
$$
\rho_ {0} = \frac {\Lambda}{4 \pi} + \delta \rho_ {0} \tag {7b}
$$
Equation (6), to order $\delta \mathbf{R}$ , now becomes
$$
2 \Lambda^ {\frac {1}{2}} (\delta \ddot {\mathbf {R}}) = - \frac {8 \pi}{3} \delta \rho_ {0}. \tag {8}
$$
Now, since the total amount of dust is conserved, the dust stress-energy obeys
$$
\rho_ {0} \mathbf {R} ^ {3} = \text {c o n s t a n t}
$$
$$
\frac {\delta \rho_ {0}}{\rho_ {0}} = - 3 \frac {\delta R}{R}. \tag {9}
$$
Equation (8) then gives
$$
\left(\delta \ddot {\mathbf {R}}\right) - \Lambda \delta \mathbf {R} = 0, \tag {10}
$$
i.e. the perturbation $\delta R$ grows exponentially in time. Hence, the universe is unstable.
