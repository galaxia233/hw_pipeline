15.15. Show that the spacelike slice $\mathbf{v} = \text{constant}(|\mathbf{v}| > 1)$ of the Schwarzschild geometry in Kruskal coordinates $\mathbf{u}, \mathbf{v}$ cannot be embedded in a Euclidean 3-space. What is the general condition on the slope $\mathrm{dv} / \mathrm{du}$ of a spacelike slice of the Schwarzschild geometry that allows it to be embedded in a Euclidean 3-space?

## Solution

Solution 15.15. In Kruskal coordinates, the Schwarzschild geometry is given by
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = \frac {3 2 M ^ {3}}{\mathrm {r}} \mathrm {e} ^ {- \mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M}} (- \mathrm {d v} ^ {2} + \mathrm {d u} ^ {2}) + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \Omega^ {2} \tag {1a}
$$
where
$$
\left(\frac {\mathrm {r}}{2 \mathrm {M}} - 1\right) \mathrm {e} ^ {\mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M}} = \mathrm {u} ^ {2} - \mathrm {v} ^ {2}. \tag {1b}
$$
Setting $\mathbf{v} = \mathbf{\theta}$ constant, and $\theta = \pi /2$ in Equation (1a) and equating the line element to a Euclidean surface of revolution gives
$$
\frac {3 2 \mathrm {M} ^ {3}}{\mathrm {r}} \mathrm {e} ^ {- \mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M}} \mathrm {d u} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2} = [ 1 + (\mathrm {d z} / \mathrm {d r}) ^ {2} ] \mathrm {d r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2} \tag {2}
$$
or
$$
1 + \left(\frac {\mathrm {d} z}{\mathrm {d r}}\right) ^ {2} = \frac {3 2 \mathrm {M} ^ {3}}{\mathrm {r}} \mathrm {e} ^ {- \mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M}} \left(\frac {\mathrm {d u}}{\mathrm {d r}}\right) ^ {2}.
$$
We can calculate du/dr from Equation (1b) to arrive at
$$
1 + \left(\frac {\mathrm {d} z}{\mathrm {d r}}\right) ^ {2} = \frac {\mathrm {r}}{2 \mathrm {M}} \frac {\mathrm {e} ^ {\mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M}}}{(\mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M} - 1) \mathrm {e} ^ {\mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M}} + \mathrm {v} ^ {2}}. \tag {3}
$$
For embedding, $z$ must be a real function, so we must have
$$
\frac {\mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M} \mathrm {e} ^ {\mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M}}}{(\mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M} - 1) \mathrm {e} ^ {\mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M}} + \mathrm {v} ^ {2}} \geq 1. \tag {4}
$$
From Equation (4), we conclude
$$
r / 2 M \geq \log v ^ {2}.
$$
Thus, for $|v| > 1$ , there will be a minimum value of $r$ inside which the embedding breaks down.
Geometrically, the necessary condition for embedding is that
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} (\text {c i r c u m f e r e n c e})}{\mathrm {d} (\text {p r o p e r r a d i u s})} \leq 2 \pi .
$$
We now consider the general condition on dv/du which allows Euclidean embedding: Suppose we have a slice described by $\mathbf{v}(\mathbf{u})$ . Then
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = \frac {3 2 \mathrm {M} ^ {3}}{\mathrm {r}} \mathrm {e} ^ {- \mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M}} \left[ 1 - \left(\frac {\mathrm {d v}}{\mathrm {d u}}\right) ^ {2} \right] \mathrm {d u} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2} \tag {6} \\ = \frac {3 2 M ^ {3}}{r} e ^ {- r / 2 M} \left[ 1 - \left(\frac {d v}{d u}\right) ^ {2} \right] \left[ \frac {(r / 8 M ^ {2}) e ^ {r / 2 M}}{(u - v \frac {d v}{d u})} \right] ^ {2} d r ^ {2} + r ^ {2} d \phi^ {2} \\ \end{array}
$$
and we require
$$
\frac {\mathrm {r}}{2 \mathrm {M}} \mathrm {e} ^ {\mathrm {r} / 2 \mathrm {M}} \left[ 1 - \left(\frac {\mathrm {d v}}{\mathrm {d u}}\right) ^ {2} \right] \geq \left(\mathrm {u} - \mathrm {v} \frac {\mathrm {d v}}{\mathrm {d u}}\right) ^ {2} \tag {7}
$$
at each r.
