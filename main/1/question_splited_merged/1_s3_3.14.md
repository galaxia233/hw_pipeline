Problem 3.14. Show that a second rank tensor $\mathbf{F}$ which is antisymmetric in one coordinate frame $(\mathbf{F}_{\mu \nu} = -\mathbf{F}_{\nu \mu})$ is antisymmetric in all frames. Show that the contravariant components are also antisymmetric $(\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu} = -\mathbf{F}^{\nu \mu})$ . Show that symmetry is also coordinate invariant.

## Solution

Solution 3.14. We need only transform and check the results for antisymmetry:
$$
\mathbf {F} _ {\overline {{\mu}} \overline {{\nu}}} = \Lambda_ {\overline {{\mu}}} ^ {\alpha} \Lambda_ {\overline {{\nu}}} ^ {\beta} \mathbf {F} _ {\alpha \beta} = - \Lambda_ {\overline {{\mu}}} ^ {\alpha} \Lambda_ {\overline {{\nu}}} ^ {\beta} \mathbf {F} _ {\beta \alpha} = - \Lambda_ {\overline {{\mu}}} ^ {\beta} \Lambda_ {\overline {{\nu}}} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {F} _ {\alpha \beta} = - \mathbf {F} _ {\overline {{\nu}} \overline {{\mu}}}.
$$
$$
\mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} = \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu a} \mathbf {g} ^ {\nu \beta} \mathbf {F} _ {a \beta} = - \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu a} \mathbf {g} ^ {\nu \beta} \mathbf {F} _ {\beta a} = - \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \beta} \mathbf {g} ^ {\nu a} \mathbf {F} _ {a \beta} = - \mathbf {F} ^ {\nu \mu}.
$$
For variety, we treat the symmetric case by a different argument: Consider the tensor $\mathfrak{A}^{\mu \nu} = \mathsf{S}^{\mu \nu} - \mathsf{S}^{\nu \mu}$ , where $\mathsf{S}^{\mu \nu}$ is a symmetric tensor. Since $\mathfrak{A}^{\mu \nu}$ vanishes (by hypothesis) and since $\mathfrak{A}^{\mu \nu}$ is a tensor, it vanishes in all coordinate systems, hence $\mathsf{S}^{\mu \nu}$ is symmetric in all coordinate systems.
