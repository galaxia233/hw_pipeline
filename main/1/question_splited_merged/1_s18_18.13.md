Problem 18.13. A (weak) plane gravitational wave travelling in the $x$ -direction is normally incident on a slab of cement. The cement absorbs energy $E$ from the plane wave. Show (e.g. as a result of $T^{\mu \nu}$ ; $\nu = 0$ ) that the slab must absorb $x$ -momentum $E$ also, and find the relationship between the rate at which energy and $x$ -momentum are absorbed.

## Solution

Solution 18.13. Inside the material we have
$$
\mathbf {T} _ {\mathbf {G W}, \mu} ^ {0 \mu} = \mathbf {T} _ {\mathbf {G W}, \mathbf {i}} ^ {0 \mathbf {i}} = \mathbf {F} ^ {0}
$$
where $\mathbf{F}^0$ is the rate, per unit volume, at which external forces are doing work on the gravitational waves. Isolate a chunk of the cement of cross section A and put a Gaussian "pill box" around it. From Gauss' theorem in three dimensions we have
$$
\int \mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {G W}} ^ {0 \mathrm {i}} \mathrm {d} \Sigma_ {\mathrm {i}} = \int \mathrm {T} _ {, \mathrm {i}} ^ {0 \mathrm {i}} \mathrm {d V o l}.
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \left. \right.\left( \right.\mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {G W}} ^ {0 \mathrm {x}} \left. \right| _ {\text {o u t}} - \mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {G W}} ^ {0 \mathrm {x}} \left. \right| _ {\text {i n}}\left. \right) \mathrm {A} = \int \mathrm {F} ^ {0} \mathrm {d} \text {V o l}. \\ = \text {r a t e} \\ = - \text {r a t e} \\ \end{array}
$$
and hence:
(rate at which momentum is absorbed per unit area per unit time) = (rate at which energy is absorbed per unit area per unit time).
