Problem 9.26. If the Riemann curvature is isotropic, the Riemann curvature tensor can be written as
$$
\mathrm {R} _ {a \beta \gamma \delta} = \mathrm {K} (\mathrm {g} _ {a \gamma} \mathrm {g} _ {\beta \delta} - \mathrm {g} _ {a \delta} \mathrm {g} _ {\beta \gamma}).
$$
Use the Bianchi identities to show (Schur's theorem) that $\mathbf{K}$ must be a constant.

## Solution

Solution 9.26. Since the metric is always covariantly constant
$$
\mathsf {R} _ {a \beta \gamma \delta ; \lambda} = \mathsf {K} _ {, \lambda} (\mathsf {g} _ {a \gamma} \mathsf {g} _ {\beta \delta} - \mathsf {g} _ {a \delta} \mathsf {g} _ {\beta \gamma}) .
$$
Now substitute in the Bianchi identities
$$
0 = \mathrm {R} _ {a} \beta \gamma \delta ; \lambda + \mathrm {R} _ {a} \beta \lambda \gamma ; \delta + \mathrm {R} _ {a} \beta \delta \lambda ; \gamma
$$
and contract on $\alpha, \gamma$ and $\beta, \delta$ to find $K_{,\lambda} = 0$ i.e. $K$ is constant.
