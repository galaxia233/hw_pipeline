Problem 19.14. Show that the two second-order equations of Problem 19.13 are equivalent to the first order equations
$$
\dot {\mathbf {R}} ^ {2} + \mathbf {k} = \frac {8 \pi G}{3} \rho \mathbf {R} ^ {2} \tag {1}
$$
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} \mathrm {R}} (\rho \mathrm {R} ^ {3}) = - 3 \mathrm {p R} ^ {2}. \tag {2}
$$

## Solution

Solution 19.14. Simply eliminating $\overline{\mathbf{R}}$ from the simultaneous equations in Problem 19.13 gives the first equation here. The second follows from this, from the identity $\frac{1}{2} \mathrm{d}[(\dot{\mathbf{R}})^2] / \mathrm{d}\mathbf{R} = \ddot{\mathbf{R}}$ , and from Equation (1) of Problem 19.13:
$$
\frac {1}{2} \frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} \mathrm {R}} \left(\frac {8 \pi \mathrm {G}}{3} \rho \mathrm {R} ^ {2}\right) = \frac {1}{2} \frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} \mathrm {R}} (\dot {\mathrm {R}}) ^ {2} = \ddot {\mathrm {R}} = - \frac {4}{3} \pi \mathrm {G} (\rho + 3 \mathrm {p}) \mathrm {R}
$$
$$
\frac {d}{d R} (\rho R ^ {2}) = - (\rho + 3 p) R
$$
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} R} \left(\rho R ^ {3}\right) = - 3 p R ^ {2}.
$$
