Problem 7.3. Consider the familiar metric space
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = \mathrm {d r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \theta^ {2}.
$$
(c) Using the fact that the metric space is just flat 2-dimensional!
Euclidean space, write down the general equation for a straight line in $\mathbf{r}$ , $\theta$ coordinates, and show that the straight line satisfies the equation in (b).

## Solution

(c) The equation for a straight line is
$$
r = L / \cos (\theta - a)
$$
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}{\mathrm {d} \theta} = \frac {\sin (\theta - \alpha)}{\cos^ {2} (\theta - \alpha)} L
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \left(\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}{\mathrm {d} \theta}\right) ^ {2} + \mathbf {r} ^ {2} = \mathbf {L} ^ {2} \left[ \frac {\sin^ {2} (\theta - \alpha)}{\cos^ {4} (\theta - \alpha)} \right. \\ \left. + \frac {1}{\cos^ {2} (\theta - a)} \right] = \frac {\mathrm {L} ^ {2}}{\cos^ {4} (\theta - a)} = \frac {\mathrm {r} ^ {4}}{\mathrm {L} ^ {2}} . \\ \end {array}
$$

Thus, all straight lines satisfy the geodesic equation.
