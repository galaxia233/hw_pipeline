Problem 8.25. In an arbitrary spacetime manifold (not necessarily homogeneous or isotropic), pick an initial spacelike hypersurface $S_{\mathbf{I}}$ , place an arbitrary coordinate grid $(\mathbf{x}^1, \mathbf{x}^2, \mathbf{x}^3)$ on it, eject geodesic world lines orthogonal to it, and give these world lines the coordinates $(\mathbf{x}^1, \mathbf{x}^2, \mathbf{x}^3) = \text{constant}, \mathbf{x}^0 \equiv t = t_{\mathbf{I}} + \tau$ where $\tau$ is proper time along the world line,
beginning with $\tau = 0$ on $\mathbf{S}_{\mathbf{I}}$ . Show that in this coordinate system ("Gaussian normal coordinates") the metric takes on the synchronous form
$$
d s ^ {2} = - d t ^ {2} + g _ {i j} d x ^ {i} d x ^ {j}.
$$

## Solution

Solution 8.25. In general, $\mathrm{ds}^2 = \mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta}\mathrm{dx}^\alpha \mathrm{dx}^\beta$ . Since $\mathbf{x}^{\mathrm{i}} = \mathrm{constant}$ along any of the geodesics, $\mathrm{ds}^2 = \mathbf{g}_{00}\mathrm{dt}^2$ along any of the geodesics. But along the geodesics $\mathrm{ds}^2 = -\mathrm{d}\tau^2$ , so $\mathbf{g}_{00} = -1$ everywhere. Let $\mathbf{e}_\alpha$ be
the coordinate basis vectors, and let $\mathbf{u} = \mathrm{d} / \mathrm{d}\tau$ be the tangent vector field to the geodesics (i.e. $\mathbf{u} = \mathbf{e}_0$ ). Since $\mathbf{u}$ is orthogonal to $\mathbf{S}_{\mathbf{I}}$ , then $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{e}_i = \mathbf{e}_0 \cdot \mathbf{e}_i = \mathbf{g}_{0\mathbf{i}} = 0$ at $r = 0$ , and
$$
d \left(\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {i}\right) / d \tau = \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \left(\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {e} _ {i}\right) = 0 + \mathbf {u} \cdot \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {e} _ {i} = \mathbf {u} \cdot \nabla_ {\mathbf {e} _ {i}} \mathbf {u}
$$
since the curves are geodesics $(\nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u} = 0)$ and since $\mathbf{e}_{\mathbf{i}}$ and $\mathbf{u}$ form a coordinate basis $([\mathbf{e}_{\mathbf{i}},\mathbf{u}] = 0)$ . This last result vanishes
$$
\mathbf {u} \cdot \nabla_ {\mathbf {e} _ {\mathbf {i}}} \mathbf {u} = \frac {1}{2} \nabla_ {\mathbf {e} _ {\mathbf {i}}} (\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {u}) = 0
$$
and therefore $\mathbf{u}\cdot \mathbf{e}_{\mathbf{i}} = \mathbf{g}_{0\mathbf{i}} = 0$ everywhere.
