Problem 7.19. Show that Fermi-Walker transport along a geodesic curve is the same as parallel transport.

## Solution

Solution 7.19. Fermi-Walker transport has the differential equation
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {x} = (\mathbf {u} \otimes \mathbf {a} - \mathbf {a} \otimes \mathbf {u}) \cdot \mathbf {x},
$$
where $\mathbf{u}$ is the tangent vector to the curve and $\mathbf{a} = \nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u} \equiv \mathrm{D}\mathbf{u} / \mathrm{d}\tau$ . If the curve is a geodesic, it satisfies the geodesic equation $\nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u} = 0$ , so the Fermi-Walker transport law becomes
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {x} = 0,
$$
which is just the parallel transport equation.
