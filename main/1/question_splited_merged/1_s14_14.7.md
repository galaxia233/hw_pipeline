Problem 14.7. If $\mathbf{u}$ is the 4-velocity of a perfect gas undergoing adiabatic stationary flow in a stationary gravitational field, prove (the relativistic Bernoulli equation) that along the flow lines
$$
u _ {0} = \text {c o n s t a n t} \times n / (\rho + p),
$$
where $\mathfrak{n} =$ baryon number density.

## Solution

Solution 14.7. Since the gravitational field is stationary, there is a time-Killing vector $\pmb{\xi} = \partial/\partial t$ . Dot this into the Euler equation (Problem 14.3) for the flow of a perfect gas:
$$
(\rho + \mathrm {p}) \boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u} = - \boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \nabla \mathrm {p} - \boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \mathbf {u} \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {p}. \tag {1}
$$
But
$$
\boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u} = \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} (\boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \mathbf {u}) - (\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \boldsymbol {\xi}) \cdot \mathbf {u} = \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} (\boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \mathbf {u})
$$
since by Killing's equation $\nabla \pmb{\xi}$ is antisymmetric. Also, $\pmb{\xi} \cdot \nabla \mathbf{p} = \partial \mathbf{p} / \partial t = 0$ for stationary flow. Putting $\mathbf{u}_0 = \mathbf{u} \cdot \pmb{\xi}$ , we find $(\rho + \mathfrak{p}) \mathrm{d}\mathbf{u}_0 / \mathrm{d}\tau = \mathbf{u}_0 \mathrm{d}\mathbf{p} / \mathrm{d}\tau$ [Equation 2]. For adiabatic flow of a perfect fluid, the first law of thermodynamics (Problem 5.19) is $\mathrm{d}\rho = (\rho + \mathfrak{p}) \mathrm{d}\mathfrak{n} / \mathfrak{n}$ . [Equation 3]. From Equations (2) and (3)
$$
\frac {d u _ {0}}{u _ {0}} = \frac {d n}{n} - \frac {d (\rho + p)}{\rho + p}
$$
and hence $\mathbf{u}_0 = \text{constant} \times \frac{\mathbf{n}}{\rho + \mathbf{p}}$ .
