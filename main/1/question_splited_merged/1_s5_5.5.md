Problem 5.5. A rod has cross sectional area A and mass per unit length $\mu$ . Write down the stress-energy tensor inside the rod when the rod is under a tension F. (Assume that the tension is uniformly distributed over the cross section.)

## Solution

Solution 5.5. We are given the linear mass density as $\mu$ , so $\mathbf{T}^{00} = \mu / \mathbf{A}$ . In the rest frame of the rod there is no energy flow so $\mathbf{T}^{0i} = 0$ . Choose the $z$ direction to be along the rod axis. By symmetry [see the argument in Problem 5.4] the off-diagonal stresses $\mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{xy}}$ , $\mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{xz}}$ , $\mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{yz}}$ , must be zero. Since the tension is in the $z$ -direction, $\mathbf{T}^{zz} = -\mathbf{F} / \mathbf{A}$ . Since there is no applied stress in the $x$ or $y$ directions (therefore no transport of $x$ or $y$ momentum) $\mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{xx}} = \mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{yy}} = 0$ .
