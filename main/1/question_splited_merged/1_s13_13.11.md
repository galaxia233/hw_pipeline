Problem 13.11. For the "nearly Newtonian" metric
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = - (1 + 2 \Phi) \mathrm {d t} ^ {2} + (1 - 2 \Phi) \delta_ {\mathrm {j k}} \mathrm {d x} ^ {\mathrm {j}} \mathrm {d x} ^ {\mathrm {k}}
$$
calculate, to lowest nonvanishing order in $\Phi$ , the components of the Landau-Lifschitz stress-energy pseudotensor $t_{\mathbf{L}\cdot \mathbf{L}}^{\alpha \beta}$ (Landau and Lifschitz p. 306). Assume that the field is changing so slowly in time that time derivatives of $\Phi$ can be neglected compared to spatial derivatives.

## Solution

Solution 13.11. The components of the Landau-Lifschitz pseudotensor are given by the rather awesome expression
$$
\begin{array}{l} (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {a \beta} _ {\mathrm {L} - \mathrm {L}} = (1 6 \pi) ^ {- 1} \left[ g ^ {a \beta}, _ {\lambda} g ^ {\lambda \mu}, _ {\mu} - g ^ {a \lambda}, _ {\lambda} g ^ {\beta \mu}, _ {\mu} + \frac {1}{2} g ^ {a \beta} g _ {\lambda \mu} ^ {\lambda \nu}, _ {\rho} g ^ {\rho \mu}, _ {\nu} \right. \\ - \mathrm {g} ^ {\alpha \lambda} \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu} \mathfrak {g} ^ {\beta \nu} _ {, \rho} \mathfrak {g} ^ {\mu \rho} _ {, \lambda} - \mathrm {g} ^ {\beta \lambda} \mathrm {g} _ {\mu \nu} \mathfrak {g} ^ {a \nu} _ {, \rho} \mathfrak {g} ^ {\mu \rho} _ {, \lambda} + \underline {{\mathrm {g} _ {\lambda \mu} \mathrm {g} ^ {\nu \rho} _ {\mathfrak {g}} ^ {a \lambda} _ {, \nu} \mathfrak {g} ^ {\beta \mu}}} _ {, \rho} \\ \left. + \frac {1}{8} \left(2 \mathrm {g} ^ {\alpha \lambda} \mathrm {g} ^ {\beta \mu} - \mathrm {g} ^ {\alpha \beta} \mathrm {g} ^ {\lambda \mu}\right) \left(2 \mathrm {g} _ {\nu \rho} \mathrm {g} _ {\sigma \tau} - \mathrm {g} _ {\sigma \rho} \mathrm {g} _ {\nu \tau}\right) \mathfrak {g} ^ {\nu \tau} _ {, \lambda} \mathfrak {g} ^ {\rho \sigma} _ {, \mu} \right] \\ \end{array}
$$
where
$$
g ^ {a \beta} \equiv (- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} g ^ {a \beta}.
$$
For the metric we are considering
$$
g ^ {0 0} = - (1 - 2 \phi) ^ {\frac {3}{2}} (1 + 2 \Phi) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}}, \quad g ^ {i j} = (1 - 4 \Phi^ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \delta^ {i j}, \quad g ^ {0 i} = 0.
$$
Notice that all terms in the expression for $t^{a\beta}$ involve the product of the derivatives of two $g$ 's, so to lowest order $t^{a\beta}$ will go as the product of the first derivative of $\Phi$ . We need to keep therefore, only the terms proportioned to $\Phi_{,i}$ when we calculate the derivatives of the $g$ 's:
$$
{ } ^ { \mathbf { g } } _ { , \mathrm { i } } ^ { 0 0 } \approx 4 \phi _ { , \mathrm { i } } \qquad \mathrm { g } _ { , \mathrm { k } } ^ { \mathrm { i j } } \approx - 4 \phi \phi _ { , \mathrm { k } } \delta ^ { \mathrm { i j } } \approx 0 .
$$
If we keep only the $g_{,i}^{00}$ type terms, only the underlined terms in the expression for $t_{\mathbf{L} - \mathbf{L}}^{\alpha \beta}$ survive and we find
$$
t _ {L - L} ^ {\alpha \beta} = (1 6 \pi) ^ {- 1} \left[ - g _ {, i} ^ {i j} g ^ {\alpha 0} g ^ {\beta 0} g _ {, j} + \frac {1}{8} \left(2 g ^ {\alpha 1} g ^ {\beta m} - g ^ {\alpha \beta} g _ {, j} ^ {l m}\right) g _ {, 1} ^ {0 0} g _ {, m} ^ {0 0} \right].
$$
From this, with $\mathbf{g}^{\alpha \beta} \approx \eta^{\alpha \beta}$ and $\mathfrak{g}_{,i}^{00} = 4\phi_{,i}$ we easily find
$$
\begin{array}{l} t _ {L - L} ^ {0 0} = (1 6 \pi) ^ {- 1} \left[ - \delta^ {i j} g _ {, i} ^ {0 0} g _ {, j} ^ {0 0} + \frac {1}{8} \delta^ {1 m} g _ {, 1} ^ {0 0} g _ {, m} ^ {0 0} \right] = \frac {- 7}{8 \pi} \delta^ {i j} \Phi_ {, i} \Phi_ {, j} \\ t _ {\mathbf {L} - \mathbf {L}} ^ {0 \mathrm {i}} = 0 \\ \end{array}
$$
$$
t _ {\mathrm {L} - \mathrm {L}} ^ {\mathrm {i j}} = (1 6 \pi) ^ {- 1} \left[ \frac {1}{8} \left(2 \delta^ {\mathrm {i l}} \delta^ {\mathrm {j m}} - \delta^ {\mathrm {i j}} \delta^ {\mathrm {l m}} g _ {, 1 g} ^ {0 0}, _ {\mathrm {m}} ^ {0 0} \right] \right. = (4 \pi) ^ {- 1} \left(\Phi_ {, \mathrm {i}} \Phi_ {, \mathrm {j}} - \frac {1}{2} \delta_ {\mathrm {i j}} \Phi_ {, \mathrm {m}} \Phi^ {\mathrm {, m}}\right).
$$
Note that the spatial components $t_{\mathbf{L} - \mathbf{L}}^{\mathbf{ij}}$ agree with the stress tensor given in Problem 12.8.
