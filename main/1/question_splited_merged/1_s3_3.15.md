Problem 3.15. Let $\mathbf{A}_{\mu \nu}$ be an antisymmetric tensor so that $\mathbf{A}_{\mu \nu} = -\mathbf{A}_{\nu \mu}$ ; and let $\mathbf{S}^{\mu \nu}$ be a symmetric tensor so that $\mathbf{S}^{\mu \nu} = \mathbf{S}^{\nu \mu}$ . Show that $\mathbf{A}_{\mu \nu} \mathbf{S}^{\mu \nu} = 0$ . Establish the following two identities for any arbitrary tensor $\mathbf{V}_{\mu \nu}$ :
$$
\mathrm {V} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {A} _ {\mu \nu} = \frac {1}{2} \left(\mathrm {V} ^ {\mu \nu} - \mathrm {V} ^ {\nu \mu}\right) \mathrm {A} _ {\mu \nu}, \qquad \mathrm {V} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {S} _ {\mu \nu} = \frac {1}{2} \left(\mathrm {V} ^ {\mu \nu} + \mathrm {V} ^ {\nu \mu}\right) \mathrm {S} _ {\mu \nu}.
$$

## Solution

Solution 3.15. By the given symmetries $\mathbf{A}_{\mu \nu} \mathbf{S}^{\mu \nu} = -\mathbf{A}_{\nu \mu} \mathbf{S}^{\nu \mu}$ , but $\mu, \nu$ are dummy indices so we can interchange $\mu$ and $\nu$ to get
$$
\mathbf {A} _ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {S} ^ {\mu \nu} = - \mathbf {A} _ {\nu \mu} \mathbf {S} ^ {\mu \nu} = - \mathbf {A} _ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {S} ^ {\mu \nu}.
$$
Thus $\mathbf{A}_{\mu \nu} \mathbf{S}^{\mu \nu} = 0$ .
Any tensor $\mathbf{V}_{\mu \nu}$ can be written as a sum of its symmetric part $\tilde{\mathbf{V}}_{\mu \nu} \equiv \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{V}_{\mu \nu} + \mathbf{V}_{\nu \mu})$ and its antisymmetric part $\tilde{\mathbf{V}}_{\mu \nu} \equiv \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{V}_{\mu \nu} - \mathbf{V}_{\nu \mu})$ , thus
$$
\mathbf {V} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {A} _ {\mu \nu} = \tilde {\mathbf {V}} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {A} _ {\mu \nu} + \tilde {\bar {\mathbf {V}}} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {A} _ {\mu \nu} = \tilde {\bar {\mathbf {V}}} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {A} _ {\mu \nu} = \frac {1}{2} (\mathbf {V} ^ {\mu \nu} - \mathbf {V} ^ {\nu \mu}) \mathbf {A} _ {\mu \nu}
$$
$$
\mathrm {V} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {S} _ {\mu \nu} = \tilde {\mathrm {V}} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {S} _ {\mu \nu} + \tilde {\bar {\mathrm {V}}} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {S} _ {\mu \nu} = \tilde {\mathrm {V}} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {S} _ {\mu \nu} = \frac {1}{2} (\mathrm {V} ^ {\mu \nu} + \mathrm {V} ^ {\nu \mu}) \mathrm {S} _ {\mu \nu}.
$$
