Problem 16.9. Express the Chandrasekhar and Oppenheimer-Volkoff upper mass limits (for white dwarfs and neutron stars, respectively) as dimensional combinations of fundamental constants and the mass of the nucleon and electron. Similarly express the limiting radii corresponding to these mass limits.

## Solution

Solution 16.9. Increasing the mass of a degenerate star makes the material more compact until the Fermi energy of the gas (electron gas in case of white dwarf; neutron gas in the case of neutron star) rises to a relativistic level. If A is the number of baryons in the star, and R is the radius of the star, then the Fermi energy is approximately (in the relativistic regime)
$$
E _ {F} \sim \frac {\pi c}{R} A ^ {\frac {1}{3}} \tag {1}
$$
(In this equation we have ignored such details as the fact that there are twice as many pressure supporting fermions per baryon in a neutron star as in a white dwarf.) The gravitational mass-energy per fermion is approximately
$$
E _ {G} \sim - \frac {G A m _ {B} ^ {2}}{R} \tag {2}
$$
where $\mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{B}}$ is the mass of a baryon.
Note that both the compressional and gravitational energy depend in the same way upon R. The decisive question is therefore the sign of the total coefficient of $\mathbf{R}^{-1}$ . When it is positive, R will increase, the Fermi energy will become nonrelativistic, the energy of compression will then fall faster than $\mathbf{R}^{-1}$ and a stable equilibrium will be achieved at some finite R. When the coefficient is negative, collapse will set in and continue. The critical number of baryons at which the star becomes unstable is thus obtained by equating $\mathbf{E}_{\mathbf{G}}$ and $\mathbf{E}_{\mathbf{F}}$ :
$$
\mathrm {A} _ {\text {c r i t .}} = \left(\frac {\hbar c}{G m _ {B} ^ {2}}\right) ^ {\frac {3}{2}} \approx 1 0 ^ {5 7}. \tag {3}
$$
Equation (3) corresponds to a critical limiting mass of
$$
\mathrm {M} _ {\text {c r i t .}} = \mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {B}} \mathrm {A} _ {\text {c r i t .}} \approx 2 \mathrm {M} _ {\odot} \tag {4}
$$
and limiting radii of
$$
\begin{array}{l} R _ {c r i t.} \sim \bar {n c} A _ {c r i t.} ^ {\frac {1}{3}} \times \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} \left(m _ {e} c ^ {2}\right) ^ {- 1} & \text {f o r w h i t e d w a r f} \\ \left(m _ {B} c ^ {2}\right) ^ {- 1} & \text {f o r n e u t r o n s t a r} \end{array} \right. \\ \approx \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} 5 \times 1 0 ^ {8} \mathrm {c m} & \text {f o r w h i t e d w a r f} \\ 2. 7 \times 1 0 ^ {5} \mathrm {c m} & \text {f o r n e u t r o n s t a r} \end{array} \right. \\ \end{array}
$$
