Problem 6.3. The metric for the surface of a globe of the Earth is
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = \mathrm {a} ^ {2} (\mathrm {d} \lambda^ {2} + \cos^ {2} \lambda \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2})
$$
where $\lambda$ is the latitude and $\phi$ is the longitude. The metric of a flat map of the world, with Cartesian coordinates $x$ and $y$ is $\mathrm{ds}^2 = \mathrm{dx}^2 + \mathrm{dy}^2$ ; however we are not interested in this geometry, but in that of the globe it represents. What is the metric of the globe expressed in $x$ and $y$ coordinates for (b) a stereographic projection map of the world?

## Solution

(b) For the stereographic projection, it is slightly easier to use the usual polar angle $\theta = 90^{\circ} - \lambda$ . Let $(\theta, \phi)$ be the coordinates of a point on the sphere, and $(\theta_{0}, \phi_{0})$ the spherical polar coordinates of the projected point. Clearly $\phi_{0} = \phi$ . The distance of the projected point from the axis is $\rho = 2a \tan(\theta / 2)$ , so we introduce coordinates
![](images/7305e7542c6b95d7fc175fd2800ac570a00340526e130e9ee3658dd1784149e1.jpg)
$$
\mathbf {x} = \rho \cos \phi = 2 a \tan \left(\frac {1}{2} \theta\right) \cos \phi
$$
$$
y = \rho \sin \phi = 2 a \tan \left(\frac {1}{2} \theta\right) \sin \phi
$$
and it is easily verified that
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = \mathrm {a} ^ {2} (\mathrm {d} \theta^ {2} + \sin^ {2} \theta \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2}) = \cos^ {4} \left(\frac {1}{2} \theta\right) (\mathrm {d x} ^ {2} + \mathrm {d y} ^ {2}).
$$
Here the distortion is least near $\theta = 0$ , the north pole. This projection is said to be conformal, because $(\mathrm{ds}^2)_{\mathrm{sphere}} = \mathbf{g}(\mathrm{ds}^2)_{\mathrm{map}}$ , where $\mathbf{g}$ is some function, in this case equal to $\cos^4 (\theta /2)$ . A conformal projection preserves angles (see Problem 6.7).
