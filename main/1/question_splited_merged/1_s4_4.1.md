Problem 4.1. Find the magnetic field $\underline{\mathsf{B}}$ from a current I in an infinitely long straight wire, by appropriate Lorentz transformations and superpositions of the electric field of an infinitely long straight charge distribution.

## Solution

Solution 4.1. Suppose the wire lies on the $z$ -axis of cylindrical polar coordinates and has proper charge density $\rho_0$ . In the rest frame of the wire $\mathbf{J}^0 = \rho_0$ and $\underline{\mathbf{J}} = 0$ , and from Gauss' law the only nonvanishing component of the field tensor is $\mathbf{E}^{\mathrm{r}} = \rho_0\mathbf{A} / 2\pi \mathbf{r} = \mathbf{F}^{0\mathrm{r}}$ , where $\mathbf{A}$ is the cross sectional area of the wire. If the wire is moving in the $+z$ direction with velocity $\beta$ relative to the lab, the Lorentz transformation $\Lambda_{0}^{0'} = \gamma$ , $\Lambda_{z}^{0'} = \beta y$ gives
$$
J ^ {0 ^ {\prime}} = \gamma \rho _ {0} \quad J ^ {z ^ {\prime}} = \beta \gamma \rho _ {0}
$$
$$
\mathbf {B} ^ { \hat { \phi } } = \mathbf {F} ^ {z ^ { \prime } \mathbf {r } ^ { \prime }} = \boldsymbol { \Lambda } _ {^ { \prime } 0} ^ {z ^ { \prime }} \mathbf {F} ^ {0 \mathbf {r}} = \beta \gamma \rho _ {0} \mathbf {A} / 2 \pi \mathbf {r } \qquad \mathbf {E} ^ { \mathbf {r } ^ { \prime }} = \gamma \rho _ {0} \mathbf {A} / 2 \pi \mathbf {r } .
$$
$(\mathbf{B}^{\hat{\phi}}$ is the "physical component," i.e. the component related to the unit vector $\mathbf{e}_{\phi}$ .) If we now superpose the field of a similar wire of charge density $-\gamma \rho_{0}$ , at rest in the lab, the total charge density and $\underline{\mathbf{E}}$ field both cancel. The current $\mathrm{I} = \int \mathrm{J}^{\mathbf{z}} \mathrm{d}\mathbf{x} \mathrm{d}\mathbf{y} = \beta \gamma \rho_{0} \mathbf{A}$ produces only a B field $\mathbf{B}^{\hat{\phi}} = \mathrm{I} / 2\pi \mathrm{r}$ .
