15.17. In the preceding problem (15.16) show that the set of coordinate-stationary observers are all in free fall and have zero energy (i.e. they fell in from infinity with zero initial velocity).

## Solution

Solution 15.17. A coordinate-stationary observer in Problem 15.16 satisfies $z =$ constant (see solution to 15.16). By Equation (5) of Solution 15.16, this implies
$$
\mathrm {d t} = - \mathrm {F} ^ {\prime} \mathrm {d r} = - \left(\frac {\mathrm {r}}{2 \mathrm {M}}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {- 1} \mathrm {d r} \tag {1}
$$
according to Equation (7) of 15.16. From this equation we get
$$
\left(\mathrm {d r} / \mathrm {d t}\right) ^ {2} = (2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {2}. \tag {2}
$$
Comparison of Equation (2) with Equation (3) of Solution 15.3 (with $\mathfrak{u}_0 = 1$ for a particle falling from rest at infinity) shows that Equation (2) represents a radially falling particle, with unit energy-to-rest-mass ratio at infinity.
