Problem 7.2. For a 2-dimensional flat, Euclidean space described by polar coordinates $\mathbf{r}$ , $\theta$ , assume that the geodesics are the usual straight lines.
(b) Next, in the coordinates $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y}$ which are related to $\mathbf{r}, \theta$ in the usual way, take the covariant structure to be given by $\Gamma_{\mathbf{x}\mathbf{x}}^{\mathbf{x}} = \Gamma_{\mathbf{x}\mathbf{y}}^{\mathbf{x}} = \dots = 0$ . Using the transformation law for connection coefficients find the connection coefficients in the $\mathbf{r}, \theta$ coordinates.

## Solution

(b) From $r^2 = x^2 + y^2$ , and $\cot \theta = x / y$ we get a transformation matrix
$$
\mu = \mathbf {x} \quad \mu = \mathbf {y}
$$
$$
L _ {\mu} ^ {\alpha^ {\prime}} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \cos \theta & \sin \theta \\ - \frac {\sin \theta}{r} & \frac {\cos \theta}{r} \end{array} \right]
$$
and its inverse
$$
\beta^ {\prime} = \mathbf {r} \quad \beta^ {\prime} = \theta
$$
$$
L ^ {\mu} _ {\beta^ {\prime}} = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} \cos \theta & - r \sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & r \cos \theta \end{array} \right]
$$
(Example: $\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{r}\cos \theta \Rightarrow \mathrm{d}\mathbf{x} = \mathrm{d}\mathbf{r}\cos \theta -\mathbf{r}\sin \theta \mathrm{d}\theta \Rightarrow$ first row above.)
The transformation law for the $\Gamma$ 's (Problem 7.1) is
$$
\Gamma_ {\beta^ {\prime} \gamma^ {\prime}} ^ {a ^ {\prime}} = \mathrm {L} _ {\rho} ^ {a ^ {\prime}} \mathrm {L} _ {\beta} ^ {\mu} \mathrm {L} _ {\gamma^ {\prime}} ^ {\nu} \Gamma_ {\mu \nu} ^ {\rho} + \mathrm {L} _ {\mu} ^ {a ^ {\prime}} \mathrm {L} _ {\beta^ {\prime}, \gamma^ {\prime}} ^ {\mu}.
$$
Since $\Gamma_{\mu \nu}^{\rho} = 0$ in cartesian coordinates, only the second term contributes, and straightforward differentiation and matrix multiplication gives the desired results.
