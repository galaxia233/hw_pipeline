Problem 19.16. For a Friedmann cosmology what equations relating $\mathfrak{p}$ , $\rho$ , and $\mathbf{R}(t)$ result from the equation of energy conservation, $\mathbf{T}_{\mu ;\nu}^{\nu} = 0$ ?

## Solution

Solution 19.16. Using $\mathbf{T}_0^0 = -\rho$ , and $\mathbf{T}_{\mathbf{j}}^{\mathbf{j}}$ (no sum) = $\mathbf{p}$ , and $\mathbf{p}_{,\mathbf{j}} = 0$ (homogeneity) the $\mu = \mathbf{j}$ component is
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = \mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {j}, \nu} ^ {\mathrm {j}} = \mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {j}, \nu} ^ {\mathrm {v}} - \mathrm {T} _ {\alpha} ^ {\nu} \mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {j} \nu} ^ {\alpha} + \mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {j}} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {T} _ {\alpha \nu} ^ {\nu} \\ = p, j + \rho \Gamma_ {j 0} ^ {0} - p \Gamma_ {j k} ^ {k} + p \Gamma_ {j \nu} ^ {\nu} = (\rho + p) \Gamma_ {j 0} ^ {0} = 0 \\ \end{array}
$$
which is a tautology. The $\mu = 0$ component gives
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = \mathrm {T} _ {0; \nu} ^ {\nu} = \mathrm {T} _ {0, \nu} ^ {\nu} - \mathrm {T} _ {a} ^ {\nu} \Gamma_ {0 \nu} ^ {a} + \mathrm {T} _ {0} ^ {a} \Gamma_ {a \nu} ^ {\nu} \\ = - \frac {\mathrm {d} \rho}{\mathrm {d} t} + \rho \Gamma_ {0 0} ^ {0} - (\rho + p) \left(\log | g ^ {\frac {1}{2}}\right), _ {0} \\ \end{array}
$$
where $\Gamma_{\alpha \beta}^{\alpha} = (\log |g|^{\frac{1}{2}}),\beta$ has been used (see Problem 7.7). Thus
$$
\mathrm {d} \rho / \mathrm {d t} = - \frac {(\rho + \mathrm {p})}{\mathrm {R} ^ {3}} \frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d t}} (\mathrm {R} ^ {3})
$$
and
$$
\mathrm {d} (\rho \mathrm {R} ^ {3}) = - 3 \mathrm {p R} ^ {2} \mathrm {d R}
$$
which we recognize as one of the first-order Friedmann equations (see Problem 19.14).
