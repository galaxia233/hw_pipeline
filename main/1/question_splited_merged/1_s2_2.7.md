Problem 2.7. A particle of rest mass $m$ collides elastically with a stationary particle of equal mass. The incident particle has kinetic energy $T_0$ . What is its kinetic energy after the collision, if the scattering angle is $\theta$ ?

## Solution

Solution 2.7. Let subscripts 0 and 1 denote respectively the initially moving, and the initially stationary particle. Balancing 4-momentum before and after the collision, we have
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {P} _ {0} + \mathbf {P} _ {1} = \mathbf {P} _ {0 ^ {\prime}} + \mathbf {P} _ {1 ^ {\prime}} \\ (\mathbf {P} _ {0} + \mathbf {P} _ {1} - \mathbf {P} _ {0 ^ {\prime}}) ^ {2} = \mathbf {P} _ {1} ^ {2}, \\ - 3 \mathrm {m} ^ {2} + 2 \mathbf {P} _ {0} \cdot \mathbf {P} _ {1} - 2 \mathbf {P} _ {0 ^ {\prime}} \cdot (\mathbf {P} _ {0} + \mathbf {P} _ {1}) = - \mathrm {m} ^ {2}. \\ \end{array}
$$
Now we put in
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {P} _ {0} = (\mathrm {E}, \underline {{\mathbf {P}}}), \quad \mathrm {E} = \mathrm {m} + \mathrm {T} _ {0} \\ \mathbf {P} _ {1} = (\mathrm {m}, \underline {{0}}), \quad \mathbf {E} ^ {\prime} = \mathrm {m} + \mathbf {T} ^ {\prime} \\ \mathbf {P} _ {0 ^ {\prime}} = \left(\mathrm {E} ^ {\prime}, \underline {{\mathrm {P}}} ^ {\prime}\right), \quad \underline {{\mathrm {P}}} \cdot \underline {{\mathrm {P}}} ^ {\prime} = \mathrm {P P} ^ {\prime} \cos \theta , \\ \end{array}
$$
to find
$$
- \mathrm {m} ^ {2} - \mathrm {E m} + \mathrm {E} ^ {\prime} (\mathrm {E} + \mathrm {m}) - \mathrm {P P} ^ {\prime} \cos \theta = 0
$$
i.e.
$$
\left(E ^ {2} - m ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \left(E ^ {\prime 2} - m ^ {2}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \cos \theta = \left(E ^ {\prime} - m\right) (E + m).
$$
Squaring and factoring the difference of squares, then gives the kinetic energy of the scattered particle:
$$
\begin{array}{l} (\mathrm {E} - \mathrm {m}) (\mathrm {E} ^ {\prime} + \mathrm {m}) \cos^ {2} \theta = (\mathrm {E} ^ {\prime} - \mathrm {m}) (\mathrm {E} + \mathrm {m}) \\ \mathrm {T} _ {0} \left(\mathrm {T} ^ {\prime} + 2 \mathrm {m}\right) \cos^ {2} \theta = \mathrm {T} ^ {\prime} \left(\mathrm {T} _ {0} + 2 \mathrm {m}\right) \\ \mathrm {T} ^ {\prime} \left(- \mathrm {T} _ {0} \cos^ {2} \theta + \mathrm {T} _ {0} + 2 \mathrm {m}\right) = 2 \mathrm {m} \mathrm {T} _ {0} \cos^ {2} \theta \\ T ^ {\prime} = \frac {2 m T _ {0} \cos^ {2} \theta}{2 m + T _ {0} \sin^ {2} \theta}. \\ \end{array}
$$
