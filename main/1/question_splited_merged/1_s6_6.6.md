Problem 6.6. Show that a contraction of a vector $\mathbf{V}$ with the “projection tensor” $\mathbf{P} \equiv \mathbf{g} + \mathbf{u} \otimes \mathbf{u}$ projects $\mathbf{V}$ into the 3-surface orthogonal to the 4-velocity vector $\mathbf{u}$ . If $\mathbf{n}$ is a unit spacelike vector show that
$$
\mathbf {P} \equiv \mathbf {g} - \mathbf {n} \otimes \mathbf {n}
$$
is the corresponding projection operator. Show that there is no unique projection operator orthogonal to a null vector.

## Solution

Solution 6.6. Let $\mathbf{A}$ be an arbitrary vector. We want to see if $\mathbf{P} \cdot \mathbf{A}$ is orthogonal to $\mathbf{u}$ , i.e. if $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{P} \cdot \mathbf{A} = 0$ :
$$
\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {P} \cdot \mathbf {A} = \mathbf {u} ^ {\alpha} (\mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} + \mathrm {u} _ {\alpha} \mathrm {u} _ {\beta}) \mathbf {A} ^ {\beta} = \mathbf {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {A} _ {\alpha} + (\mathrm {u} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {u} _ {\alpha}) \mathrm {u} _ {\beta} \mathbf {A} ^ {\beta} = \mathrm {u} _ {\alpha} \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha} - \mathrm {u} _ {\beta} \mathbf {A} ^ {\beta} = 0.
$$
Also it is easy to see that if $\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0$ , then $\mathbf{P} \cdot \mathbf{A} = \mathbf{A}$ , so $\mathbf{A}$ is unaffected by projection. If $\mathbf{n}$ is a unit spacelike vector, we have
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {n} \cdot \mathbf {P} \cdot \mathbf {A} = \mathfrak {n} ^ {\alpha} \left(\mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} - \mathrm {n} _ {\alpha} \mathrm {n} _ {\beta}\right) \mathbf {A} ^ {\beta} \\ = \mathrm {n} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {A} _ {\alpha} - \left(\mathrm {n} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {n} _ {\alpha}\right) \mathrm {n} _ {\beta} \mathrm {A} ^ {\beta} \\ = \mathrm {n} _ {\alpha} \mathrm {A} ^ {\alpha} - \mathrm {n} _ {\beta} \mathrm {A} ^ {\beta} = 0. \\ \end{array}
$$
Likewise a vector already orthogonal to $\mathbf{n}$ is unaffected by projection. Suppose now that $\mathbf{P}$ is a projection operator orthogonal to a null vector $\mathbf{k}$ , so that $\mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{P} \cdot \mathbf{A} = 0$ for all $\mathbf{A}$ . It is easy to see that $\mathbf{P} + \text{constant} \times \mathbf{k} \otimes \mathbf{k}$ is also a projection operator since $\mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{k} = 0$ ; so $\mathbf{P}$ is not unique. (The set of null projection operators is not empty since it is easy to
check that for any 4-vector $\mathbf{w}$ , one is $\mathbf{P} = \mathbf{g} - \mathbf{w} \otimes \mathbf{k} / \mathbf{k} \cdot \mathbf{w}$ . However, there are no symmetric null projection operators at all.)
