Problem 8.24. If $\mathbf{F}_{\mu \nu} = \mathbf{A}_{\nu ;\mu} - \mathbf{A}_{\mu ;\nu},$ prove that $\mathbf{F}_{[\mu \nu ;\lambda]} = 0$

## Solution

Solution 8.24. If forms and exterior differentiation are used, this problem is trivial. The first equation gives $\mathbf{F} = \mathrm{d}\tilde{\mathbf{A}}$ so, $\mathrm{d}\mathbf{F} = \mathrm{d}\mathbf{d}\tilde{\mathbf{A}} = 0$ , since $\mathrm{d}\mathbf{d}$ is always zero. $\mathbf{F}_{[\mu \nu ;\lambda ]}$ is exactly the components of $\mathrm{d}\mathbf{F}$ , so we are done. The trick is that the total antisymmetrization implicit in the $\mathbf{d}$ 's keeps any connection coefficient from entering the problem. If components are used, the proof is straightforward but more tedious.
