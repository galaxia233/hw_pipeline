Problem 9.29. What is the extrinsic curvature of the $\tau =$ constant slice of the metric, $\mathrm{ds}^2 = -\mathrm{d}\tau^2 + \mathrm{a}^2(\tau) [\gamma_{ij}(\mathbf{x}^k) \mathrm{dx}^i \mathrm{dx}^j]$ ?

## Solution

Solution 9.29. The metric has the form
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = - \mathrm {d} \tau^ {2} + \mathrm {g} _ {\mathrm {i j}} \mathrm {d x} ^ {\mathrm {i}} \mathrm {d x} ^ {\mathrm {j}}
$$
where
$$
\mathbf {g} _ {\mathbf {i j}} = \mathsf {a} ^ {2} (\boldsymbol {\tau}) \gamma_ {\mathbf {i j}} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\mathbf {k}}) .
$$
The normal vector to the $\tau =$ constant surfaces is $\mathbf{n} = \partial /\partial \tau$ . Thus
$$
\mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {i j}} = - \mathbf {e} _ {\mathrm {j}} \cdot \nabla_ {\mathrm {i}} \mathbf {n} = \mathbf {n} \cdot \nabla_ {\mathrm {i}} \mathbf {e} _ {\mathrm {j}} = \Gamma_ {\mathrm {n j i}} = - \frac {1}{2} \mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {i j}, \mathrm {n}} = \frac {- \mathrm {a} , r}{\mathrm {a}} \mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {i j}}.
$$
