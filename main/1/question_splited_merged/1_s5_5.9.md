Problem 5.9. Consider a system of discrete particles of charge $\mathbf{q}_{\mathbf{i}}$ and mass $\mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{i}}$ interacting through electromagnetic forces. From the explicit expression for $\mathrm{T}^{\mu \nu}$ of the particles show that the total $\mathrm{T}^{\mu \nu}$ (particles plus field) is conserved, i.e. that $\mathrm{T}^{\mu \nu}_{,\nu} = 0$ .

## Solution

Solution 5.9. For simplicity assume Minkowski coordinates. The stress energy for the particles is
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {\mathbf {P}} ^ {\mu \nu} (\mathbf {x}) = \sum_ {\mathbf {i}} \mathsf {m} _ {\mathbf {i}} \int \mathsf {u} _ {\mathbf {i}} ^ {\mu} (\boldsymbol {\tau} _ {\mathbf {i}}) \mathsf {u} _ {\mathbf {i}} ^ {\nu} (\boldsymbol {\tau} _ {\mathbf {i}}) \delta^ {4} [ \mathbf {x} - \mathbf {x} _ {\mathbf {i}} (\boldsymbol {\tau} _ {\mathbf {i}}) ] \mathrm {d} \boldsymbol {\tau} _ {\mathbf {i}}.
$$
Thus
$$
\mathbf {T} _ {\textbf {P}, \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = \sum_ {\textbf {i}} \mathbf {m} _ {\textbf {i}} \int \mathbf {u} _ {\textbf {i}} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {u} _ {\textbf {i}} ^ {\nu} (\partial / \partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\nu}) \delta^ {4} [ \mathbf {x} - \mathbf {x} _ {\textbf {i}} (\boldsymbol {\tau} _ {\mathbf {i}}) ] \mathrm {d} \boldsymbol {\tau} _ {\mathbf {i}}.
$$
The delta function depends only on $\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{x}_{\mathbf{i}}$ so we can replace $\partial/\partial \mathbf{x}^{\nu}$ by $-\partial/\partial \mathbf{x}_{\mathbf{i}}^{\nu}$ . Since $\mathbf{u}_{\mathbf{i}}^{\nu}\partial/\partial \mathbf{x}_{\mathbf{i}}^{\nu} = \mathbf{d}/\mathrm{d}\tau_{\mathbf{i}}$ we have then
$$
\begin{array}{l} T _ {P, \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = - \sum_ {i} m _ {i} \int u _ {i} ^ { \mu } (d / d r _ {i}) \delta^ {4} [ x - x _ {i} (r _ {i}) ] d r _ {i} \\ = \sum_ {i} m _ {i} \int \left(\mathrm {d} u _ {i} ^ {\mu} / \mathrm {d} \tau_ {i}\right) \delta^ {4} \left[ x - x _ {i} \left(\tau_ {i}\right) \right] \mathrm {d} \tau _ {i}, \\ \end{array}
$$
where the second result follows from integration by parts.
Now for the $i^{\text{th}}$ particle
$$
\mathrm {m _ {i} d u _ {i} ^ {\mu} / d \tau_ {i}} = \mathrm {q _ {i} F _ {\nu} ^ {\mu} (x _ {i}) u _ {i} ^ {\nu}},
$$
so, with the result of Problem 4.7,
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {T} _ {\mathrm {P}, \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = \sum_ {\mathrm {i}} \mathrm {q} _ {\mathrm {i}} \int \mathrm {F} _ {\nu} ^ { \mu } \mathrm {u} _ {\mathrm {i}} ^ { \nu } \delta^ {4} \left[ \mathrm {x} - \mathrm {x} _ {\mathrm {i}} \left(r _ {i} \right) \right] \mathrm {d} r _ {i} \\ = \mathbf {F} _ {\mu} ^ { \nu } \mathbf {J} ^ { \mu }. \\ \end{array}
$$
But, from Problem 4.15,
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {\mathbf {E M}, \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = (4 \pi) ^ {- 1} \mathrm {F} ^ { \mu \alpha } \mathrm {F} _ {\alpha , \nu} ^ { \nu } = - \mathrm {F} ^ { \mu \alpha } \mathrm {J} _ { \alpha }
$$
so that $(\mathbf{T}_{\mathbf{p}}^{\mu \nu} + \mathbf{T}_{\mathbf{EM}}^{\mu \nu})_{,\nu} = 0.$
