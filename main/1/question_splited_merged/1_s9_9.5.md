Problem 9.5. Find the Christoffel symbols and Riemann curvature components for the two dimensional spacetime:
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = \mathrm {d v} ^ {2} - \mathrm {v} ^ {2} \mathrm {d u} ^ {2}.
$$

## Solution

Solution 9.5. The only nonvanishing Christoffel symbols are $\Gamma_{\mathbf{uu}}^{\mathbf{v}} = \mathbf{v}$ and $\Gamma_{\mathbf{uv}}^{\mathbf{u}} = \Gamma_{\mathbf{vu}}^{\mathbf{u}} = \mathbf{v}^{-1}$ , so that
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathsf {R} _ {\mathsf {v u v u}} = \mathsf {R} _ {\mathsf {u v u}} ^ {\mathsf {v}} = \Gamma_ {\mathsf {u u}, \mathsf {v}} ^ {\mathsf {v}} - \Gamma_ {\mathsf {v u}, \mathsf {u}} ^ {\mathsf {v}} + \Gamma_ {\mathsf {v} \alpha} ^ {\mathsf {v}} \Gamma_ {\mathsf {u u}} ^ {\alpha} - \Gamma_ {\mathsf {u} \alpha} ^ {\mathsf {v}} \Gamma_ {\mathsf {u v}} ^ {\alpha} \\ = 1 - 0 + 0 - 1 = 0. \\ \end{array}
$$
Since there is only one independent Riemann component in two dimensions we conclude that $\mathbf{R}_{\alpha \beta \gamma \delta} = 0$ , and the spacetime is flat. (This was also shown by a coordinate transformation in Problem 6.1.)
