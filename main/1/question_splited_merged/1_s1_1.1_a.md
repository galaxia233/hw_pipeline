Problem 1.1. The 4-velocity $\mathbf{u}$ corresponds to 3-velocity $\mathbf{v}$ . Express:
(a) $\mathbf{u}^0$ in terms of $|\underline{\mathbf{v}}|$

## Solution

Solution 1.1. $\mathbf{u} = (\gamma, \gamma \mathbf{v})$ , where $\gamma \equiv (1 - \mathbf{v}^2)^{-\frac{1}{2}} = \mathbf{u}^0 = \mathrm{d}t / \mathrm{d}\tau$ . Thus:
(a) $\mathbf{u}^0 = (1 - \mathbf{v}^2)^{-\frac{1}{2}}$   
(b) $\mathbf{u}^{\mathrm{j}} = (1 - \underbrace{\mathbf{v}^{2}})^{-\frac{1}{2}}\mathbf{v}^{\mathrm{j}}$   
(c) $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u} = -1$ since it is a 4-velocity, so
$$
\mathbf {u} ^ {0} = \left[ 1 + \left(\mathbf {u} ^ {1}\right) ^ {2} + \left(\mathbf {u} ^ {2}\right) ^ {2} + \left(\mathbf {u} ^ {3}\right) ^ {2} \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \equiv \left(1 + \mathbf {u} ^ {\mathrm {j}} \mathbf {u} _ {\mathrm {j}}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}
$$
(d) $\mathrm{d} / \mathrm{d}\tau = (\mathrm{d}t / \mathrm{d}\tau)\mathrm{d} / \mathrm{d}t = (1 - \underline{\mathrm{v}}^2)^{-\frac{1}{2}}\mathrm{d} / \mathrm{d}t$   
(e) $v^{j} = u^{j} / u^{0} = u^{j}(1 + u^{i}u_{i})^{-\frac{1}{2}}$   
(f) $\left|\widetilde{\mathbf{v}}\right| = \left[1 - \left(\mathbf{u}^{0}\right)^{-2}\right]^{\frac{1}{2}},$ from (a).
