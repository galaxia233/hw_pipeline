Problem 19.24. What is the apparent angular size of an object of proper diameter $\ell$ seen at redshift $z$ in a matter dominated Friedmann cosmology with present parameters $\mathbf{H}_0$ and $\mathbf{q}_0$ ? (Analogous results for apparent proper motion and apparent luminosity follow from Problem 19.9.)

## Solution

Solution 19.24.
![](images/23b04c5eb40bcdf283d55f484c6874171dae3b8408c586be8ae9fa7c7731c348.jpg)
From the figure and the Robertson-Walker line element, $\ell = [\mathfrak{r}_1\mathsf{R}(\mathfrak{t}_1)]\delta$ . We can express $\mathsf{R}(\mathfrak{t}_1)$ as $\mathsf{R}_0 / (1 + z)$ ; the hard part is now to compute $\mathbf{r}_1$ : Use the $\eta, \chi$ form of the metric from Problem 19.5. Then $\mathbf{r}_1 = \Sigma (\chi_1)$ . The path of a photon is $0 = \mathbb{R}^2 (\mathrm{d}\chi^2 - \mathrm{d}\eta^2)$ , so $\chi_1 = \eta (\mathfrak{t}_0) - \eta (\mathfrak{t}_1)$ , and using the formula for $\sin (\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{B})$ , $\sinh (\mathbf{A} - \mathbf{B})$ we have:
$$
\mathbf {r} _ {1} = \boldsymbol {\Sigma} \left(\eta_ {0}\right) \mathbf {C} \left(\eta_ {1}\right) - \boldsymbol {\Sigma} \left(\eta_ {1}\right) \mathbf {C} \left(\eta_ {0}\right). \tag {1}
$$
[Here C means cos, 1, or cosh for $k = 1$ , 0, -1.] Now we need the formula relating $\eta$ of an epoch to its redshift and the current value of q (Problem 19.18):
$$
1 - \mathrm {C} (\eta) = \frac {2 \mathrm {q} _ {0} - 1}{\mathrm {q} _ {0}} \frac {1}{1 + z}
$$
$$
C (\eta) = \frac {q _ {0} z - q _ {0} + 1}{q _ {0} (1 + z)}.
$$
(This is true for all three values of k.) With this result we have
$$
\Sigma (\eta) = \frac {\left| 2 q _ {0} - 1 \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \left(2 q _ {0} z + 1\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{q _ {0} (1 + z)}
$$
$$
\mathbf {r _ {1}} = \frac {\left| 2 \mathbf {q} _ {0} - 1 \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{\mathbf {q} _ {0} ^ {2} (1 + z)} \left[ 1 - \mathbf {q} _ {0} + \mathbf {q} _ {0} z - (1 - \mathbf {q} _ {0}) (1 + 2 \mathbf {q} _ {0} z) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \right].
$$
So the apparent angle is
$$
\delta = \ell / r _ {1} R (t _ {1}) = \ell (1 + z) / r _ {1} R _ {0},
$$
and when we use Equation (3) of Problem 9.15 to eliminate $\mathbf{R}_0$ we get, finally,
$$
\delta = \frac {\ell \mathrm {H} _ {0} (1 + z) ^ {2} \mathrm {q} _ {0} ^ {2}}{\left[ 1 - \mathrm {q} _ {0} + \mathrm {q} _ {0} z - (1 - \mathrm {q} _ {0}) (1 + 2 \mathrm {q} _ {0} z) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \right]}.
$$
