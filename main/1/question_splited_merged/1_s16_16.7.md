Problem 16.7. Evaluate the 4 components of the equation $\mathbf{T}^{\alpha \beta}$ ; $\beta = 0$ for the stress energy tensor describing a static, spherically-symmetric perfect fluid star.

## Solution

Solution 16.7. The standard form for the metric of a static, spherically symmetric star is
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = - \mathrm {e} ^ {2 \Phi} \mathrm {d t} ^ {2} + \mathrm {e} ^ {2 \Lambda} \mathrm {d r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \Omega^ {2}. \tag {1}
$$
The equation $\mathbf{T}^{\alpha \beta}$ ; $\beta = 0$ is most easily evaluated by recalling that it is equivalent to the first law of thermodynamics
$$
\mathrm {d} \rho / \mathrm {d} \tau = \frac {\rho + \mathrm {p}}{\mathrm {n}} \frac {\mathrm {d n}}{\mathrm {d} \tau} \tag {2}
$$
(see Problem 5.19) and the Euler equation
$$
\left(\rho + p\right) u _ {a}; \beta u ^ {\beta} = - p, a - p, \beta u ^ {\beta} u _ {a} \tag {3}
$$
(see Problem 14.3). For a static star, both sides of Equation (2) vanish identically. The only nonzero component of $\mathbf{u}$ is $\mathbf{u}^{\mathrm{t}}$ and $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u} = -1$ implies $\mathbf{u}^{\mathrm{t}} = \mathbf{e}^{-\Phi}$ . Since only $\mathbf{p}_{,\mathbf{r}}$ is nonzero, the only nontrivial component of Equation (3) is
$$
\begin{array}{l} - p, r = (\rho + p) u _ {r; \beta} u ^ {\beta} = - (\rho + p) \Gamma_ {r \beta} ^ {\alpha} u _ {\alpha} u ^ {\beta} \\ = - (\rho + p) \Gamma_ {r t} ^ {t} u _ {t} u ^ {t} = (\rho + p) \Phi_ {, r}. \\ \end{array}
$$
