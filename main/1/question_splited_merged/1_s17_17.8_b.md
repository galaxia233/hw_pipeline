Problem 17.8 (b) An observer (not necessarily freely-falling) orbits a Kerr black hole in the equatorial $(\theta = \pi /2)$ plane.
(b) Suppose that the circular orbit lies in the ergosphere (the orbital radius is outside the horizon at $\mathbf{r}_{+}$ but inside the stationary limit at $\mathbf{r}_0$ ).
Show that the observer cannot remain at rest with respect to a distant observer. That is, show that $\Omega$ for the observer must be nonzero.

## Solution

(b) From Equation (1) we have
$$
\mathrm {Y} \equiv \mathrm {g} _ {0 0} + 2 \Omega \mathrm {g} _ {0 \phi} + \Omega^ {2} \mathrm {g} _ {\phi \phi} <   0. \tag {2}
$$
The observer can be coordinate stationary $(\Omega = 0)$ only where $\mathbf{g}_{00} < 0$ , i.e. only for $\mathbf{r} > \mathbf{r}_0 = 2\mathbf{M}$ in the equatorial plane.
