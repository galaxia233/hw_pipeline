Problem 5.1. Calculate the nonzero components in an inertial frame S of the stress-energy tensor for the following systems:
(c) Two such rings of particles, one rotating clockwise, the other counter-clockwise, at the same radius a. The particles do not collide or interact with each other in any way.

## Solution

(c) If we have an additional ring rotating in the opposite direction then that ring's stress-energy is the same as that in (b), with the sign of $\beta = \omega a$ reversed. We can then add the two cases. In the sum, terms linear in $\beta$ disappear; terms quadratic in $\beta$ are doubled. In polar coordinates, the nonzero components are
$$
\mathbf {T} ^ {0 0} = \mathbf {T} ^ {\hat {\phi} \hat {\phi}} / \beta^ {2} = \gamma \mathrm {N m} \delta (\mathrm {r} - \mathrm {a}) \delta (\mathrm {z}) / \pi \mathrm {a}.
$$
