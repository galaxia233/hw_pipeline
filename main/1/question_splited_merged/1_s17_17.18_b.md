Problem 17.18 (b) The “Zero Angular Momentum Observers” (ZAMO's) in the Kerr geometry have basis 1-forms
$$
\begin{array}{l} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {t}} = \left| g _ {t t} - \omega^ {2} g _ {\phi \phi} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \widetilde {d t} \\ \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} = \left(\mathrm {g} _ {\phi \phi}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \left(\widetilde {\mathrm {d} \phi} - \omega \widetilde {\mathrm {d t}}\right) \\ \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} = \left(\boldsymbol {\Sigma} / \Delta\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \overline {{\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}} \\ \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} = \Sigma^ {\frac {1}{2}} \widetilde {\mathbf {d} \theta} \\ \end{array}
$$
where $\omega \equiv -\mathbf{g}_{t\phi} / \mathbf{g}_{\phi \phi}$
(b) Find the dual basis vectors.

## Solution

(b) The dual basis $\mathbf{e}_{\hat{\alpha}}$ is defined by $<  \tilde{\pmb{\omega}}^{\hat{\alpha}},\mathbf{e}_{\hat{\beta}} > = \delta_{\beta}^{a}$ . If $\tilde{\pmb{\omega}}^{\hat{\alpha}} = \mathbf{A}_{\beta}^{\hat{\alpha}}\tilde{\pmb{\omega}}^{\beta}$ and $\mathbf{e}_{\hat{\beta}} = \mathbf{B}_{\hat{\beta}}^{\gamma}\mathbf{e}_{\gamma}$ , then the matrix $\mathbf{B}_{\hat{\beta}}^{\gamma}$ must be the transposed inverse of $\mathbf{A}_{\beta}^{\alpha}$ . To find $\mathbf{B}$ from A we can separate the t-phi piece from the (diagonal) r-0 piece:
$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega }} ^ {\hat {\mathfrak {t}}} \\ \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega }} ^ {\hat {\phi}} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} | \mathrm {g} _ {\mathrm {tt}} - \omega^ {2} \mathrm {g} _ {\phi \phi} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} & 0 \\ - \omega (\mathrm {g} _ {\phi \phi}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} & (\mathrm {g} _ {\phi \phi}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} \widetilde {\mathrm {d} t} \\ \widetilde {\mathrm {d} f} \end{array} \right]
$$
implies that
$$
\left[ \begin{array}{c} \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} \\ \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\phi}}} \end{array} \right] = \left[ \begin{array}{c c} | \mathbf {g} _ {\mathbf {tt}} - \omega^ {2} \mathbf {g} _ {\boldsymbol {\phi} \boldsymbol {\phi}} | ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} & \omega | \mathbf {g} _ {\mathbf {tt}} - \omega^ {2} \mathbf {g} _ {\boldsymbol {\phi} \boldsymbol {\phi}} | ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \\ 0 & (\mathbf {g} _ {\boldsymbol {\phi} \boldsymbol {\phi}}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \end{array} \right] \left[ \begin{array}{c} \mathbf {e} _ {\mathbf {t}} \\ \mathbf {e} _ {\boldsymbol {\phi}} \end{array} \right].
$$
Since the $r-\theta$ piece is diagonal, it is trivially inverted:
$$
\mathbf {e} _ {\mathrm {\hat {r}}} = (\Delta / \Sigma) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {e} _ {\mathrm {\hat {r}}} \qquad \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\theta}} = \Sigma^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {e} _ {\theta}.
$$
