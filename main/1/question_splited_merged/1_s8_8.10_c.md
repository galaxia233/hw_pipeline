Problem 8.10. [Note: This problem assumes a familiarity with the Cartan calculus, beyond the scope of most relativity texts. Succeeding problems do not depend on it.] In the language of differential forms the generalized
Stokes' theorem is
$$
\int_ {\Omega} \mathrm {d} \boldsymbol {\theta} = \int_ {\partial \Omega} \boldsymbol {\theta}.
$$
To what does this theorem reduce, in the following cases
(c) $\Omega$ is 3-dimensional, $\pmb{\theta} = \mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}\mathbf{d}^{2}\pmb{\Sigma}_{\mu \nu}$ where $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ is antisymmetric.

## Solution

(c) 
$$
\boldsymbol {\theta} = \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {d} ^ {2} \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\mu \nu} = \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon_ {\mu \nu \alpha \beta} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\alpha} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\beta} \tag {c}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {d} \boldsymbol {\theta} = \frac {1}{2} \mathbf {d} \left(\mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} \varepsilon_ {\mu \nu \alpha \beta}\right) \wedge \widetilde {\mathbf {d x}} ^ {\alpha} \wedge \widetilde {\mathbf {d x}} ^ {\beta} \\ = \frac {1}{2} | \mathbf {g} | ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} (| \mathbf {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu}) _ {, \lambda} \varepsilon_ {\mu \nu \alpha \beta} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\lambda} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\alpha} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\beta} \\ \end{array}
$$
where we have taken $\mathbf{d}\varepsilon_{\mu \nu \alpha \beta}$ as in part b. Now
$$
\begin{array}{l} \varepsilon_ {\mu \nu a \beta} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\lambda} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\alpha} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\beta} = \varepsilon_ {\mu \nu a \beta} (1 / 6) \delta_ {\kappa \sigma \tau} ^ {\lambda a \beta} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\kappa} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\sigma} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\tau} \\ = - (1 / 6) \varepsilon_ {\mu \nu \alpha \beta} \varepsilon^ {\gamma \lambda \alpha \beta} \varepsilon_ {\gamma \kappa \sigma \tau} \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\kappa} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\sigma} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d x}} ^ {\tau} \\ = - \varepsilon_ {\mu \nu a \beta} \varepsilon^ {\gamma \lambda a \beta} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\gamma} \\ = 2 \delta_ {\mu \nu} ^ {\gamma \lambda} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\gamma}, \\ \end{array}
$$
and by Problem 7.7(i) we have
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {d} \boldsymbol {\theta} = | \mathbf {g} | ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} (| \mathbf {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu}), _ {\lambda} \delta_ {\mu \nu} ^ {\gamma \lambda} \mathbf {d} ^ {3} \boldsymbol {\Sigma} _ {\gamma} \\ = 2 F ^ {\mu \nu} _ {; \nu} d ^ {3} \Sigma_ {\mu}, \\ \end{array}
$$
and finally
$$
\int_ {\partial \Omega} \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} \mathrm {d} ^ {2} \pmb {\Sigma} _ {\mu \nu} = 2 \int_ {\Omega} \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} _ {; \nu} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} \pmb {\Sigma} _ {\mu} .
$$
