Problem 17.16 (c) Find the asymptotic form of $\Phi$ for $\mathbf{r} \to \infty$ .

## Solution

(c) It is convenient (but not essential) to introduce a “tortoise” coordinate $\mathbf{r}^*$ which satisfies $\mathrm{dr}^* / \mathrm{dr} = (\mathbf{r}^2 + \mathbf{a}^2) / \Delta$ . Note that the interval $(\mathbf{r}_+, \infty)$ in the r-coordinate is stretched to $(- \infty, \infty)$ in $\mathbf{r}^*$ . Equation (4) now becomes
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathrm {R}}{\mathrm {d r} ^ {* 2}} + \frac {2 \mathrm {r} \Delta}{\left(\mathrm {r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {a} ^ {2}\right) ^ {2}} \frac {\mathrm {d R}}{\mathrm {d r} ^ {*}} + \left[ \omega^ {2} + \frac {\mathrm {a} ^ {2} \mathrm {m} ^ {2} - 4 \operatorname {M a r m} \omega - \Delta \mathrm {A}}{\left(\mathrm {r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {a} ^ {2}\right) ^ {2}} \right] \mathrm {R} = 0. \tag {5}
$$
As $\mathbf{r} \to \infty$ , we get
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathrm {R}}{\mathrm {d r} ^ {* 2}} + \frac {2}{\mathrm {r}} \frac {\mathrm {d R}}{\mathrm {d r} ^ {*}} + \omega^ {2} \mathrm {R} \approx 0
$$
which has solutions
$$
R \sim r ^ {- 1} e ^ {\pm i \omega r ^ {*}}
$$
corresponding to ingoing and outgoing waves.
