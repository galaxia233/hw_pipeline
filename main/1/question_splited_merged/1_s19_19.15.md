Problem 19.15. For a Friedmann cosmology derive the relations
$$
\frac {8 \pi G \rho}{3} = \left(\frac {\mathrm {k}}{\mathrm {R} ^ {2}} + \mathrm {H} ^ {2}\right) \tag {1}
$$
$$
- 8 \pi G p = \frac {k}{R ^ {2}} + H ^ {2} (1 - 2 q). \tag {2}
$$
If the cosmology is matter-dominated $(\rho >> p)$ show that
$$
\frac {\mathrm {k}}{\mathrm {R} ^ {2}} = (2 \mathrm {q} - 1) \mathrm {H} ^ {2} \tag {3}
$$
$$
\frac {8 \pi G \rho}{3} = 2 q H ^ {2}. \tag {4}
$$
If it is radiation dominated $(\mathfrak{p} \approx 1/3 \rho)$ show that
$$
\frac {k}{R ^ {2}} = (q - 1) H ^ {2} \tag {5}
$$
$$
\frac {8 \pi G \rho}{3} = q H ^ {2}. \tag {6}
$$

## Solution

Solution 19.15. Equation (1) follows immediately from the definition $\mathbf{H} \equiv \dot{\mathbf{R}} / \mathbf{R}$ and the first-order Friedmann equation (Problem 19.14)
$$
\dot {\mathbf {R}} ^ {2} + \mathbf {k} = \frac {8 \pi \mathbf {G}}{3} \rho \mathbf {R} ^ {2}.
$$
If we now take $\mathrm{d} / \mathrm{d}\mathbf{R}$ on this equation, use the identity $\frac{1}{2}\mathrm{d}[(\dot{\mathbf{R}})^2] / \mathrm{d}\mathbf{R} = \ddot{\mathbf{R}}$ , the other first order equation $\mathrm{d}(\rho \mathbf{R}^3) / \mathrm{d}\mathbf{R} = -3\mathbf{p}\mathbf{R}^2$ and the definition $\mathbf{q} \equiv -\ddot{\mathbf{R}}\mathbf{R} / \dot{\mathbf{R}}^{2}$ , we get Equation (2). If $\rho >> \mathfrak{p}$ then the left side of Equation (2) is negligible compared with the right side, and Equation (3) results. Equation (4) is immediate upon substituting Equation (3) into Equation (1). If $\mathfrak{p} = 1 / 3\rho$ , then eliminating $\rho$ from Equations (1) and (2) gives Equation (5), while eliminating the $\mathbf{k} / \mathbf{R}^2$ term gives Equation (6).
