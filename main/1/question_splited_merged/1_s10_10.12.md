10.12. If $\mathbf{T}$ is the energy-momentum tensor, and $\pmb{\xi}$ is a time Killing vector, show that the integral over a whole spacelike hypersurface
$$
\int_ {\mathbf {F}} \mathbf {T} _ {\beta} ^ {\alpha} \xi^ {\beta} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} \Sigma_ {\alpha}
$$
is independent of the choice of spacelike hypersurface F.

## Solution

Solution 10.12. From Problem 10.11, the integrand $\mathbf{J}^{\alpha} \equiv \mathbf{T}^{\alpha}_{\beta} \xi^{\beta}$ is a conserved quantity: $\mathbf{J}^{\alpha}_{;a} = 0$ . The result then follows from the identity $\mathbf{J}^{\alpha}_{;a} = (|\mathbf{g}|^{\frac{1}{2}} \mathbf{J}^{\alpha})_{,a} |\mathbf{g}|^{-\frac{1}{2}}$ and from Gauss's theorem:
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = \int J _ {; a} ^ {a} | g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} d ^ {4} x = \int (| g | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} J ^ {a}) _ {, a} d ^ {4} x = \oint J ^ {a} d ^ {3} \Sigma_ {a} \\ = \int_ {\mathrm {F} _ {2}} \mathrm {J} ^ {a} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} \Sigma_ {a} - \int_ {\mathrm {F} _ {1}} \mathrm {J} ^ {a} \mathrm {d} ^ {3} \Sigma_ {a}. \\ \end{array}
$$
Here we have assumed that $\mathbf{J} \rightarrow 0$ sufficiently rapidly at infinity that the contributions to the integral from the edges can be neglected. Thus the integral is independent of the spacelike hypersurface $\mathbf{F}$ .
