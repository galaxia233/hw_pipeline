Problem 13.19. In the linearized gravitational theory, show that the equations of motion for matter $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}_{;\nu} = 0$ are inconsistent with the field equations for the metric perturbation. Show that this inconsistency is of second order in the metric perturbation, hence negligible to first order.

## Solution

Solution 13.19. The equations of motion $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}_{;\nu} = 0$ are clearly gauge invariant, but in the Lorentz gauge (see Problem 13.15) $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}_{,\nu} = -(16\pi)^{-1}\square \overline{\mathbf{h}}^{\mu \nu}_{,\nu} = 0$ rather than $\mathbf{T}_{\mu \nu}^{j\nu} = 0$ . The difference between $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}_{;\nu}$ and $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}_{,\nu}$ is of order $\mathbf{T}\Gamma$ . But both $\mathbf{T}$ and $\Gamma$ are of the order of $\mathfrak{h}_{\alpha \beta}$ so the inconsistent terms are of second order in the deviation of the metric from flatness.
