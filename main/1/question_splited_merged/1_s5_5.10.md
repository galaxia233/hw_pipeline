Problem 5.10. The specific intensity $\mathbf{I}_{\nu}$ of radiation measures the intensity of radiation at a particular frequency $\nu$ in a particular direction. It is defined as the flux per unit frequency interval, per unit solid angle. Show that $\mathbf{I}_{\nu} / \nu^{3}$ is a Lorentz invariant.

## Solution

Solution 5.10. Consider a small number, $\mathbf{dN}$ , of photons with a small spread in frequency and position, moving in a narrow cone, and impinging on a small area in a short time. By definition
$$
I _ { \nu } = \frac {d (\text {e n e r g y})}{d (\text {f r e q u e n c y}) d (t i m e)} \frac {d [ \text {s o l i d a n g l e}]}{d [ \text {c o n e}]} \frac {d [ \text {c r o s s - s e c t i o n a l}]}{d [ \text {a r e a}]}.
$$
With no loss of generality take the narrow cone of the motion, whose size is $d$ (solid angle), to be along the $z$ -direction, then
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {d} (\text {e n e r g y}) = \mathrm {h} \nu \mathrm {dN} \\ d (\text {s o l i d a n g l e}) = d v ^ {x} d v ^ {y} = d (p ^ {x} / E) d (p ^ {y} / E) = d p ^ {x} d p ^ {y} / h ^ {2} \nu^ {2} \\ d (t i m e) = d z / c = d z \\ d (\text {c r o s s s e c t i o n a l a r e a}) = d x d y \\ d (f r e q u e n c y) = d (h \nu) / h = d p ^ {z} / h \\ \end{array}
$$
so that
$$
I _ { \nu } = \frac {d N}{d x d y d z} \frac {h ^ {4} \nu^ {3}}{d p ^ {x} d p ^ {y} d p ^ {z}}.
$$
Number density in phase space, $\mathrm{dN} / \mathrm{d}^3\mathbf{x}\mathrm{d}^3\mathbf{p}$ , is a Lorentz invariant (see Problem 3.34) so $\mathrm{I}_{\nu} / \nu^{3}$ is invariant.
