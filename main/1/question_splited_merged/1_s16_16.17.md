Problem 16.17. Show that for a rigidly rotating, self-gravitating, perfect fluid star
$$
\nabla \mathbf {p} = (\rho + \mathbf {p}) \nabla \log \mathbf {u} ^ {\mathrm {t}}.
$$
Here $\mathbf{u}^{\dagger}$ is a component of the 4-velocity of the fluid in the canonical coordinate system adapted to the Killing vectors (i.e. $\pmb{\xi}_{(t)} = \partial/\partial t, \pmb{\xi}_{(\phi)} = \partial/\partial \phi$ ).

## Solution

Solution 16.17. The non-vanishing components of the fluid 4-velocity are $\mathbf{u}^{\mathrm{t}}$ and $\mathbf{u}^{\phi} = \Omega \mathbf{u}^{\mathrm{t}}$ , i.e.
$$
\mathbf {u} = \mathbf {u} ^ {\mathrm {t}} \left(\frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {t}} + \Omega \frac {\partial}{\partial \phi}\right).
$$
Let
$$
\pmb {\xi} = \frac {\partial}{\partial t} + \Omega \frac {\partial}{\partial \phi} = \pmb {\xi} _ {(t)} + \Omega \pmb {\xi} _ {(\phi)}.
$$
Then $\pmb{\xi}$ is a Killing vector, since $\Omega$ is a constant. By Problem 10.14, we have
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u} = \nabla \log | \boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \boldsymbol {\xi} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}
$$
where, since $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u} = -1$ , $|\pmb{\xi} \cdot \pmb{\xi}|^{\frac{1}{2}} = 1 / \mathbf{u}^{\mathbf{t}}$ . Thus, the Euler equation (see Problem 14.3) for the fluid becomes
$$
(\rho + p) \nabla \log (u ^ {t}) ^ {- 1} = - \nabla p - (\nabla_ {(u ^ {t} \xi)} p) u.
$$
Since $\mathfrak{p}$ is independent of $t$ and $\phi$ , it follows that $\nabla_{\xi} \mathfrak{p} = 0$ . Thus,
$$
(\rho + p) \nabla \log u ^ {t} = \nabla p.
$$
