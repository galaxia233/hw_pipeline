Problem 3.34. A group of $\mathbf{N}$ particles is seen to occupy a volume $\mathrm{d}x \, \mathrm{d}y \, \mathrm{d}z \, \mathrm{d}\mathbf{P}^{\mathbf{x}} \, \mathrm{d}\mathbf{P}^{\mathbf{y}} \, \mathrm{d}\mathbf{P}^{\mathbf{z}}$ in 6-dimensional phase space, so that the number
density of particles in phase space $\mathfrak{N}$ is given by
$$
\mathbf {N} = \mathcal {N} d \mathbf {x} d \mathbf {y} d z d P ^ {\mathbf {x}} d P ^ {\mathbf {y}} d P ^ {\mathbf {z}}.
$$
Show that $\mathfrak{N}$ is a Lorentz invariant, i.e. that all observers will compute the same numerical value for $\mathfrak{N}$ .

## Solution

Solution 3.34. Clearly the number of particles $\mathbf{N}$ is invariant. We need to show that $\mathrm{d}x\mathrm{d}y\mathrm{d}z\mathrm{d}\mathbf{P}^{\mathbf{x}}\mathrm{d}\mathbf{P}^{\mathbf{y}}\mathrm{d}\mathbf{P}^{\mathbf{z}}$ is also invariant. If the particles are moving with 4-velocity $\mathbf{u}$ relative to the observer's xyz coordinate system, he measures an invariant 3-volume element occupied by them (see Problem 3.32),
$$
\mathrm {d} ^ {3} \mathrm {V} = (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {u} ^ {0} \mathrm {d x} \mathrm {d y} \mathrm {d z}
$$
and an invariant 3-momentum element occupied by them (see Problem 3.33),
$$
\mathbf {d} ^ {3} \mathbf {P} = (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {d P ^ {x}} \mathbf {d P ^ {y}} \mathbf {d P ^ {z}} \left(\frac {- 1}{\mathbf {u} _ {0}}\right).
$$
Since he measures with an orthonormal frame, we have $-\mathbf{g} = \mathbf{1}$ and $\mathbf{u}^0 = -\mathbf{u}_0$ , so
$$
\mathrm {d} x \mathrm {d} y \mathrm {d} z \mathrm {d} P ^ {x} \mathrm {d} P ^ {y} \mathrm {d} P ^ {z} = \mathrm {d} ^ {3} V \mathrm {d} ^ {3} P
$$
which is an invariant.
