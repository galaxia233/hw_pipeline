Problem 21.11. The vacuum geometries exterior to and interior to a collapsing spherical shell of dust are the Schwarzschild geometry
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = - \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) \mathrm {d t} ^ {2} + \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {- 1} \mathrm {d r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \mathrm {d} \Omega^ {2}
$$
for the exterior, and the flat geometry
$$
\mathbf {d s} ^ {2} = - \mathbf {\nabla d T} ^ {2} + \mathbf {\nabla d r} ^ {2} + \mathbf {\nabla r} ^ {2} \mathbf {\nabla d} \Omega^ {2}
$$
for the interior. [The radial coordinates in these metrics both, clearly, have the property that $4\pi r^2$ is the proper surface area of the spherical surfaces $r = \text{constant}$ , and $t$ or $T = \text{constant}$ .]
Show that for the collapsing spherical shell of dust the "rest mass of the shell" $\mu \equiv 4\pi R^2 (\tau)\sigma$ is constant. Here $\sigma$ is the surface mass density of the shell and the area of the shell as a function of proper shell time is $4\pi R^{2}(\tau)$ . Derive the equation of motion of the shell
$$
\mathbf {M} = \mu \left[ 1 + \left(\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {R}}{\mathrm {d} \tau}\right) ^ {2} \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}} - \frac {\mu^ {2}}{2 \mathbf {R}}
$$
and integrate the equation to find (in implicit form) $\mathbf{R}(\tau)$ in the case $\mathrm{d}\mathbf{R} / \mathrm{d}\tau = 0$ at $\mathbf{R} = \infty$ .

## Solution

Solution 21.11. If $\tau$ is proper time measured by an observer at rest in the dust, then the motion of the shell is specified by $\mathbf{r}(\tau)$ , where $\mathbf{r}$ is the radial coordinate of the shell in either the interior or exterior metric. The metric of the shell can be specified by
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = - \mathrm {d} \tau^ {2} + \mathrm {R} ^ {2} (\tau) \left(\mathrm {d} \theta^ {2} + \sin^ {2} \theta \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2}\right).
$$
Here $4\pi \mathbf{R}^2 =$ the surface area of the shell at $\pmb{\tau}$ , and thus $\mathbb{R}(\pmb {\tau}) = \mathbb{r}(\pmb {\tau})$
From the second relation in Problem 21.10 we have that
$$
0 = \frac {\mathrm {d} \sigma}{\mathrm {d} \tau} + \sigma \mathrm {u} ^ {\mathrm {i}} _ {\mathrm {i}} = (\sigma \mathrm {u} ^ {\mathrm {i}}) _ { \mathrm {i}} = (\sigma (^ {(3)} \mathrm {g}) ^ { \frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {u} ^ { \mathrm {i}}) _ { \mathrm {, i}} / (^ {(3)} \mathrm {g}) ^ { \frac {1}{2}}.
$$
But $\mathbf{u}^{\tau} = 1$ and $^{(3)}\mathbf{g} = \mathbb{R}^{4}(\tau)$ so that $(\sigma \mathbb{R}^2)_\tau = 0$ . This implies that $4\pi \mathbb{R}^2\sigma = \mu$ , which we identify with the rest mass of the shell, is a constant.
To find the equation of motion we use the junction condition from Problem 21.10
$$
\left[ \mathrm {K} _ { \theta \theta} \right] = 8 \pi \sigma \left(\mathrm {u} _ { \theta} \mathrm {u} _ { \theta} + \frac {1}{2} ^ {(3)} \mathrm {g} _ { \theta \theta}\right) = 4 \pi \sigma^ {(3)} \mathrm {g} _ { \theta \theta} = \mu .
$$
Evaluating $\mathbf{K}_{\theta \theta}$ we have
$$
\mathrm {K} _ { \theta \theta} = - \mathrm {n} _ { \theta ; \theta} = \mathrm {n} _ { \alpha} \Gamma _ { \theta \theta} ^ { \alpha} = - \frac {1}{2} \mathrm {n} ^ { \mathrm {r}} \mathrm {g} _ { \theta \theta , \mathrm {r}} = - \mathrm {r} \mathrm {n} ^ { \mathrm {r}}
$$
and
$$
\left[ \mathrm {K} _ { \theta \theta} \right] = - \mathrm {r} \left(\mathrm {n} ^ { \mathrm {r} +} - \mathrm {n} ^ { \mathrm {r} -}\right) = \mu
$$
where $\mathfrak{n}^{r+}$ and $\mathfrak{n}^{r-}$ are the radial components of the normal evaluated in the exterior and the interior geometry.
By using $\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{n} = 0$ and $\mathbf{n} \cdot \mathbf{n} = -\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{u} = 1$ , we can evaluate the components of $\mathbf{u}$ and $\mathbf{n}$ . Exterior to the shell we have
$$
\begin{array}{l} 1 = \left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r}\right) \left(u ^ {t}\right) ^ {2} - \left(u ^ {r}\right) ^ {2} / \left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r}\right) \\ 0 = n _ {r} u ^ {r} + n _ {t} u ^ {t} \\ 1 = - \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {- 1} \left(\mathrm {n} _ { \mathrm {t}}\right) ^ {2} + \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) \left(\mathrm {n} _ { \mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {2}, \\ \end{array}
$$
and by eliminating $\mathbf{u}^{\mathrm{t}}, \mathbf{n}_{\mathrm{t}}$ we find
$$
n _ { r} ^ { +} = \left[ \frac {1 + (u ^ {r}) ^ {2} / \left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r}\right)}{\left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r}\right)} \right] ^ { \frac {1}{2}}.
$$
On the shell $\mathbf{r} = \mathbf{R}(r)$ and $\mathbf{u}^{\mathrm{r}} = \mathrm{d}\mathbf{R} / \mathrm{d}r\equiv \dot{\mathbf{R}}$ so the contravariant component of $\mathbf{n}$ , exterior to the shell is
$$
\mathbf {n} ^ { \mathbf {r} +} = \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathbf {M}}{\mathbf {R}} + \dot {\mathbf {R}} ^ {2}\right) ^ { \frac {1}{2}}.
$$
The calculation for $\mathfrak{n}^{\mathbf{r} - }$ is identical, and we get the same answer as above with M set to zero. Thus we have
$$
\mu = - \mathsf {R} (\mathsf {n} ^ { \mathsf {r} +} - \mathsf {n} ^ { \mathsf {r} -}) = - \mathsf {R} \bigg \{\left[ 1 - \frac {2 \mathsf {M}}{\mathsf {R}} + \dot {\mathsf {R}} ^ {2} \right] ^ { \frac {1}{2}} - \left[ 1 + \dot {\mathsf {R}} ^ {2} \right] ^ { \frac {1}{2}} \bigg \}
$$
which can be solved for $\mathbf{M}$ to give the equation of motion
$$
\mathrm {M} = \mu \left(1 + \dot {\mathrm {R}} ^ {2}\right) ^ { \frac {1}{2}} - \mu^ {2} / 2 \mathrm {R}.
$$
If $\mathbf{R} = 0$ at $\mathbf{R} = \infty$ (i.e. infall from rest at $\infty$ ), then $\mathbf{M} = \mu$ and
$$
\dot {\mathbf {R}} = - \frac {\mu}{2 \mathbf {R}} \left(1 + \frac {4 \mathbf {R}}{\mu}\right) ^ { \frac {1}{2}}.
$$
This can easily be integrated to give
$$
\frac {4}{\mu} \tau = - \frac {1}{3} \left(1 + \frac {4 \mathrm {R}}{\mu}\right) ^ { \frac {3}{2}} + \left(1 + \frac {4 \mathrm {R}}{\mu}\right) ^ { \frac {1}{2}} - \frac {2}{3},
$$
where we have taken $\tau = 0$ to correspond to $\mathbf{R} = 0$ .
