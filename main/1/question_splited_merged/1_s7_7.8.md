Problem 7.8. Let $\mathbf{A} \equiv \det(\mathbf{A}_{\mu\nu})$ where $\mathbf{A}_{\mu\nu}$ is a second rank tensor. Show that $\mathbf{A}$ is not a scalar. (i.e., Show that its value changes under coordinate transformations.) Since $\mathbf{A}$ is not a scalar one cannot define $\mathbf{A}_{;a} = \mathbf{A}_{,a}$ . How should $\mathbf{A}_{;a}$ be defined (in terms of $\mathbf{A}_{,a}$ and $\mathbf{A}$ )?

## Solution

Solution 7.8. Under a coordinate transformation,
$$
\mathbf {A} _ {\overline {{\mu}} \overline {{\nu}}} = \frac {\partial_ {\mathbf {x}} \overline {{\mu}}}{\partial_ {\mathbf {x}} \overline {{\mu}}} \frac {\partial_ {\mathbf {x}} \overline {{\nu}}}{\partial_ {\mathbf {x}} \overline {{\nu}}} \mathbf {A} _ {\mu \nu}
$$
implies that
$$
\overline {{{\mathbf {A}}}} = \det  \left(\frac {\partial_ {\mathbf {x}} ^ {\mu}}{\partial_ {\mathbf {x}} \overline {{{\boldsymbol {\mu}}}}}\right) \det  \left(\mathbf {A} _ {\mu \nu}\right) \det  \left(\frac {\partial_ {\mathbf {x}} ^ {\nu}}{\partial_ {\mathbf {x}} \overline {{{\boldsymbol {\nu}}}}}\right) ^ {\mathbf {T}} = \mathbf {J} ^ {2} \mathbf {A}
$$
where $J$ is the Jacobian, $\det(\partial_{\mathbf{x}}^{\mu} / \partial_{\mathbf{x}}^{\overline{\mu}})$ , and where the superscript $T$ denotes matrix transpose. We want $A_{;a}$ to transform according to the vector analog of the transformation law for $A$ . (A is called a "density of weight 2".) Since $A_{;a}$ must be linear in $A$ , set
$$
\mathrm {A} _ {; a} = \mathrm {A} _ {, a} + \mathrm {K} _ {a} \mathrm {A}
$$
where $\mathbf{K}_a$ must be determined. This can be done by demanding that $\mathbf{g}$ , the determinant of the metric tensor, have vanishing covariant derivative:
$$
0 = \mathbf {g} _ {; \alpha} = \mathbf {g} _ {, \alpha} + \mathbf {K} _ {\alpha} \mathbf {g} = 2 \mathbf {g} \Gamma^ {\beta} _ {\beta \alpha} + \mathbf {K} _ {\alpha} \mathbf {g}
$$
by Problem 7.7. Thus
$$
\mathbf {K} _ {\alpha} = - \mathbf {\nabla} 2 \Gamma^ {\beta} \mathbf {\nabla} _ {\beta \alpha} \mathbf {\nabla}.
$$
The generalization to densities of weight $W$ (i.e. they transform with a $J^W$ ) can be done by considering powers of $g$ . The result is that
$$
\mathbf {K} _ {\alpha} = - \mathbf {\nabla} \mathbf {W} \Gamma^ {\beta} \boldsymbol {\beta} _ {\alpha} \mathbf {\nabla}.
$$
