Problem 21.10. A thin shell of dust in vacuum has surface density of mass $\sigma$ as measured by an observer comoving with the dust. If $\mathbf{u}$ is the 4-velocity of the dust, show that
$$
\begin{array}{l} \left[ \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {i j}} \right] = 8 \pi \sigma \left(\mathrm {u} _ {\mathrm {i}} \mathrm {u} _ {\mathrm {j}} + \frac {1}{2} ^ {(3)} \mathrm {g} _ {\mathrm {i j}}\right) \\ \frac {\mathrm {d} \sigma}{\mathrm {d} \tau} = - \sigma \mathbf {u} ^ {\mathbf {i}} | _ {\mathbf {i}} \\ \mathbf {a} ^ {+} - \mathbf {a} ^ {-} = 4 \pi \sigma \mathbf {n} \\ \mathbf {a} ^ {+} + \mathbf {a} ^ {-} = 0. \\ \end{array}
$$
Here $\mathbf{a}^{+}$ and $\mathbf{a}^{-}$ are the 4-accelerations measured on the outside and inside of the shell, respectively.

## Solution

Solution 21.10. The surface stress energy of the thin shell of dust is $S^{\alpha \beta} = \sigma u^{\alpha}u^{\beta}$ . From the solution to Problem 21.8, we have
$$
\left[ \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {j}} ^ {\mathrm {i}} \right] = 8 \pi \left\{\sigma \mathrm {u} ^ {\mathrm {i}} \mathrm {u} _ {\mathrm {j}} - \frac {1}{2} \delta_ {\mathrm {j}} ^ {\mathrm {i}} (- \sigma) \right\}
$$
or
$$
\left[ \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {i j}} \right] = 8 \pi \sigma \left\{\mathrm {u} _ {\mathrm {i}} \mathrm {u} _ {\mathrm {j}} + \frac {1}{2} ^ {(3)} \mathrm {g} _ {\mathrm {i j}} \right\}. \tag {1}
$$
Since there is no stress-energy outside the shell $\left[\mathbf{T}_{\mathbf{i}}^{\mathbf{n}}\right] = 0$ and the equation of motion (see Problem 21.9) gives us
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = S _ {i} ^ {m} \left| _ {m} = \sigma_ {, m} u ^ {m} u _ {i} + \sigma u ^ {m} \right| _ {m} u _ {i} + \sigma u _ {i} \left| _ {m} u ^ {m} \right. \\ = \frac {\mathrm {d} \sigma}{\mathrm {d} r} u _ {i} + \sigma u _ {i} u ^ {m} | _ {m} + \sigma u _ {i} | _ {m} u ^ {m} \tag {2} \\ \end{array}
$$
By contracting this with $\mathbf{u}$ we verify the second relation
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \sigma}{\mathrm {d} r} + \sigma \left. u ^ {m} \right| _ {m} = 0. \tag {3}
$$
Comparison with Equation (2) shows that $\mathbf{u}_{\mathbf{i}}|_{\mathbf{m}}\mathbf{u}^{\mathbf{m}} = 0$ . Thus
$$
\mathbf {a} = \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u} = u ^ {i} \nabla_ {i} \left(u ^ {j} e _ {j}\right) = u ^ {i} \left(u ^ {j} \mid_ {i} e _ {j} + u ^ {j} K _ {i j} n\right) = u ^ {i} u ^ {j} K _ {i j} n. \tag {4}
$$
(We have used Equation (2) of Solution 9.32 to get the second-last equality.) The third relation now follows from Equations (1) and (4):
$$
\mathbf {a} ^ {+} - \mathbf {a} ^ {-} = \mathbf {u} ^ {\mathrm {i}} \mathbf {u} ^ {\mathrm {j}} \left[ \mathbf {K} _ {\mathrm {i j}} \right] \mathbf {n} = 4 \pi \sigma \mathbf {n}.
$$
To verify the fourth relation we need to show
$$
\left(\mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {i j}} ^ {+} + \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {i j}} ^ {-}\right) \mathrm {u} ^ {\mathrm {i}} \mathrm {u} ^ {\mathrm {j}} = 0.
$$
We do this by using the initial value equation $\left[\mathbf{G}_{\mathbf{n}}^{\mathbf{n}}\right] = 8\pi \left[\mathbf{T}_{\mathbf{n}}^{\mathbf{n}}\right] = 0$ . Since (3)R is continuous, this gives us
$$
\begin{array}{l} 0 = \left[ \operatorname {T r} \left(\mathrm {K} ^ {2}\right) - \left(\operatorname {T r} \mathrm {K}\right) ^ {2} \right] \\ = K _ {j} ^ {i +} K _ {i} ^ {j +} - K _ {j} ^ {i -} K _ {i} ^ {j -} - \left(K _ {i} ^ {i +}\right) ^ {2} + \left(K _ {i} ^ {i -}\right) ^ {2} \\ = \left(\mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {j}} ^ {\mathrm {i} +} + \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {j}} ^ {\mathrm {i} -}\right) \left\{\mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {i}} ^ {\mathrm {j} +} - \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {j}} ^ {\mathrm {i} -} - ^ {(3)} \mathrm {g} ^ {\mathrm {i j}} \left(\mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {a}} ^ {\mathrm {a} +} - \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {a}} ^ {\mathrm {a} -}\right) \right\} \\ = \left(\mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {j}} ^ {\mathrm {i} +} + \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {j}} ^ {\mathrm {i} -}\right) \left\{\left[ \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {i}} ^ {\mathrm {j}} \right] - \delta_ {\mathrm {i}} ^ {\mathrm {j}} \operatorname {T r} [ \mathrm {K} ] \right\}, \\ \end{array}
$$
where we have used the easily verified fact that $\mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{j}}^{\mathbf{i} + }$ and $\mathbf{K}_{\mathbf{j}}^{\mathbf{i} - }$ commute. Since
$$
\left[ \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {i}} ^ {\mathrm {j}} \right] = 8 \pi \sigma \left(\mathrm {u} ^ {\mathrm {j}} \mathrm {u} _ {\mathrm {i}} + \frac {1}{2} ^ {(3)} \delta_ {\mathrm {i}} ^ {\mathrm {j}}\right).
$$
it follows that
$$
\left[ K _ {i} ^ {i} \right] = 4 \pi \sigma
$$
and that
$$
\left[ \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {i}} ^ {\mathrm {j}} \right] - \delta_ {\mathrm {i}} ^ {\mathrm {j}} \left[ \mathrm {K} _ {\mathrm {i}} ^ {\mathrm {i}} \right] = 8 \pi \sigma \left(\mathrm {u} ^ { \mathrm {j}} \mathrm {u} _ { \mathrm {i}}\right)
$$
and therefore finally
$$
\left(\mathbf {K} _ { \mathbf {j}} ^ { \mathbf {i} +} + \mathbf {K} _ { \mathbf {j}} ^ { \mathbf {i} -}\right) \mathbf {u} ^ { \mathbf {j}} \mathbf {u} _ { \mathbf {i}} = 0.
$$
