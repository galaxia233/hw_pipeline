## Solution

(c) Consider the time derivative of the spin:
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d S} _ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d t}} = - \frac {1}{2} \varepsilon_ {\alpha} \beta \gamma \delta \left(\frac {\mathrm {d J} ^ {\beta \gamma}}{\mathrm {d t}} \mathrm {u} ^ {\delta} + \mathrm {J} ^ {\beta \gamma} \frac {\mathrm {d u} ^ {\delta}}{\mathrm {d t}}\right).
$$
The first term on the right vanishes from part (a). The second term vanishes because the system has no forces acting on it $(\mathrm{du}^{\delta} / \mathrm{dt} = 0)$ . Thus $\mathrm{dS}^{\alpha} / \mathrm{dt} = 0$ .
