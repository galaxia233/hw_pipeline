15.20. Show that the Schwarzschild metric is also a solution of the Brans-Dicke theory of gravity. (For the Brans-Dicke field equations, see e.g. MTW p. 1070.)

## Solution

Solution 15.20. The Brans-Dicke field equations can be put into the form
$$
\mathrm {G} ^ {\mu \nu} + \mathrm {F} ^ {\mu \nu} \left(\Phi_ {; \alpha}, \Phi_ {; \alpha} \beta\right) = \mathrm {T} ^ {\mu \nu}, \tag {1a}
$$
$$
\square \Phi = T _ {\mu} ^ {\mu} \tag {1b}
$$
where every term of $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ is proportional to derivatives of the scalar field $\Phi$ . In vacuum, a solution of Equation (1b) is
$$
\Phi = \text {c o n s t a n t}. \tag {2}
$$
Substituting Equation (2) into (1a), one obtains
$$
\mathbf {G} ^ {\mu \nu} = 0,
$$
which are the vacuum Einstein equation. Consequently, the Schwarzschild metric is a solution of the vacuum Brans-Dicke equations. (The most general static, spherically symmetric vacuum metric in Brans-Dicke theory has two arbitrary parameters corresponding to mass and scalar charge. A spherical black hole has zero scalar charge and is the Schwarzschild solution.)
