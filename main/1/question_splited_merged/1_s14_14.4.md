Problem 14.4. Derive the general relativistic equation of hydrostatic
equilibrium,
$$
\frac {- \partial \mathbf {p}}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\nu}} = (\rho + \mathbf {p}) \frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\nu}} \log (- \mathbf {g} _ {0 0}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}
$$
and compare it to the Newtonian equation.

## Solution

Solution 14.4. The Euler equation for a perfect fluid is
$$
(\rho + p) \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u} = - \nabla p - \mathbf {u} \nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} p.
$$
Hydrostatic equilibrium implies the existence of a time Killing vector $\pmb{\xi}$ . The fluid 4-velocity, according to Problem 13.9, must be parallel to this Killing vector, so
$$
\mathbf {u} = \boldsymbol {\xi} / | \boldsymbol {\xi} |.
$$
(In components, this means that only $\mathbf{u}^{\mathbf{i}} = 0$ , where $\pmb{\xi} = \partial/\partial t$ .) From Problem 10.14 we know
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u} = \frac {1}{2} \nabla \log | \boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \boldsymbol {\xi} |.
$$
We also know that $\partial \mathfrak{p} / \partial t = 0$ , or $\nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathfrak{p}\propto \nabla_{\boldsymbol{\xi}}\mathfrak{p} = 0$ , and therefore
$$
\nabla \mathfrak {p} = - (\rho + \mathfrak {p}) \nabla \log | \boldsymbol {\xi} \cdot \boldsymbol {\xi} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}}.
$$
But $\pmb{\xi} \cdot \pmb{\xi} = (\partial/\partial t) \cdot (\partial/\partial t) = \mathbf{g}_{00}$ , so $|\pmb{\xi} \cdot \pmb{\xi}|^{\frac{1}{2}} = (-\mathbf{g}_{00})^{\frac{1}{2}}$ , from which the result follows.
In the Newtonian limit, $\mathfrak{p} \ll \rho$ and $\mathbf{g}_{00} \approx -(1 + 2\Phi)$ where the Newtonian potential $\Phi \ll 1$ . Thus
$$
\frac {\partial \mathrm {p}}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\nu}} \approx - \rho \frac {\partial}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\nu}} \frac {1}{2} \log (1 + 2 \Phi) \approx - \rho \frac {\partial \Phi}{\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\nu}}..
$$
