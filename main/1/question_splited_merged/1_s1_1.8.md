Problem 1.8. Frame $S'$ moves with velocity $\underline{\beta}$ relative to frame $S$ . A bullet in frame $S'$ is fired with velocity $\underline{v'}$ at an angle $\theta'$ with respect to the forward direction of motion. What is this angle $\theta$ as measured in $S$ ? What if the bullet is a photon?

## Solution

Solution 1.8. Not only are lengths contracted, but also times are dilated. In S the bullet's velocity is
$$
\mathbf {v} = (\Delta \mathbf {x} / \Delta t, \Delta \mathbf {y} / \Delta t).
$$
From the transformation formulas for $\Delta x$ and $\Delta t$
$$
\mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x}} = \Delta \mathbf {x} / \Delta t = (\mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime}} + \beta) / (1 + \beta \mathbf {v} _ {\mathbf {x} ^ {\prime}}).
$$
Also, since $\Delta \mathbf{y} = \Delta \mathbf{y}'$ , we have
$$
\mathbf {v _ {y}} = \Delta \mathbf {y} / \Delta t = \Delta \mathbf {y} ^ {\prime} (1 - \beta^ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} / (\Delta t ^ {\prime} + \beta \Delta \mathbf {x} ^ {\prime}) = \mathbf {v _ {y}} ^ {\prime} (1 - \beta^ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} / (1 + \beta \mathbf {v _ {x}} ^ {\prime}).
$$
The direction of motion then is given by
$$
\tan \theta = v _ {y} / v _ {x} = v _ {y} ^ {\prime} (1 - \beta^ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} / (v _ {x ^ {\prime}} + \beta) = \tan \theta^ {\prime} (1 - \beta^ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} / (1 + \beta / v _ {x ^ {\prime}}).
$$
Except for the factor of $(1 - \beta^2)^{\frac{1}{2}}$ , this change in direction is identical to the Galilean result. It represents a "funneling" of the motion toward the $x$ axis. For a photon, $v_{x}^{2} + v_{y}^{2} = 1$ , $v_{x} = \cos \theta$ , so that
$$
\cos \theta = \frac {\cos \theta^ {\prime} + \beta}{1 + \beta \cos \theta^ {\prime}}, \quad \tan \theta = \frac {\tan \theta^ {\prime} (1 - \beta^ {2}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}{1 + \beta \sec \theta^ {\prime}}.
$$
