Problem 9.11. An infinitesimal circuit in the shape of a parallelogram can be specified by the differential displacements $\mathbf{u}$ , $\mathbf{v}$ representing the sides of the parallelogram. Let a vector $\mathbf{A}$ be parallel transported around this circuit. (i.e. Displace it successively by $\mathbf{u}$ , $\mathbf{v}$ , $-\mathbf{u}$ , $-\mathbf{v}$ .) Show that the change in $\mathbf{A}$ due to the transport around this circuit is
$$
\delta \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha} = - \mathbf {R} _ {\beta \gamma \delta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {A} ^ {\beta} \mathbf {u} ^ {\gamma} \mathbf {v} ^ {\delta}.
$$

## Solution

Solution 9.11. Choose locally flat coordinates at the starting point $\mathbf{P}$ . Thus $\Gamma_{\beta \gamma}^{\alpha} = 0$ at $\mathbf{P}$ but $\Gamma_{\beta \gamma}^{\alpha} = \Gamma_{\beta \gamma, \delta}^{\alpha} \mathbf{u}^{\delta}$ after displacement $\mathbf{u}$ . When we displace $\mathbf{A}$ from $\mathbf{P}$ by $\mathbf{u}$ we find that its components become
$$
\begin{array}{l} [ \mathbf {A} (\text {d i s p l a c e d b y} \mathbf {u}) ] ^ {\alpha} = \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha} + \mathbf {A} _ {, \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {u} ^ {\beta} \\ \mathbf {\Gamma} = \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha} + \mathbf {A} _ {; \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {u} ^ {\beta} - \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {A} ^ {\gamma} \mathbf {u} ^ {\beta} = \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha}. \\ \end{array}
$$
When we displace, again, by $\mathbf{v}$ the new components are
$$
\begin{array}{l} [ \mathbf {A} (\text {d i s p l a c e d b y u t h e n v}) ] ^ {\alpha} = \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha} + \mathbf {A} _ {; \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {v} ^ {\beta} - \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {A} ^ {\gamma} \mathbf {v} ^ {\beta} \\ = \mathrm {A} ^ {\alpha} - \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {A} ^ {\gamma_ {\mathrm {v}} \beta} = \mathrm {A} ^ {\alpha} - \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta , \delta} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {A} ^ {\gamma_ {\mathrm {v}} \beta_ {\mathrm {u}} \delta}. \\ \end{array}
$$
We could have reached this same point by travelling around the other side of the parallelogram to find, similarly
$$
[ \mathbf {A} (\text {d i s p l a c e d b y} - \mathbf {v} \text {t h e n} - \mathbf {u}) ] ^ {a} = \mathbf {A} ^ {a} - \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta , \delta} ^ {a} \mathbf {A} ^ {\gamma} \mathbf {u} ^ {\beta} _ {\mathbf {v}} ^ {\delta}.
$$
The change in A in making the complete circuit is our first result minus our second
$$
\delta \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha} = - \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta , \delta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {A} ^ {\gamma_ {\mathbf {v}} \beta_ {\mathbf {u}} \delta} + \Gamma_ {\gamma \beta , \delta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {A} ^ {\gamma_ {\mathbf {u}} \beta_ {\mathbf {v}} \delta} = - \mathbf {R} _ {\gamma \delta \beta} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {A} ^ {\gamma_ {\mathbf {v}} \beta_ {\mathbf {u}} \delta}.
$$
