Problem 2.11. Consider the reaction $1 + 2 \rightarrow 3 + 4$ . The lab frame is defined to be the one in which $\underline{\mathbf{P}}_2 = 0$ . The C.M. frame is defined to be the one in which $\underline{\mathbf{P}}_1^{\mathbf{C.M.}} + \underline{\mathbf{P}}_2^{\mathbf{C.M.}} = 0$ . Show that:
(c) $\mathbf{P}_1^{\mathbf{C},\mathbf{M}.} = \mathfrak{m}_2\mathbf{P}_1 / \mathbf{E}_{\mathrm{total}}^{\mathbf{C},\mathbf{M}}.$

## Solution

(c) By using the result of part (b) we have
$$
\begin{array}{l} \left(\mathbf {P} _ {1} ^ {\mathbf {C . M .}}\right) ^ {2} = \left(\mathbf {E} _ {1} ^ {\mathbf {C . M .}}\right) ^ {2} - \mathbf {m} _ {1} ^ {2} = \left(\frac {\mathbf {m} _ {1} ^ {2} + \mathbf {E} _ {1} \mathbf {m} _ {2}}{\mathbf {E} _ {\mathrm {t o t}} ^ {\mathbf {C . M .}}}\right) ^ {2} - \mathbf {m} _ {1} ^ {2} \\ = \cdot \frac {\left(m _ {1} ^ {2} + E _ {1} m _ {2}\right) ^ {2} - m _ {1} ^ {2} \left(m _ {1} ^ {2} + m _ {2} ^ {2} + 2 E _ {1} m _ {2}\right)}{\left(E _ {\text {t o t}} ^ {C . M .}\right) ^ {2}} = \frac {m _ {2} ^ {2} \left(E _ {1} ^ {2} - m _ {1} ^ {2}\right)}{\left(E _ {\text {t o t}} ^ {C . M .}\right) ^ {2}} = \frac {m _ {2} ^ {2} P _ {1} ^ {2}}{\left(E _ {\text {t o t}} ^ {C . M .}\right) ^ {2}}, \\ \end{array}
$$
and therefore $\mathbf{P}_1^{\mathbf{C},\mathbf{M}.} = \mathfrak{m}_2\mathbf{P}_1 / \mathbf{E}_{\mathrm{total}}^{\mathbf{C},\mathbf{M}}.$
