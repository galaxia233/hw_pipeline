Problem 2.1. (Compton scattering.) A photon of wavelength $\lambda$ hits a stationary electron (mass $m_{e}$ ) and comes off with wavelength $\lambda'$ at an angle $\theta$ . Derive the expression
$$
\lambda^ {\prime} - \lambda = \left(\mathrm {h} / \mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {e}}\right) (1 - \cos \theta).
$$

## Solution

Solution 2.1. From 4-momentum conservation we have
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {e}} + \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {\gamma}} = \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {e}} ^ {\prime} + \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {\gamma}} ^ {\prime}.
$$
(Here $\gamma$ indicates the photon; prime denotes values after scattering.) Since we are not interested in the electron's final momentum we use a generally useful trick to eliminate it:
$$
\left(\mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {e}} + \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} - \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime}\right) ^ {2} = \mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {e}} ^ {2}, = - \mathbf {m} _ {\mathrm {e}} ^ {2},
$$
or, since $\mathbf{P}_{\gamma}^{2} = 0$
$$
- \mathbf {m} _ {\mathbf {e}} ^ {2} + 2 \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {e}} \cdot \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} - 2 \mathbf {P} _ {\mathbf {e}} \cdot \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime} - 2 \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} \cdot \mathbf {P} _ {\gamma} ^ {\prime} = - \mathbf {m} _ {\mathbf {e}} ^ {2}.
$$
In the lab frame, we have
$$
\mathbf {P} _ {\mathrm {e}} = \left(\mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {e}}, 0\right)
$$
$\mathbf{P}_{\gamma} = (\mathrm{h} / \lambda ,\mathrm{h} / \lambda \underline{\mathsf{e}}_{\mathbf{i}}),\quad \underline{\mathsf{e}}_{\mathbf{i}} = \text{unit 3 - vector in incident direction},$
$\mathbf{P}_{\gamma}^{\prime} = (\mathrm{h} / \lambda^{\prime},\mathrm{h} / \lambda^{\prime}\underline{\mathsf{e}}_{0}),\underline{\mathsf{e}}_{0} = \text{unit 3 - vector in outgoing direction},$
so that
$$
- \mathrm {\frac {m _ {e} h}{\lambda}} + \mathrm {\frac {m _ {e} h}{\lambda^ {\prime}}} + \mathrm {\frac {h ^ {2}}{\lambda \lambda^ {\prime}}} - \mathrm {\frac {h ^ {2}}{\lambda \lambda^ {\prime}}} \cos \theta = 0 .
$$
Multiplying through by $\lambda \lambda'$ , we find
$$
\lambda^ {\prime} - \lambda = \left(\mathrm {h} / \mathrm {m} _ {\mathrm {e}}\right) (1 - \cos \theta).
$$
