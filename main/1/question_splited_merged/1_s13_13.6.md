Problem 13.6. Normally it is assumed that a physically possible $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}$ must satisfy the (weak) energy condition, $\mathbf{T}^{00} \geq 0$ for all physical observers. Assume that $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}$ has a timelike eigenvector; how can a given single observer determine whether the $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}$ he measures satisfies the condition?

## Solution

Solution 13.6. Given $\mathbf{T}_{\alpha}^{\beta}$ in his own orthonormal frame, the observer can find its four eigenvalues and eigenvectors:
$$
\mathrm {T} _ {\alpha} ^ {\beta} \mathrm {W} _ {\beta} = \lambda \mathrm {W} _ {\alpha}. \tag {1}
$$
We are given that one eigenvector is timelike. Normalize it so that $\mathbf{W}_{\alpha}\mathbf{W}^{\alpha} = -1$ . If its components are $\mathbf{W}^{\alpha} = (\gamma, \gamma \mathbf{v}^{\mathrm{j}})$ , then perform a Lorentz transformation with velocity $\mathbf{v}^{\mathrm{j}}$ to the rest frame of $\mathbf{W}$ . In that frame $\mathbf{W}^{\hat{\alpha}} = (1, 0)$ and Equation (1) gives $\mathbf{T}^{\hat{0}\hat{0}} = -\lambda_{\text{timelike}} \equiv \rho$ , and $\mathbf{T}^{\hat{0}\hat{\mathbf{k}}} = 0$ . The $3 \times 3$ matrix $\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{j}}\hat{\mathbf{k}}}$ can be diagonalized by a spatial rotation, and from Equation (1) we see that the diagonal elements are the remaining
eigenvalues $\lambda_{\mathbf{i}} = \mathbf{p}_{\mathbf{i}}$ with $\mathbf{i} = 1,2,3$ . Now let $\mathbf{u}$ be an arbitrary
4-velocity, with components $\mathbf{u}^{\alpha} = (\overline{\gamma},\overline{\gamma}\overline{\mathbf{v}}^{\mathrm{j}})$ in the frame of the observer for whom $\mathbf{T}^{\alpha \beta}$ is diagonal. The weak energy condition is that $\mathbf{T}^{\alpha \beta}\mathbf{u}_{\alpha}\mathbf{u}_{\beta}\geq 0$ that is
$$
\rho + \bar {\mathrm {v}} _ {1} ^ {2} \mathrm {p} _ {1} + \bar {\mathrm {v}} _ {2} ^ {2} \mathrm {p} _ {2} + \bar {\mathrm {v}} _ {3} ^ {2} \mathrm {p} _ {3} \geq 0 \tag {2}
$$
where $\overline{\mathbf{v}}^{\mathbf{j}}$ is arbitrary and $\overline{\mathbf{v}}^2\leq 1$ . The necessary and sufficient condition that Equation (2) be satisfied is
$$
\rho \geq 0 \quad \rho + p _ {i} \geq 0 \tag {3}
$$
a test which can be made by the original observer after he has solved Equation (1). For the case when $\mathbf{T}^{\alpha \beta}$ has no timelike eigenvector, see Hawking and Ellis, p. 89.
