Problem 8.26.
(b) Suppose $\mathbf{g}_{\mu \nu}$ and $\overline{\mathbf{g}}_{\mu \nu}$ have the same geodesics. Then show that
$$
\mathsf {S} _ {\mu \nu } ^ {\lambda} = \delta_ {\mu} ^ {\lambda} \Psi_ {\nu} + \delta_ {\nu} ^ {\lambda} \Psi_ {\mu}
$$
where $\Psi_{\mu}$ are the components of a vector.

## Solution

(b) We cannot compare the geodesic equations of $\mathbf{g}$ and $\overline{\mathbf{g}}$ directly since they would involve different affine parameters $\tau$ and $\overline{\tau}$ . If $\mathfrak{t}$ is an arbitrary function of $\tau$ , a change of variable makes the geodesic equation
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {x} ^ {\lambda}}{\mathrm {d t} ^ {2}} + \Gamma_ {\mu \nu} ^ {\lambda} \frac {\mathrm {d x} ^ {\mu}}{\mathrm {d t}} \frac {\mathrm {d x} ^ {\nu}}{\mathrm {d t}} - \frac {\mathrm {d x} ^ {\lambda}}{\mathrm {d t}} \frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \tau}{\mathrm {d t} ^ {2}} \frac {\mathrm {d t}}{\mathrm {d} \tau} = 0 .
$$
To eliminate the $\mathrm{d}r / \mathrm{d}t$ term, multiply by $\mathrm{dx}^{\alpha} / \mathrm{dt}$ and antisymmetrize on $\alpha$ and $\lambda$ to get
$$
\mathfrak {u} ^ {\alpha} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathfrak {u} ^ {\lambda}}{\mathrm {d} t} - \mathfrak {u} ^ {\lambda} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathfrak {u} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d} t} + (\Gamma_ {\mu \nu} ^ {\lambda} \mathfrak {u} ^ {\alpha} - \Gamma_ {\mu \nu} ^ {\alpha} \mathfrak {u} ^ {\lambda}) \mathfrak {u} ^ {\mu} \mathfrak {u} ^ {\nu} = 0 ,
$$
where $\mathbf{u}^{\alpha}\equiv \mathrm{d}\mathbf{x}^{\alpha} / \mathrm{d}t$ . If the world lines $\mathbf{u}^{\alpha}(\mathfrak{t})$ are geodesics for both $\mathbf{g}$ and $\overline{\mathbf{g}}$ then the above equation must also hold with $\Gamma$ replaced by $\overline{\Gamma}$ . Subtracting these two equations gives
$$
(\delta_ {\sigma} ^ {a} \mathsf {S} _ {\mu \nu} ^ {\lambda} - \delta_ {\sigma} ^ {\lambda} \mathsf {S} _ {\mu \nu} ^ {a}) \mathfrak {u} ^ {\sigma} \mathfrak {u} ^ {\mu} \mathfrak {u} ^ {\nu} = 0 ,
$$
which implies that
$$
\delta_ {(\sigma} ^ {\alpha} \mathsf {S} _ {\mu) \nu} ^ {\lambda} - \delta_ {(\sigma} ^ {\lambda} \mathsf {S} _ {\mu) \nu} ^ {\alpha} = 0 .
$$
When this equation is contracted on $\alpha$ and $\nu$ the result is
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathsf {S} _ {\sigma \mu} ^ {\lambda} = \delta_ {\sigma} ^ {\lambda} \Phi_ {\mu} + \delta_ {\mu} ^ {\lambda} \Phi_ {\sigma} \\ \Phi_ {\alpha} \equiv \frac {1}{5} S _ {\alpha \mu} ^ {\mu}. \\ \end{array}
$$
[See also L. P. Eisenhart, Riemannian Geometry (Princeton University Press, 1962), Section 40.]
