10.6. Show that any Killing vector is a solution of the equation
$$
\xi_ {; \lambda} ^ {\nu ; \lambda} + \mathrm {R} _ {\sigma} ^ {\nu} \xi^ {\sigma} = 0.
$$
Find a Lagrangian-type variational principle from which this equation can be derived.

## Solution

Solution 10.6. For the commutation of second derivatives of any vector
$$
\xi_ {\mu ; \nu \lambda} - \xi_ {\mu ; \lambda \nu} = \mathsf {R} _ {\mu \sigma \lambda \nu} \xi^ {\sigma}.
$$
Now we use the Killing equation $\xi_{\mu ;\nu} = -\xi_{\nu ;\mu}$ and contract on $\mu$ and $\lambda$
$$
\xi^ {\nu ; \lambda} _ {; \lambda} + R _ {\sigma} ^ {\nu} \xi^ {\sigma} = - (\xi_ {; \mu} ^ {\mu}) ^ {; \nu}.
$$
The term on the right vanishes since $\xi_{; \mu}^{\mu} = 0$ by the Killing equation, and we are left with the desired result. The variational principle is easy to write down if we note that $\xi_{\mu; \nu}$ is antisymmetric, just like the electromagnetic field tensor $\mathbf{F}_{\mu \nu}$ . We know that the Lagrangian $\frac{1}{4} \mathbf{F}_{\mu \nu} \mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ gives a term $\mathbf{F}_{; \nu}^{\mu \nu}$ when we write $\mathbf{F}_{\mu \nu} = \mathbf{A}_{\nu; \mu} - \mathbf{A}_{\mu; \nu} = \mathbf{A}_{\nu, \mu} - \mathbf{A}_{\mu, \nu}$ and vary $\mathbf{A}_{\mu}$ . Thus the Lagrangian density can be taken to be
$$
\mathcal {L} = \xi_ {\mu ; \nu} \xi^ {\mu ; \nu} - \frac {1}{2} R _ {\mu \nu} \xi^ {\mu} \xi^ {\nu}
$$
and $\delta \int \mathcal{L}|\mathbf{g}|^{\frac{1}{2}}\mathrm{d}^{4}\mathbf{x} = 0$ will give the correct equation. This can be verified by finding the Euler-Lagrange equations for this Lagrangian.
