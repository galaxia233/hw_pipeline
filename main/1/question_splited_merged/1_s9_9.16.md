Problem 9.16. Show that Bianchi identities imply that the Einstein tensor
$$
\mathsf {G} _ {\mu \nu} \equiv \mathsf {R} _ {\mu \nu} - \frac {1}{2} \mathsf {g} _ {\mu \nu} \mathsf {R}
$$
has vanishing divergence (i.e. $\mathbf{G}_{\nu ;\mu}^{\mu} = 0$ ).

## Solution

Solution 9.16. By contracting the Bianchi identities,
$$
\mathsf {R} ^ {\alpha} _ {\beta \gamma \delta ; \epsilon} + \mathsf {R} ^ {\alpha} _ {\beta \epsilon \gamma ; \delta} + \mathsf {R} ^ {\alpha} _ {\beta \delta \epsilon ; \gamma} = 0 ,
$$
first on $\alpha$ and $\gamma$ then on $\beta$ and $\delta$ we get
$$
\begin{array}{l} R _ {\beta \delta ; \epsilon} + R _ {\beta \epsilon ; \delta} + R _ {\beta \delta \epsilon ; a} ^ {a} = 0 \\ R _ {; \epsilon} - R ^ {\beta} _ {\epsilon ; \beta} - R ^ {\alpha} _ {\epsilon ; \alpha} = 0. \\ \end{array}
$$
Thus the divergence of the Einstein tensor
$$
\mathbf {G} _ {\epsilon ; \nu} ^ {\nu} = \left(\mathbf {R} _ {\epsilon} ^ {\nu} - \frac {1}{2} \delta_ {\epsilon} ^ {\nu} \mathbf {R}\right); \nu = \mathbf {R} _ {\epsilon ; \nu} ^ {\nu} - \frac {1}{2} \mathbf {R} _ {; \epsilon}
$$
must vanish.
