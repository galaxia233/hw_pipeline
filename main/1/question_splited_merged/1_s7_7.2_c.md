Problem 7.2. For a 2-dimensional flat, Euclidean space described by polar coordinates $\mathbf{r}$ , $\theta$ , assume that the geodesics are the usual straight lines.
(c) Finally, from the line element $\mathrm{ds}^2 = \mathrm{dr}^2 +\mathrm{r}^2\mathrm{d}\theta^2$ find the Christoffel symbols, in the usual way, as derivatives of the metric coefficients $\mathbf{g}_{\mu \nu}$ . (All three methods, of course, must give the same Christoffel symbols.)

## Solution

(c) We now use
$$
\Gamma_ {\beta \gamma} ^ {a} = \frac {1}{2} \left. \mathbf {g} ^ {a \mu} \left(\mathbf {g} _ {\mu \beta , \gamma} + \mathbf {g} _ {\mu \gamma , \beta} - \mathbf {g} _ {\beta \gamma , \mu}\right) \right..
$$
For the metric $\mathrm{ds}^2 = \mathrm{dr}^2 +\mathrm{r}^2\mathrm{d}\theta^2$ the only nonvanishing derivative is $\mathbf{g}_{\theta \theta ,\mathbf{r}} = 2\mathbf{r}$ . Thus $\Gamma_{\beta \gamma}^{a} = 0$ unless 2 indices are $\theta$ 's and one is an $\mathbf{r}$ :
$$
\begin{array}{l} \Gamma_ {\theta \theta} ^ {r} = g ^ {r r} \left(- \frac {1}{2} g _ {\theta \theta , r}\right) = - r \\ \Gamma_ {\mathrm {r} \theta} ^ {\theta} = \mathbf {g} ^ {\theta \theta} \left(\frac {1}{2} \mathbf {g} _ {\theta \theta , \mathrm {r}}\right) = \frac {1}{\mathrm {r}}. \\ \end {array}
$$
