Problem 9.25. Suppose that the curvature $\mathbf{K}$ at a point $\mathbf{P}$ , as defined in Problem 9.23, does not depend on the 2-surface which is chosen through that point. Show then that
$$
\mathrm {R} _ {a \beta \gamma \delta} = \mathrm {K} (\mathrm {g} _ {a \gamma} \mathrm {g} _ {\beta \delta} - \mathrm {g} _ {a \delta} \mathrm {g} _ {\beta \gamma}) .
$$

## Solution

Solution 9.25. Define
$$
\mathrm {W} _ {a \gamma \beta \delta} \equiv \mathrm {K} \left(\mathrm {g} _ {a \beta} \mathrm {g} _ {\gamma \delta} - \mathrm {g} _ {a \delta} \mathrm {g} _ {\beta \gamma}\right) - \mathrm {R} _ {a \gamma \beta \delta} \tag {1}
$$
and note that $\mathbb{W}_{\alpha \gamma \beta \delta}$ has the same symmetries as $\mathbb{R}_{\alpha \gamma \beta \delta}$ . If $\mathbf{K}$ is independent of the vectors $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ at the given point, then by the definition of $\mathbf{K}$ (Problem 9.23)
$$
\mathrm {W} _ {\alpha \gamma \beta \delta} \mathrm {A} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {A} ^ {\beta} \mathrm {B} ^ {\gamma} \mathrm {B} ^ {\delta} = 0 \tag {2}
$$
for all A, B, thus
$$
\mathrm {W} _ {a \gamma \beta \delta} + \mathrm {W} _ {\beta \delta a \gamma} + \mathrm {W} _ {a \delta \beta \gamma} + \mathrm {W} _ {\beta \gamma a \delta} = 0. \tag {3}
$$
The symmetries of $\mathbb{W}_{\alpha \gamma \beta \delta}$ allow us to rewrite Equation (3) as
$$
\mathrm {W} _ {\alpha \gamma \beta \delta} = \mathrm {W} _ {\alpha \delta \gamma \beta}. \tag {4}
$$
Permute $(\gamma \beta \delta)$ cyclically:
$$
\mathrm {W} _ {\alpha \delta \gamma \beta} = \mathrm {W} _ {\alpha \beta \delta \gamma}. \tag {5}
$$
Substitute Equations (4) and (5) in the cyclic identity
$$
\mathrm {W} _ {\alpha \gamma \beta \delta} + \mathrm {W} _ {\alpha \delta \gamma \beta} + \mathrm {W} _ {\alpha \beta \delta \gamma} = 0 \tag {6}
$$
and find
$$
\mathbf {W} _ {\alpha \gamma \beta \delta} = 0
$$
which is the required result.
