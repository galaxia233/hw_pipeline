Problem 8.20. Find the most general vector field which is everywhere parallel-propagated along itself. Fermi-Walker transported along itself. Lie transported along itself.

## Solution

Solution 8.20. Let the vector field be $\mathbf{u}(\mathfrak{p})$ . If it is parallel propagated along itself, we have $\nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u} = 0$ . This is just the geodesic equation, so the field must be a field of tangent vectors to a space-filling congruence of geodesics.
If the field is F-W transported along itself, we have
$$
\nabla_ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u} = (\mathbf {u} \otimes \mathbf {a} - \mathbf {a} \otimes \mathbf {u}) \cdot \mathbf {u} \tag {1}
$$
where $\mathbf{a} = \nabla_{\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u}$ . Thus
$$
\mathbf {a} = \mathbf {u} (\mathbf {a} \cdot \mathbf {u}) - \mathbf {a} (\mathbf {u} \cdot \mathbf {u}). \tag {2}
$$
Now dot $\mathbf{u}$ into this and get $\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{u} = \mathbf{u}^2 (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{u}) - (\mathbf{a} \cdot \mathbf{u}) \mathbf{u}^2 = 0$ . From (2) we have that either $\mathbf{u}^2 = -1$ or $\mathbf{a} = 0$ . The first possibility means that any field of properly normalized 4-velocities is F-W transported along itself. The second says that the tangent vectors of any congruence of geodesics are also, even if they are not normalized to $-1$ .
The Lie transport law is
$$
\mathbf {0} = \mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {u}} \mathbf {u} \equiv [ \mathbf {u}, \mathbf {u} ]
$$
which is satisfied by any vector field.
