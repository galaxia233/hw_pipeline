Problem 7.14. Prove the general relativity version of Fermat's principle: In any static metric $(\mathbf{g}_{0j} = \mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta,0} = 0)$ , consider all null curves between two points in space, $\mathbf{x}^j = \mathbf{a}^j$ and $\mathbf{x}^j = \mathbf{b}^j$ . Each such null curve $\mathbf{x}^j(t)$ requires a particular coordinate time $\Delta t$ to get from $\mathbf{a}^j$ to $\mathbf{b}^j$ . Show that the curves of extremal time $\Delta t$ are null geodesics of the spacetime.

## Solution

Solution 7.14. Start with the geodesic equation
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {x} ^ {a}}{\mathrm {d} \lambda^ {2}} + \Gamma_ {\beta \gamma} ^ {a} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\beta}}{\mathrm {d} \lambda} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma}}{\mathrm {d} \lambda} = 0 .
$$
Change variables from affine parameter $\lambda$ to coordinate time $t$ and use
$$
0 = d t ^ {2} + \left(g _ {i j} / g _ {0 0}\right) d x ^ {i} d x ^ {k} \tag {1}
$$
to get
$$
\mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {j k}} \frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathbf {k}}}{\mathrm {d} t ^ {2}} + \Gamma_ {\mathrm {j k l}} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathbf {k}}}{\mathrm {d} t} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\ell}}{\mathrm {d} t} - \Gamma_ {\mathrm {j 0 0}} \frac {\mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {k l}}}{\mathbf {g} _ {0 0}} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathbf {k}}}{\mathrm {d} t} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\ell}}{\mathrm {d} t} + \frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathrm {t / d} \lambda^ {2}}{(\mathrm {d} t / \mathrm {d} \lambda) ^ {2}} \mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {j k}} \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {x} ^ {\mathbf {k}}}{\mathrm {d} t} = 0 .
$$
Combine this with the time part of the geodesic equation
$$
\frac {\left(\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathrm {t} / \mathrm {d} \lambda^ {2}\right)}{\left(\mathrm {d t} / \mathrm {d} \lambda\right) ^ {2}} = - 2 \Gamma_ {0 \mathrm {k} 0} \frac {\mathrm {d x} ^ {\mathrm {k}} / \mathrm {d t}}{\mathrm {g} _ {0 0}}
$$
and use the expression for the $\Gamma$ 's in terms of the metric to get
$$
\gamma_ {j k} \frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} x ^ {k}}{\mathrm {d} t ^ {2}} + \frac {1}{2} \left(\gamma_ {j k, l} + \gamma_ {j l, k} - \gamma_ {k l, j}\right) \frac {\mathrm {d} x ^ {k}}{\mathrm {d} t} \frac {\mathrm {d} x ^ {\ell}}{\mathrm {d} t} = 0, \tag {2}
$$
where $\gamma_{jk} \equiv -g_{jk} / g_{00}$ . Notice that this is just a geodesic equation with affine parameter $t$ in the 3-dimensional manifold with metric $\gamma_{jk}$ . From Equation (1) it is clear then that the solution of Equation (2) extremizes $\int dt$ ; Fermat's principle holds. [See also C. Moller, The Theory of Relativity (Oxford University Press, 2nd ed., 1972), p. 308.]
