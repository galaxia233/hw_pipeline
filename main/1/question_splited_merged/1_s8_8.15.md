Problem 8.15. Show that Lie differentiation commutes with the operation of contraction.

## Solution

Solution 8.15. Contraction may be thought of as multiplication by the constant Kronecker-delta tensor $\delta_{\nu}^{\mu}$ . But the Lie derivative of the tensor $\delta_{\nu}^{\mu}$ is zero, as we can easily prove in a coordinate frame,
$$
\mathcal {L} _ {\mathbf {x}} (\delta_ {\nu} ^ {\mu}) = \delta_ {\nu , \lambda} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {x} ^ {\lambda} + \delta_ {\lambda} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {x} _ {, \nu} ^ {\lambda} - \delta_ {\nu} ^ {\lambda} \mathbf {x} _ {, \lambda} ^ {\mu} = 0 + \mathbf {x} _ {, \nu} ^ {\mu} - \mathbf {x} _ {, \nu} ^ {\mu} = 0 ,
$$
and therefore the contraction operator $\delta_{\nu}^{\mu}$ can go on either side of $\mathfrak{L}_{\mathbf{x}}$ .
