Problem 19.40. For the congruence of galaxy world lines in a Robertson-Walker cosmology find $\theta, \omega_{\alpha \beta}$ and $\sigma_{\alpha \beta}$ . (See Problem 5.18 for definitions.) Do the same for the anisotropic metric
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = - \mathrm {d t} ^ {2} + \mathrm {e} ^ {2 a} \mathrm {d x } ^ {2} + \mathrm {e} ^ {2 b} \mathrm {d y } ^ {2} + \mathrm {e} ^ {2 c} \mathrm {d z } ^ {2}
$$
where a, b, and c are functions of t and where x, y and z are coordinates comoving with the galaxies.

## Solution

Solution 19.40. Since $\mathbf{u} = \mathbf{e}_0$ we have
$$
\mathrm {u} _ {\alpha ; \beta} = \mathrm {u} _ {\alpha , \beta} - \Gamma^ {\gamma} _ {\alpha \beta} \mathrm {u} _ {\gamma} = 0 + \Gamma^ {0} _ {\alpha \beta}
$$
so that
$$
\theta = \mathrm {u} _ {\alpha ; \beta} \mathrm {g} ^ {\alpha \beta} = \Gamma _ {\alpha \beta} ^ {0} \mathrm {g} ^ {\alpha \beta} = - (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} [ \mathrm {g} ^ {0 \alpha} (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} ], a \tag {1}
$$
(see part (f) of Problem 7.7). Hence for a Robertson-Walker cosmology
$$
\theta = (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} (- \mathbf {g}) _ {, 0} ^ {\frac {1}{2}} = 3 \dot {\mathbf {R}} / \mathbf {R}.
$$
The rotation is
$$
\omega_ {\alpha \beta} = \mathrm {P} _ {\beta} ^ {\gamma} \mathrm {u} _ {[ a; \gamma ]} = 0
$$
since $\Gamma_{\alpha \beta}^{0}$ is symmetric. For the shear we have
$$
\sigma_ {\alpha \beta} = \mathrm {P} _ {\beta} ^ {\gamma} \mathrm {u} _ {(\alpha ; \gamma)} - \frac {1}{3} \theta \mathrm {P} _ {\alpha \beta}
$$
$$
\sigma_ {0 a} = 0
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \sigma_ {i j} = u _ {(i, j)} - \frac {1}{3} \theta g _ {i j} \\ = - \frac {1}{2} \left(\mathrm {g} _ {0 \mathrm {i}, \mathrm {j}} + \mathrm {g} _ {0 \mathrm {j}, \mathrm {i}} - \mathrm {g} _ {\mathrm {i j}, 0}\right) - \frac {\dot {\mathrm {R}}}{\mathrm {R}} \mathrm {g} _ {\mathrm {i j}} \\ = \frac {1}{2} g _ {i j, 0} - \dot {R} / R g _ {i j} = 0 \\ \end{array}
$$
For the anisotropic metric, Equation (1) still holds, so
$$
\begin{array}{l} \theta = (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} (- \mathrm {g}) _ {, 0} ^ {\frac {1}{2}} = \dot {\mathrm {a}} + \dot {\mathrm {b}} + \dot {\mathrm {c}} \\ \omega_ {\alpha \beta} = 0 \\ \sigma_ {0 \alpha} = 0 \\ \sigma_ {i j} = \frac {1}{2} g _ {i j, 0} - \frac {1}{3} \theta g _ {i j} = \frac {1}{3} A g _ {i j} \\ \end{array}
$$
where
$$
A = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} 2 \dot {a} - \dot {b} - \dot {c} & \text {f o r} \quad i = j = x \\ 2 \dot {b} - \dot {a} - \dot {c} & \text {f o r} \quad i = j = y \\ 2 \dot {c} - \dot {a} - \dot {b} & \text {f o r} \quad i = j = z. \end{array} \right.
$$
