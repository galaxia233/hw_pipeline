Problem 8.3. In 3-dimensional Euclidean space, what is a necessary and sufficient condition on a field of one-forms $\tilde{\sigma}$ for there to exist a function $f$ such that $\tilde{\sigma} = \widetilde{\mathrm{df}}$ ?

## Solution

Solution 8.3. In a cartesian coordinate basis the condition is that $\sigma_{i,j} = \sigma_{j,i}$ . This is equivalent to the basis-free requirement that the curl of $\pmb{\sigma}$ (the vector equivalent of $\tilde{\pmb{\sigma}}$ ) vanish.
