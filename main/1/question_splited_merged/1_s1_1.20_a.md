Problem 1.20. A particle of rest mass $m$ and 4-momentum $p$ is examined by an observer with 4-velocity $u$ . Show that:
(a) the energy he measures is $\mathbf{E} = -\mathbf{p}\cdot \mathbf{u}$

## Solution

Solution 1.20. In the rest frame of the observer,
$$
\mathbf {u} ^ {0} = 1, \quad \mathbf {u} ^ {\mathbf {j}} = 0
$$
$$
\mathbf {p} ^ {0} = - \mathbf {p} _ {0} = \mathbf {E}
$$
$$
\mathbf {p} ^ {\mathrm {j}} = \mathbf {p} _ {\mathrm {j}} = \underline {{\mathbf {p}}}
$$
so, we verify the invariant formulas in this easiest frame:
(a) $\mathbf{E} = -\mathbf{p}_0\mathbf{u}^0 = -\mathbf{p}\cdot \mathbf{u}$
(b) $\mathbf{m}^2 = \mathbf{E}^2 - |\widetilde{\mathbf{p}}|^2 = -\mathbf{p}_0 \mathbf{p}^0 - \mathbf{p}_j \mathbf{p}^j = -\mathbf{p} \cdot \mathbf{p}$
(c) $\left|\underline{\mathbf{p}}\right| = \left(\mathbf{E}^{2} - \mathfrak{m}^{2}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}} = \left[(\mathbf{p}\cdot \mathbf{u})^{2} + \mathbf{p}\cdot \mathbf{p}\right]^{\frac{1}{2}}$
(d) $\left|\underline{\mathbf{v}}\right| = \frac{\left|\underline{\mathbf{p}}\right|}{\underline{\mathbf{E}}} = \left[\frac{\underline{\mathbf{E}}^2 - \underline{\mathbf{m}}^2}{\underline{\mathbf{E}}^2}\right]^{\frac{1}{2}} = \left[1 + \frac{\underline{\mathbf{p}}\cdot\underline{\mathbf{p}}}{(\underline{\mathbf{p}}\cdot\underline{\mathbf{u}})^2}\right]^{\frac{1}{2}}$
(e) In the rest frame,
$$
\mathbf {v} ^ {0} = - \mathbf {u} ^ {0} - \frac {\mathbf {p} ^ {0}}{(- E)} = - 1 + 1 = 0
$$
$$
v ^ {j} = - u ^ {j} - \frac {p ^ {j}}{(- E)} = 0 + \frac {d x ^ {j}}{d t}.
$$
