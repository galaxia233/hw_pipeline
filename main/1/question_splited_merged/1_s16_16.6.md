Problem 16.6. How many algebraically independent components of $\mathbf{T}^{\mu \nu}$ are there in a spherically symmetric configuration?

## Solution

Solution 16.6. Examine the components at any point in an orthonormal tetrad $[\mathbf{e}_{\hat{\mathbf{t}}}, \mathbf{e}_{\hat{\mathbf{r}}}, \mathbf{e}_{\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}}, \mathbf{e}_{\hat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}}]$ . Spherical symmetry implies the components are invariant under rotations:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\theta}} \rightarrow \cos a \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\theta}} + \sin a \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\phi}} \\ \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\phi}} \rightarrow - \sin \alpha \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\theta}} + \cos \alpha \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\phi}}. \\ \end{array}
$$
The components $\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{t}}\hat{\mathbf{t}}}$ , $\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{t}}\hat{\mathbf{r}}}$ and $\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{r}}\hat{\mathbf{r}}}$ are invariant under rotations. The pair $(\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{t}}\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}}, \mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{t}}\hat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}})$ transforms like a 2-dimensional vector under rotations and so is not invariant. Thus $\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{t}}\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}} = \mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{t}}\hat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}} = 0$ and similarly $\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{r}}\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}} = \mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{r}}\hat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}} = 0$ . The only 2-dimensional matrix invariant under rotations is a multiple of the identity matrix, so
$$
\left( \begin{array}{c c} \mathbf {T} _ {\widehat {\theta}} \widehat {\theta} & \mathbf {T} _ {\widehat {\theta}} \widehat {\phi} \\ \mathbf {T} _ {\widehat {\phi}} \widehat {\theta} & \mathbf {T} _ {\widehat {\phi}} \widehat {\phi} \end{array} \right) = \mathbf {T} _ {\widehat {\theta}} \widehat {\theta} \left( \begin{array}{c c} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{array} \right).
$$
Thus, there are four independent components: $\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{t}}\hat{\mathbf{t}}}$ , $\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{t}}\hat{\mathbf{r}}}$ , $\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\mathbf{r}}\hat{\mathbf{r}}}$ and $\mathbf{T}_{\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}\hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}} = \mathbf{T}_{\hat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}\hat{\boldsymbol{\phi}}}$ .
