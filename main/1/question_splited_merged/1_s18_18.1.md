Problem 18.1. A Massachusetts motorist shakes his fist angrily at another motorist. What fraction of his expended energy goes into gravitational radiation?

## Solution

Solution 18.1. To find the gravitational radiation we use the square of the third time derivative of the quadrupole moment
$$
\text {P o w e r} = \frac {\mathrm {G}}{5 \mathrm {c} ^ {5}} \left(\ddagger\right) ^ {2} \sim \frac {\mathrm {G M} ^ {2} \mathrm {L} ^ {4}}{5 \mathrm {c} ^ {5} \mathrm {t} ^ {6}}.
$$
The appropriate parameters for the mean Massachusetts motorist: $\mathbf{M} =$ mass of fist and forearm $\sim 2 \times 10^{3} \mathrm{~gm}$ , $\mathbf{L} =$ length of forearm $\sim 50 \mathrm{~cm}$ , $\mathbf{t} =$ period of average shake $\sim .2$ sec. The calculation in cgs units then yields a radiative power on the order of $2 \times 10^{-43}$ ergs/sec.
To calculate his total energy expended, use the fact that muscles are almost totally nonconservative, so every shake uses up an energy roughly equal to the arms maximum kinetic energy:
$$
\text {P o w e r} = \frac {1}{2} M \frac {L ^ {2}}{t ^ {2}} \cdot \frac {1}{t} \approx 3 \times 1 0 ^ {8} \text {e r g / s e c}.
$$
The fractional "efficiency" is thus $\sim 10^{-51}$ .
