15.19. A scalar field satisfies $\square \Phi = 0$ . Show that in the Schwarzschild geometry $\Phi$ can be decomposed into spherical harmonic components $(Y_{\ell_m} = \text{spherical harmonic})$ as
$$
\Phi = \mathbf {\boldsymbol {r}} ^ {- 1} \psi (\mathbf {\boldsymbol {r}}, t) \mathbf {\boldsymbol {Y}} _ {\ell_ {\mathrm {m}}} (\theta , \phi)
$$
where $\psi$ satisfies
$$
\begin{array}{l} \psi_ {, \mathrm {t t}} - (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) [ (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) \psi_ {, \mathrm {r}} ], _ {\mathrm {r}} + \mathrm {V} _ {\varrho} (\mathrm {r}) \psi = 0 \\ \mathrm {V} _ {\ell} (\mathrm {r}) \equiv (1 - 2 \mathrm {M} / \mathrm {r}) \left[ \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {3}} + \frac {\ell (\ell + 1)}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} \right]. \\ \end{array}
$$

## Solution

Solution 15.19. The form of $\square \Phi$ for a diagonal metric is
$$
\square \Phi = \nabla \cdot (\nabla \Phi) = (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \left[ (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {g} ^ {\alpha \beta} \Phi_ {, \beta} \right], a \tag {1}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \square \Phi = (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \left[ \left\{\left(- \mathbf {g}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {g} ^ {0 0} \Phi , _ {0} \right\}, _ {0} + \left\{\left(- \mathbf {g}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathbf {g} ^ {\mathrm {r r}} \Phi , _ {\mathrm {r}} \right\}, _ {\mathrm {r}} \right. \tag {2} \\ + \{(- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} g ^ {\theta} \theta \Phi , \theta \}, \theta + \{(- g) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} g ^ {\phi} \phi \Phi , \phi \}, \phi \rbrack . \\ \end{array}
$$
In the Schwarzschild metric, curvature coordinates,
$$
\mathrm {g} ^ {0 0} = - \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {- 1}, \quad \mathrm {g} ^ {\mathrm {r r}} = \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) \tag {3}
$$
and $\mathbf{g}^{\theta \theta}$ and $\mathbf{g}^{\phi \phi}$ have their flat space form. It is clear that the third and fourth terms of Equation (2) have their flat space form (with flat space angular operator $\mathbf{L}^2$ ), and thus, Equation (2) has the form
$$
\square \Phi = - \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {- 1} \Phi , _ {0 0} + \frac {1}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} \left[ \mathrm {r} ^ {2} \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) \Phi , _ {\mathrm {r}} \right], _ {\mathrm {r}} + \frac {\mathrm {L} ^ {2} \Phi}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}}. \tag {4}
$$
The field equations $\square \Phi = 0$ then imply that
$$
0 = \Phi , _ {0 0} - \frac {1}{r ^ {2}} \left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r}\right) \left[ r ^ {2} \left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r}\right) \Phi , _ {r} \right], _ {r} - \frac {L ^ {2}}{r ^ {2}} \left(1 - \frac {2 M}{r}\right) \Phi . \tag {5}
$$
Let $\Phi$ have the form
$$
\Phi = \frac {1}{r} \psi (r, t) Y _ {\ell m} (\theta , \phi). \tag {6}
$$
Substituting Equation (6) into Equation (5), we have
$$
\psi_ {, 0 0} - \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) \left[ \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) \psi_ {, \mathrm {r}} \right] _ {, \mathrm {r}} + \mathrm {V} _ {\ell} (\mathrm {r}) \psi = 0 \tag {7a}
$$
where
$$
\mathrm {V} _ {\ell} (\mathrm {r}) = \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) \left[ \frac {2 \mathrm {M}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {3}} + \frac {\ell (\ell + 1)}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} \right] \quad . \tag {7b}.
$$
