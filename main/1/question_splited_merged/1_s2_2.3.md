Problem 2.3. Show that it is impossible for an isolated free electron to absorb or emit a photon.

## Solution

Solution 2.3. By conservation of 4-momentum, we would have to have
$$
\mathbf {p} _ {\gamma} + \mathbf {p} _ {\mathrm {e}} = \mathbf {p} _ {\mathrm {e}} ^ {\prime}, \tag {1},
$$
where $\mathbf{p}_{\gamma}$ is the photon momentum, $\mathbf{p}_{e}$ and $\mathbf{p}_{e}^{\prime}$ are the electron's momentum when the photon is/is not present.
Squaring both sides of Equation (1)
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {p} _ {\gamma} \cdot \mathbf {p} _ {\gamma} + 2 \mathbf {p} _ {\mathbf {e}} \cdot \mathbf {p} _ {\gamma} + \mathbf {p} _ {\mathbf {e}} \cdot \mathbf {p} _ {\mathbf {e}} = \mathbf {p} _ {\mathbf {e}} ^ {\prime} \cdot \mathbf {p} _ {\mathbf {e}} ^ {\prime} \\ 0 + 2 \mathbf {p} _ {\mathbf {e}} \cdot \mathbf {p} _ {\gamma} - m _ {\mathbf {e}} ^ {2} = - m _ {\mathbf {e}} ^ {2} \\ \mathbf {p} _ {\mathbf {e}} \cdot \mathbf {p} _ {\gamma} = 0. \\ \end{array}
$$
But in the frame where $\mathbf{p}_{\mathbf{e}} = (\mathfrak{m},\underline{\mathfrak{0}})$ and $\mathbf{p}_{\gamma} = (\mathbf{E},\underline{\mathbf{p}})$ , this says that the photon energy must be zero, i.e. there is no photon. Thus the process cannot occur.
