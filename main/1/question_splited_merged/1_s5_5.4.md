Problem 5.4. Find the stress-energy tensor for a uniform magnetic field. What is the average stress-energy if the B field is static but "chaotic" i.e. the direction of the B field varies, and is isotropic on the average?

## Solution

Solution 5.4. We can always choose the spatial coordinates to diagonalize the 3-dimensional stress tensor $\mathbf{T}^{\mathrm{ij}}$ . It is obvious by symmetry that one of the spatial directions which is singled out is the direction of $\underline{\mathbf{B}}$ , and that the choice of the other two orthogonal directions is arbitrary. If we choose $\underline{\mathbf{B}} = \underline{\mathbf{B}}_{z}$ it follows that $\mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{xx}} = \mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{yy}}$ and that $\mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{xy}} = \mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{xz}} = \mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{yz}} = 0$ . Since there is clearly no energy transport in this static problem $\mathbf{T}^{0\mathrm{i}} = 0$ . The energy density of a magnetic field is $\mathbf{T}^{00} = \mathbf{B}^2 /8\pi$ .
We must now calculate the “pressures” $\mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{xx}}$ , $\mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{yy}}$ , $\mathbf{T}^{\mathbf{zz}}$ . We do this by considering a box $\Delta x$ , $\Delta y$ , $\Delta z$ of the field and imagining it expanded adiabatically. (An adiabatic expansion keeps constant the magnetic flux $\mathbf{B}\Delta x\Delta y$ - i.e. the number of “lines” of $\mathbf{B}$ in the volume.) The energy in the box is
$$
\tilde {\mathbf {G}} = (\mathbf {B} ^ {2} / 8 \pi) \Delta \mathbf {x} \Delta \mathbf {y} \Delta \mathbf {z} = (\mathbf {B} \Delta \mathbf {x} \Delta \mathbf {y}) ^ {2} \Delta \mathbf {z} / 8 \pi \Delta \mathbf {x} \Delta \mathbf {y}.
$$
The pressures can now easily be found:
$$
\mathrm {T} ^ {\mathbf {x x}} = - \frac {1}{\Delta \mathbf {y} \Delta z} \frac {\mathrm {d} \widetilde {\boldsymbol {\xi}}}{\mathrm {d} (\Delta \mathbf {x})} = \mathrm {B} ^ {2} / 8 \pi = \mathrm {T} ^ {\mathbf {y y}}
$$
$$
\mathrm {T} ^ {\mathbf {z z}} = - \frac {1}{\Delta x \Delta y} \frac {\mathrm {d} \tilde {\mathcal {E}}}{\mathrm {d} (\Delta z)} = - \mathrm {B} ^ {2} / 8 \pi
$$
so that
$$
\mathbf {T} ^ {\mu \nu}   =   \frac {\mathbf {B} ^ {2}}{8 \pi} \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} 1 & & & \\ & 1 & & 0 \\ & & 1 & \\ 0 & & & - 1 \end{array} \right].
$$
To find the stress energy of a chaotic field start by choosing a set of orthogonal axes and average the $\underline{\mathbf{B}}$ fields in the $\mathbf{x}$ , $\mathbf{y}$ , and $\mathbf{z}$ directions. From our previous result, the averaging gives
$$
<   \mathrm {T} ^ {\mu \nu} > = \frac {\mathrm {B} ^ {2}}{8 \pi} \left[ \begin{array}{c c c c} 1 & & & \\ & \frac {1}{3} & & \\ & & \frac {1}{3} & \\ & & & \frac {1}{3} \end{array} \right]
$$
where $\mathbf{B}$ is the average strength of the field. If we picked any other orthogonal directions we would get the same result because the $3 \times 3$ identity matrix block is invariant under rotations; hence the above result also represents the average over all directions.
