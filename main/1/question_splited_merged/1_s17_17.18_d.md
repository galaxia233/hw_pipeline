Problem 17.18 (d) The “Zero Angular Momentum Observers” (ZAMO's) in the Kerr geometry have basis 1-forms
$$
\begin{array}{l} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {t}} = \left| g _ {t t} - \omega^ {2} g _ {\phi \phi} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \widetilde {d t} \\ \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} = \left(\mathrm {g} _ {\phi \phi}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \left(\widetilde {\mathrm {d} \phi} - \omega \widetilde {\mathrm {d t}}\right) \\ \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} = \left(\boldsymbol {\Sigma} / \Delta\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \overline {{\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}} \\ \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} = \Sigma^ {\frac {1}{2}} \widetilde {\mathbf {d} \theta} \\ \end{array}
$$
where $\omega \equiv -\mathbf{g}_{t\phi} / \mathbf{g}_{\phi \phi}$
(d) The ZAMO is not an inertial observer; show that the acceleration is $\mathbf{a} = \frac{1}{2}\nabla \log |\mathbf{g}_{\mathrm{tt}} - \omega^2\mathbf{g}_{\phi \phi}|$ .

## Solution

(d) The 4-velocity of the ZAMO is
$$
\mathbf {u} = \mathbf {e} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} = \left| \mathbf {g} _ {\mathbf {tt}} - \omega^ {2} \mathbf {g} _ {\phi \phi} \right| ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \left(\mathbf {e} _ {\mathbf {t}} + \omega \mathbf {e} _ {\phi}\right).
$$
The vectors $\mathbf{e}_t$ and $\mathbf{e}_{\phi}$ are Killing vectors. The vector field $\mathbf{e}_t + \omega \mathbf{e}_{\phi}$ is not a Killing vector because $\omega$ is not constant. However any particular ZAMO is moving along the Killing vector $\pmb{\xi} = \mathbf{e}_t + \omega_0\mathbf{e}_{\phi}$ ( $\omega_0$ a fixed constant, which is chosen to equal $\omega$ at the radius of the particular ZAMO under consideration). Thus the results of Problem 10.14 apply and we get
$$
\mathbf {a} = \frac {1}{2} \nabla \log | \pmb {\xi} \cdot \pmb {\xi} | = \frac {1}{2} \nabla \log | \mathbf {g} _ {tt} + 2 \omega_ {0} \mathbf {g} _ {t \phi} + \omega_ {0} ^ {2} \mathbf {g} _ {\phi \phi} |.
$$
When we set $\mathbf{g}_{t\phi} = -\omega \mathbf{g}_{\phi \phi}$ and evaluate at $\omega = \omega_0$ this becomes
$$
\mathbf {a} = \frac {1}{2} \nabla \log \left| \mathbf {g} _ {\mathrm {tt}} - \omega^ {2} \mathbf {g} _ {\phi \phi} \right|.
$$
