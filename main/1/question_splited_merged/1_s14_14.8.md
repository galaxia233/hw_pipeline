Problem 14.8. Show that the relativistic Bernoulli equation of Problem 14.7 reduces to the correct Newtonian limit for slow velocities and weak gravitational fields.

## Solution

Solution 14.8. The Newtonian limit of relativistic hydrodynamics is obtained by choosing a global, nearly-Lorentz, frame in which
$$
\begin{array}{l} \rho = \rho_ {0} (1 + \pi), \quad \pi \ll 1 \\ \mathbf {g} _ {0 0} = - (1 + 2 \phi), \quad | \phi | \ll 1 \\ p / \rho_ {0} \stackrel {\cdot} {<  } <   1, \quad v ^ {2} \ll 1. \\ \end{array}
$$
Here $\rho_0 = \mathfrak{nm}_{\mathbf{B}}$ is the rest-mass density ( $\mathfrak{m}_{\mathbf{B}} = \text{mean baryon rest mass}$ ), $\pi$ is the specific internal energy, $\phi$ is the Newtonian potential and $\mathbf{v}$ is 3-velocity of the fluid. Since
$$
\mathbf {u} ^ { \widehat {0}} = \left(1 - \mathbf {v} ^ {2}\right) ^ { - \frac {1}{2}} \approx 1 + \frac {1}{2} \mathbf {v} ^ {2},
$$
and
$$
\mathsf {u} _ {0} = \left(- \mathsf {g} _ {0 0}\right) ^ { \frac {1}{2}} \mathsf {u} ^ { \hat {0}} \approx 1 + \frac {1}{2} \mathsf {v} ^ {2} + \phi ,
$$
the Bernoulli equation becomes
$$
\left(1 + \frac {1}{2} v ^ {2} + \phi\right) = \frac { \text {c o n s t a n t} \times n}{\rho_ {0} (1 + \pi + p / \rho_ {0})}
$$
that is
$$
\left(1 + \frac {1}{2} v ^ {2} + \phi\right) \left(1 + \pi + p / \rho_ {0}\right) = c o n s t a n t
$$
and finally
$$
\frac {1}{2} v ^ {2} + \phi + \pi + p / \rho_ {0} = c o n s t a n t.
$$
