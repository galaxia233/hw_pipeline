Problem 3.9. Show that the determinant of the metric tensor $\mathbf{g} \equiv \det(\mathbf{g}_{\mu \nu})$ is not a scalar.

## Solution

Solution 3.9. Under the coordinate transformation $\mathbf{x}^{\mu} \rightarrow \overline{\mathbf{x}}^{\mu}(\mathbf{x}^{\nu})$ the transformation of $\mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta}$ is
$$
\mathbf {g} _ {\overline {{\mu}} \overline {{\nu}}} = \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \frac {\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}}{\partial \overline {{\mathbf {x}}} ^ {\mu}} \frac {\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\beta}}{\partial \overline {{\mathbf {x}}} ^ {\nu}},
$$
and therefore the transformation of $\mathbf{g}$ is
$$
\overline {{\mathbf {g}}} = \det  (\mathbf {g} _ {\overline {{\mu}} \overline {{\nu}}}) = \det  (\mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta}) \det  \left(\frac {\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}}{\partial \overline {{\mathbf {x}}} ^ {\mu}}\right) \det  \left(\frac {\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\beta}}{\partial \overline {{\mathbf {x}}} ^ {\nu}}\right) = \mathbf {g} \left[ \det  \left(\frac {\partial \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}}{\partial \overline {{\mathbf {x}}} ^ {\mu}}\right) \right] ^ {2}.
$$
Since $\overline{\mathbf{g}}\neq \mathbf{g}$ ， $\overline{\mathbf{g}}$ is not a scalar.
