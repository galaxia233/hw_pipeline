Problem 6.7. Show that a conformal transformation of a metric, i.e. $\mathbf{g}_{\alpha}\beta \rightarrow \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x}^{\mu})\mathbf{g}_{\alpha}\beta$ for an arbitrary function $f$ , preserves all angles. (Figure out how to define angles!) Show that all null curves remain null curves.

## Solution

Solution 6.7. Let $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ be two vectors in the metric space. Obviously we want the angle to be related to the dot product $\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B}$ . We also want it to be unaffected by scaling $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ , so the natural choice is
$\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B} / (|\mathbf{A}| |\mathbf{B}|)$ , which is $\cos \theta$ in the familiar Euclidean case. Now under $\mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta} \rightarrow \mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x}^{\mu}) \mathbf{g}_{\alpha \beta}$ we have
$$
\frac {\mathbf {A} \cdot \mathbf {B}}{(| \mathbf {A} | | \mathbf {B} |)} = \frac {\mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {B} ^ {\beta}}{\left(\mathbf {g} _ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {A} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {A} ^ {\nu} \mathbf {g} _ {\rho \sigma} \mathbf {B} ^ {\rho} \mathbf {B} ^ {\sigma}\right) ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \to \frac {\mathbf {f} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma}) \mathbf {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \mathbf {A} ^ {\alpha} \mathbf {B} ^ {\beta}}{\left[ \mathbf {f} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma}) \mathbf {g} _ {\mu \nu} \mathbf {A} ^ {\mu} \mathbf {A} ^ {\nu} \mathbf {f} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma}) \mathbf {g} _ {\rho \sigma} \mathbf {B} ^ {\rho} \mathbf {B} ^ {\sigma} \right] ^ {\frac {1}{2}}}
$$
but the f's cancel, giving the same value as before. Null curves remain null curves because the square of their tangent vector remains zero
$$
0 = \boldsymbol {\ell} \cdot \boldsymbol {\ell} = \mathbf {g} _ {\mu \nu} \ell^ {\mu} \ell^ {\nu} \rightarrow \mathrm {f} (\mathbf {x} ^ {\gamma}) \mathbf {g} _ {\mu \nu} \ell^ {\mu} \ell^ {\nu} = 0.
$$
