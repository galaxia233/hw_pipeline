Problem 9.22. At a given instant, the coordinate accelerations of $n$ nearby test particles are measured. What is the smallest $n$ required to measure all components of $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ ? of $\mathbf{R}_{\nu \rho \sigma}^{\mu}$ ?

## Solution

Solution 9.22. In a local Lorentz frame, the acceleration of any particle is related to $\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu}$ by the Lorentz force law:
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {x} ^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d} r ^ {2}} = \frac {\mathrm {e}}{\mathrm {m}} \mathbf {F} ^ {\alpha} _ {\beta} \mathrm {u} ^ {\beta}.
$$
It is convenient to think of the 6 components of $\mathbf{F}_{\beta}^{\alpha}$ as the vectors $\underline{\mathbf{E}}$ and $\underline{\mathbf{B}}$ . In the comoving frame of one particle, it has zero velocity and so feels no magnetic forces. Measuring its three components of acceleration gives $\underline{\mathbf{E}}$ . Measuring the acceleration of a second particle as seen in that frame gives only two components of $\underline{\mathbf{B}}$ , since the components of $\underline{\mathbf{B}}$ along the direction of motion of the second particle produces no acceleration. Thus at least three particles are required to measure all components of $\mathbf{F}_{\beta}^{\alpha}$ .
The reader is invited to figure out the answer for the Riemann tensor from the geodesic deviation equation
$$
\frac {\mathrm {D} ^ {2} \xi^ {\alpha}}{\mathrm {d} \tau^ {2}} = - \mathrm {R} _ {\beta \gamma \delta} ^ {\alpha} \mathrm {u} ^ {\beta} \xi^ {\gamma} \mathrm {u} ^ {\delta}.
$$
