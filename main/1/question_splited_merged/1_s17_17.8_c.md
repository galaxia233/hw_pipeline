Problem 17.8 (c) An observer (not necessarily freely-falling) orbits a Kerr black hole in the equatorial $(\theta = \pi /2)$ plane.
(c) If the observer is in the region $\mathbf{r}_{-} < \mathbf{r} < \mathbf{r}_{+}$ , show that he cannot remain at constant radius.

## Solution

(c) The discriminant of $\mathbf{Y}$ defined in Equation (2) is
$$
(\mathbf {g} _ {0 \phi}) ^ {2} - \mathbf {g} _ {0 0} \mathbf {g} _ {\phi \phi} = \mathbf {r} ^ {2} - 2 \mathbf {M r} + \mathbf {a} ^ {2} = (\mathbf {r} - \mathbf {r} _ {+}) (\mathbf {r} - \mathbf {r} _ {-}),
$$
and is negative if $\mathbf{r}_{-} < \mathbf{r} < \mathbf{r}_{+}$ . If the discriminant of $\mathbf{Y}$ is negative, then $\mathbf{Y}$ has the same sign for any $\Omega$ , i.e. the same sign as $\mathbf{g}_{\phi \phi}$ which is positive. This violates Equation (2), which assumed only timelike motion and constant $\mathbf{r}$ . The observer therefore cannot remain at constant $\mathbf{r}$ .
Note that as $\mathbf{r} \to \mathbf{r}_+$ , then $\Omega$ goes to $-\mathbf{g}_{0\phi} / \mathbf{g}_{00} = \mathbf{a} / 2\mathbf{M}\mathbf{r}_+$ .
