Problem 14.21. Show that Maxwell's equations are invariant under the "conformal transformation"
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {g} _ {\alpha \beta} \rightarrow \tilde {\mathrm {g}} _ {\alpha \beta} = \mathrm {f g} _ {\alpha \beta} \\ \mathbf {F} _ {\alpha \beta} \rightarrow \tilde {\mathbf {F}} _ {\alpha \beta} = \mathbf {F} _ {\alpha \beta} \\ \mathrm {J} _ {\mu} \rightarrow \tilde {\mathrm {J}} _ {\mu} = \mathrm {f} ^ {- 2} \mathrm {J} _ {\mu}, \\ \end{array}
$$
where $f$ is an arbitrary function of position.

## Solution

Solution 14.21. Under the conformal transformation the four Maxwell
equations
$$
\mathbf {F} _ {[ \alpha \beta , \nu ]} = 0 = \tilde {\mathbf {F}} _ {[ \alpha \beta , \nu ]}
$$
are clearly invariant. The other four Maxwell equations are
$$
\mathbf {F} _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = \left| \mathbf {g} \right| ^ {- \frac {1}{2}} \left[ \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} | \mathbf {g} | ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \right] _ {, \nu} = 4 \pi \mathbf {J} ^ {\mu} .
$$
Since
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathbf {F} ^ {\mu \nu} = \mathbf {g} ^ {\mu \alpha} \mathbf {g} ^ {\nu \beta} \tilde {\mathbf {F}} _ {\alpha \beta} = \mathbf {f} ^ {2} \tilde {\mathbf {g}} ^ {\mu \alpha} \tilde {\mathbf {g}} ^ {\nu \beta} \tilde {\mathbf {F}} _ {\alpha \beta} = \mathbf {f} ^ {2} \tilde {\mathbf {F}} ^ {\mu \nu} \\ \tilde {\mathbf {g}} \equiv \operatorname {D e t} \left(\tilde {\mathbf {g}} _ {\alpha \beta}\right) = \mathbf {f} ^ {4} \mathbf {g} \\ \end{array}
$$
it follows that $\left|\mathbf{g}\right|^{\frac{1}{2}}\mathbf{F}^{\mu \nu} = \left|\tilde{\mathbf{g}}\right|^{\frac{1}{2}}\tilde{\mathbf{F}}^{\mu \nu}$ so that
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {F} _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = \frac {\mathrm {f} ^ {2}}{\left| \tilde {\mathrm {g } } \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}}} \left[ \left| \tilde {\mathrm {g }} \right| ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \tilde {\mathrm {F }} ^ {\mu \nu} \right] _ {, \nu} = 4 \pi \mathrm {J} ^ {\mu} = 4 \pi \mathrm {g} ^ {\mu a} \mathrm {J} _ {a} \\ = 4 \pi (\mathrm {f g} ^ {\mu a}) \mathrm {f} \tilde {\mathrm {J}} _ {a} = 4 \pi \mathrm {f} ^ {2} \tilde {\mathrm {J}} ^ {\mu}, \\ \end{array}
$$
and therefore
$$
\tilde {\mathrm {F }} _ {; \nu} ^ {\mu \nu} = 4 \pi \tilde {\mathrm {J }} ^ {\mu}.
$$
