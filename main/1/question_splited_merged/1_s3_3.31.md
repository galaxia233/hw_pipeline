Problem 3.31. Show that the invariant proper volume element in 4-dimensional space is given by
$$
\mathrm {d V} = (- \mathbf {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d} ^ {4} \mathbf {x}
$$
where $\mathrm{d}^4\mathbf{x} = \mathrm{dxdydzdt}$ in the coordinate system of the metric $\mathbf{g}_{\mu \nu}$ .

## Solution

Solution 3.31. Let barred coordinates represent an orthonormal frame where the metric is $\eta_{\mu \nu}$ . Then $\mathrm{dV} \equiv \mathrm{d}^4 \overline{\mathbf{x}} = \operatorname*{det}(\partial \overline{\mathbf{x}} / \partial \mathbf{x}) \mathrm{d}^4 \mathbf{x}$ because the Jacobian determinant relates volumes. Now
$$
- \mathbf {g} = - \operatorname * {d e t} \left(\mathbf {g} _ {\alpha} \boldsymbol {\beta}\right) = - \operatorname * {d e t} \left(\frac {\partial_ {\mathbf {X}} \overline {{{\mu}}}}{\partial_ {\mathbf {X}} ^ {\alpha}} \frac {\partial_ {\mathbf {X}} \overline {{{\nu}}}}{\partial_ {\mathbf {X}} ^ {\beta}} \eta_ {\overline {{{\mu}}}} \overline {{{\nu}}}\right) = \left[ \det \left(\partial_ {\overline {{{\mathbf {X}}}}} / \partial_ {\mathbf {X}}\right) \right] ^ {2} \left(- \det \eta\right),
$$
so that
$$
(- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} = \det  (\partial \overline {{\mathbf {x}}} / \partial \mathbf {x})
$$
and hence
$$
\mathrm {d V} = (- \mathrm {g}) ^ {\frac {1}{2}} \mathrm {d} ^ {4} \mathrm {x}.
$$
