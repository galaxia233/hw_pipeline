Problem 10.1. Solve Killing's equations to find the Killing vector fields of the 2-sphere:
$$
\mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = \mathrm {d} \theta^ {2} + \sin^ {2} \theta \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2}.
$$

## Solution

Solution 10.1. The metric is $\mathrm{ds}^2 = \mathrm{d}\theta^2 +\sin^2\theta \mathrm{d}\phi^2$ ; the connection coefficients are $\Gamma_{\theta \phi}^{\phi} = \Gamma_{\phi \theta}^{\phi} = \cot \theta ;\Gamma_{\phi \phi}^{\theta} = -\sin \theta \cos \theta ;$ all others are zero. Now:
$$
\xi_ {\alpha ; \beta} + \xi_ {\beta ; \alpha} = \xi_ {\alpha , \beta} + \xi_ {\beta , \alpha} - 2 \Gamma_ {\alpha \beta} ^ {\mu} \xi_ {\mu} = 0.
$$
$$
\alpha = \beta = \phi : \xi_ {\phi , \phi} = - \xi_ {\theta} \sin \theta \cos \theta \tag {1}
$$
$$
\alpha = \beta = \theta : \xi_ {\theta , \theta} = 0; \quad \therefore \xi_ {\theta} = \mathrm {f} (\phi) \tag {2}
$$
$$
\alpha = \theta , \beta = \phi : \xi_ {\theta , \phi} + \xi_ {\phi , \theta} = 2 \cot \theta \xi_ {\phi}. \tag {3}
$$
Substitution of Equation (2) in Equation (1) gives $\xi_{\phi, \phi} = -\mathrm{f}(\phi) \sin \theta \cos \theta$ which implies
$$
\xi_ {\phi} = - \mathbf {F} (\phi) \sin \theta \cos \theta + \mathbf {g} (\theta) \tag {4}
$$
where $\mathbf{F}(\phi) \equiv \int \mathrm{d}\mathbf{f}\mathrm{d}\phi$ . Next substitute Equations (4) and (2) in Equation (3):
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {f}}{\mathrm {d} \phi} + \frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {g}}{\mathrm {d} \theta} - \mathbf {F} (\cos^ {2} \theta - \sin^ {2} \theta) = 2 \cot \theta \mathbf {g} - 2 \cos^ {2} \theta \mathbf {F}
$$
or
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {f}}{\mathrm {d} \phi} + \mathbf {F} (\phi) = - \left(\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {g}}{\mathrm {d} \theta} - 2 \cot \theta \mathbf {g} (\theta)\right).
$$
Since the left side is a function only of $\phi$ and the right side only of $\theta$ , each side must be a constant:
$$
\mathrm {d f} / \mathrm {d} \phi + \int \mathrm {f} \mathrm {d} \phi = \mathrm {b}, \tag {5}
$$
$$
\mathrm {d} \mathbf {g} / \mathrm {d} \theta - 2 \cot \theta \mathbf {g} = - \mathbf {b}. \tag {6}
$$
Equation (6) has an integrating factor $\exp [-2\int \cot \theta \mathrm{d}\theta ] = \sin^{-2}\theta$ , giving
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d}}{\mathrm {d} \theta} \left(\frac {\mathrm {g}}{\sin^ {2} \theta}\right) = \frac {- \mathrm {b}}{\sin^ {2} \theta}
$$
$$
\mathbf {g} (\theta) = (\mathbf {b} \cot \theta + \mathbf {c}) \sin^ {2} \theta . \tag {7}
$$
Equation (5) is solved by differentiation:
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} ^ {2} \mathbf {f}}{\mathrm {d} \phi^ {2}} + \mathbf {f} = 0,
$$
$$
\mathbf {f} = \mathrm {d} \cos \phi + \mathbf {e} \sin \phi
$$
$$
\mathbf {F} = \mathrm {d} \sin \phi - \mathrm {e} \cos \phi .
$$
(The integration constant is included in $\mathbf{g}(\theta)$ .) Substituting this result in Equation (5)
$$
- d \sin \phi + e \cos \phi + d \sin \phi - e \cos \phi = b
$$
shows that $\mathsf{b} = 0$ , and hence from Equations (2) and (7) that
$$
\xi_ {\theta} = \mathrm {d} \cos \phi + \mathrm {e} \sin \phi = \xi^ {\theta}
$$
$$
\xi_ {\phi} = c \sin^ {2} \theta - \sin \theta \cos \theta (d \sin \phi - e \cos \phi) = \sin^ {2} \theta \xi^ {\phi}.
$$
Thus
$$
\boldsymbol {\xi} = (\mathrm {d} \cos \phi + \mathrm {e} \sin \phi) \frac {\partial}{\partial \theta} + [ \mathrm {c} - \cot \theta (\mathrm {d} \sin \phi - \mathrm {e} \cos \phi) ] \frac {\partial}{\partial \phi}
$$
is the most general Killing vector. Note that it is a linear combination of the three Killing vectors
$$
\frac {\partial}{\partial \phi}
$$
$$
- \left(\cos \phi \frac {\partial}{\partial \theta} - \cot \theta \sin \phi \frac {\partial}{\partial \phi}\right)
$$
$$
\sin \phi \frac {\partial}{\partial \theta} + \cot \theta \cos \phi \frac {\partial}{\partial \phi}
$$
which are just the usual angular momentum operators $\mathbf{L}_{\mathbf{z}}$ , $\mathbf{L}_{\mathbf{x}}$ and $\mathbf{L}_{\mathbf{y}}$ , the generators of the rotation group.
