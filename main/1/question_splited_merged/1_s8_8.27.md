Problem 8.27. Compute the connection coefficients of the following metric in an orthonormal frame
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {d s} ^ {2} = - \mathrm {e} ^ {2 \alpha} \mathrm {d t} ^ {2} + \mathrm {e} ^ {2 \beta} \mathrm {d r} ^ {2} + \mathrm {e} ^ {2 \gamma} (\mathrm {d} \theta^ {2} + \sin^ {2} \theta \mathrm {d} \phi^ {2}) \\ a, \beta , \gamma = f u n c t i o n s o f r a n d t. \\ \end{array}
$$

## Solution

Solution 8.27. The difficult way to do this problem is to compute connection coefficients in a coordinate frame. Here we will use the method of "rotation 1-forms." (See MTW, Section 14.5 for details.) Choose an orthonormal frame as follows:
$$
\begin{array}{l} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} = \mathbf {e} ^ {\alpha} \tilde {\mathbf {d t}} \\ \tilde {\mathbf {w}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {\beta \tilde {\mathbf {d r}}} \\ \tilde {\mathbf {w}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} = \mathbf {e} ^ {\gamma} \tilde {\mathbf {d} \theta} \\ \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} = e ^ {\gamma} \sin \theta \widetilde {d \phi}. \\ \end{array}
$$
The key formulas are the definition of $\tilde{\omega}^{\hat{a}}\hat{\beta}$
$$
\mathbf {d} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\alpha}} = - \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\alpha}} \hat {\boldsymbol {\beta}} \wedge \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\beta}},
$$
and the relation of the forms to the Christoffel symbols
$$
\Gamma_ {\hat {\mu} \hat {\lambda}} ^ {\hat {\nu}} \tilde {\pmb {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\lambda}} = \tilde {\pmb {\omega}} _ {\hat {\mu}} ^ {\hat {\nu}}.
$$
We now use these formulas for the given metric; let dot denote $\partial/\partial t$ and prime denote $\partial/\partial r$ :
$$
\mathrm {d} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {\alpha_ {\alpha^ {\prime}} \widetilde {\mathrm {d r}} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d t}}} = \alpha^ {\prime} \mathrm {e} ^ {- \beta \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {r}}} \wedge \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}}} \tag {1}
$$
$$
\mathrm {d} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {\beta} \dot {\boldsymbol {\beta}} \widetilde {\mathrm {d t}} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d r}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {- \alpha} \dot {\boldsymbol {\beta}} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} \wedge \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \tag {2}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {d} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\theta}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {\gamma} \dot {\gamma} \widetilde {\mathrm {d t}} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d} \theta} + \mathrm {e} ^ {\gamma} \gamma^ {\prime} \widetilde {\mathrm {d r}} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d} \theta} \\ = e ^ {- \alpha} \dot {\gamma} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {t}} \wedge \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\theta}} + e ^ {- \beta} \gamma^ {\prime} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {r}} \wedge \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\theta}} \tag {3} \\ \end{array}
$$
$$
\begin{array}{l} \mathrm {d} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {\gamma} \dot {\gamma} \sin \theta \widetilde {\mathrm {d t}} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d}} \phi + \mathrm {e} ^ {\gamma} \gamma^ {\prime} \sin \theta \widetilde {\mathrm {d r}} \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d}} \phi + \mathrm {e} ^ {\gamma} \cos \theta \widetilde {\mathrm {d}} \theta \wedge \widetilde {\mathrm {d}} \phi \\ = e ^ {- \alpha} \dot {\gamma} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {t}} \wedge \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} + e ^ {- \beta} \gamma^ {\prime} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {r}} \wedge \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} + \cot \theta e ^ {- \gamma} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\theta}} \wedge \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} \tag {4} \\ \end{array}
$$
But we also know that $\mathbf{d}\tilde{\pmb{\omega}}^{\hat{\alpha}} = -\tilde{\pmb{\omega}}^{\hat{\alpha}}\hat{\beta} \wedge \tilde{\pmb{\omega}}^{\hat{\beta}}$ and $\tilde{\pmb{\omega}}_{\hat{\alpha}}\hat{\beta} = -\tilde{\pmb{\omega}}_{\hat{\beta}}\hat{\alpha}$ , where indices are raised and lowered with $\eta_{\hat{\alpha}}\hat{\beta}$ . For $\hat{\alpha} = \hat{t}$ we have
$$
\mathbf {d} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} = - \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} \wedge \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} - \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} \wedge \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} - \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\phi}}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} \wedge \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\phi}}} .
$$
Comparing this with Equation (1), we guess that
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\mathrm {r}}} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}} = \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {r}}} = \alpha^ {\prime} \mathrm {e} ^ {- \beta} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}} + \mathrm {K} _ {1} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {r}}} \tag {5}
$$
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\mathrm {t}} _ {\hat {\theta}} = \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} _ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}} =. \mathrm {K} _ {2} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} \tag {6}
$$
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\phi}} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}} = \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}} ^ {\hat {\phi}} = \mathrm {K} _ {3} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\phi}} \tag {7}
$$
where $\mathbf{K}_1, \mathbf{K}_2, \mathbf{K}_3$ are functions we must find. Similarly, Equation (2) implies that
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} = \mathbf {e} ^ {- \alpha} \dot {\boldsymbol {\beta}} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} + \mathrm {K} _ {4} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} \tag {8}
$$
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} _ {\hat {\theta}} = - \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} = \mathrm {K} _ {5} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} \tag {9}
$$
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \hat {\phi} = - \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\phi}} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} = \mathrm {K} _ {6} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\phi}}. \tag {10}
$$
Equation (3) implies
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {- a} \dot {\gamma} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} \tag {11}
$$
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {- \beta} \gamma^ {\prime} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} \tag {12}
$$
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} _ {\hat {\phi}} = - \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\phi}} _ {\hat {\theta}} = \mathrm {K} _ {7} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\phi}}, \tag {13}
$$
and Equation (4) gives
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} \hat {\phi} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} = e ^ {- a} \dot {\gamma} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} \hat {\phi} \tag {14}
$$
$$
\tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} _ {\hat {\mathrm {r}}} = \mathrm {e} ^ {- \beta} \gamma^ {\prime} \tilde {\omega} ^ {\hat {\phi}} \tag {15}
$$
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\phi}} _ {\hat {\theta}} = \cot \theta \mathrm {e} ^ {- \gamma} \dot {\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}}} ^ {\hat {\phi}}. \tag {16}
$$
Comparing Equations (5)-(16), we find
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} = \alpha^ {\prime} \mathrm {e} ^ {- \beta} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} + \dot {\beta} \mathrm {e} ^ {- \alpha} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}}
$$
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}} \hat {\theta} = \mathrm {e} ^ {- a} \dot {\gamma} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\theta}}
$$
$$
\tilde {\pmb {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathrm {t}}} \hat {\pmb {\phi}} = \mathbf {e} ^ {- a} \dot {\gamma} \tilde {\pmb {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\phi}}
$$
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} = - \mathrm {e} ^ {- \beta} \gamma^ {\prime} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}}
$$
$$
\tilde {\pmb {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \hat {\phi} = - \mathrm {e} ^ {- \beta} \gamma^ {\prime} \tilde {\pmb {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\phi}}
$$
$$
\tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\phi}}} = - \cot \theta \mathrm {e} ^ {- \gamma} \tilde {\boldsymbol {\omega}} ^ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\phi}}} .
$$
3ut $\Gamma_{\hat{\mu}\hat{\lambda}}^{\hat{\nu}}\tilde{\omega}^{\hat{\lambda}} = \tilde{\omega}_{\hat{\mu}}^{\hat{\nu}}$ so the nonvanishing connection coefficients are
$$
\begin{array}{r l} \Gamma_ {\widehat {r t}} ^ {\widehat {t}} & = \Gamma_ {\widehat {t t}} ^ {\widehat {r}} = a ^ {\prime} e ^ {- \beta} \end{array}
$$
$$
\Gamma^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} _ {\mathbf {\hat {r r}}} = \Gamma^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} _ {\mathbf {\hat {t r}}} = \dot {\beta} \mathrm {e} ^ {- a}
$$
$$
\Gamma^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} \hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}} = \Gamma_ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} ^ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\theta}}} = \Gamma_ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\phi}}} ^ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} _ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\phi}}} = \Gamma_ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} ^ {\hat {\boldsymbol {\phi}}} _ {\hat {\mathbf {t}}} = \dot {\gamma} \mathbf {e} ^ {- \alpha}
$$
$$
\Gamma^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} _ {\hat {\theta}} \hat {\theta} = - \Gamma_ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} ^ {\hat {\theta}} _ {\hat {\theta}} = \Gamma^ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} _ {\hat {\phi}} \hat {\phi} = - \Gamma^ {\hat {\phi}} _ {\hat {\mathbf {r}}} \hat {\phi} = - \gamma^ {\prime} \mathrm {e} ^ {- \beta}
$$
$$
\Gamma^ {\hat {\theta}} _ {\hat {\phi}} \hat {\phi} = - \Gamma^ {\hat {\phi}} _ {\hat {\theta}} \hat {\phi} = - \cot \theta e ^ {- \gamma}.
$$
