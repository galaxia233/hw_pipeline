Problem 19.22. Suppose that at the time of hydrogen recombination (which, say, occurred at a redshift of $z = 1500$ ) the deceleration parameter was $q = 0.5002$ . What would $q_0$ be today? Repeat for $q = 0.4998$ at $z = 1500$ . (Assume a matter-dominated universe.)

## Solution

Solution 19.22. From the solution of Problem 19.18, if $\mathbf{q}_0 > 0.5$ we must have a $\mathbf{k} = +1$ cosmology which expands according to
$$
R (t) / R _ {0} = \frac {q _ {0}}{2 q _ {0} - 1} (1 - \cos \eta). \tag {1}
$$
Setting $\mathbf{R}(t) = \mathbf{R}_0$ , so that $\eta = \eta_0$ , gives a formula which relates $\eta_0$ to $\mathbf{q}_0$ at the present epoch. The epoch is arbitrary, however, so it also relates $\eta$ and $\mathbf{q}$ at any epoch. Putting $\mathbf{q} = 0.5002$ gives $1 - \cos \eta = .0008$ at $z = 1500$ . Substituting this back into Equation (1) and putting $\mathbf{R}_0 / \mathbf{R}(t) = 1 + z = 1501$ , we can solve for $\mathbf{q}_0$ , getting
$$
\mathrm {q} _ {0} = [ 2 - (1 + z) (1 - \cos \eta) ] ^ {- 1} = 1. 2 5.
$$
If $\mathbf{q}_0 < 0.5$ , the $\mathbf{k} = -1$ analogy of Equation (1) is
$$
R (t) / R _ {0} = \frac {q _ {0}}{1 - 2 q _ {0}} (\cosh \eta - 1) \tag {2}
$$
so $\mathbf{q} = .4998$ gives $\cosh \eta - 1 = .0008$ at $z = 1500$ and
$$
\mathbf {q} _ {0} = [ 2 + (1 + z) (\cosh \eta - 1) ] ^ {- 1} = 0. 3 1 2.
$$
