Problem 15.5. Derive a first-order differential equation for the trajectory (r as a function of $\phi$ ) for equatorial orbits in the Schwarzschild geometry.

## Solution

Solution 15.5. Choose the equatorial plane to be $\theta = \pi /2$ . Then $\mathbf{u}^{\theta} = 0$ . One first integral of the motion is the normalization of the 4-velocity
which implies
$$
\mathrm {g} ^ {0 0} \mathrm {u} _ {0} ^ {2} + \mathrm {g} ^ {\phi \phi} \mathrm {u} _ {\phi} ^ {2} + \mathrm {g} _ {\mathrm {r r}} \mathrm {u} ^ { \mathrm {r} } ^ {2} = - 1. \tag {1}
$$
From the Killing vectors $\partial/\partial t$ and $\partial/\partial \phi$ two more first integrals are
$$
u _ {\phi} = \tilde {L} = \text {c o n s t a n t}
$$
$$
u _ {0} = - \bar {\mathbf {E}} = \text {c o n s t a n t}
$$
where $\tilde{\mathbf{L}}$ and $\tilde{\mathbf{E}}$ are respectively the angular momentum per unit rest mass and energy per unit rest mass. Equation (1) now implies
$$
\left(\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}{\mathrm {d} \tau}\right) ^ {2} = \frac {- 1 - \mathbf {g} ^ {0 0} \tilde {\mathbf {E}} ^ {2} - \mathbf {g} ^ {\phi \phi} \tilde {\mathbf {L}} ^ {2}}{\mathbf {g} _ {\mathbf {r r}}} \tag {2}
$$
or with the explicit metric functions:
$$
\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r} / \mathrm {d} \tau = \pm \left\{\left[ - 1 + \tilde {\mathrm {E}} ^ {2} \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {m}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) ^ {- 1} - \frac {\tilde {\mathrm {L}} ^ {2}}{\mathrm {r} ^ {2}} \right] \left(1 - \frac {2 \mathrm {m}}{\mathrm {r}}\right) \right\} ^ {\frac {1}{2}}. \tag {3}
$$
From $\mathbf{u}^{\phi} = \mathbf{g}^{\phi}\tilde{\mathbf{L}}$ we have $\mathrm{d}\phi /\mathrm{d}\tau = \tilde{\mathrm{L}} /\mathrm{r}^2$ . Combining this with Equation (3) gives us finally
$$
\frac {\mathrm {d} \mathbf {r}}{\mathrm {d} \phi} = \pm \frac {\mathbf {r} ^ {2}}{\tilde {\mathbf {L}}} \left\{\left[ - 1 + \tilde {\mathbf {E}} ^ {2} (1 - 2 \mathbf {M} / \mathbf {r}) ^ {- 1} - \frac {\tilde {\mathbf {L}} ^ {2}}{\mathbf {r} ^ {2}} \right] (1 - 2 \mathbf {M} / \mathbf {r}) \right\} ^ {\frac {1}{2}}.
